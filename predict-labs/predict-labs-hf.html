🧪 Heart Failure: HFrEF vs HFpEF

Predict what labs you'd order and what results you'd expect before seeing the data.

Lab / FindingInterpretation
BNP / NT-proBNP↑↑ = volume overload / ventricular wall stress. BNP >400 = likely HF. <100 = HF unlikely. Useful to distinguish cardiac vs pulmonary dyspnea
Echo (EF)EF <40% = HFrEF; EF ≥50% = HFpEF; 40-49% = HFmrEF
CXRCephalization (upper lobe diversion), Kerley B lines, pleural effusions, cardiomegaly, pulmonary edema ('bat wing')
TroponinMay be mildly elevated in acute decompensation (demand ischemia) — does not necessarily indicate MI
BMPCheck Na (dilutional hyponatremia = poor prognosis), K (diuretics deplete), Cr/BUN (cardiorenal syndrome)

🎯 Clinical Application

Practice Strategy: When presented with a clinical scenario, mentally predict the expected lab values BEFORE looking at results. This builds clinical reasoning — pattern → hypothesis → expected findings → compare with actual.
