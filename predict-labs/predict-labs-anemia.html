🧪 Anemia: Microcytic vs Normocytic vs Macrocytic

Predict what labs you'd order and what results you'd expect before seeing the data.

Lab / FindingInterpretation
MCV<80 = microcytic (iron def, thalassemia, chronic disease, sideroblastic); >100 = macrocytic (B12/folate def, liver disease)
Reticulocyte count↑ = adequate marrow response (hemolysis, acute blood loss); ↓ = underproduction
Iron studiesLow iron + low ferritin + high TIBC = iron deficiency; Low iron + HIGH ferritin + low TIBC = chronic disease
Peripheral smearTarget cells = thalassemia/liver; Spherocytes = hereditary spherocytosis/autoimmune hemolysis; Schistocytes = TTP/HUS/DIC
LDH / Haptoglobin / Indirect bili↑LDH + ↓haptoglobin + ↑indirect bili = hemolysis

🎯 Clinical Application

Practice Strategy: When presented with a clinical scenario, mentally predict the expected lab values BEFORE looking at results. This builds clinical reasoning — pattern → hypothesis → expected findings → compare with actual.
