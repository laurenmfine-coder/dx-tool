🦵 Deep Vein Thrombosis

Clinical Features

  • • Unilateral leg swelling (>3cm difference)
  • • Pain, warmth, erythema
  • • Pitting edema
  • • Palpable cord
  • • Homan's sign (unreliable)
⚠️ 50% are asymptomatic!

Proximal vs Distal

Proximal (above knee)
Iliac, femoral, popliteal. Higher PE risk → Treat!
Distal (below knee)
Calf veins. Lower risk → Serial US or treat if symptomatic

🫁 Pulmonary Embolism

Clinical Features

  • • Dyspnea (most common)
  • • Pleuritic chest pain
  • • Tachycardia, tachypnea
  • • Hypoxia (may have normal O2!)
  • • Cough, hemoptysis
  • • Syncope (suggests massive PE)
  • • Leg symptoms (only 50%)

PE Severity Classification

Low-risk: Hemodynamically stable, no RV strain
Submassive: Stable but RV dysfunction (echo/CT) or elevated troponin/BNP
Massive: Hypotension (SBP <90) or requiring pressors

ECG Findings
• Sinus tachycardia (most common)
• S1Q3T3 (classic but rare)
• Right heart strain pattern
• New RBBB
• T wave inversions V1-V4
CXR Findings
• Often normal!
• Hampton's hump (wedge infarct)
• Westermark sign (oligemia)
• Fleischner sign (large PA)
• Atelectasis, effusion
CT-PA Findings
• Filling defect in PA
• RV enlargement (RV:LV >1)
• Septal bowing into LV
• Reflux of contrast into IVC

💊 Treatment

Anticoagulation Options

DOACs (preferred):
Apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, dabigatran. No bridging needed for apixaban/rivaroxaban
LMWH:
Enoxaparin. Preferred in cancer, pregnancy
UFH:
If massive PE, renal failure, or need for reversal
Warfarin:
If on it already; needs bridging. Goal INR 2-3

Duration of Treatment

Provoked (surgery, trauma, immobility):
3 months
Unprovoked (no clear cause):
≥3 months, then assess risk/benefit of indefinite
Cancer-associated:
Indefinite (while cancer active). LMWH or DOAC
Recurrent unprovoked:
Indefinite anticoagulation

⚠️ Massive PE: Consider Thrombolytics

Indications:
  • • Hemodynamic instability (SBP <90, shock)
  • • Cardiac arrest from PE
  • • Consider in submassive with deterioration
Options:
  • • Systemic tPA (alteplase 100mg over 2h)
  • • Catheter-directed thrombolysis
  • • Surgical embolectomy (last resort)

IVC Filter Indications

Absolute
  • • Acute VTE + absolute contraindication to anticoagulation
  • • Recurrent PE despite therapeutic anticoagulation
Relative/Controversial
  • • Massive PE with residual DVT
  • • Poor cardiopulmonary reserve
  • • Free-floating iliofemoral thrombus
Retrievable filters preferred. Remove when anticoagulation can be started.
