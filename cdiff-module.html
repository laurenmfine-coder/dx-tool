⚙️ Clostridioides difficile Infection

Pathophysiology | Infectious Disease

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Toxin-mediated disease; disruption of normal flora is key risk factor
  • Test for toxin (not just organism) - PCR alone may detect colonization
  • Oral vancomycin or fidaxomicin first-line (not metronidazole)
  • Stop inciting antibiotic if possible
  • Fecal transplant for recurrent disease

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Toxin-Mediated Colitis

C. diff toxins cause mucosal damage and inflammation.

Pathway

Antibiotic disruption of gut flora→C. diff overgrowth→Toxin A (enterotoxin) + Toxin B (cytotoxin)→Epithelial damage + inflammation→Pseudomembrane formation

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Watery diarrhea after antibiotics

Mechanism: Toxin-induced secretory diarrhea

Significance: Classic presentation; can occur up to 8 weeks after antibiotics

Pseudomembranes on colonoscopy

Mechanism: Inflammatory exudate + necrotic debris

Significance: Pathognomonic but not always present; colonoscopy not always needed

Toxic megacolon

Mechanism: Severe inflammation → colonic dilation + systemic toxicity

Significance: Surgical emergency; colectomy may be needed
