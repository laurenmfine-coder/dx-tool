About These Tools

These interactive tools are designed to help medical students develop clinical reasoning skills beyond traditional didactic learning. Each tool focuses on different aspects of clinical decision-making:

Clinical Reasoning Tool

Focuses on the systematic approach to patient encounters: generating differentials from limited information, selecting high-yield history questions, and performing targeted physical exams. Ideal for OSCE preparation.

Environmental Dx Tool

Emphasizes how context matters in diagnosis. Traditional imaging and lab reports don't capture environmental exposures, occupational hazards, or social factors that significantly impact differential diagnosis.
