🫁 Respiratory Failure

Two Types of Respiratory Failure

Type 1: Hypoxemic
PaO₂ < 60 mmHg on room air
Mechanism:
Problem with O₂ getting into blood
A-a gradient usually INCREASED
Causes:
  • • V/Q mismatch (most common)
  • • Shunt (blood bypasses alveoli)
  • • Diffusion impairment
  • • Low inspired O₂ (altitude)
Examples: Pneumonia, ARDS, PE, pulmonary edema
Type 2: Hypercapnic
PaCO₂ > 45 mmHg
Mechanism:
Problem with ventilation (CO₂ removal)
A-a gradient often NORMAL
Causes:
  • • CNS depression (drugs, stroke)
  • • Neuromuscular (GBS, MG, ALS)
  • • Chest wall (obesity, kyphosis)
  • • Airway obstruction (COPD, asthma)
Think: Can't move air = CO₂ builds up

The A-a Gradient: Your Diagnostic Friend

A-a = PAO₂ - PaO₂
PAO₂ = (FiO₂ × 713) - (PaCO₂ / 0.8)
Normal A-a = 2.5 + (Age/4) ... roughly < 15 if young, < 25 if elderly
Normal A-a Gradient
Lungs are fine! Problem is:
  • • Hypoventilation (CNS, NM, chest wall)
  • • Low inspired O₂ (altitude)
Elevated A-a Gradient
Lung parenchyma problem:
  • • V/Q mismatch
  • • Shunt
  • • Diffusion impairment

5 Mechanisms of Hypoxemia

1. V/Q Mismatch (Most common!)
↑ A-a

Ventilation and perfusion don't match. Some areas over-perfused (shunt-like), some under-perfused (dead space).

Examples: COPD, asthma, pneumonia, PE

✓ Responds to supplemental O₂

2. Shunt
↑ A-a

Blood bypasses ventilated alveoli entirely. "Wasted" perfusion.

Examples: ARDS, pneumonia (consolidated), AVM, intracardiac shunt

✗ Does NOT respond well to O₂ (blood never sees it!)

3. Diffusion Impairment
↑ A-a

Thickened alveolar-capillary membrane slows O₂ transfer.

Examples: Pulmonary fibrosis, ILD

✓ Responds to O₂, worsens with exercise

4. Hypoventilation
Normal A-a

Not breathing enough → CO₂ rises → O₂ falls (but lung itself is fine)

Examples: Opioids, obesity hypoventilation, NM disease

✓ Responds to O₂ (and ventilatory support)

5. Low Inspired O₂
Normal A-a

Less O₂ in inspired air → Less O₂ in blood

Examples: High altitude, confined space

✓ Responds to supplemental O₂

ARDS: Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome

Berlin Criteria

  • • Timing: Within 1 week of insult
  • • CXR: Bilateral opacities (not effusions/nodules)
  • • Origin: Not fully explained by cardiac failure
  • • Oxygenation: P/F ratio ≤ 300 on PEEP ≥ 5

Severity by P/F Ratio

Mild:200-300
Moderate:100-200
Severe:< 100

ARDS Mechanism

Insult (sepsis, aspiration, trauma) → Inflammatory cascade → Alveolar-capillary damage → Protein-rich edema → Surfactant dysfunction → Atelectasis + Shunt

ARDS Management Principles

  • • Low tidal volume: 6 mL/kg ideal body weight (lung protective)
  • • Plateau pressure: Keep < 30 cmH₂O
  • • PEEP: Keep alveoli open (recruit collapsed units)
  • • Prone positioning: Improves V/Q matching in severe ARDS
  • • Conservative fluids: Avoid worsening pulmonary edema
