📖 Teaching Point

CLE embryology: During branching morphogenesis (weeks 5-17), the airways divide 23 times. Bronchial cartilage develops to support the airways. In CLE, deficient or absent cartilage in a lobar bronchus causes the airway to collapse during expiration → air trapping → progressive hyperinflation. The alveoli are structurally normal but overinflated. This differs from CPAM, where the alveolar architecture is abnormal (hamartomatous proliferation).

📖 Teaching Point

Lung development stages: 1) Embryonic (weeks 3-7): lung bud from foregut endoderm, trachea separates from esophagus. 2) Pseudoglandular (weeks 5-17): ALL conducting airways complete (23 generations), bronchial cartilage/smooth muscle develop. 3) Canalicular (weeks 16-26): respiratory bronchioles, vascularization, type I and II pneumocytes differentiate, first surfactant at 20 weeks. 4) Saccular (weeks 24-36): terminal sacs expand, air-blood barrier thins → viability. 5) Alveolar (36 weeks-8 years): alveolar septation, 50M at birth → 300M by age 8.

📖 Teaching Point

Congenital lung lesion comparison: CLE = normal alveoli, deficient cartilage, air trapping, LUL most common, surgery if symptomatic. CPAM = hamartomatous proliferation of terminal bronchioles, cystic or solid, pleuropulmonary blastoma risk → typically resected. Sequestration = aberrant lung tissue with systemic arterial supply, not connected to airways. Intralobar (within pleura, presents with recurrent infections) vs extralobar (own pleura, 60% associated with CDH). Hybrid lesions (features of both CPAM and sequestration) exist.

📖 Teaching Point

Surfactant: Produced by Type II pneumocytes. Composed primarily of dipalmitoylphosphatidylcholine (DPPC/lecithin). Reduces surface tension at air-liquid interface, preventing alveolar collapse. L/S ratio ≥2 = mature lungs (amniocentesis). Phosphatidylglycerol (PG) present = mature. Surfactant deficiency → neonatal respiratory distress syndrome (RDS/HMD). Treatment: exogenous surfactant (beractant, calfactant, poractant). Prevention: antenatal betamethasone (two doses 24h apart) for anticipated preterm delivery 24-34 weeks.

📖 Teaching Point

Foregut derivatives (endoderm-lined): Pharynx, esophagus, stomach, proximal duodenum (to ampulla of Vater), liver, gallbladder, pancreas, trachea/bronchi/lungs. The respiratory system is a ventral outgrowth of the foregut — this is why TEF occurs (failed separation). Midgut: distal duodenum to proximal 2/3 transverse colon (SMA supply). Hindgut: distal 1/3 transverse colon to upper anal canal (IMA supply). Remember: endoderm forms the epithelial LINING; mesoderm forms the muscular wall and vasculature.
