MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Virchow's Triad underlies venous thromboembolism (VTE): stasis (immobility, heart failure), endothelial injury (surgery, trauma), and hypercoagulability (malignancy, Factor V Leiden, pregnancy).

🔑 Key Concept: DVT most commonly originates in the deep veins of the calf (posterior tibial, peroneal) and propagates proximally. Only proximal DVTs (popliteal and above) carry significant PE risk. About 50% of proximal DVTs lead to PE if untreated.

⚠️ PE Pathophysiology: Thrombus embolizes to pulmonary arteries → increased pulmonary vascular resistance → acute right heart strain → RV dilation → decreased LV filling → hemodynamic collapse. Massive PE = cardiovascular collapse with >50% vascular bed obstruction.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis

DVT Presentation

Unilateral leg swelling, pain, warmth, erythema. Homan sign (calf pain with dorsiflexion) is unreliable. Use Wells score for DVT to stratify risk.

PE Presentation

FeatureDetails
SymptomsDyspnea (most common), pleuritic chest pain, cough, hemoptysis, syncope
SignsTachypnea (most common sign), tachycardia, hypoxia, JVD (RV failure)
Massive PEHypotension (SBP <90), syncope, PEA/cardiac arrest

3. High risk / positive D-dimerCompression ultrasound (gold standard)CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA) (gold standard)
4. If CTPA contraindicated—V/Q scan (best if normal CXR)
🔑 D-dimer: High sensitivity, low specificity. Useful for ruling OUT VTE in low-risk patients. Elevated in pregnancy, cancer, infection, post-op — use age-adjusted cutoff (age × 10 µg/L for patients >50).

⚠️ Massive PE Management: Hemodynamically unstable (SBP <90) → systemic thrombolytics (tPA/alteplase). Contraindication to thrombolytics → catheter-directed therapy or surgical embolectomy. Start heparin immediately while arranging definitive therapy.

IVC Filter Indications

Absolute contraindication to anticoagulation (active bleeding) OR recurrent PE despite adequate anticoagulation. Retrievable filters preferred — remove once anticoagulation can resume.
