⚙️ Croup (Laryngotracheobronchitis)

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Pediatrics

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Parainfluenza virus is the most common cause
  • Dexamethasone (single dose) is the treatment of choice
  • Nebulized epinephrine for moderate-severe cases (observe 2-4 hours)
  • Peak age 6 months to 3 years; worse at night

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Subglottic Inflammation

Viral infection causes edema at the narrowest point of the pediatric airway.

Pathway

Parainfluenza virus (most common)→ Subglottic mucosal inflammation→ Edema at cricoid ring (narrowest point)→ Inspiratory stridor→ Barking cough

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Barking (seal-like) cough

Mechanism: Air forced through narrowed subglottic area

Clinical Significance: Pathognomonic for croup

Inspiratory stridor

Mechanism: Turbulent flow through narrowed extrathoracic airway

Clinical Significance: Indicates upper airway obstruction

Steeple sign on XR

Mechanism: Subglottic narrowing creates pointed appearance

Clinical Significance: Classic radiographic finding (XR not routinely needed)
