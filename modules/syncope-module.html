MECHANISM
Classification

Syncope = transient loss of consciousness from global cerebral hypoperfusion with rapid, spontaneous recovery.

TypeMechanismKey Features
Vasovagal (neurocardiogenic)Excessive vagal tone → bradycardia + vasodilationMOST COMMON overall. Triggers: prolonged standing, emotional stress, pain. Prodrome: lightheadedness, warmth, nausea, diaphoresis, pallor
OrthostaticInadequate BP response to standingDrop ≥20 SBP or ≥10 DBP within 3 min of standing. Causes: dehydration, medications (BBs, ACEi, diuretics, alpha-blockers), autonomic neuropathy (DM, Parkinson)
Cardiac — ArrhythmicTachyarrhythmia or bradyarrhythmiaSudden onset without prodrome. VT, SVT, complete heart block, sick sinus, long QT → torsades
Cardiac — StructuralObstruction to outflowAortic stenosis, HCM, PE, cardiac tamponade, atrial myxoma. EXERTIONAL syncope = high risk
NeurologicCerebrovascular (rare true syncope)Vertebrobasilar insufficiency, subclavian steal. Seizure is NOT syncope (post-ictal state, tongue-biting, incontinence, tonic-clonic activity)
⚠️ CARDIAC syncope = highest mortality. Red flags: exertional syncope, syncope while supine/sitting, preceding palpitations, family hx of sudden death, structural heart disease, abnormal EKG. These patients need urgent workup.
CLINICAL
Evaluation

High-Yield Workup

TestWhen
12-lead EKGALL patients with syncope (identifies arrhythmia, long QT, Brugada, WPW, heart block)
Orthostatic vitalsALL patients (lying → standing: check BP/HR at 0, 1, 3 min)
EchocardiogramSuspected structural disease (murmur, HF signs, exertional syncope)
Continuous monitoring (Holter/event/loop)Suspected arrhythmia not captured on EKG
Tilt table testSuspected vasovagal syncope when diagnosis is unclear
EP studySuspected arrhythmia in high-risk patients with non-diagnostic workup
🔑 San Francisco Syncope Rule (CHESS): CHF hx, Hematocrit <30%, EKG abnormality, Shortness of breath, Systolic BP <90. Any positive = high risk → admit. Useful for risk stratification but doesn't replace clinical judgment.
TREATMENT
Management by Type
TypeManagement
VasovagalReassurance, education (avoid triggers, recognize prodrome, counterpressure maneuvers — leg crossing, hand grip). Hydration + salt. Midodrine for recurrent refractory cases
OrthostaticTreat underlying cause. Hydration, compression stockings, rise slowly, avoid triggers. Fludrocortisone or midodrine if persistent. Review medications
Cardiac — ArrhythmicPacemaker (bradycardia/heart block), ICD (VT/VF/sudden death risk), ablation, antiarrhythmics
Cardiac — StructuralTreat underlying: AVR for AS, myectomy/ablation for HCM, anticoagulation for PE
🔑 Young Athlete with Exertional Syncope: Think HCM (#1 cause of sudden cardiac death in young athletes). Family hx of sudden death + systolic murmur that increases with Valsalva/standing. Workup: echo (asymmetric septal hypertrophy). Management: activity restriction, ICD if high risk.
