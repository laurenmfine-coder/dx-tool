MECHANISM
MSE Components

The Mental Status Examination (MSE) is the psychiatric equivalent of the physical exam. It is an objective, structured assessment of a patient's current mental functioning at the time of interview. It is NOT the patient's history\u2014it is what the examiner observes.

The MSE Components

ComponentWhat to AssessKey Terminology
AppearanceGrooming, hygiene, dress, age appearance, body habitus, notable featuresWell-groomed, disheveled, malodorous, age-appropriate
Behavior/PsychomotorEye contact, cooperation, agitation, retardation, mannerisms, ticsPsychomotor retardation, agitation, catatonia, akathisia
SpeechRate, rhythm, volume, tone, spontaneityPressured, impoverished, monotone, dysarthric, latency
MoodPatient's SUBJECTIVE emotional state (in their words)"I feel sad," "anxious," "great"\u2014use patient's own words in quotes
AffectOBJECTIVE emotional expression observed by examinerRange (full, constricted, flat), quality (euthymic, dysphoric, euphoric), congruence, reactivity, lability
Thought ProcessHOW the patient thinks (form of thought)Linear, circumstantial, tangential, loose associations, flight of ideas, word salad, thought blocking, clang associations
Thought ContentWHAT the patient thinks aboutDelusions, obsessions, phobias, suicidal/homicidal ideation, ideas of reference, paranoia
PerceptionsSensory experiences without external stimuliAuditory/visual/tactile hallucinations, illusions, depersonalization, derealization
CognitionOrientation, attention, memory, abstractionAlert and oriented x4, intact attention (serial 7s), intact recent/remote memory
InsightPatient's awareness of their illnessGood, fair, poor, absent
JudgmentPatient's decision-making abilityGood, fair, poor, impaired
\U0001f511 Mood vs Affect: This is the #1 tested MSE distinction. Mood = subjective (what the patient says they feel; always in quotes). Affect = objective (what the examiner observes). They may be incongruent: a patient says "I'm fine" (mood) while crying (affect = tearful, incongruent with stated mood).
CLINICAL
Thought Process Abnormalities

Thought Process Terminology

TermDefinitionClassic Association
CircumstantialExcessive detail but eventually reaches the pointAnxiety, OCD, personality disorders
TangentialGoes off-topic and NEVER returns to the pointMania, psychosis
Loose associationsIdeas shift between unrelated topics without logical connectionSchizophrenia
Flight of ideasRapid shifting between topics with some tangential connectionMania
Word saladCompletely incoherent, no discernible meaningSevere psychosis
Thought blockingSudden interruption of thought mid-sentenceSchizophrenia
Clang associationsWords connected by sound rather than meaning ("cat, hat, bat")Mania, psychosis
NeologismsMade-up words with personal meaningSchizophrenia
PerseverationRepetition of same response despite new stimuliOrganic brain disease, psychosis
\U0001f511 Circumstantial vs Tangential: The key difference is destination. Circumstantial = takes detours but ARRIVES at the point. Tangential = goes off course and NEVER returns. Think of it as: circumstantial takes the scenic route; tangential gets lost.

Types of Delusions

TypeDefinitionExample
Paranoid/PersecutoryBelief that others intend harm"The FBI is monitoring my phone"
GrandioseInflated sense of power or identity"I am the chosen one sent to save humanity"
ErotomanicBelief that someone famous is in love with them"The news anchor sends me secret messages of love"
SomaticFalse belief about body function"My organs are rotting inside me"
Ideas of referenceBelief that random events are personally directed"The TV broadcast was speaking directly to me"
Thought insertion/withdrawal/broadcastingBelief that thoughts are placed in, removed from, or broadcast from their mindSchneiderian first-rank symptoms of schizophrenia
\u26a0\ufe0f Always assess safety: Every MSE must document suicidal ideation (SI) and homicidal ideation (HI). Ask directly: "Are you having thoughts of hurting yourself or others?" Direct questioning does NOT increase risk of suicide\u2014this is a well-established finding.
TREATMENT
MSE in Clinical Context

Pattern Recognition by Diagnosis

DiagnosisClassic MSE Pattern
Major DepressionPsychomotor retardation, poor eye contact, impoverished speech, mood "sad," flat/constricted affect, ruminations about guilt/worthlessness, possible SI
ManiaPsychomotor agitation, pressured speech, mood "great/on top of the world," euphoric/labile affect, flight of ideas, grandiose delusions, decreased need for sleep
SchizophreniaPoor grooming, flat/blunted affect, loose associations, auditory hallucinations, paranoid delusions, poor insight
DeliriumFluctuating attention, disorientation, visual hallucinations, psychomotor agitation OR hypoactivity, incoherent speech
DementiaIntact attention early, impaired memory (especially recent), confabulation (Korsakoff), possible word-finding difficulty
\U0001f511 Auditory vs Visual hallucinations: Auditory hallucinations are more common in psychiatric illness (especially schizophrenia). Visual hallucinations should raise suspicion for ORGANIC causes: delirium, intoxication, withdrawal, Lewy body dementia, or seizures. This distinction is frequently tested.

Documenting the MSE

The MSE should be documented in a systematic format. Use objective, descriptive language. Avoid judgmental terms. Report mood in the patient's own words (in quotes). Describe affect using standard terminology. Document the presence or absence of SI/HI explicitly.
