🔴 Bladder Cancer

Hematuria Workup, Staging & BCG Therapy

📚 Overview

Bladder cancer is the most common malignancy of the urinary tract. ~90% are urothelial (transitional cell) carcinoma. Most present with painless gross hematuria.

"Field defect" concept: The entire urothelium is exposed to carcinogens in urine. Patients with bladder TCC are at risk for upper tract TCC and vice versa. ~5% will develop upper tract tumors.

🔍 Clinical Presentation

Symptoms

  • Painless gross hematuria - most common (85%); often intermittent
  • Microscopic hematuria
  • Irritative voiding symptoms (frequency, urgency) - especially CIS
  • Flank pain (if ureteral obstruction)
  • Advanced: weight loss, bone pain, pelvic mass
⚠️ Key Principle: Any patient with gross hematuria needs a complete urologic workup (cystoscopy + upper tract imaging) regardless of whether bleeding stops or other cause is found.

📷 Diagnosis

Hematuria Workup

HEMATURIA WORKUP ↓ Urinalysis (confirm RBCs, rule out infection) ↓ Imaging: CT urogram (upper tracts + bladder) ↓ Cystoscopy (direct visualization of bladder) ↓ If tumor seen → TURBT (transurethral resection)

Cystoscopy Findings

  • Papillary: Frond-like projections (most common appearance)
  • Sessile: Broad-based, solid
  • Flat (CIS): Velvety red patches; high-grade by definition

TURBT (Transurethral Resection of Bladder Tumor)

TURBT is DIAGNOSTIC and THERAPEUTIC:
  • Resects visible tumor
  • Provides tissue for histology and staging
  • MUST include detrusor muscle to assess invasion
  • Repeat TURBT in 4-6 weeks for high-grade Ta, T1, or incomplete resection

Key Distinction: Muscle invasion (T2+) changes management dramatically:
  • NMIBC (Ta, Tis, T1) → TURBT + intravesical therapy; bladder preserved
  • MIBC (T2+) → Radical cystectomy ± chemotherapy

💊 Treatment: Non-Muscle Invasive (NMIBC)

Risk Stratification

RiskFeaturesTreatment
LowSingle, small, low-grade TaTURBT alone; surveillance
IntermediateMultiple/recurrent low-grade Ta; large tumorsTURBT + intravesical chemo (mitomycin)
HighHigh-grade Ta, T1, CISTURBT + BCG induction + maintenance

Intravesical Therapies

BCG (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin):
  • Live attenuated Mycobacterium bovis
  • Induces immune response against tumor
  • Most effective intravesical therapy for high-risk NMIBC
  • Induction: Weekly x6 weeks
  • Maintenance: Essential for efficacy (monthly → quarterly)
Side effects: Dysuria, frequency, flu-like symptoms, BCG sepsis (rare but serious)
Mitomycin C:
  • Chemotherapy agent
  • Single dose post-TURBT reduces recurrence
  • Used for low/intermediate risk or BCG shortage
⚠️ BCG Contraindications:
  • Gross hematuria (risk of systemic absorption)
  • Traumatic catheterization
  • Active UTI
  • Immunocompromised patients
  • Within 2 weeks of TURBT

🔪 Treatment: Muscle-Invasive (MIBC)

Standard of Care

MUSCLE-INVASIVE BLADDER CANCER (T2+) ↓ Staging workup (CT chest/abd/pelvis, ± PET, ± bone scan) ↓ Neoadjuvant chemotherapy (cisplatin-based) ↓ RADICAL CYSTECTOMY • Men: Remove bladder, prostate, seminal vesicles • Women: Remove bladder, uterus, ovaries, anterior vagina • Pelvic lymph node dissection ↓ Urinary diversion: • Ileal conduit (most common) • Neobladder (orthotopic) • Continent cutaneous reservoir

Why Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy?

  • 5% absolute survival benefit
  • Treats micrometastatic disease
  • Better tolerated before surgery
  • Cisplatin-based (MVAC or gemcitabine/cisplatin)

Alternatives for Non-Surgical Candidates

  • Trimodal therapy: TURBT + radiation + chemotherapy
  • Bladder preservation option
  • Requires close surveillance

📈 Surveillance

NMIBC Surveillance

RiskCystoscopy Schedule
Low risk3 months, then yearly if negative
Intermediate3 months x2 years, then 6-12 monthly
High risk3 months x2 years, then 6 months x2 years, then yearly
Recurrence rates: NMIBC has high recurrence rate (50-70%). Lifelong surveillance is required.

🧠 Quick Quiz

A 65-year-old smoker presents with painless gross hematuria. Cystoscopy shows a papillary tumor. TURBT pathology shows high-grade urothelial carcinoma invading lamina propria with detrusor muscle present and uninvolved. What is the stage?

A. Ta
B. T1
C. T2
D. Tis
Correct: B - T1
T1 invades the lamina propria (submucosa) but NOT the muscle. The presence of uninvolved detrusor muscle confirms adequate sampling and that there is no muscle invasion. This is high-risk NMIBC requiring BCG therapy.
