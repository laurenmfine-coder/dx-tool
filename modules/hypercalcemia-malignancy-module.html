⚙️ Hypercalcemia of Malignancy

Oncology

🔬 Pathophysiology

PTHrP-Mediated (80%)

Tumor secretes PTH-related peptide mimicking PTH.

Tumor produces PTHrP→Binds PTH receptor→Osteoclast activation→Renal Ca reabsorption→Hypercalcemia

Osteolytic Mets (20%)

Direct bone destruction releases calcium.

Bone metastases→Local osteoclast activation→Bone resorption→Calcium release→Hypercalcemia

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Stones, bones, groans, moans

Mechanism: Multi-organ effects of hypercalcemia

Shortened QT interval

Mechanism: Calcium speeds cardiac repolarization

🔑 Key Points

✓ PTHrP (80%) vs osteolytic mets (20%)
✓ Common: squamous lung, breast, renal, myeloma
✓ IV saline FIRST, then bisphosphonates
✓ PTH is suppressed (low)
✓ Poor prognosis marker
