Teaching Point

PUV: ONLY occurs in MALES (the prostatic urethra is a male structure). Incidence: ~1/5000-8000 male births. Most common type: Type I (most common) — posterior folds radiating from the verumontanum. The obstruction is downstream of the bladder → everything upstream dilates: bladder wall thickens (detrusor hypertrophy), ureters dilate, kidneys swell (hydronephrosis). Fetal urine is the primary source of amniotic fluid after 16 weeks → obstruction → oligohydramnios.

Teaching Point

Potter sequence components: (1) PULMONARY HYPOPLASIA — most lethal; (2) FLATTENED FACIES — recessed chin, flattened nose, low-set ears from compression; (3) LIMB DEFORMITIES — clubfoot, joint contractures from compression; (4) GROWTH RESTRICTION. Causes of severe oligohydramnios: bilateral renal agenesis (classic Potter syndrome), PUV, bilateral MCDK, prolonged premature rupture of membranes (PPROM), uteroplacental insufficiency. Note: amniotic fluid source shifts from fetal skin transudation (early) to fetal URINE (after 16 weeks).

Teaching Point

Cloacal division: The cloaca is the common cavity for the urogenital and GI systems. The urorectal septum divides it by week 7. ANTERIOR: urogenital sinus → upper (vesical) part → bladder; middle (pelvic) part → prostatic + membranous urethra (M) or entire urethra (F); lower (phallic) part → penile urethra (M) or vestibule (F). POSTERIOR: anorectal canal → rectum + anal canal. The perineal body marks where the urorectal septum meets the cloacal membrane.

Teaching Point

PUV management: (1) IMMEDIATE: catheter drainage (decompress bladder); (2) DEFINITIVE: primary endoscopic valve ablation when infant is stable; (3) IF TOO SMALL: vesicostomy (cutaneous diversion); (4) LONG-TERM: monitor renal function, bladder function (many develop 'valve bladder' — poor compliance, incomplete emptying). Prognosis depends on: degree of renal dysplasia (from prolonged obstruction), nadir serum creatinine after decompression (<1.0 mg/dL by 1 year = good prognosis).

Teaching Point

GDNF/RET signaling: THE critical initiating event for permanent kidney development. GDNF (ligand, from MM) → RET (receptor, on UB) → UB outgrowth + branching. Loss of function: RET mutations → renal agenesis (bilateral = fatal Potter; unilateral = compensatory hypertrophy). Gain of function: RET mutations → MEN2A (medullary thyroid carcinoma + pheochromocytoma + hyperparathyroidism) or MEN2B (medullary thyroid carcinoma + pheochromocytoma + mucosal neuromas + marfanoid habitus). Same gene, opposite effect depending on mutation type!

Key Takeaways

✓Triad: bilateral hydronephrosis + megacystis + oligohydramnios in a MALE
✓Potter sequence: pulmonary hypoplasia (lethal) + flat facies + limb deformities
✓Fetal urine = main amniotic fluid source after 16 weeks → obstruction → oligohydramnios
✓Treatment: catheter decompression → endoscopic valve ablation (definitive)
✓GDNF/RET: essential for UB outgrowth; loss → renal agenesis; gain → MEN2
