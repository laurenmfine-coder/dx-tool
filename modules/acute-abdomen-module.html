Approach to Acute Abdominal Pain

The acute abdomen requires systematic evaluation to identify surgical emergencies. Peritoneal signs (guarding, rigidity, rebound) suggest perforation, ischemia, or severe inflammation requiring urgent intervention.

CLINICAL
Surgical Emergencies
⚠️ Cannot-Miss Diagnoses:
1. Perforated viscus: sudden severe pain → rigid abdomen → free air on upright CXR/CT. Surgery STAT
2. Mesenteric ischemia: pain out of proportion to exam. Elderly + A-fib. CT angiography. Mortality 60-80%
3. AAA rupture: hypotension + pulsatile mass + back pain. Unstable → OR immediately
4. Ectopic pregnancy: reproductive-age female + pain → β-hCG FIRST
5. Strangulated obstruction: SBO with fever, peritoneal signs, lactic acidosis → OR

Appendicitis

Most common surgical emergency. Periumbilical pain migrating to RLQ (McBurney point), anorexia, low-grade fever. Signs: Rovsing (RLQ pain with LLQ palpation), Psoas sign (retrocecal), Obturator sign (pelvic). CT sensitivity >95%. Treatment: laparoscopic appendectomy.

🔑 Peritoneal Signs: Involuntary guarding (board-like rigidity — most reliable sign), rebound tenderness, pain with coughing. Indicates surgical peritonitis until proven otherwise.

🔑 Decision Rule: Peritoneal signs + unstable = emergent OR. Peritoneal signs + stable = CT then OR. No peritoneal signs = workup and observe.
