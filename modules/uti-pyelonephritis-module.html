Classification & Microbiology

UTI Classification

Uncomplicated: lower tract (cystitis) or upper tract (pyelonephritis) in a premenopausal, non-pregnant woman with normal urinary anatomy. Complicated: male, pregnant, structural abnormality, catheter, immunocompromised, recurrent, renal stones.

Organisms

OrganismKey Facts
E. coli (75-95%)Most common cause of UTI. P-fimbriae adhere to uroepithelium. Produces urease only in some strains.
Staphylococcus saprophyticus2nd most common in young sexually active women. Novobiocin-resistant (distinguishes from S. epidermidis).
Klebsiella pneumoniaeEncapsulated, mucoid. Common in diabetics and catheterized patients.
Proteus mirabilisUrease producer → alkaline urine → STAGHORN calculi (struvite/magnesium ammonium phosphate). Swarming motility on agar.
EnterococcusCommon in catheterized/hospitalized patients. Inherently resistant to cephalosporins.
🔑 Struvite stones: Proteus (and other urease producers: Klebsiella, Pseudomonas, Ureaplasma) split urea → ammonia → alkaline pH → magnesium ammonium phosphate crystals → staghorn calculi (fill the renal pelvis). Coffin-lid crystals on UA.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Treatment

Cystitis

Symptoms: dysuria, frequency, urgency, suprapubic pain. No fever (fever = think pyelonephritis). UA: pyuria (WBC ≥10/hpf), positive leukocyte esterase, positive nitrites (Enterobacteriaceae convert nitrate → nitrite; Enterococcus and Staph do NOT produce nitrites).

Treatment (uncomplicated): Nitrofurantoin 5 days (first-line), TMP-SMX 3 days, fosfomycin single dose. Avoid fluoroquinolones for uncomplicated cystitis (reserve for complicated UTI/pyelonephritis).

Pyelonephritis

Symptoms: fever, flank pain, CVA tenderness + lower UTI symptoms. Get urine culture before antibiotics (not needed in uncomplicated cystitis).

⚠️ Pyelonephritis treatment: Outpatient (mild): ciprofloxacin 7 days or TMP-SMX 14 days (if susceptible). Inpatient (severe/pregnant/vomiting): IV ceftriaxone or fluoroquinolone. Pregnant: cephalosporins or ampicillin + gentamicin (NO fluoroquinolones, NO TMP-SMX in 1st trimester).

Special Populations

Pregnancy: Treat ALL bacteriuria (even asymptomatic) — untreated ASB in pregnancy → 40% progress to pyelonephritis → preterm labor risk. Screen with urine culture at first prenatal visit. Catheter-associated UTI (CAUTI): Most common hospital-acquired infection. Remove/replace catheter + treat based on culture. Don't treat asymptomatic bacteriuria in catheterized patients (except pregnant).
