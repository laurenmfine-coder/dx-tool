MECHANISM
Developmental Domains

Development is assessed across four domains: gross motor, fine motor, language, and social/cognitive. Milestones follow a cephalocaudal (head-to-toe) and proximal-to-distal progression. Board exams test age-specific milestone recognition and red flags for delay.

\u26a0\ufe0f Regression is ALWAYS pathological. Loss of previously acquired milestones (language, motor, social) requires urgent evaluation for neurodegenerative disease, metabolic disorders, Rett syndrome, or CNS pathology. This is never "normal."

Screening Schedule (AAP)

Developmental screening with validated tool (ASQ, PEDS) at 9, 18, and 30 months. Autism-specific screening (M-CHAT-R) at 18 and 24 months. Any parental concern warrants formal evaluation regardless of screening results.

\U0001f511 Autism early signs: No babbling by 12 months, no gesturing by 12 months, no single words by 16 months, no 2-word phrases by 24 months, ANY loss of language/social skills at any age. Early intervention before age 3 significantly improves outcomes.

Age-Based Guidance

AgeKey Guidance Topics
NewbornSafe sleep (back to sleep, firm surface, no soft bedding), breastfeeding support, jaundice monitoring
2-6 moTummy time for motor development, introduction of solids at ~6 months, rear-facing car seat
6-12 moChildproofing, choking hazards, transition to sippy cup, dental care begins with first tooth
12-24 moLimit screen time, read daily, poison prevention, transition to whole milk at 12 months
2-5 yrToilet training readiness (18-24mo), preschool socialization, water safety, bicycle helmets
\U0001f511 Screen time: AAP recommends NO screen time before 18 months (except video chat). 18-24 months: high-quality programming with parent co-viewing only. 2-5 years: limit to 1 hour/day. This is frequently tested in the context of language delay counseling.
