MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Asthma = chronic airway inflammation → bronchial hyperresponsiveness + reversible airflow obstruction + mucus hypersecretion.

Pathologic Triad

1. Bronchospasm (smooth muscle contraction) 2. Airway inflammation (eosinophilic infiltration) 3. Mucus plugging (goblet cell hyperplasia)

🔑 Asthma vs COPD: Asthma = reversible obstruction (FEV1 improves ≥12% and ≥200 mL with bronchodilator), eosinophilic, atopy, young onset, variable symptoms. COPD = fixed obstruction, neutrophilic, smoking history, progressive, FEV1/FVC <0.70 post-bronchodilator.

Triggers

Allergens, viral URI (#1 in children), exercise, cold air, NSAIDs/aspirin (Samter triad: asthma + nasal polyps + aspirin sensitivity), GERD, smoke, occupational exposures.

CLINICAL
Severity Classification
⚠️ Severe/Life-Threatening Exacerbation:
• Cannot speak full sentences, accessory muscle use, RR >30, HR >120
• Silent chest = ominous sign (minimal air movement — too tight to wheeze)
• PEF <25% predicted, SpO2 <92%, PaCO2 normal or rising (sign of fatigue — should be LOW in asthma exacerbation due to hyperventilation. Normal/elevated PaCO2 = impending respiratory failure)
• Altered consciousness = imminent arrest

Step Therapy (Chronic Management)

StepPreferred
1 (Intermittent)PRN low-dose ICS-formoterol (GINA 2023) or PRN SABA
2 (Mild persistent)Low-dose ICS daily
3 (Moderate)Low-dose ICS-LABA
4 (Mod-severe)Medium-dose ICS-LABA
5 (Severe)High-dose ICS-LABA + LAMA ± biologic (omalizumab, mepolizumab, dupilumab)
TREATMENT
Acute Exacerbation Management
LineTreatment
FirstContinuous nebulized albuterol (SABA) + ipratropium (SAMA) × 3 doses in first hour. O2 to maintain SpO2 ≥94%
SecondSystemic corticosteroids within 1 hour (prednisone 40-60 mg PO or methylprednisolone IV). Takes 4-6h for effect
ThirdIV magnesium sulfate 2g over 20 min (smooth muscle relaxation — for severe exacerbation not responding to initial therapy)
FourthEpinephrine (SC/IM), IV terbutaline, heliox (helium-oxygen), ketamine (bronchodilator properties). Consider intubation
🔑 Intubation in Asthma: Last resort — high risk of breath stacking and barotrauma. Use ketamine for sedation (bronchodilator properties). Low RR, prolonged expiratory time. Accept permissive hypercapnia. AVOID histamine-releasing agents (morphine, atracurium).
