Pattern Recognition

A toxidrome is a syndrome of signs/symptoms suggesting a specific class of poisoning. Pattern recognition allows rapid identification and targeted treatment.

CLINICAL
Distinguishing Similar Toxidromes
🔑 Sympathomimetic vs Anticholinergic: Both have mydriasis, tachycardia, agitation. Key distinction: SKIN. Sympathomimetic = DIAPHORETIC (wet). Anticholinergic = DRY. "Dry as a bone" = anticholinergic. "Sweating profusely" = sympathomimetic.
🔑 Opioid vs Sedative-Hypnotic: Both have CNS depression. Key distinction: PUPILS. Opioid = pinpoint miosis. Sedative-hypnotic = normal or mildly small pupils. Give naloxone trial if unsure.

TREATMENT
General Approach & Antidotes

Initial Management of Any Poisoning

ABCs → decontamination (activated charcoal within 1-2h if safe) → specific antidote if available → supportive care.

PoisonAntidote
AcetaminophenN-acetylcysteine (NAC)
OpioidsNaloxone
BenzodiazepinesFlumazenil (use cautiously — seizure risk)
OrganophosphatesAtropine + pralidoxime (2-PAM)
Methanol/ethylene glycolFomepizole (alcohol dehydrogenase inhibitor)
TCAsSodium bicarbonate
β-blockersGlucagon
CCBsCalcium, glucagon, high-dose insulin
WarfarinVitamin K + PCC (4-factor)
DigoxinDigoxin-specific Fab antibodies (Digibind)

Carbon monoxide100% O2 ± hyperbaric
CyanideHydroxocobalamin or nitrite/thiosulfate kit
