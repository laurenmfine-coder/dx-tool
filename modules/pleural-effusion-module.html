Transudative vs Exudative

Pleural effusion = abnormal fluid accumulation in the pleural space. Light criteria differentiate transudative (systemic cause) from exudative (local/pleural cause).

🔑 Light Criteria (Exudate if ANY one met):
1. Pleural protein / Serum protein > 0.5
2. Pleural LDH / Serum LDH > 0.6
3. Pleural LDH > 2/3 upper limit of normal serum LDH
TransudateExudate
CHF (#1 cause overall), cirrhosis (hepatic hydrothorax), nephrotic syndrome, PEPneumonia (parapneumonic), malignancy, TB, PE, rheumatoid/lupus, pancreatitis
Systemic process — treat underlying causeLocal/pleural disease — investigate further
Clear, straw-coloredMay be cloudy, bloody, or purulent

TypeFeaturesManagement

Complicated parapneumonicpH <7.2, glucose <60, LDH >1000, positive Gram stainChest tube drainage required

MalignantBloody, cytology positive. Lung cancer #1, then breast, lymphomaTherapeutic thoracentesis, pleurodesis, indwelling catheter
ChylothoraxMilky fluid, elevated triglycerides (>110 mg/dL). Thoracic duct injuryNPO/TPN (reduce chyle flow), octreotide, surgical ligation if refractory
⚠️ Indications for chest tube in parapneumonic effusion: Pus (empyema), positive Gram stain/culture, pH <7.2, glucose <60 mg/dL, loculated effusion. These will NOT resolve with antibiotics alone.
TREATMENT
Thoracentesis & Management

Thoracentesis

Diagnostic: send for cell count/diff, protein, LDH, glucose, pH, Gram stain/culture, cytology. Therapeutic: relieve symptoms (remove up to 1-1.5L to avoid re-expansion pulmonary edema).

IndicationDetails
DiagnosticNew effusion of unclear etiology, suspected infection
TherapeuticSymptomatic relief of dyspnea
Not neededSmall bilateral effusions in known CHF responding to diuretics
🔑 CXR findings: Blunting of costophrenic angle (>200 mL needed). Lateral decubitus: free-flowing fluid layers dependently (>1 cm = amenable to thoracentesis). Loculated effusion: does NOT layer — may need US-guided drainage. Massive effusion: complete opacification with mediastinal shift AWAY.
