📖 Teaching Point

Neural tube closure occurs during days 21-28 of embryonic development (weeks 3-4 post-fertilization). The cranial neuropore closes day 25, the caudal neuropore closes day 28. Failure of closure → NTDs. Folic acid supplementation BEFORE conception reduces NTD risk by 50-70%, which is why this patient's late start is significant.

📖 Teaching Point

NTD spectrum: Spina bifida occulta (closed, AFP normal) → Meningocele (open, meninges only) → Myelomeningocele (open, neural elements exposed) → Anencephaly (cranial failure). The lemon sign and banana sign on ultrasound are indirect signs of Chiari II malformation, which accompanies nearly all myelomeningoceles. Chiari II = cerebellar tonsillar herniation through the foramen magnum.

📖 Teaching Point

NTD risk factors: (1) Folate deficiency/late supplementation; (2) Medications — valproic acid, carbamazepine, methotrexate; (3) Maternal diabetes (pregestational); (4) Obesity; (5) Hyperthermia (fever, hot tubs in first trimester); (6) Prior NTD-affected pregnancy. Prevention: 0.4 mg folic acid for all women of childbearing age; 4 mg for high-risk women (prior NTD, on antiepileptics).

📖 Teaching Point

MOMS Trial (Management of Myelomeningocele Study, 2011): Prenatal repair at 19-25 weeks vs postnatal repair. Results: VP shunt reduced (40% vs 82%), improved motor outcomes at 30 months, reduced hindbrain herniation. Risks: preterm delivery (79% vs 15%), uterine complications. Criteria: singleton pregnancy, 19-25 weeks GA, myelomeningocele at T1-S1, Chiari II present.

📖 Teaching Point

Immediate postnatal management: (1) Protect the sac — moist sterile dressing, prone positioning; (2) Surgical closure within 24-72 hours; (3) Head circumference monitoring for hydrocephalus; (4) Neurosurgical evaluation for VP shunt need (~80% of myelomeningocele patients); (5) Urology evaluation for neurogenic bladder (clean intermittent catheterization usually started in the first days-weeks).

📖 Teaching Point

Myelomeningocele motor level and ambulatory prognosis: Thoracic = wheelchair-dependent; L1-L2 = exercise/household ambulation; L3-L4 = community ambulation with AFOs; L5-S1 = community ambulation, may need AFOs. Key muscles: L1-L2 (hip flexors), L3 (knee extensors), L4 (ankle dorsiflexors), L5 (toe extensors), S1 (ankle plantarflexors). Higher motor level = worse prognosis.

🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
VP shunt is placed for progressive hydrocephalus. At 3 years, the child walks with bilateral AFOs and uses CIC for bladder management. He's meeting cognitive milestones.

Key Takeaways

✓Neural tube closes days 21-28: cranial neuropore day 25, caudal neuropore day 28
✓Folic acid 0.4 mg for all women; 4 mg for high-risk (BEFORE conception)
✓Elevated MSAFP → open NTD; Low AFP → trisomy 21
✓Myelomeningocele = meninges + neural tissue; Chiari II in nearly all cases
✓MOMS trial: fetal repair reduces VP shunt need (82% → 40%)
✓Urgent postnatal closure within 24-72 hours to prevent meningitis
