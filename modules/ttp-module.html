MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

TTP (Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura): Deficiency of ADAMTS13 (a metalloprotease that cleaves vWF multimers) → ultra-large vWF multimers accumulate → spontaneous platelet aggregation → microvascular thrombi → organ ischemia + MAHA.

🔑 Key Concept: TTP = ADAMTS13 activity <10%. Acquired TTP (autoantibody against ADAMTS13) is far more common than congenital (Upshaw-Schulman syndrome). HUS is caused by Shiga toxin (from EHEC O157:H7) damaging renal endothelium.

⚠️ Classic TTP Pentad: (1) Microangiopathic hemolytic anemia (MAHA), (2) Thrombocytopenia, (3) Renal failure, (4) Neurologic changes, (5) Fever. The full pentad is rare — MAHA + thrombocytopenia alone should raise suspicion. Do NOT wait for all 5 features.

🔑 PLASMIC Score: Clinical prediction tool for TTP — includes platelet count <30K, hemolysis markers, no active cancer, no transplant, MCV <90, INR <1.5, creatinine <2.0. High score → high probability of ADAMTS13 <10%.
TREATMENT
Management

TTP Treatment

InterventionDetails
Plasma exchange (PEX)#1 treatment. Removes autoantibody + replaces ADAMTS13. Start ASAP — mortality drops from >90% to <20%
CorticosteroidsAdjunctive immunosuppression for acquired TTP
CaplacizumabAnti-vWF nanobody — prevents platelet-vWF interaction. Accelerates platelet recovery
RituximabFor refractory/relapsing TTP — targets B cells producing anti-ADAMTS13 antibodies
⚠️ NEVER transfuse platelets in TTP unless life-threatening bleeding. Platelet transfusion is like "adding fuel to the fire" — provides more platelets to form microvascular thrombi. Exception: active intracranial hemorrhage.

HUS Treatment

Typical (STEC-HUS): Supportive care — IV fluids, dialysis if needed. Do NOT give antibiotics (may worsen toxin release). Atypical HUS: Eculizumab (anti-C5 complement inhibitor) is first-line.
