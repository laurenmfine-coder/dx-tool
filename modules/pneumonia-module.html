Classification & Pathogens
TypeSettingCommon Pathogens
Community-Acquired (CAP)Outpatient, no recent hospitalization#1 S. pneumoniae. Atypicals: Mycoplasma (young adults), Legionella (contaminated water, hyponatremia), Chlamydophila
Hospital-Acquired (HAP)≥48h after admissionMRSA, Pseudomonas, Gram-negatives (Klebsiella, E. coli, Acinetobacter)
Ventilator-Associated (VAP)≥48h after intubationSame as HAP. Higher mortality. Prevention: HOB elevation, oral care, sedation vacations
AspirationAltered consciousness, dysphagia, GERDAnaerobes + oral flora. Right lower lobe (most common aspiration site due to straighter right bronchus)
🔑 Klebsiella: Alcoholics, currant jelly sputum, upper lobe cavitation. Pseudomonas: CF, bronchiectasis, immunocompromised. PCP (Pneumocystis): HIV with CD4 <200, bilateral ground-glass opacities, elevated LDH.

Diagnosis & Severity

CURB-65 Severity Score

CriteriaPoints
Confusion (new onset)1
Uremia (BUN >20)1
Respiratory rate ≥301
BP (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60)1
Age ≥651

0-1: outpatient. 2: consider admission. 3-5: ICU consideration.

⚠️ ICU Criteria (ATS/IDSA): Major: septic shock requiring vasopressors OR mechanical ventilation. Minor: RR ≥30, PaO2/FiO2 ≤250, multilobar infiltrates, confusion, BUN ≥20, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, hypothermia, hypotension requiring fluids. 1 major or ≥3 minor = ICU.
TREATMENT
Antibiotic Selection
SettingTreatment
Outpatient (healthy)Amoxicillin OR doxycycline OR macrolide (if local resistance <25%)
Outpatient (comorbidities)Respiratory fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin) OR β-lactam + macrolide
Inpatient (non-ICU)Respiratory FQ OR β-lactam (ceftriaxone) + macrolide (azithromycin)
ICUβ-lactam (ceftriaxone/ampicillin-sulbactam) + macrolide or FQ. Add vancomycin if MRSA risk
HAP/VAPAnti-pseudomonal (piperacillin-tazobactam, cefepime, or meropenem) + vancomycin or linezolid (MRSA coverage)
🔑 PCP Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX for HIV patients with CD4 <200. Treatment: TMP-SMX high dose + steroids if PaO2 <70 or A-a gradient >35.
