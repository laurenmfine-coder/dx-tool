MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Neutropenic fever is defined as ANC <500 cells/µL (or expected to decline to <500 within 48h) + single oral temperature ≥38.3°C (101°F) or sustained ≥38.0°C (100.4°F) for 1 hour. It is an oncologic emergency — infection can progress to sepsis rapidly without neutrophil defense.

🔑 Key Concept: In neutropenia, classic signs of infection (pus, infiltrate, abscess wall) may be absent because neutrophils are needed to mount these responses. Fever may be the ONLY sign of life-threatening infection. Treat empirically — don't wait for cultures.

⚠️ Time to Antibiotics: Empiric antibiotics must be given within 60 minutes of presentation. Each hour of delay increases mortality. This is as time-critical as STEMI or stroke.

MASCC ≥21: Low risk → consider outpatient oral antibiotics. MASCC <21: High risk → inpatient IV antibiotics.

Workup

Blood cultures (2 sets: peripheral + each lumen of central line), CBC, CMP, lactate, UA, chest X-ray. Culture any suspicious site. Do NOT delay antibiotics for workup completion.

TREATMENT
Management

Empiric Antibiotic Therapy

Risk LevelRegimen
High risk (inpatient)Anti-pseudomonal β-lactam monotherapy: cefepime, meropenem, or piperacillin-tazobactam
Low risk (outpatient eligible)Ciprofloxacin + amoxicillin-clavulanate (oral)
Add vancomycin if:Hemodynamic instability, skin/soft tissue infection, mucositis, catheter-related infection suspected, MRSA risk
Add antifungal if:Persistent fever after 4-7 days of antibiotics → empiric antifungal (caspofungin or voriconazole)
🔑 Don't routinely add vancomycin. Vancomycin is NOT part of standard initial empiric therapy. Add only with specific indications (hemodynamic instability, suspected line infection, mucositis, skin infection). Unnecessary vancomycin increases resistance and nephrotoxicity.

G-CSF (Granulocyte Colony-Stimulating Factor)

Filgrastim/pegfilgrastim accelerates neutrophil recovery. Consider in high-risk patients with expected prolonged neutropenia, pneumonia, or sepsis. Primary prophylaxis with G-CSF given with chemotherapy regimens with >20% febrile neutropenia risk.
