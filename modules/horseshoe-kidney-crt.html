Teaching Point

Horseshoe kidney: Most common renal fusion anomaly (~1/400-600 births, M:F = 2:1). In 90% of cases, the LOWER poles fuse (10% upper pole fusion). The fused isthmus is usually parenchymal tissue but can be fibrous. The isthmus typically lies anterior to the aorta and IVC at the L3-L5 level. Ureters pass ANTERIOR to the isthmus (important surgical anatomy).

Teaching Point

During 'ascent,' three things happen simultaneously: (1) Apparent cranial repositioning (from differential growth); (2) 90° medial rotation (hilum: anterior → medial); (3) Vascular remodeling (blood supply shifts from iliac branches → progressively higher aortic branches → final renal arteries from L1-L2 aorta). Persistent lower vessels = accessory renal arteries (~25% of the population). These are surgically important during renal transplant and AAA repair.

Teaching Point

Turner syndrome (45,X) renal associations: horseshoe kidney (~7%), collecting system anomalies, malrotation, renal ectopia. Cardiovascular: bicuspid aortic valve (most common cardiac finding), coarctation of the aorta, aortic dilation/dissection risk. Other: short stature, ovarian dysgenesis (streak gonads), webbed neck, shield chest, lymphedema at birth, coarctation. All Turner patients should have renal imaging as part of initial workup.

Teaching Point

Horseshoe kidney complications: (1) UPJ obstruction (most common — ureters drape over isthmus); (2) Nephrolithiasis (from stasis — 20% lifetime incidence); (3) UTIs (from stasis and reflux); (4) Slight increased risk of Wilms' tumor (debated — 2-8x increased, but absolute risk still very low); (5) Trauma risk (lower position, less rib protection). Most horseshoe kidneys are asymptomatic and found incidentally.

Teaching Point

Horseshoe kidney vascular anatomy: typically 2-6 renal arteries (from aorta, common iliac, internal iliac, or even IMA). The isthmus has its own blood supply. Ureters pass anterior to the isthmus. Surgical implications: must map vasculature preoperatively (CT angiography); risk of hemorrhage if accessory arteries are not identified; relevant for AAA repair (isthmus overlies aortic bifurcation area). Renal transplant from a horseshoe kidney donor is possible but technically challenging.
