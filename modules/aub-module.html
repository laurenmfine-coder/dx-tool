PALM-COEIN Classification

AUB is classified by the PALM-COEIN system (FIGO):

CategoryCauseKey Features
PolypEndometrial/cervical polypsIntermenstrual bleeding. Benign but can be premalignant. Hysteroscopic removal
AdenomyosisEndometrial glands within myometriumPainful heavy periods, diffusely enlarged "boggy" uterus. Definitive: hysterectomy. Medical: hormonal therapy
LeiomyomaFibroids (uterine)Most common pelvic tumor in women. Estrogen-dependent. Heavy regular periods, bulk symptoms. Submucosal → most bleeding
MalignancyEndometrial cancer/hyperplasiaPostmenopausal bleeding, risk with unopposed estrogen
Coagulopathyvon Willebrand, platelet disordersHeavy periods since menarche. Screen adolescents with menorrhagia
Ovulatory dysfunctionAnovulation (PCOS, thyroid, etc.)Irregular, unpredictable bleeding. PCOS = #1 cause of anovulatory AUB
EndometrialPrimary endometrial disorderRegular but heavy periods with normal ovulation
IatrogenicMedications (anticoagulants, hormones, IUD)Breakthrough bleeding on OCPs, irregular bleeding on progestin IUD (common in first 3-6 months)
Not yet classifiedOther/unknownArteriovenous malformations, endometritis, etc.
CLINICAL
Evaluation

Age-Based Approach

Age GroupMost Common CausesKey Evaluation
AdolescentAnovulation (immature HPO axis), coagulopathy (von Willebrand disease — screen!), pregnancyβ-hCG, CBC, coagulation studies
Reproductive agePregnancy complications, anovulation (PCOS), fibroids, polypsβ-hCG first! Then US, TSH, prolactin
PerimenopausalAnovulation, fibroids, polyps, endometrial hyperplasia/cancerEndometrial biopsy if >45 with AUB or risk factors
PostmenopausalEndometrial atrophy (#1), endometrial cancer, polyps, HRTTVUS (endometrial thickness) → biopsy if >4mm
🔑 First Test in Reproductive-Age AUB: Pregnancy test (β-hCG). ALWAYS rule out pregnancy first. Then: CBC (anemia?), TSH (thyroid?), coagulation studies if adolescent or heavy since menarche, pelvic US, endometrial biopsy if indicated.
TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentIndication
Hormonal (first-line medical)Combined OCPs (regulate cycles), LNG-IUD (most effective medical treatment for menorrhagia — reduces bleeding 90%+), progestins (cyclic or continuous), tranexamic acid (antifibrinolytic — reduces bleeding 40%)
NSAIDsReduce prostaglandin-mediated bleeding. Helpful for primary dysmenorrhea + menorrhagia
GnRH agonistsLeuprolide — creates medical menopause. Pre-surgical to shrink fibroids. Short-term use (bone loss)
SurgicalEndometrial ablation (done with childbearing), myomectomy (fibroids, preserves fertility), hysterectomy (definitive for fibroids, adenomyosis, refractory AUB)
🔑 Fibroids: Most common pelvic tumor. Estrogen-dependent (grow in pregnancy, shrink after menopause). Submucosal fibroids cause the most bleeding (distort endometrial cavity). Subserosal cause bulk symptoms (urinary frequency, constipation). Intramural may affect both. Malignant transformation (leiomyosarcoma) is extremely rare (<1%). Rapidly growing fibroid in postmenopausal patient → suspect sarcoma.
