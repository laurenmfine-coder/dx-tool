MECHANISM
Pathophysiology & Framework

Chest pain evaluation requires rapid risk stratification to identify the three immediately life-threatening causes: ACS, PE, and aortic dissection. A systematic approach prevents anchoring bias.

Life-Threatening Causes ("The Big 5")

DiagnosisMechanismClassic Features
ACS (STEMI/NSTEMI)Coronary plaque rupture/thrombosisSubsternal pressure, radiation to jaw/arm, diaphoresis, troponin rise
Pulmonary embolismThrombus obstructing pulmonary vasculaturePleuritic pain, dyspnea, tachycardia, risk factors (immobilization, DVT)
Aortic dissectionIntimal tear with propagating dissection"Tearing" pain radiating to back, BP differential between arms, widened mediastinum
Tension pneumothoraxAir trapping in pleural space with mediastinal shiftUnilateral absent breath sounds, hypotension, tracheal deviation, JVD
Cardiac tamponadePericardial fluid compressing chambersBeck triad: hypotension, muffled heart sounds, JVD; pulsus paradoxus
\u26a0\ufe0f Aortic dissection + thrombolytics = death. Always consider dissection before administering thrombolytics for suspected MI. A widened mediastinum on CXR or BP differential >20mmHg between arms should halt thrombolytic therapy pending CT angiography.
CLINICAL
Risk Stratification

Initial Assessment (Within 10 Minutes)

ECG within 10 minutes of arrival. Simultaneous: vital signs, bilateral BP, focused history, troponin, CXR.

\U0001f511 HEART Score interpretation: 0-3 = low risk (safe for early discharge with outpatient follow-up). 4-6 = moderate risk (observation, serial troponins, possible stress test). 7-10 = high risk (admit, cardiology consult, likely catheterization).

Wells Score (PE Risk)

Clinical signs of DVT (3), PE most likely diagnosis (3), HR >100 (1.5), immobilization/surgery (1.5), previous DVT/PE (1.5), hemoptysis (1), malignancy (1). Score \u22644 + negative D-dimer = PE effectively excluded.

TREATMENT
Algorithmic Management

STEMI Protocol

ECG diagnosis \u2192 activate cath lab (goal: door-to-balloon <90 min). Aspirin 325mg, P2Y12 inhibitor, heparin, nitroglycerin (if no RV infarction/hypotension). If PCI unavailable within 120 minutes, give thrombolytics (after ruling out dissection).

NSTEMI / Unstable Angina

Aspirin + anticoagulation + P2Y12 inhibitor. Risk stratify with TIMI/GRACE score. Early invasive strategy (cath within 24h) for high-risk features: recurrent ischemia, hemodynamic instability, rising troponin, new ST depression.

\U0001f511 Nitroglycerin caution: Contraindicated in right ventricular infarction (preload-dependent), severe aortic stenosis, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, and recent PDE-5 inhibitor use (sildenafil within 24h, tadalafil within 48h). RV infarct = inferior MI with ST elevation in V4R.
\u26a0\ufe0f Do NOT delay reperfusion. The classic teaching: "Time is muscle." Every 30-minute delay in reperfusion for STEMI increases 1-year mortality. ECG within 10 minutes, cath lab activation within 30 minutes.
