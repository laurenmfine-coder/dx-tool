CLINICAL
Clinical Features
⚠️ Epidural Hematoma — Classic Presentation:
Head trauma → brief LOC → "lucid interval" (apparent improvement) → rapid deterioration (uncal herniation: ipsilateral pupil dilation, contralateral hemiparesis → bilateral fixed dilated pupils → death). Neurosurgical emergency — craniotomy for evacuation.

Subdural Hematoma

TypeTimingCT DensityManagement
Acute<3 daysHyperdense (bright white)Surgery if >10 mm or midline shift >5 mm

🔑 Elderly + Anticoagulation + Minor Trauma → Subdural: Bridging veins stretch across enlarged subdural space (brain atrophy). Low-force injury → slow venous bleeding → progressive headache, confusion, personality changes over days-weeks. Can be bilateral.
TREATMENT
Management

ICH (Intraparenchymal)

InterventionDetails
BP controlSBP <140 (INTERACT2). Nicardipine or labetalol IV
Reverse anticoagulationWarfarin: IV vitamin K + 4-factor PCC (NOT FFP first-line). DOACs: idarucizumab (dabigatran), andexanet alfa (factor Xa inhibitors)
ICP managementHOB 30°, osmotic therapy (mannitol or hypertonic saline), EVD for hydrocephalus
SurgeryCerebellar hemorrhage >3 cm or with deterioration. Supratentorial ICH: generally no surgical benefit (STICH trials)
🔑 Reversal Agents:
• Warfarin: 4-factor PCC (Kcentra) + IV vitamin K. PCC works in minutes vs FFP (hours)
• Dabigatran: Idarucizumab (Praxbind) — specific monoclonal antibody
• Factor Xa inhibitors (rivaroxaban, apixaban): Andexanet alfa (recombinant factor Xa decoy)
• Heparin: Protamine sulfate
