MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Biliary pathology spans from asymptomatic gallstones to life-threatening cholangitis. Gallstones affect 10-15% of adults; 80% cholesterol, 20% pigmented (black = hemolysis, brown = infection).

ConditionDefinitionKey Features
CholelithiasisGallstones in GB80% asymptomatic. No treatment unless symptomatic
Biliary colicTransient cystic duct obstructionEpisodic RUQ pain after fatty meals, 30 min-6 hrs, resolves. No fever/labs abnormalities
Acute cholecystitisPersistent cystic duct obstruction → inflammationConstant pain >6h, fever, Murphy sign (+), leukocytosis. US: stones + wall thickening + pericholecystic fluid
CholedocholithiasisStone in CBDElevated bilirubin + ALP + GGT. Dilated CBD. MRCP/EUS to confirm
CholangitisInfected obstructed bile ductEMERGENCY. Charcot triad → Reynolds pentad. ERCP decompression
Gallstone pancreatitisStone at ampulla of VaterElevated lipase + biliary pattern. ERCP if cholangitis or persistent obstruction
CLINICAL
Cholangitis & Diagnosis
⚠️ Cholangitis:
Charcot triad: RUQ pain + fever/chills + jaundice
Reynolds pentad: + altered mental status + hypotension (septic shock)
Reynolds pentad = emergent ERCP. Mortality without intervention: 50%+

Diagnostic Approach

TestRole
RUQ UltrasoundFirst-line. Stones (95% sensitive), wall thickening, CBD dilation (>6mm)
HIDA scanGold standard for cholecystitis if US equivocal. Non-visualization of GB = cystic duct obstruction (97% sensitive)
MRCPNon-invasive CBD evaluation. ~95% sensitivity for stones
ERCPDiagnostic AND therapeutic. Sphincterotomy + stone extraction. Risk: post-ERCP pancreatitis (5-10%)
🔑 Murphy Sign: Inspiratory arrest during RUQ palpation. Specific for acute cholecystitis. Sonographic Murphy sign is even more specific.
TREATMENT
Management
ConditionTreatment
Asymptomatic stonesObservation. Exception: porcelain GB (cancer risk)
Biliary colicElective lap cholecystectomy
Acute cholecystitisIV abx + lap chole within 72h. Perc cholecystostomy if too sick
CholedocholithiasisERCP → cholecystectomy
CholangitisIV abx + emergent ERCP decompression
Gallstone pancreatitisSupportive + cholecystectomy same admission
🔑 Acalculous Cholecystitis: 10% of cases. ICU patients (sepsis, burns, TPN). No stones — ischemia/stasis. 30-50% mortality. Perc cholecystostomy (too sick for surgery).
