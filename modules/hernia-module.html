Direct inguinalThrough Hesselbach triangle, MEDIAL to epigastric vesselsOlder malesAcquired. Does NOT enter scrotum
FemoralBelow inguinal ligament, through femoral canalWomen (but inguinal still more common in women)HIGHEST strangulation risk (~40%). Always repair

CLINICAL
Complications
🔑 Inferior Epigastric Vessels: LATERAL = indirect. MEDIAL = direct. "MDs are Direct" — Medial = Direct. Hesselbach triangle: epigastric vessels (lateral), rectus sheath (medial), inguinal ligament (inferior).
TermDefinitionManagement
ReducibleContents pushed backElective repair
IncarceratedTrapped, cannot reduceManual reduction → urgent surgery if fails
StrangulatedIncarcerated + vascular compromise → ischemiaSURGICAL EMERGENCY. Do NOT attempt reduction (necrotic bowel risk)
⚠️ Strangulated Hernia: Non-reducible, tender, erythematous with obstructive symptoms and systemic toxicity → OR immediately. Do NOT reduce (may push dead bowel into abdomen).
TREATMENT
Surgical Repair
ApproachDetails
Tension-free mesh (Lichtenstein)Gold standard. Low recurrence (1-2%). Risk: chronic inguinodynia
Laparoscopic (TEP/TAPP)Less pain, faster recovery. Preferred for bilateral/recurrent
Watchful waitingAcceptable for minimally symptomatic inguinal hernias. NOT for femoral (always repair)
🔑 Femoral hernias: ALWAYS repair electively — 40% incarceration rate due to narrow, rigid canal.
