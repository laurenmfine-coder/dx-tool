FeatureUpper GIB (above ligament of Treitz)Lower GIB (below ligament of Treitz)
PresentationHematemesis (coffee-ground or frank blood), melena (black tarry stool)Hematochezia (bright red blood per rectum)

BUN/Cr ratioElevated >20:1 (blood protein digested in upper GI → urea absorbed)Normal
🔑 Massive upper GIB can present as hematochezia (rapid transit time). If hematochezia with hemodynamic instability, always consider upper source first. NG lavage has been largely abandoned but hematemesis/coffee-grounds confirms upper source.
CLINICAL
Workup & Risk Stratification
⚠️ Resuscitate FIRST: ABCs, 2 large-bore IVs, crystalloid, type & crossmatch. Transfuse pRBCs if Hb <7 (restrictive strategy — TRIGGER trial showed better outcomes than liberal Hb <10 threshold, especially in variceal bleeding).

Risk Scores

Glasgow-Blatchford Score (GBS): Pre-endoscopy. Identifies patients who need intervention (admission, endoscopy, transfusion). Score 0 = low risk, can be discharged.

Endoscopy

Upper GIB: EGD within 24h (urgent within 12h if high-risk features). PPI drip before endoscopy. For ulcer: Forrest classification guides management (Ia/Ib active bleed, IIa visible vessel = high risk → endoscopic therapy).

Lower GIB: Colonoscopy after prep (within 24h). If hemodynamically unstable: CTA → angiography with embolization or surgery.

TREATMENT
Management by Cause
CauseTreatment
PUD (bleeding ulcer)Endoscopic therapy (epinephrine injection + thermal/clips) + high-dose PPI (IV pantoprazole 80mg bolus → 8mg/h drip × 72h). Test & treat H. pylori. Stop NSAIDs
Esophageal varicesOctreotide IV (splanchnic vasoconstriction) + IV antibiotics (ceftriaxone — reduces mortality!) + endoscopic band ligation (EBL). TIPS for refractory. Balloon tamponade (Blakemore) as bridge. Propranolol or nadolol for prophylaxis
Diverticular bleed80% self-limited. Colonoscopic hemostasis if identified. Angiography + embolization or surgery if massive/recurrent
AngiodysplasiaEndoscopic ablation (APC). Associated with aortic stenosis (Heyde syndrome) and CKD
🔑 Variceal Bleed Bundle: Octreotide + IV ceftriaxone + EBL. Antibiotics reduce mortality and rebleeding — always give. Propranolol for secondary prophylaxis (target HR 55-60). Primary prophylaxis: propranolol or nadolol for medium/large varices.
