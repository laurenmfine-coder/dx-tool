MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Inflammation of the gallbladder, >90% from gallstone impaction in the cystic duct → bile stasis → distension → inflammation → secondary infection.

FeatureBiliary ColicAcute Cholecystitis
PainEpisodic RUQ after fatty meals, self-limited (<6h)Constant RUQ pain >6h, worsening, radiating to right scapula
FeverNoYes (with leukocytosis)
Murphy signNegativePositive (inspiratory arrest during RUQ palpation)
TreatmentElective cholecystectomyAntibiotics + cholecystectomy within 72h
🔑 Risk Factors (4 F's + more): Female, Forty (age >40), Fertile (pregnancy/OCP), Fat (obesity). Also: rapid weight loss, TPN, Native American/Hispanic, cirrhosis, Crohn (ileal disease → bile salt malabsorption → cholesterol stones).
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Special Populations

Imaging

TestUse
RUQ UltrasoundFIRST-LINE. Gallstones + GB wall thickening (>3mm) + pericholecystic fluid + sonographic Murphy sign
HIDA scanGOLD STANDARD for acute cholecystitis. Non-visualization of GB at 4h = cystic duct obstruction = cholecystitis (sensitivity 97%)
⚠️ Acalculous Cholecystitis: GB inflammation WITHOUT stones. Seen in ICU patients (sepsis, TPN, burns, major surgery — biliary stasis + ischemia). High mortality (30-50%). Diagnosis: HIDA scan (best) or US showing GB distension/wall thickening without stones. Treatment: percutaneous cholecystostomy (many too sick for surgery).

Complications

Gangrenous cholecystitis (ischemic necrosis → perforation), emphysematous cholecystitis (gas-forming organisms — diabetics, men — air in GB wall on imaging → emergent surgery), Mirizzi syndrome (stone in cystic duct compresses common hepatic duct → obstructive jaundice), gallstone ileus (stone erodes into duodenum → SBO at ileocecal valve — pneumobilia + SBO on imaging).

TREATMENT
Management

Acute cholecystitis: NPO, IV fluids, IV antibiotics (piperacillin-tazobactam or ceftriaxone + metronidazole), pain control (NSAIDs preferred — ketorolac). Cholecystectomy within 72 hours (laparoscopic preferred). Early surgery is superior to delayed.

🔑 Choledocholithiasis (CBD stone): Elevated direct bilirubin + dilated CBD on US + elevated ALP/GGT. If suspected: MRCP (non-invasive) or EUS → if confirmed → ERCP (therapeutic — sphincterotomy + stone extraction) → then cholecystectomy same admission.

Cholangitis (infected CBD): Charcot triad (RUQ pain + fever + jaundice) — present in ~70%. Reynolds pentad adds altered mental status + hypotension (= septic cholangitis → emergent ERCP for drainage).
