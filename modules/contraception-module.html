CLINICAL
Contraindications & Special Populations
⚠️ Estrogen-Containing Contraceptives CONTRAINDICATED in:
• Age ≥35 + smoking (≥15 cig/day) — VTE + stroke risk
• History of VTE/PE or known thrombophilia
• Migraine WITH aura (stroke risk)
• Breast cancer (current)
• Uncontrolled hypertension (≥160/100)
• <21 days postpartum (VTE risk)
• Active liver disease or liver tumors
Use progestin-only methods instead in these patients
🔑 Quick Reference:
• Migraine WITHOUT aura → Combined OK
• Migraine WITH aura → Estrogen contraindicated (progestin-only OK)
• Breastfeeding <6 weeks postpartum → Avoid estrogen (supply concerns)
• IUDs safe in nulliparous women and adolescents (ACOG recommendation)
• Copper IUD = most effective emergency contraception (up to 5 days, <1% failure)

Emergency Contraception
MethodWindowEfficacyNotes
Copper IUDUp to 5 days>99%MOST effective EC. Can remain as ongoing contraception
Ulipristal (Ella)Up to 5 days~98%Progesterone receptor modulator. More effective than Plan B, especially days 3-5. Prescription required
Levonorgestrel (Plan B)Up to 3 days (best <72h)~89%Less effective with higher BMI (>75 kg). OTC available. Delays ovulation
🔑 EC does NOT cause abortion. Emergency contraceptives work by delaying/preventing ovulation. They do not disrupt an established pregnancy. The copper IUD prevents fertilization and implantation. None are abortifacients.
