MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Sickle cell disease (SCD) results from a point mutation in the β-globin gene: glutamic acid → valine at position 6 (HbS). Under low O₂ conditions, HbS polymerizes → rigid sickle-shaped RBCs → vaso-occlusion, chronic hemolysis, and organ damage.

🔑 Key Concept: HbSS (homozygous) = sickle cell disease. HbAS (heterozygous) = sickle cell trait — generally asymptomatic but protective against Plasmodium falciparum malaria (explains high prevalence in malaria-endemic regions).

⚠️ Vaso-Occlusive Crisis (VOC): Sickled cells obstruct microvasculature → tissue ischemia → severe pain. Triggers: dehydration, infection, cold, hypoxia, stress. Most common reason for ED visits and hospitalization in SCD.
CLINICAL
Complications

Acute Complications

ComplicationFeaturesManagement
Vaso-occlusive crisisSevere bone/joint pain; dactylitis in infantsIV fluids, opioid analgesia, NSAIDs, incentive spirometry
Acute chest syndromeFever + new pulmonary infiltrate + respiratory symptoms. #1 cause of death in adultsAntibiotics (ceftriaxone + macrolide), exchange transfusion, supplemental O₂
Splenic sequestrationRapid spleen enlargement, Hb drop ≥2g/dL, hypovolemic shock. Children <5yrEmergent transfusion, splenectomy after 2nd episode
Aplastic crisisParvovirus B19 infection → transient red cell aplasia (reticulocyte count near zero)Transfusion; self-limited (1-2 weeks)
StrokeOccurs in 11% of children <20yr. Large vessel occlusionExchange transfusion to HbS <30%. Screen with transcranial Doppler annually (ages 2-16)

Chronic Complications

Functional asplenia (autosplenectomy by age 5) → infection risk (encapsulated organisms). Avascular necrosis (femoral head). Pulmonary hypertension. Chronic kidney disease (sickle nephropathy). Cholelithiasis (pigmented gallstones from chronic hemolysis).

🔑 Newborn Screening: All US newborns are screened for SCD via hemoglobin electrophoresis. Diagnosis at birth allows early intervention: penicillin prophylaxis (starts at 2 months through age 5) and pneumococcal vaccination to prevent overwhelming sepsis from functional asplenia.

Disease-Modifying Therapies

TherapyMechanismKey Points
HydroxyureaIncreases HbF production → reduces sicklingFirst-line disease-modifying therapy. Reduces VOC, ACS, transfusion need, and mortality
L-glutamineReduces oxidative stress in sickle RBCsFDA-approved add-on therapy
CrizanlizumabAnti-P-selectin antibody → reduces vaso-occlusionReduces VOC frequency
VoxelotorIncreases Hb-O₂ affinity → prevents polymerizationImproves hemoglobin (note: FDA withdrew approval 2024 — check current status)
Gene therapyAdds functional β-globin gene or edits BCL11ACasgevy (CRISPR), Lyfgenia (lentiviral) — potentially curative
Stem cell transplantOnly established cure (matched sibling donor)Limited by donor availability; gene therapy expanding options
⚠️ Transfusion Goal: Simple transfusion targets Hb 10 g/dL (don't overtransfuse — viscosity increases). Exchange transfusion targets HbS <30% — used for stroke, severe ACS, and preoperative preparation.
