Pathophysiology & Classification

A-fib is the most common sustained cardiac arrhythmia. Disorganized atrial electrical activity → irregularly irregular ventricular response → loss of atrial kick (15-25% of cardiac output).

TypeDefinition
ParoxysmalSelf-terminates within 7 days (usually <48h)
PersistentLasts >7 days, requires intervention to terminate
Long-standing persistent>12 months continuous
PermanentDecision made to not pursue rhythm control
🔑 EKG Hallmarks: No P waves (replaced by fibrillatory baseline), irregularly irregular R-R intervals, narrow QRS (unless aberrant conduction/bundle branch block). Ventricular rate often 110-180 bpm if uncontrolled.

Common Causes

Hypertension (#1), valvular disease (mitral stenosis), heart failure, thyrotoxicosis, alcohol ("holiday heart"), PE, post-cardiac surgery, OSA, obesity, COPD, pericarditis.

DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban) preferred over warfarin for non-valvular A-fib. Exception: mechanical heart valve or moderate-severe mitral stenosis → warfarin only.
🔑 HAS-BLED Score: Assesses bleeding risk. High score does NOT contraindicate anticoagulation — it identifies patients needing closer monitoring. The stroke risk of untreated A-fib almost always outweighs bleeding risk.
TREATMENT
Rate vs Rhythm Control
StrategyGoalMedications
Rate controlHR <110 bpm at rest (lenient) or <80 (strict)Beta-blockers (metoprolol, atenolol), non-DHP CCBs (diltiazem, verapamil), digoxin (adjunct only)
Rhythm controlRestore/maintain sinus rhythmFlecainide/propafenone (no structural heart disease), amiodarone (with structural disease), dofetilide, sotalol. Catheter ablation (PVI)
🔑 Rate vs Rhythm: AFFIRM trial showed no mortality difference. EAST-AFNET 4 trial showed early rhythm control (within 1 year of diagnosis) reduces cardiovascular outcomes. Current trend: early rhythm control preferred, especially if symptomatic or HFrEF. Catheter ablation (pulmonary vein isolation) increasingly first-line.

Cardioversion Rules

A-fib <48 hours: Can cardiovert without prior anticoagulation (low thrombus risk). A-fib ≥48 hours or unknown duration: Either 3 weeks anticoagulation before cardioversion OR TEE to exclude left atrial appendage thrombus → cardiovert if clear. Continue anticoagulation ≥4 weeks post-cardioversion regardless.
