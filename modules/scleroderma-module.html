MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Systemic sclerosis (SSc) is an autoimmune disease characterized by vascular damage, immune activation, and progressive fibrosis of skin and internal organs. The triad: 1) vasculopathy (Raynaud, PAH), 2) autoimmunity, 3) fibrosis (collagen deposition).

Two Major Subtypes

FeatureLimited Cutaneous SSc (lcSSc)Diffuse Cutaneous SSc (dcSSc)
Skin involvementDistal to elbows/knees + faceProximal + trunk + distal
AntibodyAnti-centromereAnti-Scl-70 (anti-topoisomerase I)
Key complicationPulmonary arterial hypertension (PAH)Interstitial lung disease (ILD), renal crisis
PrognosisBetter (slower progression)Worse (rapid progression, organ damage)
Old nameCREST syndrome—
🔑 CREST Syndrome: Calcinosis, Raynaud phenomenon, Esophageal dysmotility, Sclerodactyly, Telangiectasias. Associated with anti-centromere antibodies. PAH is the leading cause of death in limited SSc.
CLINICAL
Clinical Features

Skin

Raynaud phenomenon (>95% of SSc patients — often the first symptom). Skin thickening: sclerodactyly → proximal extension. Digital ulcers, calcinosis cutis, salt-and-pepper skin (hypo/hyperpigmentation).

Organ Involvement

SystemManifestationKey Details
GI (90%)Esophageal dysmotility (#1 visceral involvement)Smooth muscle fibrosis → decreased LES pressure → severe GERD + dysphagia. Watermelon stomach (GAVE) → GI bleeding

RenalScleroderma renal crisis (SRC)Acute onset severe hypertension + MAHA + renal failure. Treat with ACE INHIBITORS (captopril — proven to save lives). Do NOT use steroids (precipitate crisis)

MSKArthralgia, tendon friction rubsTendon friction rubs = poor prognostic sign
⚠️ Scleroderma Renal Crisis (SRC): Occurs in 10-15% of dcSSc, often within first 4 years. Presents with acute hypertension (often malignant), MAHA (schistocytes), thrombocytopenia, elevated creatinine. Risk factors: diffuse skin disease, anti-RNA polymerase III, corticosteroid use (≥15 mg/day prednisone). Treatment: ACE INHIBITORS (life-saving). Do NOT stop even if creatinine rises initially. 50% recover renal function.

Renal crisisACE inhibitors (captopril) — EMERGENT. Avoid steroids ≥15 mg/day
Skin fibrosisMethotrexate (early disease). Mycophenolate. No proven therapy for advanced fibrosis
🔑 Anti-RNA Polymerase III: Specific for dcSSc. Associated with scleroderma renal crisis AND concurrent malignancy screening (cancer-associated scleroderma). Check age-appropriate cancer screening in these patients.
