Postpartum Hemorrhage

Causes, risk factors, and stepwise management of postpartum hemorrhage including uterine atony and massive transfusion.

📚 2 Sections ❓ 4 MCQs ⏱ ~25 min

🔍 Etiology: The 4 Ts

Causes of PPH

TCauseFrequencyKey Features
ToneUterine atony70-80%Boggy, soft uterus that does not contract. Risk: overdistension (macrosomia, polyhydramnios, multiples), prolonged labor, chorioamnionitis, mag sulfate use
TraumaLacerations, hematomas, uterine rupture, uterine inversion20%Firm uterus + continued bleeding → inspect for lacerations. Cervical, vaginal, periurethral tears.
TissueRetained placenta/membranes10%Incomplete placenta on inspection. May need manual extraction or uterine curettage.
ThrombinCoagulopathy1%DIC (abruption, amniotic fluid embolism), pre-existing (vWD, ITP), anticoagulant use

Definition & Risk Stratification

  • Definition: Cumulative blood loss ≥1,000 mL OR bleeding + signs of hypovolemia within 24 hours of delivery
  • Primary PPH: Within 24 hours of delivery (most common)
  • Secondary PPH: 24 hours to 12 weeks postpartum (think retained products, endometritis, subinvolution)
  • Quantitative blood loss: Weigh blood-soaked materials + suction canister volumes (more accurate than visual estimation)

💊 Stepwise Management

Management Algorithm

StepInterventionDetails
1Bimanual uterine massage + oxytocinFundal massage immediately. Oxytocin 10-40 units IV. First-line for atony.
2Second-line uterotonicsMethylergonovine (Methergine) IM — avoid in HTN. Carboprost (Hemabate/15-methyl PGF2α) IM — avoid in asthma. Misoprostol 800-1000 mcg rectal.
3Intrauterine tamponadeBakri balloon or Jada device. Effective bridge while preparing for surgery.
4Surgical interventionB-Lynch (uterine compression) suture, uterine artery ligation, internal iliac ligation, hysterectomy (definitive if life-threatening).
5Massive transfusion1:1:1 ratio (pRBC:FFP:platelets). TXA 1g IV within 3 hours of delivery. Activate MTP early.

⚠️ Tranexamic Acid (TXA) — Give Early

WOMAN trial: TXA 1g IV within 3 hours of PPH onset reduces bleeding-related death by 20%. Give as early as possible — benefit decreases after 3 hours. No increase in thromboembolic events. Should be part of every PPH protocol.

Uterotonic Quick Reference

  • Oxytocin: First-line. IV infusion. Safe in all patients.
  • Methylergonovine: Causes vasoconstriction → CONTRAINDICATED in hypertension/preeclampsia
  • Carboprost (15-methyl PGF2α): Causes bronchospasm → CONTRAINDICATED in asthma
  • Misoprostol: PGE1 analog. Rectal, sublingual, or buccal. No absolute contraindications. Slower onset but widely available.
