MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Aspirin (ASA/salicylate) toxicity involves multiple mechanisms:

MechanismEffect
Direct stimulation of medullary respiratory centerEarly: respiratory alkalosis (hyperventilation) — the FIRST acid-base disturbance
Uncoupling of oxidative phosphorylationIncreased metabolic rate → hyperthermia, tachycardia, diaphoresis
Accumulation of organic acidsLate: HAGMA (lactic acid, ketoacids) superimposed on respiratory alkalosis
CNS toxicityTinnitus (earliest symptom), confusion, seizures, cerebral edema
🔑 Classic Acid-Base Pattern: Mixed respiratory alkalosis + HAGMA. Adults: initial respiratory alkalosis → mixed picture → predominant HAGMA (late). Children: may present with HAGMA earlier (less respiratory compensation). The MIXED pattern is pathognomonic for salicylate poisoning.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Severity

Clinical Features

Early: tinnitus (earliest), nausea, vomiting, hyperventilation, diaphoresis. Late: altered mental status, seizures, hyperthermia, pulmonary edema (non-cardiogenic), coagulopathy, cerebral edema.

Level (mg/dL)Severity
30-50Mild (tinnitus, GI symptoms)
50-75Moderate (hyperventilation, mixed acid-base)
75-100Severe (AMS, seizures)
>100Life-threatening (coma, multiorgan failure)
⚠️ Critical Concept — Ionization Trap: Salicylate is a weak acid. In acidic blood (low pH), more salicylate is un-ionized → crosses BBB into brain → worsens CNS toxicity. Acidemia INCREASES brain salicylate levels. This is why alkalinization is essential and acidosis is dangerous.
TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
Activated charcoalEffective even late (salicylates form bezoars, slow absorption). Multi-dose charcoal for large ingestions. Give if presenting within 2-4h (some advocate longer)
IV sodium bicarbonate (key treatment)Goal: serum pH 7.45-7.55 + urine pH 7.5-8.0. Alkalinization traps ionized salicylate in blood (prevents CNS entry) and in urine (enhances renal excretion — ion trapping). 150 mEq NaHCO3 in 1L D5W at 1.5-2× maintenance
HemodialysisDefinitive treatment for severe poisoning. Indications: level >100, renal failure, pulmonary edema, AMS/seizures, clinical deterioration despite bicarb, acidemia (pH <7.2)
⚠️ DO NOT INTUBATE unless absolutely necessary! Intubation → loss of hyperventilatory compensation → rapid CO2 rise → acidemia → massively increased brain salicylate → cardiac arrest. If intubation required: pre-bolus bicarb, match minute ventilation (very high RR on vent), prepare for emergent HD. Many salicylate deaths occur peri-intubation.
🔑 Key Electrolyte: Monitor potassium closely. Hypokalemia common and must be corrected — kidneys cannot alkalinize urine if hypokalemic (K/H exchange). Replace K aggressively for bicarb to work effectively.
