MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Clostridioides difficile

Anaerobic spore-forming gram-positive rod. Produces toxin A (enterotoxin) and toxin B (cytotoxin) → mucosal inflammation → pseudomembranous colitis. Spores resistant to alcohol-based hand sanitizer — hand washing with soap and water required.

🔑 Risk Factors: Antibiotic use (#1 — especially fluoroquinolones, clindamycin, cephalosporins, carbapenems), age >65, hospitalization, PPI use, immunosuppression. Can occur with ANY antibiotic, including those used to treat C. diff.

CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Severity

C. diff Diagnosis

Test only symptomatic patients (≥3 unformed stools/24h). Do NOT test formed stool. Do NOT test as test-of-cure (can remain positive for weeks).

TestDetails
NAAT (PCR) for toxin geneMost sensitive. But detects carriers too — only test symptomatic patients
Toxin EIA (A/B)Most specific for active disease but less sensitive
GDH + toxin algorithmGDH screen (sensitive) → if positive, confirm with toxin EIA or NAAT
⚠️ Fulminant C. diff: WBC >15,000, Cr >1.5, albumin <2.5, hypotension, ileus, toxic megacolon. Signs: distended abdomen, rebound, absent bowel sounds. CT: colonic wall thickening, thumbprinting. Mortality up to 50%. Surgery: subtotal colectomy if failing medical therapy.
TREATMENT
Management
SeverityTreatment
Non-severe (initial)Fidaxomicin 200mg BID × 10 days (preferred — lower recurrence) OR vancomycin 125mg PO QID × 10 days
SevereVancomycin 125mg PO QID × 10 days (or fidaxomicin)
FulminantVancomycin 500mg PO/NG QID + vancomycin enemas if ileus + IV metronidazole 500mg q8h. Surgery consult
Recurrence (1st)Fidaxomicin (if vanco used initially) or vancomycin taper/pulse. Bezlotoxumab (anti-toxin B monoclonal) to prevent recurrence
Recurrence (≥2nd)Fecal microbiota transplant (FMT) — most effective therapy for recurrent CDI (~90% cure rate)
🔑 Key Points:
• Oral vancomycin (not IV — vanco doesn't reach colon when given IV)
• Metronidazole is NO LONGER first-line (inferior to vancomycin per IDSA guidelines)
• Stop offending antibiotic if possible
• Contact precautions + hand washing with soap and water (alcohol-based sanitizer doesn't kill spores)
• Do NOT use anti-motility agents (loperamide) — risk of toxic megacolon
