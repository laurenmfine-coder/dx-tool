ProductContainsIndicationKey Points
PRBCsRBCs in additive solutionAnemia, acute blood loss1 unit ↑ Hb by ~1 g/dL. ABO/Rh compatible required. O-neg = universal donor.
FFPAll clotting factorsCoagulopathy, DIC, warfarin reversal (if no PCC), TTPABO compatible needed. Contains ALL factors including fibrinogen. Must thaw (30 min).
PlateletsPlatelet concentratesThrombocytopenia, platelet dysfunction1 unit raises plt by ~30,000. ABO compatible preferred but not required. Stored at ROOM TEMP (20-24°C) — highest bacterial contamination risk.
CryoprecipitateFibrinogen, vWF, Factor VIII, Factor XIIIHypofibrinogenemia (<100), DIC, vWD, hemophilia AMain use: replace fibrinogen when FFP isn't concentrated enough.
🔑 ABO system: Type A = A antigens + anti-B antibodies. Type B = B antigens + anti-A antibodies. Type AB = both antigens, no antibodies (universal plasma donor). Type O = no antigens, both antibodies (universal RBC donor). Always check ABO and Rh before transfusion.
HIGH YIELD
Transfusion Reactions
ReactionTimingMechanismTreatment
Acute hemolyticMinutesABO incompatibility → complement activation → intravascular hemolysisSTOP transfusion immediately. IV NS, diuretics. Monitor for DIC, renal failure. Clerical error is #1 cause.
Febrile non-hemolytic1-6 hoursCytokine accumulation from donor leukocytesStop transfusion, rule out hemolytic. Acetaminophen. Prevention: leukoreduced products.
Allergic (mild)During/shortly afterIgE against donor plasma proteinsAntihistamines (diphenhydramine). Can restart if mild.
AnaphylacticMinutes (after very little blood)Anti-IgA antibodies in IgA-deficient recipientEpinephrine, stop transfusion. Future: washed products (IgA removed).
TRALIWithin 6 hoursDonor anti-HLA antibodies → neutrophil activation → pulmonary edemaSupportive (O2, ventilation). Bilateral infiltrates, NON-cardiogenic edema. DO NOT give diuretics (unlike TACO).
TACOWithin 6 hoursVolume overloadDiuretics, slow rate. Elevated BNP. Responds to diuretics (unlike TRALI).
⚠️ TRALI vs TACO: Both cause respiratory distress + bilateral infiltrates within 6 hours. TRALI: normal/low BNP, no JVD, non-cardiogenic, treat supportively. TACO: elevated BNP, JVD, cardiogenic, treat with diuretics. TRALI is the #1 cause of transfusion-related death.
CLINICAL
Special Situations

Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP)

Definition: ≥10 units PRBCs in 24h (or ≥4 in 1h). Use 1:1:1 ratio (PRBCs:FFP:platelets). Give TXA within 3 hours of trauma. Monitor for: hypothermia (warm products), hypocalcemia (citrate in products chelates Ca²⁺), hyperkalemia (K⁺ leaks from stored RBCs), dilutional coagulopathy.

Special Product Modifications

Irradiated products: Prevent transfusion-associated GVHD by destroying donor lymphocytes. Indicated for: immunocompromised (chemo, BMT), premature neonates, HLA-matched products, intrauterine transfusions. CMV-negative products: For CMV-seronegative transplant recipients and neonates. Washed products: Remove plasma proteins → for IgA-deficient patients (prevent anaphylaxis).
