MECHANISM
Pathophysiology & Diagnosis

MDD involves dysregulation of monoamine neurotransmitters (serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine), though the pathophysiology extends beyond simple 'chemical imbalance' to include HPA axis dysregulation, neuroinflammation, and neuroplasticity changes.

DSM-5 Criteria (≥5 of 9 for ≥2 weeks, must include #1 or #2)

🔑 SIG E CAPS Mnemonic:
Sleep (insomnia or hypersomnia)
Interest (anhedonia — loss of interest/pleasure) *
Guilt (worthlessness, excessive guilt)
Energy (fatigue, loss of energy)
Concentration (difficulty concentrating, indecisiveness)
Appetite (change in appetite/weight)
Psychomotor (agitation or retardation)
Suicidal ideation
+ Depressed mood *
* Must have depressed mood OR anhedonia

Key Differentials

ConditionDistinction from MDD
Persistent depressive disorder (dysthymia)Depressed mood most days for ≥2 YEARS, milder than MDD
Bipolar depressionHistory of mania/hypomania. ALWAYS screen before starting antidepressants
Adjustment disorder with depressed moodSymptoms within 3 months of identifiable stressor, do not meet full MDD criteria
Bereavement/griefCan coexist with MDD. If criteria met, diagnose MDD even in grief
HypothyroidismCheck TSH — can mimic depression. Treat thyroid first
CLINICAL
Special Populations & Complications

Peripartum Depression

Onset during pregnancy or within 4 weeks postpartum (DSM-5). Affects 10-20% of mothers. Screen with Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS). Treatment: psychotherapy ± SSRI (sertraline preferred in breastfeeding). Brexanolone (IV allopregnanolone) FDA-approved for postpartum depression. Zuranolone (oral) FDA-approved 2023.

Depression with Psychotic Features

Mood-congruent delusions (guilt, worthlessness, deserved punishment, nihilistic) or hallucinations. Treatment: antidepressant + antipsychotic (or ECT). SSRI alone is insufficient.

Atypical Depression

Mood REACTIVITY (brightens with positive events) + ≥2 of: hypersomnia, hyperphagia/weight gain, leaden paralysis, rejection sensitivity. MAOIs or SSRIs preferred.

⚠️ Suicide Risk Assessment: Major risk factors: prior attempt (#1 predictor), male sex (complete more), older age, access to lethal means (firearms), substance use, hopelessness, living alone, chronic illness, recent loss/discharge. Always ask directly about suicidal ideation — asking does NOT increase risk.
TREATMENT
Pharmacotherapy & Other Modalities

First-Line: SSRIs

AgentKey Features
FluoxetineLongest half-life (4-6 days → fewer withdrawal symptoms). FDA-approved for children. Drug interactions (CYP2D6 inhibitor)
SertralineBest studied in cardiac patients and pregnancy/breastfeeding. Fewest drug interactions
EscitalopramMost selective SSRI. QTc prolongation at high doses
ParoxetineMost anticholinergic SSRI. Worst withdrawal (short half-life). Avoid in pregnancy (cardiac defects) and elderly

Second-Line & Augmentation

StrategyOptions
SNRIsVenlafaxine, duloxetine. Also treat pain (fibromyalgia, neuropathy). Venlafaxine can cause HTN
BupropionNDRI. No sexual dysfunction, no weight gain. CONTRAINDICATED in seizure disorders, eating disorders. Helps with smoking cessation
Mirtazapineα2-antagonist + 5-HT2/3 antagonist. Causes sedation + weight gain. Good for insomnia + poor appetite
AugmentationAripiprazole, quetiapine, lithium, thyroid hormone (T3), or combination antidepressants
🔑 ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy): MOST EFFECTIVE treatment for depression. Indications: treatment-resistant depression, depression with psychotic features, severe suicidality (rapid response needed), catatonia, pregnancy (when meds risky). Safe in elderly. Main side effect: retrograde amnesia (usually transient).

Serotonin Syndrome

Too much serotonergic activity (combining SSRIs + MAOIs, tramadol, linezolid, triptans). Triad: mental status changes + autonomic instability + neuromuscular hyperactivity (clonus, hyperreflexia, tremor). Treatment: stop offending agents, supportive care, cyproheptadine (5-HT2A antagonist).
