MECHANISM
Etiology
🔑 Top 3 Causes (80%): I GET SMASHED mnemonic, but remember:
1. Gallstones (~40%) — stone impacts ampulla of Vater → pancreatic duct obstruction
2. Alcohol (~30%) — direct toxic + metabolic injury to acinar cells
3. Hypertriglyceridemia (>1000 mg/dL) — third most common

Other causes: post-ERCP, medications (valproic acid, azathioprine, didanosine, thiazides), autoimmune, trauma, scorpion sting, hypercalcemia, pancreas divisum, tumors.

Pathophysiology

Premature activation of trypsinogen → trypsin within pancreatic acinar cells → autodigestion → inflammatory cascade → local necrosis + systemic inflammatory response (SIRS) → organ failure in severe cases.

CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Severity

Diagnosis (2 of 3 = Atlanta Criteria)

#Criterion
1Epigastric pain radiating to back, worse supine, improved leaning forward
2Lipase ≥3× upper limit of normal (lipase > amylase — more sensitive AND specific)
3CT findings (heterogeneous enhancement, peripancreatic fluid, necrosis)
⚠️ Severity Classification:
Mild: No organ failure, no local complications. 80% of cases.
Moderate: Transient organ failure (<48h) or local complications
Severe: Persistent organ failure (>48h). Mortality 30-50%. ICU admission

Prognostic Markers

BISAP score (BUN >25, Impaired mental status, SIRS, Age >60, Pleural effusion). ≥3 = severe. Ranson criteria (at admission + 48h). APACHE II. Hematocrit >44, BUN >20, Cr rising, CRP >150 at 48h all predict severity.

TREATMENT
Management

Cornerstone: Aggressive IV Fluids + Pain Control + NPO

InterventionDetails
IV fluidsLactated Ringer's preferred (reduces SIRS vs NS). Goal-directed: 5-10 mL/kg/hr initially, titrate to urine output >0.5 mL/kg/hr. Early aggressive hydration (first 12-24h) is critical
Pain controlIV opioids (hydromorphone, fentanyl). NSAIDs as adjunct. Morphine is acceptable despite historical concerns (no evidence it worsens)
NutritionEARLY oral feeding (within 24h if tolerating). Low-fat solid diet — NOT prolonged NPO. If unable to eat: enteral (NG/NJ tube) preferred over TPN
Gallstone pancreatitisCholecystectomy during SAME admission (after resolution). ERCP within 24h if cholangitis or persistent CBD obstruction
🔑 Complications:
• Acute peripancreatic fluid collection → pseudocyst (>4 weeks, encapsulated, no necrosis)
• Acute necrotic collection → walled-off necrosis (>4 weeks, encapsulated necrosis)
• Infected necrosis: suspect if fever/WBC rise after 7-10 days. CT-guided FNA for culture. Step-up approach: percutaneous drainage → endoscopic necrosectomy → surgery (last resort)
• Prophylactic antibiotics are NOT recommended
