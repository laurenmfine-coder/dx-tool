SBO vs LBO
FeatureSmall Bowel Obstruction (SBO)Large Bowel Obstruction (LBO)
#1 CauseAdhesions (post-surgical — 60%)Colorectal cancer (~60%)
Other causesHernias (#2, #1 if no prior surgery), Crohn stricture, intussusception, volvulus, gallstone ileusVolvulus (#2 — sigmoid > cecal), diverticular stricture, fecal impaction
PresentationColicky abdominal pain, N/V (early, bilious), distension, obstipation. High-pitched tinkling bowel soundsDistension (prominent), constipation/obstipation (early), N/V (late), pain
X-rayDilated small bowel (>3 cm), air-fluid levels, paucity of colonic gasDilated colon (>6 cm, cecum >9 cm = risk of perforation)
⚠️ Strangulation Signs (surgical emergency): Continuous (not colicky) pain, fever, tachycardia, peritoneal signs, leukocytosis, acidosis. Suggests compromised blood supply → ischemia/necrosis → perforation. CT: mesenteric haziness, bowel wall thickening, pneumatosis, portal venous gas.

TypeFeaturesTreatment
Sigmoid volvulusElderly, nursing home, psychiatric patients, chronic constipation. "Coffee bean" or "omega" sign on X-rayRigid sigmoidoscopy (decompression + detorsion) if no peritonitis → semi-elective sigmoid resection (60% recurrence without surgery). Surgery if necrosis/peritonitis
Cecal volvulusYounger patients, mobile cecum. "Kidney bean" in LUQ on X-rayCANNOT decompose endoscopically → surgery (right hemicolectomy)

Ogilvie Syndrome (Acute Colonic Pseudo-Obstruction)

Massive colonic dilation WITHOUT mechanical obstruction. Post-operative, critically ill, electrolyte imbalance. Cecal diameter >12 cm = perforation risk. Treatment: neostigmine (parasympathomimetic — reverses colonic dysmotility). Colonoscopic decompression if neostigmine fails.

TREATMENT
Management
ScenarioManagement
Partial SBO (no strangulation)Conservative: NPO, NGT decompression, IV fluids, serial exams. Water-soluble contrast (Gastrografin) — therapeutic AND prognostic (if contrast reaches colon in 24h → likely resolves)
Complete SBOSurgical consultation. If no improvement in 24-48h → surgery
Strangulated SBOEMERGENT surgery — resection of necrotic bowel
LBO from cancerUrgent surgery (resection ± diversion). Colonic stent as bridge to surgery in select cases
🔑 Closed-Loop Obstruction: Segment of bowel obstructed at TWO points (e.g., volvulus, incarcerated hernia with two transition points). High risk of ischemia/perforation because intraluminal pressure rises rapidly. CT: U-shaped or C-shaped dilated segment with mesenteric whirl. Requires emergent surgery.
