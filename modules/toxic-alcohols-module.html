MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Methanol, ethylene glycol, and isopropyl alcohol are metabolized by alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) into toxic metabolites. Parent compounds cause inebriation; metabolites cause organ damage.

AlcoholSourceToxic MetaboliteTarget Organ
MethanolWindshield fluid, solvents, moonshineFormic acid (via formaldehyde)Eyes (retinal toxicity → blindness), CNS
Ethylene glycolAntifreeze (sweet taste)Glycolic acid → oxalic acidKidneys (calcium oxalate crystals → AKI), CNS
Isopropyl alcoholRubbing alcoholAcetone (less toxic)CNS depression, GI irritation. NO acidosis (acetone is not an acid)
🔑 Key Distinction: Methanol and ethylene glycol → HIGH anion gap metabolic acidosis (HAGMA) + osmolar gap. Isopropyl alcohol → osmolar gap but NO acidosis (acetone is ketone, not acid). Positive ketones without acidosis = isopropyl alcohol clue.

⚠️ Osmolar Gap Evolution: Early: high osmolar gap (parent compound) + low/normal anion gap. As metabolized: osmolar gap DECREASES while anion gap INCREASES. Late presentation may have normal osmolar gap but severe HAGMA.
TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
Fomepizole (first-line)Alcohol dehydrogenase inhibitor — blocks conversion to toxic metabolites. 15 mg/kg IV loading. Give EARLY before metabolites accumulate
Ethanol (alternative)Competitive ADH substrate (higher affinity than methanol/EG). Difficult to dose. Fomepizole preferred
HemodialysisRemoves parent alcohol AND toxic metabolites. Indications: severe acidosis (pH <7.25), renal failure, visual changes (methanol), level >50 mg/dL, end-organ damage
Folate (methanol)Cofactor for formate metabolism → enhances elimination
Thiamine + pyridoxine (EG)Shunt metabolism away from oxalate toward less toxic metabolites
🔑 Isopropyl alcohol: Supportive care only. No fomepizole needed (acetone is not dangerously toxic). No acidosis. Patient may smell like acetone (fruity breath). Hemodialysis only for severe CNS depression or hemodynamic instability.
