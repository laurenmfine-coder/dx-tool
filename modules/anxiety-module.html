MECHANISM
Classification

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear and worry disproportionate to actual threat, with dysregulation of amygdala-prefrontal circuitry, GABA, serotonin, and norepinephrine systems.

DisorderCore FeatureDuration
Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)Excessive worry about multiple domains (work, health, family) with ≥3 somatic symptoms≥6 months
Panic DisorderRecurrent unexpected panic attacks + persistent worry about future attacks or behavioral changeRecurrent attacks
Social Anxiety DisorderFear of social situations where scrutiny/judgment is possible≥6 months
Specific PhobiaMarked fear of specific object/situation (most common anxiety disorder)≥6 months
AgoraphobiaFear of ≥2 situations: open spaces, enclosed spaces, crowds, public transport, being outside alone≥6 months
CLINICAL
Key Features

Panic Attack vs Panic Disorder

Panic attack: Discrete episode of intense fear peaking within minutes. ≥4 of: palpitations, sweating, trembling, SOB, choking sensation, chest pain, nausea, dizziness, derealization, paresthesias, chills/hot flashes, fear of losing control, fear of dying. Panic attacks can occur in ANY anxiety disorder.

Panic disorder: Recurrent UNEXPECTED panic attacks + ≥1 month of worry about future attacks OR maladaptive behavioral change (avoidance).

🔑 Key Differentials for Panic Attacks: Must rule out: MI/ACS (obtain ECG, troponin), PE (D-dimer if appropriate), pheochromocytoma (episodic HTN), hyperthyroidism (check TSH), arrhythmias, asthma, substance use (caffeine, cocaine, amphetamines, cannabis). First episode requires medical workup.

OCD and PTSD

Note: DSM-5 moved OCD and PTSD out of anxiety disorders into their own categories, but they are high-yield and closely related.

DisorderCore Feature
OCDObsessions (intrusive, unwanted thoughts) + compulsions (repetitive behaviors to reduce anxiety). Common themes: contamination/cleaning, symmetry/ordering, harm/checking, forbidden thoughts. Ego-DYSTONIC (distressing to patient)
PTSDFollowing traumatic event: re-experiencing (flashbacks, nightmares), avoidance, negative cognitions/mood, hyperarousal. Duration >1 month. If <1 month: acute stress disorder
⚠️ PTSD Timeline: Symptoms must persist >1 month for PTSD diagnosis. Acute stress disorder = 3 days to 1 month. If trauma occurred and symptoms last <3 days, it is a normal stress response. Debriefing immediately after trauma does NOT prevent PTSD and may worsen outcomes.

OCDSSRIs at HIGH DOSES (fluoxetine 40-80 mg). Clomipramine (TCA) if SSRIs failERP (Exposure and Response Prevention) — gold standard
PTSDSSRIs (sertraline, paroxetine — both FDA-approved). Prazosin for nightmares (α1 blocker)CPT (Cognitive Processing Therapy), PE (Prolonged Exposure), EMDR
🔑 Benzodiazepine Cautions: Benzos provide rapid anxiolysis but carry risks: dependence, tolerance, withdrawal (seizures), sedation, cognitive impairment, falls in elderly, respiratory depression. Use short-term or as-needed only. Avoid in substance use disorders. GAD and panic disorder treatment should aim for SSRI/SNRI as maintenance, not chronic benzos. Buspirone is a non-addictive alternative for GAD (5-HT1A partial agonist).
