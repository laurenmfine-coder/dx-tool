MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Myxedema coma is a life-threatening decompensation of severe hypothyroidism characterized by altered mental status, hypothermia, and organ dysfunction. Mortality: 25-60% even with treatment.

Pathophysiologic Cascade

SystemEffect of Severe HypothyroidismClinical Manifestation
CNS↓ cerebral metabolism, altered neurotransmitter synthesisAltered mental status, lethargy, coma, seizures. "Coma" is a misnomer — many patients are obtunded, not comatose.
Cardiovascular↓ cardiac output, ↓ HR, ↓ contractility, pericardial effusionBradycardia, hypotension, low-voltage ECG, cardiomegaly, heart failure
Respiratory↓ hypoxic/hypercapnic drive, respiratory muscle weaknessHypoventilation, hypercapnia, respiratory failure. May need intubation.
Thermoregulation↓ basal metabolic rate → inability to generate heatHypothermia (often <35°C, may be <30°C). Hallmark finding.
Metabolic↓ gluconeogenesis, ↓ free water clearanceHypoglycemia, hyponatremia (euvolemic — SIADH-like), hypercholesterolemia
🔑 Key Concept: Myxedema coma almost never occurs spontaneously — it is precipitated by a stressor in a patient with longstanding untreated/undertreated hypothyroidism. Common precipitants: infection (most common), cold exposure, sedatives/opioids, surgery, stroke, heart failure, GI bleeding, noncompliance with levothyroxine.
⚠️ Critical: Always check cortisol level and administer stress-dose steroids (hydrocortisone 100 mg IV q8h) BEFORE or simultaneously with thyroid hormone replacement. Reason: coexistent adrenal insufficiency is common (Schmidt syndrome — autoimmune polyglandular), and thyroid hormone increases cortisol metabolism. Giving T4 without cortisol can precipitate adrenal crisis.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis

Classic Presentation

Elderly woman (F:M = 4:1), winter months, found obtunded with hypothermia, bradycardia, hyponatremia, hypoglycemia. History of hypothyroidism or thyroidectomy scar. May have precipitating illness (pneumonia, UTI).

Diagnostic Labs

TestExpected FindingNotes
TSHMarkedly elevated (primary) or low/normal (central)Primary hypothyroidism is most common cause. Central (pituitary) can also cause myxedema coma.
Free T4Very low or undetectableFree T4 is more reliable than total T4 in critical illness
CortisolMust be checked — may be lowRandom cortisol <15-18 in critical illness suggests adrenal insufficiency. Cosyntropin stim test if time permits.
CBCAnemia (macrocytic), possible leukocytosis if infectionInfection may not produce fever — hypothermia masks febrile response
BMPHyponatremia, hypoglycemia, elevated creatinineHyponatremia from impaired free water clearance (ADH-like effect)
🔑 Board Pearl: Hypothermia in myxedema coma may mask fever from underlying infection. A temperature that is "normal" (37°C) in a myxedema patient may actually represent significant fever relative to their baseline. Always evaluate aggressively for infection even without fever.
TREATMENT
Emergency Management
PriorityInterventionDetails
1. Steroids FIRSTHydrocortisone 100 mg IV q8hBEFORE thyroid hormone. Rule out coexistent adrenal insufficiency. Continue until cortisol result available.
2. Thyroid HormoneIV levothyroxine (T4) loading dose200-400 μg IV load, then 50-100 μg IV daily. Some add IV T3 (liothyronine) 5-20 μg then 2.5-10 μg q8h for faster onset (T3 is the active hormone). Controversy: T4 only vs. T4+T3 combination.
3. Supportive CareICU admission, passive rewarmingPassive rewarming only (blankets). Active rewarming can cause vasodilation and cardiovascular collapse. Avoid hypothermia blankets.
4. Treat PrecipitantAntibiotics if infection suspectedLow threshold for empiric antibiotics. Blood/urine cultures. CXR.
5. Metabolic SupportDextrose, careful fluid managementD50 for hypoglycemia. Fluid restrict if hyponatremic (SIADH mechanism). Avoid NS boluses (worsens hyponatremia).
⚠️ Active Rewarming is Dangerous: Do NOT use forced-air warming blankets or warm IV fluids aggressively. Rapid rewarming causes peripheral vasodilation → cardiovascular collapse in a heart that cannot increase output. Use passive rewarming (room temperature, blankets) and let thyroid hormone replacement restore thermogenesis gradually.
