MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Thrombocytopenia (platelets <150,000/µL) results from three mechanisms: decreased production (marrow failure), increased destruction (immune or mechanical), or sequestration (splenomegaly).

Classification by Mechanism

MechanismConditionsKey Features
Immune destructionITP, drug-induced (heparin-HIT, quinine), SLEAntiplatelet antibodies; normal/↑ megakaryocytes
Mechanical destruction (MAHA)TTP, HUS, DIC, mechanical valvesSchistocytes on smear
Decreased productionAplastic anemia, leukemia, B12/folate deficiency, chemo↓ Megakaryocytes on biopsy; often pancytopenia
SequestrationSplenomegaly (portal hypertension, lymphoma)Large spleen on exam; mild thrombocytopenia (rarely <40K)
🔑 ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura): Autoantibodies (usually anti-GPIIb/IIIa) coat platelets → splenic macrophage destruction. Diagnosis of exclusion: isolated thrombocytopenia with normal WBC, Hb, and smear (no schistocytes). Bone marrow shows increased megakaryocytes.
⚠️ HIT (Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia): Type II HIT is immune-mediated (anti-PF4/heparin antibodies) → platelet activation → paradoxical THROMBOSIS (not bleeding). Occurs 5-10 days after heparin exposure. 4Ts score for pretest probability. Treatment: STOP all heparin, start non-heparin anticoagulant (argatroban, bivalirudin).
CLINICAL
Diagnostic Approach

Stepwise Workup

StepTestPurpose
1CBC with peripheral smearConfirm true thrombocytopenia (rule out pseudothrombocytopenia from EDTA clumping). Look for schistocytes (MAHA), blasts (leukemia)
2PT/PTT, fibrinogen, D-dimerNormal in ITP/TTP; Abnormal in DIC
3LDH, haptoglobin, reticulocyte countHemolysis workup — elevated in TTP/HUS/DIC
4HIV, HCV, H. pyloriSecondary causes of ITP
5ADAMTS13 activityIf MAHA present — <10% = TTP

TREATMENT
Management

ITP Treatment

SettingTreatment
Platelets >30K, no bleedingObservation (especially in children — 80% spontaneously resolve)
First-line (platelets <30K or bleeding)Corticosteroids (prednisone or dexamethasone). IVIG for rapid response (raises platelets within 24-48h)
Emergency (life-threatening bleeding)IVIG + steroids + platelet transfusion + consider anti-RhD (if Rh+)
Chronic/refractory ITPTPO receptor agonists (eltrombopag, romiplostim), rituximab, splenectomy
🔑 IVIG in ITP: Works by saturating Fc receptors on splenic macrophages → reduced platelet destruction. Fastest way to raise platelets (24-48h). Used in emergencies and pre-procedures.
