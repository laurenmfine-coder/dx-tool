📖 Teaching Point

Surfactant is produced by TYPE II PNEUMOCYTES (alveolar cells) beginning at ~24 weeks of gestation. Adequate surfactant production for independent breathing typically occurs by ~34-36 weeks. Surfactant reduces alveolar surface tension, preventing collapse (atelectasis) at end-expiration. Without surfactant → high surface tension → alveolar collapse → V/Q mismatch → hypoxemia → protein-rich exudate → hyaline membranes.

📖 Teaching Point

Surfactant biochemistry: Stored in LAMELLAR BODIES within Type II pneumocytes. Secreted into the alveolar space, it forms a thin film at the air-liquid interface. DPPC is the key surface-active molecule. Surfactant proteins: SP-A (immune defense, most abundant), SP-B (ESSENTIAL — critical for surfactant function, deficiency is fatal), SP-C (enhances lipid spreading), SP-D (immune defense). L/S ratio: lecithin rises while sphingomyelin stays constant → ratio >2.0 = mature lungs.

📖 Teaching Point

Antenatal corticosteroids: BETAMETHASONE (12mg IM × 2 doses, 24h apart) or DEXAMETHASONE (6mg IM × 4 doses, 12h apart). Indicated for all pregnancies at risk of preterm delivery between 24-34 weeks. Benefits: reduces RDS by 50%, reduces IVH and NEC. Optimal effect at 24-48 hours after first dose, lasting ~7 days. Single 'rescue' course may be given if delivery hasn't occurred within 7-14 days of first course.

📖 Teaching Point

Surfactant therapy revolutionized neonatal care (introduced clinically in 1990). Types: Natural/animal-derived (poractant alfa from porcine, beractant from bovine) or synthetic (lucinactant). Dosing: 100-200 mg/kg via ETT. May need repeat doses (up to 3). Complications: transient bradycardia/desaturation during administration, pulmonary hemorrhage (rare). Modern approach: early CPAP as first-line → surfactant via less-invasive technique (LISA/MIST — thin catheter, no intubation) if CPAP fails.

📖 Teaching Point

BPD definition: need for supplemental O2 at 36 weeks corrected GA. Old BPD (pre-surfactant) = fibrosis + overdistension from aggressive ventilation. New BPD (post-surfactant) = arrested alveolarization + simplified vasculature in very premature infants who survive with gentler ventilation. Risk factors: extreme prematurity (<28 weeks), mechanical ventilation, oxygen toxicity, infection/inflammation, PDA. Prevention: antenatal steroids, early CPAP, gentle ventilation, caffeine.

Stage 6 of 6
🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
The baby is discharged home on low-flow oxygen at 3 months of age. By 12 months corrected age, she's off oxygen and developing normally. The BPD gradually improves as new alveoli continue to form postnatally.

Key Takeaways

✓Surfactant = DPPC (lecithin) + phospholipids + proteins (SP-A, B, C, D); produced by Type II pneumocytes
✓Surfactant production begins ~24 weeks, adequate for breathing at ~34-36 weeks
✓Antenatal betamethasone accelerates Type II maturation; reduces RDS by 50%
✓RDS: premature + ground-glass CXR + air bronchograms + grunting/retractions
✓Exogenous surfactant most effective within first 2 hours (INSURE technique)
✓New BPD = arrested alveolarization (fewer, larger alveoli), not fibrotic scarring
✓Alveoli continue forming until age 8: 50M at birth → 300-400M at maturity
