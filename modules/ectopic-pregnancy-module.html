MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Implantation outside the uterine cavity. Ampulla of fallopian tube is the most common location (70%). Life-threatening if rupture occurs → hemoperitoneum → hemorrhagic shock.

Risk FactorMechanism
Prior ectopicHighest risk factor (10-25% recurrence)
PID/STI (Chlamydia)Tubal scarring and adhesions
Prior tubal surgeryIncluding tubal ligation (failure rate ~0.5%)
IUDIUD prevents intrauterine pregnancy but NOT ectopic. If pregnancy occurs with IUD → higher proportion are ectopic
Assisted reproduction (IVF)Higher ectopic rate than natural conception
SmokingImpairs tubal motility and ciliary function
CLINICAL
Diagnosis

Classic Triad

Amenorrhea + vaginal bleeding + unilateral pelvic/abdominal pain. But can present with any combination, including hemodynamic instability if ruptured.

⚠️ Ruptured Ectopic = Surgical Emergency: Sudden severe abdominal pain, peritoneal signs, tachycardia, hypotension. Cullen sign (periumbilical ecchymosis). FAST/POCUS: free fluid in pelvis. Do NOT delay for imaging if unstable → emergent surgery.

Diagnostic Algorithm

StepAction
1. β-hCGPositive pregnancy test in reproductive-age woman with pain/bleeding
2. Transvaginal USNo intrauterine pregnancy (IUP) despite β-hCG above discriminatory zone (~1500-3500 mIU/mL) = ectopic until proven otherwise
3. Serial β-hCGNormal IUP: β-hCG doubles every 48h. Ectopic: abnormal rise (<35% increase) or plateau
🔑 Discriminatory Zone: β-hCG level above which IUP should be visible on TVUS (~1500-3500). If β-hCG above this level + empty uterus = ectopic until proven otherwise. If below: pregnancy of unknown location (PUL) → serial β-hCG q48h.
TREATMENT
Management
OptionCriteria
Methotrexate (medical)Hemodynamically stable, unruptured, β-hCG <5000, no fetal cardiac activity, mass <3.5 cm, patient reliable for follow-up. Single dose IM (50 mg/m²). Follow β-hCG to zero
Salpingostomy (surgical, tube-sparing)Desire future fertility with that tube. Remove ectopic, preserve tube. Follow β-hCG (persistent trophoblast risk)
Salpingectomy (surgical, tube removal)Ruptured, hemodynamically unstable, contraindicated for MTX, failed MTX, or contralateral tube is normal. Most definitive
Expectant managementVery early, low/declining β-hCG (<200), asymptomatic. Close follow-up
🔑 Methotrexate Monitoring: Check β-hCG on days 4 and 7. Expect ≥15% decline between day 4 and 7. If <15% decline → repeat dose or surgery. Follow weekly until β-hCG = 0. Avoid: alcohol, NSAIDs, folate supplements, intercourse, sun exposure during treatment.
