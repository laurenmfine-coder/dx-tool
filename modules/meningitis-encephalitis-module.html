CLINICAL
Diagnosis
⚠️ Do NOT delay antibiotics for LP! If bacterial meningitis is suspected: blood cultures → empiric antibiotics + dexamethasone → LP when safe. CT before LP ONLY if: immunocompromised, CNS disease history, new seizure, papilledema, focal neurologic deficit, altered consciousness.

🔑 HSV Encephalitis: Most common cause of sporadic fatal encephalitis. Temporal lobe predilection → behavioral changes, seizures, aphasia. MRI: T2 hyperintensity in temporal lobes. CSF: lymphocytic pleocytosis + RBCs. PCR for HSV is gold standard. Start IV acyclovir EMPIRICALLY — do NOT wait for results.
TREATMENT
Management

Empiric Antibiotics for Bacterial Meningitis

Age/RiskEmpiric Regimen
NeonatesAmpicillin + cefotaxime (or gentamicin)
Children/Adults (3 mo-50 yr)Ceftriaxone + vancomycin
>50, immunocompromised, alcoholicsCeftriaxone + vancomycin + AMPICILLIN (for Listeria)
🔑 Dexamethasone: Give BEFORE or WITH first antibiotic dose. Reduces inflammation → decreases hearing loss and neurologic sequelae. Proven benefit primarily for S. pneumoniae meningitis. Give 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h × 4 days. Timing is critical — no benefit if given after antibiotics.

Viral & HSV Encephalitis

Viral meningitis: supportive care (self-limited). Enteroviruses most common cause. HSV encephalitis: IV acyclovir 10 mg/kg q8h × 14-21 days. Start empirically for any encephalitis — delay increases mortality from ~70% (untreated) to <20% (treated). Do NOT wait for PCR results to start acyclovir.

Prevention

Meningococcal vaccine (MenACWY at 11-12, MenB for high-risk). Pneumococcal vaccine. Hib vaccine. Close contacts of N. meningitidis: rifampin or ciprofloxacin or ceftriaxone IM prophylaxis.
