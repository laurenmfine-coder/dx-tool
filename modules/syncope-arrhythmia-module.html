Syncope & Arrhythmias

Cardiac conduction, syncope mechanisms, AFib, and ventricular arrhythmias

⚠️ High-Risk Syncope Features

Exertional syncope
Syncope while supine/sitting
Associated chest pain
Family history sudden death
Abnormal ECG findings

✅ Low-Risk Features

Prodrome (warmth, nausea)
Standing for long period
After painful stimulus
Young, no cardiac history

🗺️ Syncope Classification

Syncope
Reflex (Neurally-Mediated)
Vasovagal Situational Carotid sinus
Orthostatic
Hypovolemia Drug-induced Autonomic failure
Cardiac
Arrhythmias Structural PE/Tamponade

📊 Key Values to Remember

≥20/10 Orthostatic BP drop (SBP/DBP mmHg)
<60 bpm Symptomatic bradycardia
>100 bpm VT definition (≥3 beats)
6→12 mg Adenosine dosing IV push

📈 ECG Pattern Recognition

AFib Irregularly irregular, no P waves
AFlutter Sawtooth at 300/min, regular
WPW Short PR, delta wave, wide QRS
Torsades Polymorphic VT + long QT
3° AVB AV dissociation, regular P & QRS
