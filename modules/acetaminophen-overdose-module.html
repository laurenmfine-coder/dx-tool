MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Acetaminophen (APAP) is the #1 cause of acute liver failure in the US. Normally: 90% conjugated (glucuronidation/sulfation) → renal excretion. 5-10% → CYP2E1 → NAPQI (toxic metabolite) → glutathione conjugation → harmless.

⚠️ In overdose: Conjugation pathways saturated → more NAPQI produced → glutathione depleted → NAPQI accumulates → centrilobular hepatic necrosis (zone 3 — highest CYP concentration). NAC replenishes glutathione.

Toxic Doses

Single acute ingestion ≥150 mg/kg (or ≥7.5g in adults) → potentially hepatotoxic. Chronic ingestion >4g/day (lower threshold in alcoholics/malnourished — CYP2E1 induction + depleted glutathione). Hepatotoxicity threshold lower with: chronic alcohol use, fasting/malnutrition, CYP2E1 inducers (isoniazid, phenytoin, rifampin).

CLINICAL
Stages of Toxicity
StageTimeFeatures
I0-24hAsymptomatic or nonspecific (nausea, vomiting, malaise). Labs NORMAL. Patient feels fine — deceptively well
II24-72hRUQ pain, elevated AST/ALT (may reach >10,000), rising INR/PT, early renal dysfunction
III72-96hPeak hepatotoxicity. Fulminant hepatic failure: jaundice, coagulopathy, encephalopathy, hypoglycemia, lactic acidosis, multi-organ failure. Death if untreated
IV4-14 daysRecovery phase (if survived). Liver regeneration. Complete recovery possible even after severe injury
🔑 Rumack-Matthew Nomogram: Plot APAP level at 4h post-ingestion (or later, up to 24h). Level above treatment line → give NAC. Only valid for single acute ingestion with known time. NOT valid for: chronic ingestion, unknown time, extended-release formulations (for these → treat empirically with NAC).
TREATMENT
N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)
🔑 NAC is most effective within 8 hours of ingestion (near 100% prevention of hepatotoxicity). Still beneficial up to 24h and beyond (reduces mortality even in established liver failure).
RouteProtocol
IV NAC (preferred if vomiting/liver failure)150 mg/kg loading over 1h → 50 mg/kg over 4h → 100 mg/kg over 16h (21h total). Anaphylactoid reactions possible (rate-related)
Oral NAC140 mg/kg loading → 70 mg/kg q4h × 17 additional doses (72h total). Significant nausea/vomiting

Indications for NAC

1) APAP level above treatment line on nomogram. 2) Suspected significant ingestion with >8h delay (don't wait for level). 3) Elevated aminotransferases with history of APAP ingestion. 4) Unknown time of ingestion with detectable APAP level. 5) Evidence of liver injury.

King's College Criteria (Liver Transplant)

APAP-induced ALF: pH <7.30 (after fluid resuscitation) OR all 3: PT >100 sec (INR >6.5), Cr >3.4, Grade III-IV encephalopathy. Meet criteria → transplant evaluation urgently.
