Pathophysiology

Adrenal crisis is acute, life-threatening cortisol deficiency causing cardiovascular collapse. Most commonly occurs when a patient with chronic adrenal insufficiency (on glucocorticoid replacement) faces physiologic stress without increasing their steroid dose.

🔑 Key Concept: The #1 cause of adrenal crisis is abrupt withdrawal of exogenous glucocorticoids in a patient with HPA axis suppression. Chronic steroid use suppresses ACTH → adrenal atrophy → unable to mount cortisol response to stress.

CLINICAL
Presentation & Diagnosis

Adrenal Crisis Presentation

FeatureDetails
Hypotension/shockRefractory to fluids and vasopressors (cortisol is required for vascular tone)
ElectrolytesHyponatremia (cortisol regulates free water), hyperkalemia (if primary — aldosterone deficiency)
HypoglycemiaCortisol is a counter-regulatory hormone
GI symptomsNausea, vomiting, abdominal pain (can mimic acute abdomen)
Altered mental statusLethargy, confusion, obtundation

Diagnostic Testing

ACTH stimulation test (cosyntropin test): Give 250 µg IV cosyntropin → measure cortisol at 0 and 60 minutes. Normal: cortisol ≥18 µg/dL at 60 min. Abnormal: cortisol <18 µg/dL = adrenal insufficiency. Random AM cortisol <3 µg/dL is diagnostic; >18 µg/dL excludes AI.

🔑 Don't delay treatment for testing. In suspected adrenal crisis, give dexamethasone (doesn't interfere with cortisol assay) while waiting for ACTH stim test, OR give hydrocortisone and treat empirically if clinical suspicion is high.
TREATMENT
Management

Acute Adrenal Crisis

StepAction
1. IV fluidsAggressive NS resuscitation (often 2-3L in first hours)
2. IV hydrocortisone100mg IV bolus → 50mg IV q8h (or 200mg/24h continuous infusion). Hydrocortisone at stress doses has mineralocorticoid activity — no need for separate fludrocortisone acutely
3. Correct hypoglycemiaD50 bolus if needed, then D5NS for maintenance
4. Identify triggerInfection, surgery, trauma, steroid withdrawal — treat the precipitant

Chronic Replacement

Primary AI: Hydrocortisone 15-25mg/day (divided doses) + fludrocortisone 0.05-0.1mg/day. Secondary/Tertiary: Hydrocortisone only (aldosterone is preserved). Stress dosing: Double or triple dose during illness; IV hydrocortisone 100mg for surgery/critical illness.

⚠️ Steroid Taper Rule: Never abruptly stop chronic steroids (>3 weeks of use or any cushingoid features). Taper gradually to allow HPA axis recovery. Stress-dose coverage may be needed for up to 12 months after discontinuation.
