Renal Function & Acid-Base

AKI, CKD, Acid-Base Interpretation, Renal Tubular Acidosis

🔬 Assessing Renal Function

Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR)

GFR is the best overall measure of kidney function. Estimated using creatinine-based equations.

CKD-EPI equation (preferred) — accounts for age, sex, race, creatinine

Cockcroft-Gault (for drug dosing):
CrCl = [(140 - age) × weight(kg)] / [72 × Cr] × 0.85 if female
Creatinine Limitations: Cr is affected by muscle mass, diet, drugs (trimethoprim, cimetidine block secretion → falsely elevated Cr without true GFR change). Cystatin C is less affected by muscle mass.

⚡ Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

KDIGO Definition

  • ↑ Cr ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours, OR
  • ↑ Cr ≥1.5× baseline within 7 days, OR
  • Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6 hours

Classification: Pre-renal vs Intrinsic vs Post-renal

🩸 Pre-renal (Most Common ~55%)

Mechanism: Decreased renal perfusion (kidneys structurally normal)

Causes: Hypovolemia, CHF, cirrhosis, sepsis, NSAIDs, ACEi/ARBs

Labs: BUN:Cr >20:1, FENa <1%, UOsm >500, UNa <20

Treatment: Volume repletion, treat underlying cause

🔴 Intrinsic Renal (~40%)

Mechanism: Damage to tubules, glomeruli, interstitium, or vessels

Types:

  • ATN (Acute Tubular Necrosis): Most common intrinsic cause — ischemia or nephrotoxins
  • AIN (Acute Interstitial Nephritis): Drug-induced (NSAIDs, antibiotics, PPIs), fever + rash + eosinophils
  • Glomerulonephritis: RBC casts, proteinuria, hematuria

Labs: BUN:Cr ~10-15:1, FENa >2%, muddy brown casts (ATN)

🚫 Post-renal (~5%)

Mechanism: Urinary tract obstruction

Causes: BPH (#1 in men), kidney stones, tumor, neurogenic bladder

Diagnosis: Renal ultrasound showing hydronephrosis

Treatment: Relieve obstruction (Foley, nephrostomy)

⚖️ Acid-Base Disorders

Normal Values

pH: 7.35-7.45 | PaCO2: 35-45 mmHg | HCO3: 22-26 mEq/L

🔢 Systematic Approach to ABG Interpretation

  1. Look at pH: Acidemia (<7.35) or Alkalemia (>7.45)?
  2. Identify primary disorder: Is CO2 or HCO3 moving in direction to explain pH?
  3. Check compensation: Is the other parameter moving appropriately?
  4. If metabolic acidosis: Calculate anion gap
  5. If AG elevated: Calculate delta-delta ratio

Compensation Rules

Primary DisorderExpected Compensation
Metabolic acidosisPaCO2 = 1.5 × HCO3 + 8 (±2) — Winter's formula
Metabolic alkalosisPaCO2 ↑ 0.7 for each 1 ↑ HCO3
Acute respiratory acidosisHCO3 ↑ 1 for each 10 ↑ PaCO2
Chronic respiratory acidosisHCO3 ↑ 3.5 for each 10 ↑ PaCO2
Acute respiratory alkalosisHCO3 ↓ 2 for each 10 ↓ PaCO2
Chronic respiratory alkalosisHCO3 ↓ 5 for each 10 ↓ PaCO2
Compensation Rule: Compensation returns pH toward normal but NEVER overcorrects. If pH is normal with abnormal CO2/HCO3, suspect mixed disorder.
🧮 Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis
Anion Gap = Na - (Cl + HCO3)
Normal: 8-12 mEq/L (or 3-11 depending on lab)

High Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (HAGMA)

🧠 MUDPILES

Methanol • Uremia • DKA • Propylene glycol • Isoniazid/Iron • Lactic acidosis • Ethylene glycol • Salicylates

🧠 Alternative: GOLDMARK

Glycols (ethylene, propylene) • Oxoproline • L-Lactate • D-Lactate • Methanol • Aspirin • Renal failure • Ketoacidosis

Normal Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (NAGMA)

🧠 HARDASS

Hyperalimentation • Addison's • RTA • Diarrhea • Acetazolamide • Spironolactone • Saline (dilutional)

Delta-Delta Ratio (Δ/Δ)

Delta-Delta = (AG - 12) / (24 - HCO3)

<1: Mixed HAGMA + NAGMA
1-2: Pure HAGMA
>2: Mixed HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis
Osmolar Gap: If HAGMA, calculate osmolar gap to identify toxic alcohols. Osmolar gap = Measured Osm - Calculated Osm. Gap >10 suggests methanol, ethylene glycol, or ethanol.

RTAs cause normal anion gap metabolic acidosis due to impaired renal acid handling.

📈 Metabolic Alkalosis

Causes — Chloride Responsive vs Resistant

Chloride-Responsive (UCl <20)
  • Vomiting / NG suction
  • Diuretics (after effect)
  • Post-hypercapnia

Treat with: NS (volume + chloride)

Chloride-Resistant (UCl >20)
  • Hyperaldosteronism
  • Cushing syndrome
  • Bartter/Gitelman syndrome
  • Severe hypokalemia

Treat underlying cause

Contraction Alkalosis: Volume depletion → kidney holds onto Na (and with it, HCO3) → maintains alkalosis. Give NS to break the cycle.
💨 Respiratory Acid-Base Disorders

Respiratory Acidosis (↑CO2)

Cause: Hypoventilation → CO2 retention

  • Acute: Opioid overdose, neuromuscular disease, severe asthma/COPD exacerbation
  • Chronic: COPD, obesity hypoventilation, neuromuscular disease

Respiratory Alkalosis (↓CO2)

Cause: Hyperventilation → CO2 blown off

  • Anxiety/pain
  • Hypoxia (triggers hyperventilation)
  • Pulmonary embolism
  • Sepsis (early)
  • Salicylate toxicity (stimulates respiratory center)
  • Pregnancy (progesterone effect)
  • High altitude
📝 High-Yield Summary

AKI

  • Pre-renal: FENa <1%, BUN:Cr >20, responds to fluids
  • ATN: FENa >2%, muddy brown casts, most common intrinsic cause
  • Post-renal: Obstruction, check ultrasound for hydronephrosis

Acid-Base

  • HAGMA: MUDPILES — DKA, lactic acidosis, toxic alcohols, uremia
  • NAGMA: Diarrhea, RTA, saline infusion
  • Type 4 RTA: Only RTA with hyperkalemia (hypoaldosteronism)
  • Metabolic alkalosis: Check urine Cl — responsive (<20) vs resistant (>20)
  • Winter's formula: Expected CO2 = 1.5 × HCO3 + 8
