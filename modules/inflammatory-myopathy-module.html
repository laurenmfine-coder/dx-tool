Classification

Inflammatory myopathies are autoimmune conditions causing proximal muscle weakness with elevated muscle enzymes. Three major types:

FeatureDermatomyositis (DM)Polymyositis (PM)Inclusion Body Myositis (IBM)
PathologyComplement-mediated microangiopathy (perimysial inflammation, perifascicular atrophy)CD8+ T cell-mediated endomysial inflammationRimmed vacuoles, inclusion bodies. Degenerative + inflammatory
WeaknessProximal symmetricProximal symmetricDistal + proximal (asymmetric). Finger flexors, knee extensors (quadriceps)
SkinHeliotrope rash, Gottron papules, V-sign, shawl signNoneNone
CancerStrong association (15-30%)Moderate associationNone
AgeAdults (or children)Adults >20Elderly >50 (mostly men)
Treatment responseGood (steroids + steroid-sparing)Good (steroids + steroid-sparing)POOR (resistant to immunosuppression)
🔑 Key Antibodies: Anti-Jo-1 = antisynthetase syndrome (myositis + ILD + mechanic's hands + arthritis + Raynaud + fever). Anti-Mi-2 = classic DM with good prognosis. Anti-MDA5 = amyopathic DM with rapidly progressive ILD. Anti-SRP = necrotizing myopathy (severe, poor prognosis). Anti-TIF1γ / Anti-NXP2 = cancer-associated DM.

FindingDescription
Heliotrope rashViolaceous (purple-lilac) discoloration of upper eyelids ± periorbital edema
Gottron papulesErythematous/violaceous papules over MCPs, PIPs, elbows, knees (extensor surfaces). PATHOGNOMONIC for DM
V-signErythema over anterior chest/neck in V-distribution
Shawl signErythema over upper back, shoulders, posterior neck
Mechanic's handsCracked, fissured skin on lateral fingers (associated with antisynthetase syndrome/anti-Jo-1)
Calcinosis cutisCalcium deposits in skin (especially juvenile DM)

Diagnostic Workup

CK (creatine kinase): Elevated 10-50x normal in active disease. Most sensitive muscle enzyme. Aldolase: also elevated. EMG: myopathic pattern (short, small, polyphasic potentials with fibrillations). MRI: edema in affected muscles (guides biopsy location). Muscle biopsy: gold standard — perifascicular atrophy (DM), endomysial CD8+ infiltrate (PM), rimmed vacuoles (IBM).

⚠️ Malignancy Screening: DM has 15-30% cancer association (highest in first 1-3 years after diagnosis). Most common cancers: ovarian, lung, GI, breast, lymphoma. Screen with CT chest/abdomen/pelvis, age-appropriate cancer screening, anti-TIF1γ/anti-NXP2 positivity increases suspicion. Consider PET-CT if high clinical suspicion.

🔑 Steroid Myopathy vs Active Disease: If a patient on chronic steroids has worsening weakness: CK normal → steroid myopathy (taper steroids). CK elevated → disease flare (increase immunosuppression). This distinction is clinically critical.
