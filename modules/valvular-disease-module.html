🔑 Dynamic Maneuvers:
• Valsalva / Standing: Decreases preload → most murmurs DECREASE, EXCEPT HCM and MVP (get louder/earlier)
• Squatting / Leg raise: Increases preload → most murmurs INCREASE, EXCEPT HCM and MVP (get softer/later)
• Inspiration: Increases venous return to RIGHT heart → right-sided murmurs louder (Carvallo sign)
CLINICAL
Key Valvular Conditions

Aortic Stenosis (AS)

Most common valvular disease requiring surgery. Causes: degenerative calcification (elderly, #1), bicuspid aortic valve (younger patients, most common congenital cardiac anomaly). Classic triad: Syncope, Angina, Dyspnea (SAD). Once symptoms develop, prognosis is poor without intervention (2-3 year survival with angina, 1-2 years with syncope, <1 year with HF).

Mitral Stenosis (MS)

Almost always from rheumatic heart disease (Group A Strep → rheumatic fever → valve scarring). LA enlargement → A-fib → thrombus → stroke. Symptoms worsen during pregnancy (increased blood volume/HR). Severe MS: valve area <1.0 cm² (normal 4-6 cm²).

Acute vs Chronic Mitral Regurgitation

⚠️ Acute MR (papillary muscle rupture post-MI, endocarditis, chordae rupture): sudden severe MR → LA cannot accommodate → acute pulmonary edema → cardiogenic shock. EMERGENCY — requires emergent surgery. Chronic MR: gradual LA/LV dilation compensates → may be asymptomatic for years until LV dysfunction develops.
