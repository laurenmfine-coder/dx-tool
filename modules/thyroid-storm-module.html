MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Thyroid storm is a life-threatening exaggeration of thyrotoxicosis with multisystem decompensation. It is NOT simply a higher level of thyroid hormone — rather, it involves catecholamine hypersensitivity, cytokine release, and acute decompensation of compensatory mechanisms. Mortality: 10-30% even with treatment.

MechanismEffectManifestation
↑ Catecholamine sensitivityThyroid hormone upregulates β-adrenergic receptorsSevere tachycardia, hypertension, hyperthermia, agitation
↑ Metabolic rateMassively increased oxygen consumptionHyperthermia (often >40°C/104°F), diaphoresis
Cardiac effects↑ chronotropy/inotropy, atrial irritabilityAfib (most common arrhythmia), high-output heart failure, cardiovascular collapse
GI effects↑ motility, hepatic dysfunctionDiarrhea, nausea, vomiting, jaundice (ominous sign — hepatic failure)
🔑 Key Concept: Thyroid storm is a clinical diagnosis using the Burch-Wartofsky Point Scale (BWPS). Score ≥45 = highly suggestive, 25-44 = impending storm, <25 = unlikely. Parameters: temperature, CNS effects, GI/hepatic dysfunction, heart rate, heart failure, and atrial fibrillation. Lab values (T3, T4) may not differ from uncomplicated thyrotoxicosis.
⚠️ Precipitants: Thyroid storm rarely occurs spontaneously. Common triggers: infection (most common), surgery, trauma, DKA, MI, radioactive iodine therapy, iodinated contrast, thyroid hormone overdose, withdrawal of antithyroid drugs, pregnancy/labor.
TREATMENT
Five-Pillar Management

Treatment Must Address ALL Pathways

PillarMedicationMechanism & Details
1. Block New SynthesisPTU (preferred) or methimazolePTU 500-1000 mg load, then 250 mg q4h. PTU preferred because it also blocks T4→T3 peripheral conversion. Methimazole 20 mg q4-6h if PTU unavailable.
2. Block ReleaseIodine (SSKI or Lugol's)MUST give ≥1 hour AFTER thionamide to prevent iodine from being incorporated into new thyroid hormone (Jod-Basedow effect). SSKI 5 drops q6h or Lugol's 10 drops q8h.
3. Block ConversionPTU (above) + dexamethasone + propranololDexamethasone 2 mg IV q6h blocks peripheral T4→T3 conversion AND treats possible relative adrenal insufficiency. Propranolol also blocks conversion.
4. Block EffectsBeta-blockersPropranolol 60-80 mg PO q4-6h (preferred — also blocks T4→T3) or esmolol IV drip. Target HR <100. Caution in heart failure — may need short-acting esmolol.
5. SupportiveCooling, fluids, treat precipitantAggressive cooling (acetaminophen + cooling blankets). Avoid aspirin — displaces T4 from binding proteins, increasing free T4. IV fluids + dextrose for dehydration/hypermetabolism. Identify and treat precipitant.
⚠️ Critical Timing: Iodine must be given ≥1 HOUR after thionamide (PTU/methimazole). Giving iodine first provides substrate for new hormone synthesis, potentially worsening the storm. The thionamide blocks the enzyme (TPO) first, then iodine safely reduces hormone release through the Wolff-Chaikoff effect.
