📖 Teaching Point

Clubfoot incidence: 1/1000 live births. Male:Female = 2:1. Bilateral in 50%. Etiology is multifactorial: genetic (family history increases risk 30×), mechanical (oligohydramnios), and neuromuscular. Most cases (80%) are 'idiopathic.' Important: always evaluate for underlying conditions — myelomeningocele, arthrogryposis, chromosomal anomalies, constriction bands. If other anomalies are present, the clubfoot is 'syndromic' and harder to treat.

📖 Teaching Point

3 axes of limb development: (1) PROXIMAL-DISTAL: AER (FGFs) → maintains progress zone proliferation; (2) ANTERIOR-POSTERIOR: ZPA (SHH) → thumb vs pinky; (3) DORSAL-VENTRAL: Wnt7a (dorsal ectoderm) → extensor vs flexor. Timing: Upper limb buds appear day 26, lower at day 28. Complete digit separation by week 8 (apoptosis of interdigital tissue). Lower limbs rotate MEDIALLY (that's why knees point forward), upper limbs rotate LATERALLY (elbows point backward).

The Ponseti method is initiated at 1 week of age. Weekly long-leg plaster casts are applied, each progressively correcting the deformity. The sequence of correction is: cavus first, then adductus and varus together, equinus last.

📖 Teaching Point

Ponseti correction sequence: (1) Cavus — corrected by supinating the forefoot relative to the hindfoot in the first cast. (2) Adductus + Varus — corrected simultaneously over 4-5 casts by abducting the foot with counter-pressure on the lateral talar head (NOT the calcaneus). (3) Equinus — corrected LAST with percutaneous Achilles tenotomy (85% of cases need this). Total: 5-7 weekly casts → tenotomy → boots and bar bracing.

📖 Teaching Point

Achilles tenotomy in Ponseti method: Performed percutaneously (small stab incision) in ~85% of clubfoot patients. Done in clinic or procedure room. Cast for 3 weeks post-tenotomy. Complications are rare (<1%): bleeding, incomplete cut, infection. The tendon regenerates reliably in infants. After cast removal, boots-and-bar bracing (Denis Browne bar) is started: 23 hours/day for 3 months, then nighttime only until age 4-5.

📖 Teaching Point

Ponseti bracing protocol: Phase 1 = full-time (23 hours/day) for 3 months. Phase 2 = nighttime + naps only (12-14 hours/day) until age 4-5 years. The boots are set at 60-70° external rotation (bilateral clubfoot) or 40° affected + 20° normal (unilateral). The bar keeps the feet apart. Common reasons for non-compliance: skin irritation, inconvenience, cultural barriers. Relapse management: repeat casting first, then if recurrent, anterior tibialis tendon transfer (age 2.5-5 years).

Stage 6 of 6
🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
The parents follow the bracing protocol diligently. At age 5, the child has normally functioning feet and plays soccer. The bracing is discontinued.

Key Takeaways

✓Clubfoot (CTEV): CAVE = Cavus, Adductus, Varus, Equinus; RIGID (doesn't correct passively)
✓Limb development: AER (proximal-distal), ZPA/SHH (anterior-posterior), Wnt7a (dorsal-ventral)
✓Lower limbs rotate MEDIALLY; upper limbs rotate LATERALLY
✓Ponseti method: serial casting (correct CAVE in order) → Achilles tenotomy → boots and bar
✓Equinus corrected LAST to avoid iatrogenic rocker-bottom foot
✓Achilles tenotomy: tendon regenerates at lengthened position in 3 weeks
✓Bracing compliance is the #1 factor: without it, 60-80% relapse
