⚙️ Acute Sinusitis

ENT

🔬 Pathophysiology

Sinus Obstruction

Obstruction of sinus ostia leads to infection.

Mucosal edema (viral URI)→Ostia obstruction→Mucus accumulation→Bacterial overgrowth→Acute bacterial sinusitis

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Facial pain/pressure over sinuses

Mechanism: Inflammation and pressure in obstructed sinus

Purulent nasal discharge

Mechanism: Bacterial infection

🔑 Key Points

✓ Viral >> bacterial (only 0.5-2% of URIs)
✓ Bacterial if: >10 days, severe, or biphasic
✓ Amoxicillin-clavulanate first-line
✓ Imaging not routinely needed
✓ Complications: orbital cellulitis, intracranial extension
