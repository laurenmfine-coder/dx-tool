MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

NMS: life-threatening reaction to dopamine antagonists (antipsychotics) or rapid withdrawal of dopaminergic agents. Central dopamine blockade in hypothalamus (thermoregulation) and basal ganglia (muscle rigidity).

FeatureDetails
TriggersTypical antipsychotics (haloperidol #1), atypical antipsychotics, metoclopramide, droperidol. Also: withdrawal of levodopa/dopamine agonists in Parkinson patients
OnsetDays to weeks after starting/increasing antipsychotic (usually within 2 weeks). Can occur at any time
TetradFARM: Fever (>38°C, may reach 40°C+), Autonomic instability (tachycardia, labile BP, diaphoresis), Rigidity (lead-pipe, generalized), Mental status changes (confusion → coma)
LabsCK markedly elevated (often >1000, can reach >100,000), leukocytosis, elevated LFTs, metabolic acidosis, myoglobinuria
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Differential
⚠️ NMS vs Serotonin Syndrome vs Malignant Hyperthermia:
FeatureNMSSerotonin SyndromeMalignant Hyperthermia
TriggerDopamine antagonistsSerotonergic drugsInhaled anesthetics, succinylcholine
OnsetDays-weeksHours (within 24h)Minutes-hours (intraoperative)
RigidityLead-pipe (generalized)Clonus + hyperreflexia (especially lower extremities)Masseter spasm, generalized
Key distinguisherLead-pipe rigidity, SLOW onsetCLONUS (most distinguishing feature), rapid onsetIntraoperative setting
TreatmentDantrolene + bromocriptineCyproheptadine (5-HT antagonist)Dantrolene
🔑 Clonus = Serotonin Syndrome. Lead-pipe rigidity = NMS. This is the single most reliable clinical distinction between the two conditions.
TREATMENT
Management
StepAction
1. Stop offending agentDiscontinue ALL antipsychotics/dopamine antagonists immediately
2. Supportive careICU. Aggressive cooling (ice packs, cooling blankets). IV fluids for rhabdomyolysis prevention. Monitor CK, renal function, electrolytes
3. DantroleneDirect muscle relaxant (blocks calcium release from sarcoplasmic reticulum → reduces rigidity and heat generation). 1-2.5 mg/kg IV
4. BromocriptineDopamine agonist — reverses central dopamine blockade. 2.5-10 mg PO/NG TID. Alternative: amantadine
🔑 Mortality: 10-20% (down from 30%+ with early recognition). Death from: renal failure (rhabdomyolysis → myoglobinuria), respiratory failure, cardiovascular collapse, DIC. Can re-challenge with antipsychotic after full resolution (2+ weeks), using low-potency agent with slow titration.
