Essential Exposure Questions for Lead

🏠 Housing
  • • Age of home? (Pre-1978 = lead paint)
  • • Recent renovations/paint chipping?
  • • Pica behavior? (eating paint chips)
  • • Type of plumbing? (Lead pipes)
👷 Parental Occupation
  • • Battery manufacturing?
  • • Construction/demolition?
  • • Radiator repair?
  • • Painting/paint removal?
  • • Take-home exposure on clothes?
🎯 Hobbies/Other
  • • Imported toys/ceramics?
  • • Traditional remedies (azarcon, greta)?
  • • Fishing sinkers/ammunition?
  • • Stained glass work?
🌍 Environmental
  • • Near highways/industrial sites?
  • • Contaminated soil from past industry?
  • • Water source testing?

Layer 2: Molecular Mechanisms

Understanding WHY lead is toxic explains the clinical findings

Lead's Three Main Mechanisms of Toxicity

1️⃣ Enzyme Inhibition (SH Groups)
Mechanism: Pb²⁺ has high affinity for sulfhydryl (-SH) groups → inhibits enzymes that depend on them
Key targets:
• δ-ALA dehydratase → blocks heme synthesis → anemia
• Ferrochelatase → can't insert Fe²⁺ into protoporphyrin → elevated FEP
This explains basophilic stippling + microcytic anemia!
2️⃣ Calcium Mimicry
Mechanism: Pb²⁺ substitutes for Ca²⁺ in signaling pathways and bone
• Crosses BBB via Ca²⁺ channels → neurotoxicity
• Deposits in bone (95% of body burden) → "lead lines" on X-ray
• Disrupts neurotransmitter release (Ca²⁺-dependent)
This explains neurological effects + why chelation works slowly (bone reservoir)
3️⃣ Oxidative Stress
Mechanism: Pb depletes glutathione + generates ROS
• ↓ Antioxidant capacity
• ↑ Lipid peroxidation
• Mitochondrial dysfunction
Contributes to multi-organ damage especially renal tubules

Layer 3: Multi-System Effects

Lead affects nearly every organ system - connect mechanism to manifestation

🧠 Neurological (Most Concerning)

  • • Children: Developmental delay, ↓ IQ, behavioral problems
  • • Adults: Peripheral neuropathy (wrist/foot drop)
  • • Encephalopathy at high levels
Mechanism: Ca²⁺ mimicry → disrupts neurotransmission + myelin

🩸 Hematologic

  • • Microcytic, hypochromic anemia
  • • Basophilic stippling
  • • Elevated FEP/ZPP
Mechanism: δ-ALA dehydratase + ferrochelatase inhibition

🦴 Musculoskeletal

  • • "Lead lines" on X-ray (metaphyses)
  • • Lead stored in bone (95%)
  • • Released during bone turnover
Mechanism: Ca²⁺ mimicry → substitutes in hydroxyapatite

🟢 GI

  • • Abdominal pain ("lead colic")
  • • Constipation
  • • Blue "Burton line" on gingiva
Mechanism: Smooth muscle spasm + Pb-sulfide deposits

🔵 Renal

  • • Proximal tubular dysfunction
  • • Fanconi syndrome (chronic)
  • • Interstitial nephritis
Mechanism: Oxidative stress + mitochondrial damage

🩺 Reproductive

  • • ↓ Sperm count/motility
  • • Miscarriage, preterm birth
  • • Crosses placenta → fetal effects
Critical: No safe level during pregnancy!

Blood Lead Level (BLL) Reference Values

< 3.5 µg/dL Reference value (97.5th percentile for children)
3.5-44 µg/dL Elevated - investigate source, consider chelation if >45
45-69 µg/dL Chelation therapy indicated
≥70 µg/dL Medical emergency - hospitalize for IV chelation
Note: CDC updated reference value from 5 to 3.5 µg/dL in 2021. There is NO safe level.

Layer 4: Chelation Logic

Understand WHY each agent is used and when

💊 Succimer (DMSA) - Oral
Mechanism: Water-soluble dithiol → binds Pb via SH groups → renal excretion
When: BLL 45-69 µg/dL (moderate toxicity), outpatient
Advantage: Oral, doesn't redistribute Pb to brain
Side effects: GI upset, rash, ↑ liver enzymes
💉 CaNa2EDTA - IV
Mechanism: Pb²⁺ displaces Ca²⁺ from EDTA → stable complex → renal excretion
When: BLL ≥70 µg/dL OR symptomatic encephalopathy
Key point: MUST use CaNa2EDTA, not Na2EDTA (would chelate Ca²⁺ → fatal hypocalcemia!)
Caution: Requires adequate urine output
💉 Dimercaprol (BAL) - IM
Mechanism: Lipophilic dithiol → enters cells including CNS → binds Pb
When: Lead encephalopathy - given BEFORE EDTA
Why before EDTA? EDTA mobilizes Pb from bone; BAL prevents redistribution to brain
Side effects: Painful injection, HTN, tachycardia

Treatment Decision Tree

BLL < 45: Identify & remove source, recheck in 1-3 months
BLL 45-69: Succimer (DMSA) oral × 19 days
BLL ≥70 without encephalopathy: CaNa2EDTA IV × 5 days
BLL ≥70 WITH encephalopathy: BAL IM first → then CaNa2EDTA (after 4 hours)
