MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Antiphospholipid syndrome (APS) is an autoimmune disorder characterized by arterial and/or venous thrombosis and pregnancy morbidity in the presence of persistently positive antiphospholipid antibodies. Can be primary (isolated) or secondary (usually with SLE).

🔑 The Paradox: Antiphospholipid antibodies cause a prolonged aPTT in vitro (lupus anticoagulant effect) but a PROthrombotic state in vivo. The antibodies activate endothelial cells, platelets, and complement → thrombosis, NOT bleeding.

Three Antiphospholipid Antibodies

AntibodyTestKey Points
Lupus anticoagulant (LA)Functional coagulation assay (dRVVT, mixing study does NOT correct)Most specific, strongest predictor of thrombosis. Prolongs aPTT. Named confusingly — causes thrombosis, not bleeding
Anti-cardiolipin (aCL)ELISA (IgG, IgM)IgG > IgM for clinical significance. Medium-high titers meaningful
Anti-β2 glycoprotein IELISA (IgG, IgM)Most specific ELISA test. Independent risk factor for thrombosis

Diagnostic criteria (revised Sapporo/Sydney): ≥1 clinical criterion (thrombosis or pregnancy morbidity) + ≥1 lab criterion (any of the 3 antibodies positive on 2 occasions ≥12 weeks apart).

CLINICAL
Clinical Manifestations

Thrombotic Events

TypeCommon SitesKey Details
Venous (more common)DVT/PE (most common), cerebral venous sinus, hepatic veins (Budd-Chiari), renal veinsDVT is the single most common manifestation of APS
ArterialStroke/TIA (most common arterial), MI, digital ischemia, renal artery, mesentericStroke in a young person → screen for APS. Multi-territory strokes suggest APS

Obstetric Morbidity

Recurrent early miscarriage (≥3 before 10 weeks) OR ≥1 late pregnancy loss (>10 weeks) OR preeclampsia/HELLP/placental insufficiency requiring delivery <34 weeks. Mechanism: placental thrombosis and inflammation.

Other Manifestations

Livedo reticularis (lace-like, reticular skin pattern), thrombocytopenia (mild, typically 50-100K — NOT an indication for anticoagulation withholding), cardiac valve disease (Libman-Sacks-like), APS nephropathy (thrombotic microangiopathy).

⚠️ Catastrophic APS (CAPS): Rare but life-threatening. Rapid (<1 week) multi-organ thrombosis (≥3 organs) with small vessel occlusion → multi-organ failure. Mortality ~50%. Treatment: anticoagulation + steroids + plasma exchange + IVIG (all 4 together). Triggered by infection, surgery, medication changes, or anticoagulation discontinuation.

🔑 Critical Points:
• Warfarin, NOT DOACs: Several trials (TRAPS, ASTRO-APS) showed higher thrombosis rates with rivaroxaban vs warfarin in triple-positive APS. DOACs are NOT recommended.
• Triple positivity (LA + aCL + anti-β2GP1 all positive) = highest thrombotic risk → lifelong warfarin essential.
• Pregnancy: Warfarin is teratogenic → switch to LMWH (enoxaparin) + aspirin. Warfarin crosses placenta; heparin does not.
• INR monitoring: Lupus anticoagulant can interfere with aPTT-based monitoring. Use chromogenic factor X assay if needed.
