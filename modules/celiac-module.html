MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Celiac disease: autoimmune reaction to gliadin (component of gluten — found in wheat, barley, rye) → T-cell mediated destruction of small intestinal villi → malabsorption.

FeatureDetails
GeneticsHLA-DQ2 (95%) or HLA-DQ8 (5%). Necessary but not sufficient — ~30% of population has DQ2/DQ8 but only ~1% develops celiac
HistologyVillous atrophy, crypt hyperplasia, intraepithelial lymphocytosis (Marsh classification)
LocationProximal small bowel (duodenum/jejunum)
AssociationsType 1 DM, thyroid disease, Down syndrome, Turner syndrome, IgA deficiency, dermatitis herpetiformis
🔑 Dermatitis Herpetiformis: Intensely pruritic, grouped vesicles on extensor surfaces (elbows, knees, buttocks). Pathognomonic for celiac disease (even without GI symptoms). Biopsy of uninvolved skin: granular IgA deposits at dermal papillae. Treatment: dapsone + gluten-free diet.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Malabsorption Syndromes

Celiac Diagnosis

StepTest
ScreeningAnti-tissue transglutaminase IgA (tTG-IgA) — most sensitive and specific screening test. Check total IgA simultaneously (IgA deficiency → false negative tTG-IgA → use IgG-based tests)
ConfirmationUpper endoscopy with duodenal biopsy (gold standard). Must be ON gluten-containing diet at time of testing
GeneticsHLA-DQ2/DQ8 — high negative predictive value. If negative, celiac is essentially ruled out

Other Malabsorption Causes

ConditionKey Feature
Tropical spruePost-infectious malabsorption in tropical regions. Responds to antibiotics (tetracycline + folate)
Whipple diseaseTropheryma whipplei. Arthralgia + diarrhea + weight loss + PAS-positive macrophages on biopsy
Lactose intoleranceLactase deficiency → osmotic diarrhea. Hydrogen breath test diagnostic
Chronic pancreatitisFat malabsorption (steatorrhea), calcifications on imaging. Fecal elastase <200
Small bowel bacterial overgrowth (SIBO)Hydrogen/methane breath test. Treat: rifaximin
TREATMENT
Management

Celiac Disease

Lifelong strict gluten-free diet (GFD) — wheat, barley, rye eliminated. Oats are generally safe (but cross-contamination risk). Monitor: symptom resolution, tTG-IgA normalization (6-12 months), consider repeat biopsy if symptoms persist.

⚠️ Complications of Untreated/Refractory Celiac:
• Iron deficiency anemia (#1 presentation in adults — proximal absorption impaired)
• Osteoporosis (calcium/vitamin D malabsorption) — DEXA scan at diagnosis
• Enteropathy-associated T-cell lymphoma (EATL) — rare but serious
• Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
• Refractory celiac: persistent symptoms despite strict GFD → evaluate compliance → if truly refractory → immunosuppression, evaluate for lymphoma
🔑 Nutrient Deficiencies by Location:
• Duodenum/jejunum: iron, folate, calcium (celiac territory)
• Terminal ileum: B12, bile salts (Crohn territory)
• Diffuse: fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) in fat malabsorption (pancreatic insufficiency, cholestatic disease)
