MECHANISM
Classification by Volume Status

Hyponatremia = serum Na+ <135 mEq/L. Most common electrolyte disorder in hospitalized patients.

VolumeMechanismCausesUNa
HypovolemicNa+ AND water loss, but more Na+ lostGI losses (vomiting, diarrhea), diuretics (thiazides > loops), burns, third-spacingRenal: >20
Extra-renal: <20
EuvolemicWater retention with relatively normal Na+SIADH (#1 euvolemic cause), hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, psychogenic polydipsia, beer potomania>20 (SIADH)
HypervolemicTotal body Na+ AND water increased, but water moreCHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome<20 (avid renal Na+ retention)
🔑 SIADH Criteria: Euvolemic hypo-osmolar hyponatremia + urine osmolality >100 (inappropriately concentrated) + UNa >20 + normal thyroid/adrenal function. Causes: CNS disease, pulmonary disease (PNA, SCLC), drugs (SSRIs, carbamazepine, cyclophosphamide), pain, nausea, post-surgical.
CLINICAL
Symptoms & Diagnosis

Symptoms by Na+ Level

Na+ LevelSymptoms
125-135Often asymptomatic or mild (nausea, headache)
120-125Nausea, malaise, confusion, headache
<120Seizures, coma, brain herniation, respiratory arrest
⚠️ Acute (<48h) severe hyponatremia is a medical emergency. Brain swelling → herniation → death. Requires emergent correction with hypertonic saline. BUT chronic hyponatremia must be corrected SLOWLY (brain has adapted).

Diagnostic Algorithm

1. Confirm true hyponatremia (check serum osmolality — must be <280). 2. Rule out pseudohyponatremia (hyperglycemia → corrected Na+ = measured Na+ + 1.6 per 100 glucose >100). 3. Check volume status clinically. 4. Check urine osmolality + UNa.

TREATMENT
Management
SettingTreatmentRate
Severe symptomatic (seizures, coma)3% hypertonic saline 100-150 mL IV bolus over 10-20 min. Repeat 1-2× as neededGoal: raise Na+ by 4-6 mEq/L in first few hours to stop symptoms
Chronic (>48h)Slow correction addressing underlying causeMaximum 8-10 mEq/L per 24h
SIADHFluid restriction (800-1000 mL/day). Salt tabs + loop diuretic (free water excretion). Tolvaptan (V2 antagonist) for refractory
HypovolemicIsotonic saline (NS) to restore volumeMonitor Na+ q2-4h
HypervolemicFluid + salt restriction. Diuretics for underlying causeSlow correction
⚠️ Osmotic Demyelination Syndrome (ODS/CPM): Correcting Na+ too rapidly (>10-12 mEq/24h) in CHRONIC hyponatremia → central pontine myelinolysis. Symptoms: quadriplegia, dysarthria, dysphagia, "locked-in" syndrome. IRREVERSIBLE. High risk: alcoholics, malnourished, hypokalemia, liver disease. If overcorrected: give D5W or desmopressin to re-lower Na+.
