MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Shock is inadequate tissue perfusion leading to cellular hypoxia and organ dysfunction. Defined by hypotension (MAP <65 or SBP <90) with signs of end-organ hypoperfusion (altered mental status, oliguria, elevated lactate).

Classification of Shock

TypeMechanismCOSVRPCWPClassic Cause
Hypovolemic↓ Preload↓↑↓Hemorrhage, dehydration
CardiogenicPump failure↓↑↑MI, cardiomyopathy, valvular
DistributiveVasodilation↑(early)↓↓Sepsis, anaphylaxis, neurogenic
ObstructiveMechanical obstruction↓↑VariablePE, tamponade, tension pneumo
🔑 Distinguishing Feature: SVR is LOW only in distributive shock (vasodilation). All other types have HIGH SVR (compensatory vasoconstriction). On exam: distributive = warm extremities; others = cold/clammy extremities.
⚠️ Cardiac Tamponade (Beck's Triad): Hypotension + distended neck veins (JVD) + muffled heart sounds. Pulsus paradoxus >10 mmHg. Echo shows pericardial effusion with RA/RV diastolic collapse. Emergency: pericardiocentesis.

Key Labs

Lactate is the critical marker of tissue hypoperfusion. Lactate >2 mmol/L = tissue hypoxia. >4 mmol/L = severe shock. Serial lactate clearance guides resuscitation adequacy.

TREATMENT
Management

Type-Specific Treatment

TypeTreatment
HypovolemicIV crystalloid (30 mL/kg bolus), blood products for hemorrhage, source control
CardiogenicAvoid fluids. Inotropes (dobutamine), vasopressors (norepinephrine), IABP/Impella, reperfusion if MI
SepticIV fluids (30 mL/kg crystalloid), antibiotics within 1 hour, norepinephrine (first-line vasopressor), source control
AnaphylacticEpinephrine IM 0.3-0.5mg (first-line), IV fluids, H1/H2 blockers, steroids
Obstructive (PE)Thrombolytics for massive PE, fluids cautiously
Obstructive (tamponade)Pericardiocentesis. Fluids as bridge to maintain preload
Obstructive (tension pneumo)Needle decompression (2nd ICS MCL) → chest tube
⚠️ First-Line Vasopressor: Norepinephrine is the first-line vasopressor for most shock states (especially septic). Vasopressin is the second-line add-on. Epinephrine is first-line for anaphylaxis and considered in cardiogenic shock.
