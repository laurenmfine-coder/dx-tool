Teaching Point

Failed hyperoxia test: give 100% FiO₂ for 10 min → if PaO₂ stays <150 mmHg (or SpO₂ stays low), the cyanosis is CARDIAC (shunt mixing), not pulmonary (ventilation). This is because in cyanotic CHD, the cyanosis is from mixing of deoxygenated blood into the systemic circulation — supplemental O₂ can't fix a structural mixing problem.

🔬 Embryology: AP Septum Spiraling
The AP septum normally spirals as it divides the truncus arteriosus into the aorta and PA. In D-TGA, the septum descends straight down without spiraling, so the aorta connects to the RV and the PA connects to the LV.

Teaching Point

TGA mixing: The only way to survive is through mixing sites: (1) PFO/ASD — atrial level mixing, (2) PDA — great vessel level mixing, (3) VSD — ventricular level mixing. Emergency management: PGE₁ infusion to keep PDA open + balloon atrial septostomy (Rashkind procedure) to enlarge the PFO. Definitive: arterial switch operation (Jatene) — swap the great arteries back to their correct ventricles + reimplant coronary arteries. Must be done within first 2-3 weeks (before LV deconditions).

Teaching Point

Rashkind balloon atrial septostomy: Can be performed at bedside under echo guidance in the NICU — no need for the cath lab in emergent situations. Technique: balloon catheter through umbilical vein or femoral vein → through PFO → inflate in LA → pull forcefully back into RA → tears septum primum. This buys time (days to weeks) until the arterial switch operation can be performed.

Teaching Point

Arterial switch operation (Jatene): Gold standard for D-TGA since the 1980s. Steps: (1) Transect aorta and PA above the valves; (2) Excise coronary buttons from the old aortic root; (3) Reimplant coronary buttons into the neo-aorta (old PA root); (4) Connect the great arteries to the correct ventricles using the LeCompte maneuver (PA brought anterior to aorta). Surgical mortality <3-5% at experienced centers. Long-term outcomes are excellent.

Teaching Point

TOF is the most common CYANOTIC CHD (after the neonatal period). The degree of cyanosis depends on the severity of RVOT obstruction — if mild, the patient may be 'pink TOF' initially. Tet spells (hypercyanotic episodes): triggered by crying, feeding, straining → ↓SVR → ↑R→L shunting → severe cyanosis → can be fatal. Treatment: knee-to-chest position (↑SVR), morphine (↓ agitation), phenylephrine (↑SVR), oxygen. Definitive: complete surgical repair (close VSD + relieve RVOT obstruction).

Teaching Point

DiGeorge/22q11.2 deletion (CATCH-22): Most common microdeletion syndrome (~1/4000). Cardiac defects in ~75%: truncus arteriosus, interrupted aortic arch type B, TOF, VSD. Immunodeficiency from thymic aplasia: T-cell deficiency → viral/fungal susceptibility. Hypocalcemia from hypoparathyroidism. Also: palatal anomalies, learning difficulties, psychiatric issues. Spectrum includes velocardiofacial syndrome (Shprintzen). Most cases are de novo mutations.

🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
The TGA baby does well after arterial switch — normal saturations at discharge. The TOF baby undergoes complete repair at 6 months. The DiGeorge patient requires truncus repair, calcium supplementation, and immunology follow-up.
