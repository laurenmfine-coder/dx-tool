Psychosis & Schizophrenia Spectrum

Differential diagnosis of psychosis, schizophrenia criteria, positive vs negative symptoms, and first-episode management.

📚 2 Sections ❓ 4 MCQs ⏱ ~30 min

🔍 Psychosis Differential

Causes of Psychosis

  • Primary psychiatric: Schizophrenia, schizoaffective, brief psychotic disorder, delusional disorder, bipolar with psychotic features, MDD with psychotic features
  • Substances: Stimulants (meth, cocaine), cannabis, hallucinogens, PCP, alcohol withdrawal (DTs), anticholinergics
  • Medical: Delirium (any cause), autoimmune encephalitis (anti-NMDA receptor), neurosyphilis, HIV, SLE cerebritis, Wilson disease, Huntington disease
  • Endocrine/Metabolic: Thyroid storm, Cushing syndrome, hepatic encephalopathy, uremia, porphyria
  • Key principle: ALWAYS rule out medical/substance causes before diagnosing primary psychotic disorder

Schizophrenia Spectrum Disorders

DisorderDurationKey Feature
Brief psychotic disorder1 day - 1 monthFull recovery; often after stressor
Schizophreniform1-6 monthsSame symptoms as schizophrenia but shorter duration
Schizophrenia>6 months≥2 symptoms (≥1 must be hallucinations, delusions, or disorganized speech) for ≥1 month active + 6 months total
SchizoaffectiveVariableSchizophrenia criteria + mood episode (major depression or mania) + ≥2 weeks of psychosis WITHOUT mood symptoms
Delusional disorder>1 monthNon-bizarre delusions WITHOUT other psychotic symptoms; functioning relatively preserved

🧠 Schizophrenia: Positive & Negative Symptoms

Positive vs Negative Symptoms

FeaturePositive (excess)Negative (deficit)
NatureAdded abnormal experiencesLost normal functions
ExamplesHallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech/behaviorFlat affect, alogia (poverty of speech), avolition, anhedonia, social withdrawal
Treatment responseRespond well to antipsychoticsPoor response to typical antipsychotics; atypicals somewhat better
NeurobiologyMesolimbic dopamine excessMesocortical dopamine deficit; glutamate dysfunction
PrognosisBetter prognostic indicatorWorse prognostic indicator (predicts functional decline)

First-Episode Psychosis (FEP) Workup

  • Rule out medical causes: CBC, CMP, TSH, RPR/VDRL, HIV, UDS, B12, folate, UA
  • Brain imaging: MRI preferred (rule out structural lesion, tumor, demyelination)
  • Consider if young woman with new psychosis: Anti-NMDA receptor encephalitis panel (associated with ovarian teratoma)
  • Treatment: Low-dose atypical antipsychotic (risperidone, aripiprazole). Start low, go slow. FEP patients are more sensitive to side effects and respond to lower doses.
  • Critical window: Duration of untreated psychosis (DUP) predicts outcome — shorter DUP = better prognosis. Early treatment is essential.

Schneider's First-Rank Symptoms

  • Auditory hallucinations: Voices conversing with each other, running commentary on patient's actions, thought echo
  • Thought disorders: Thought insertion (thoughts placed by external force), thought withdrawal (thoughts removed), thought broadcasting (others can hear thoughts)
  • Passivity experiences: Made feelings, made impulses, made actions (controlled by external force)
  • Delusional perception: Normal perception given delusional significance
  • Note: Not pathognomonic for schizophrenia but highly suggestive. Can occur in bipolar mania with psychosis.
