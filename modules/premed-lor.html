🎯 The LOR Strategy Framework

A strong letter comes from a physician who can write specific stories about you — not generic praise. Your job is to give them those stories over time.

📋 The LOR Timeline

1️⃣
Months 1-3: Be PresentShow up consistently. Ask thoughtful questions. Learn their patients' names. Be reliable.
2️⃣
Months 3-6: Show GrowthAsk progressively better questions. Reference things you've learned. Start predicting diagnoses. The physician will notice.
3️⃣
Months 6-9: DeepenHave conversations about medicine beyond the patient — healthcare systems, ethics, your motivations. Show your character.
4️⃣
Month 9+: The AskWhen you ask for a letter, provide your evidence packet: key interactions, growth moments, specific examples they can reference.

📦 The Evidence Packet

When you ask for a letter, give the physician: (1) A summary of your relationship and time together, (2) 3-5 specific moments that demonstrate your growth, curiosity, or character, (3) Your personal statement or key themes, (4) Schools you're applying to and deadlines. This transforms a generic letter into a powerful, specific endorsement.

⚡ Quick Wins for Better Letters

→ Ask questions that show you've been thinking, not just watching
→ Follow up: "Last week you mentioned X — I read about it and wanted to ask..."
→ Share what you're learning: "I used the platform to look up that condition"
→ Be honest about what you don't understand — curiosity > pretending to know
→ After a meaningful interaction, write it down (this tool helps!)
→ Ask early — give physicians at least 6 weeks before the deadline
