CLINICAL
Key Cancers in Detail

Endometrial Cancer

#1 GYN cancer. Risk = unopposed estrogen: obesity (#1 risk — aromatase converts androgens to estrogen in fat), PCOS, nulliparity, early menarche/late menopause, tamoxifen (agonist in uterus), Lynch syndrome (hereditary). Protective: OCPs, progesterone, pregnancy. Presentation: postmenopausal bleeding (always warrants endometrial biopsy). Type I (80%): endometrioid, estrogen-dependent, good prognosis. Type II (20%): serous/clear cell, aggressive, p53 mutations.

Ovarian Cancer

#1 GYN cancer killer (75% present stage III-IV). Risk: BRCA1/2 (lifetime risk 15-45%), family history, nulliparity, early menarche/late menopause, endometriosis. Protective: OCPs (40-50% risk reduction), tubal ligation, salpingectomy, breastfeeding. Marker: CA-125 (elevated but not specific — also elevated in endometriosis, PID, cirrhosis, pregnancy).

⚠️ Ovarian Cancer Presentation: Vague symptoms: bloating, early satiety, abdominal/pelvic pain, urinary urgency. Often diagnosed late. Imaging: transvaginal US (complex adnexal mass). Treatment: surgical debulking + platinum-based chemotherapy (carboplatin + paclitaxel).

Cervical Cancer

HPV 16 & 18 → persistent infection → cervical intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN) → invasive cancer (over years-decades). Screening: Pap smear age 21-65 (every 3 yr), HPV co-testing age 30-65 (every 5 yr). HPV vaccine: 9-valent (Gardasil 9), ages 9-45. Abnormal Pap → colposcopy with biopsy.

TREATMENT
Management
CancerTreatment
EndometrialStaging: surgical (TAH-BSO + lymph node assessment). Adjuvant: radiation ± chemo based on stage/risk. Progesterone therapy option for young patients wanting fertility preservation (grade 1 only)
OvarianMaximal surgical debulking (goal: no visible residual disease) + platinum-taxane chemotherapy. BRCA+ → PARP inhibitors (olaparib). Maintenance: bevacizumab or PARP inhibitors
CervicalCIN 1: observe. CIN 2-3: excision (LEEP/cone). Invasive: early → radical hysterectomy. Advanced → concurrent chemoradiation (cisplatin)
🔑 Lynch Syndrome (HNPCC): Mismatch repair gene mutations → increased risk of endometrial cancer (40-60% lifetime — higher than colon in women!), colon, ovarian, and other cancers. Screen with annual endometrial biopsy starting age 30-35. Consider prophylactic hysterectomy after childbearing.
