MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

PD = progressive loss of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra pars compacta → dopamine deficiency in the basal ganglia (striatum). Lewy bodies (α-synuclein aggregates) are the histologic hallmark.

🔑 Cardinal Features (TRAP):
Tremor — resting ("pill-rolling"), 4-6 Hz, worse at rest, improves with movement
Rigidity — cogwheel (ratchety resistance to passive movement)
Akinesia/Bradykinesia — slowness of movement, reduced amplitude, difficulty initiating (most disabling feature)
Postural instability — late feature, impaired balance, falls
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Differential

Clinical Features

FeatureDetails
Asymmetric onsetTypically starts unilateral (one hand/arm). Key differentiator from other parkinsonism
Masked faciesReduced facial expression (hypomimia)
MicrographiaSmall, cramped handwriting
Shuffling gaitShort steps, stooped posture, reduced arm swing, festination
Non-motorREM sleep behavior disorder, constipation, anosmia, depression, dementia (late)
⚠️ Parkinsonism vs Parkinson Disease:
• Drug-induced parkinsonism: antipsychotics (haloperidol), metoclopramide. SYMMETRIC, no tremor typically, reversible
• Vascular parkinsonism: lower body predominant, stepwise progression
• MSA: early autonomic failure, cerebellar signs, poor levodopa response
• PSP: early falls, vertical gaze palsy, axial rigidity
• Lewy body dementia: dementia BEFORE or within 1 year of motor symptoms, visual hallucinations
TREATMENT
Management
MedicationMechanismKey Points
Levodopa/CarbidopaDopamine precursor + peripheral decarboxylase inhibitorMOST EFFECTIVE. Motor complications with prolonged use: wearing-off, dyskinesias, on-off fluctuations. Start when symptoms impair function
Dopamine agonistsPramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotineYounger patients to delay levodopa. Risk: impulse control disorders (gambling, hypersexuality), somnolence
MAO-B inhibitorsSelegiline, rasagilineMild benefit. May be initial monotherapy for early disease. Serotonin syndrome risk with SSRIs
COMT inhibitorsEntacapone, tolcaponeExtend levodopa effect. Tolcapone: hepatotoxicity monitoring
AmantadineNMDA antagonist, mild dopaminergicTreats levodopa-induced dyskinesias
Deep brain stimulationSTN or GPi stimulationFor motor fluctuations/dyskinesias refractory to medication. Does NOT slow progression
🔑 NEVER abruptly stop levodopa/dopaminergic drugs — risk of NMS-like syndrome (akinetic crisis). Always taper gradually.
