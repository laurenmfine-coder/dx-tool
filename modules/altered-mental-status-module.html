MECHANISM
Pathophysiology & Framework

Altered mental status (AMS) reflects dysfunction of the ascending reticular activating system (ARAS) in the brainstem, bilateral cerebral cortices, or both. The mnemonic AEIOU-TIPS ensures no life-threatening cause is missed.

AEIOU-TIPS Differential

LetterCategoryExamples

\U0001f511 Delirium vs Dementia: Delirium = acute, fluctuating, attention impaired, usually reversible. Dementia = chronic, progressive, memory impaired, attention preserved early. Delirium can be superimposed on dementia.
CLINICAL
Systematic Evaluation
\u26a0\ufe0f Check fingerstick glucose IMMEDIATELY. Hypoglycemia is the most rapidly reversible cause of AMS and can cause permanent brain damage if missed.

Focused Exam Clues

FindingSuggests
Pinpoint pupilsOpioid overdose \u2192 naloxone
Dilated pupilsSympathomimetics, anticholinergics, herniation
Unilateral fixed/dilatedUncal herniation \u2192 neurosurgical emergency
Neck stiffness + feverMeningitis \u2192 empiric antibiotics before LP
Focal deficitsStroke, mass lesion \u2192 CT head
AsterixisHepatic or uremic encephalopathy
Track marksIV drug use \u2192 opioid OD, endocarditis

Standard Workup

Fingerstick glucose, CBC, BMP, LFTs, ammonia, UA, blood cultures, ABG, tox screen, TSH, CT head. Add LP if meningitis suspected (after CT if focal deficits). EEG if nonconvulsive status epilepticus considered.

TREATMENT
Empiric Management

Empiric Interventions

AgentIndicationKey Point
Dextrose (D50)HypoglycemiaGive after fingerstick confirms low or empirically if unavailable
NaloxoneOpioid overdosePinpoint pupils, respiratory depression; start 0.4mg IV, titrate
ThiamineWernicke preventionGive BEFORE glucose in alcoholic/malnourished patients
FlumazenilBenzo ODUse cautiously \u2014 seizure risk in chronic benzo users. NOT routine
\U0001f511 Thiamine before glucose: In alcoholic/malnourished patients, glucose without thiamine can precipitate Wernicke encephalopathy by depleting thiamine reserves. Classic triad: confusion, ophthalmoplegia, ataxia.
\u26a0\ufe0f Flumazenil: Avoid in chronic benzo use (seizure risk), unknown ingestion, or seizure history. NOT part of routine empiric AMS management.
