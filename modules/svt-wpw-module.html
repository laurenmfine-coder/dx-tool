MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Supraventricular tachycardia (SVT) encompasses tachyarrhythmias originating above the bundle of His. The most common mechanism is re-entry through the AV node (AVNRT ~60%) or an accessory pathway (AVRT ~30%).

SVT Types

TypeMechanismECG Features
AVNRT (60%)Re-entrant circuit within or near AV node (dual pathways: fast and slow)Narrow complex, regular, 150-250 bpm. Pseudo-R' in V1, pseudo-S in II/III/aVF (retrograde P waves buried in QRS).
AVRT (30%)Re-entry using accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent in WPW)Orthodromic (narrow complex, 95%): down AV node, up accessory pathway. Antidromic (wide complex, 5%): down accessory pathway, up AV node.
Atrial Tachycardia (10%)Focal automaticity or micro-reentry in atriumAbnormal P-wave morphology, variable conduction. May have warm-up/cool-down pattern.
🔑 Key Concept: WPW (Wolff-Parkinson-White) = ECG pattern of delta wave + short PR interval + wide QRS in a patient with an accessory pathway. The delta wave represents early ventricular depolarization through the accessory pathway (pre-excitation). The pathway can conduct in both directions, creating the substrate for AVRT.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & WPW Danger

ECG Recognition

SVT presents as a regular, narrow-complex tachycardia at 150-250 bpm (usually ~180 bpm). Sudden onset and offset ("paroxysmal"). Patients report palpitations, chest tightness, dizziness, and polyuria (ANP release from atrial stretch).

WPW + Atrial Fibrillation: The Lethal Combination

ScenarioConductionDanger
Normal SVT in WPW (orthodromic AVRT)Down AV node → up accessory pathwayNarrow complex, regular. Treat normally.
Afib with WPWRapid conduction down accessory pathway bypassing AV nodeLIFE-THREATENING. Irregular, wide complex, very rapid (>300 bpm possible). Can degenerate to VFib.
⚠️ NEVER give AV nodal blockers (adenosine, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, digoxin) in Afib with WPW! These drugs block the AV node but leave the accessory pathway unblocked → ALL atrial impulses conduct through the accessory pathway → ventricular rate can exceed 300 bpm → ventricular fibrillation → death. Treat with procainamide or electrical cardioversion.
TREATMENT
Management Algorithm

Stable SVT

StepInterventionDetails
1stVagal maneuversModified Valsalva (blow into syringe for 15 seconds, then immediately lie supine with legs elevated). Carotid massage (one side at a time, auscultate first for bruits). Cold water face immersion (dive reflex).
2ndAdenosine6 mg rapid IV push followed by 20 mL NS flush. If no effect in 1-2 min: 12 mg. Can repeat 12 mg × 1. Give through large proximal IV (antecubital or central). Warn patient: transient chest tightness, flushing, sense of impending doom (half-life 6-10 seconds).
3rdCalcium channel blocker or beta-blockerDiltiazem 15-20 mg IV or metoprolol 5 mg IV. NOT if WPW suspected or wide complex.
RefractorySynchronized cardioversion50-100 J biphasic. Sedate first if possible.

Afib with WPW (Pre-excited Afib)

Procainamide (slows conduction through accessory pathway) or ibutilide. If unstable: immediate synchronized cardioversion. NEVER: adenosine, diltiazem, metoprolol, verapamil, digoxin, amiodarone (controversial — some include it as contraindicated).

Long-term Management

Catheter ablation is curative for AVNRT, AVRT, and WPW (success rate >95% for accessory pathway ablation). Recommended for symptomatic WPW, high-risk accessory pathways, and recurrent SVT.
