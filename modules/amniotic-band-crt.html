📖 Teaching Point

ABS pathogenesis (extrinsic theory): Amnion ruptures early → fetus contacts sticky chorion surface → mesodermal bands from amnion/chorion entangle fetal structures → progressive constriction. This explains why defects are random, asymmetric, and non-hereditary. The spectrum ranges from mild (shallow skin grooves) to severe (complete amputations, facial clefts, body wall defects). Incidence: ~1 in 1,200-15,000 births. Important: ABS is a DISRUPTION (breakdown of previously normal structure), not a MALFORMATION (intrinsically abnormal development).

📖 Teaching Point

Birth defect classification: 1) MALFORMATION: intrinsically abnormal developmental process (e.g., VSD, cleft lip from failed fusion). Often genetic → may recur. 2) DEFORMATION: abnormal form from mechanical forces on normal tissue (e.g., clubfoot from oligohydramnios). Usually correctable. 3) DISRUPTION: destruction of previously normal tissue by external event (e.g., ABS, vascular accidents causing limb amputation, thalidomide). Low recurrence. 4) DYSPLASIA: abnormal cellular organization (e.g., skeletal dysplasias, ectodermal dysplasia). Often genetic → may recur. Sequence: one primary defect leads to cascade (e.g., Potter sequence: renal agenesis → oligohydramnios → limb deformities + pulmonary hypoplasia + flat facies).

📖 Teaching Point

ABS treatment spectrum: Shallow grooves: observation if asymptomatic, elective Z-plasty for cosmesis. Deep constriction rings: staged Z-plasty release (two stages, 6-12 weeks apart). Lymphedema: may need additional procedures (excision of redundant tissue). Digital fusions (acrosyndactyly): surgical separation, often with skin grafting. Amputations: prosthetic fitting, occupational therapy. Craniofacial clefts: typically atypical (not along lines of fusion) — may require complex reconstruction.

📖 Teaching Point

Limb reduction classification: TRANSVERSE (across the limb axis — like an amputation, e.g., ABS constriction) vs LONGITUDINAL (along the limb axis — preaxial/radial or postaxial/ulnar, e.g., Holt-Oram = radial ray defect + cardiac). Radial ray defects (absent/hypoplastic thumb, radius) are associated with: Holt-Oram (AD, cardiac), Fanconi anemia (AR, pancytopenia), TAR syndrome (thrombocytopenia with absent radii — but thumbs present!), VACTERL. Thalidomide: blocks angiogenesis → phocomelia (seal limbs), most sensitive period weeks 4-8.

📖 Teaching Point

Potter sequence: a SEQUENCE (single primary defect → cascade). Primary: bilateral renal agenesis (or any cause of severe oligohydramnios — bilateral MCDK, bilateral UPJ obstruction, PPROM). Secondary: oligohydramnios → pulmonary hypoplasia (lethal), limb contractures, Potter facies. The lungs are the critical component — death is from respiratory failure, not renal failure (dialysis could theoretically replace kidneys). Amniotic fluid sources: early = mainly transudate. Mid/late = fetal urine (major) + fetal lung secretions.
