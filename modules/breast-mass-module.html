MECHANISM
Evaluation

Triple assessment: clinical exam + imaging + tissue sampling.

AgeCommon MassesFirst Imaging
<30FibroadenomaUltrasound (dense tissue limits mammo)
30-50Fibrocystic, fibroadenoma, cancerDiagnostic mammogram + US
>50Cancer until proven otherwiseMammogram + US + biopsy
🔑 Suspicious Features: Fixed, irregular, hard, painless, skin dimpling, peau d'orange, nipple retraction, bloody discharge, axillary LAD.
CLINICAL
Common Breast Conditions
ConditionFeaturesManagement
FibroadenomaYoung women. Firm, rubbery, mobile ("breast mouse")Observe if <2 cm. Biopsy if growing
Fibrocystic changesMost common. Cyclical bilateral pain, nodularityReassurance, NSAIDs. No cancer risk (non-proliferative)
Intraductal papilloma#1 cause bloody nipple dischargeDuct excision
Phyllodes tumorLarge, rapidly growing, leaf-like histologyWide excision ≥1 cm margins. No LN mets
Fat necrosisPost-trauma. Mimics cancer on imagingCore biopsy to exclude malignancy
⚠️ Breast Cancer: Most common cancer in women. Risks: age (#1), BRCA1/2 (60-80% lifetime), family hx, early menarche, late menopause, nulliparity. Most common type: invasive ductal (70-80%). Inflammatory breast cancer: erythema, peau d'orange, NO mass — worst prognosis.

🔑 Receptor-Based Treatment:
• ER/PR+: Tamoxifen (pre-menopause) or aromatase inhibitor (post-menopause)
• HER2+: Trastuzumab — monitor for cardiotoxicity (decreased LVEF)
• Triple negative: Chemo. BRCA+ → PARP inhibitors

Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy

If SLN negative → no axillary dissection (avoids lymphedema). 1-2 positive SLN → radiation (ACOSOG Z0011 — no benefit to complete dissection for lumpectomy patients).
