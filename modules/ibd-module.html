CLINICAL
Extraintestinal Manifestations & Diagnosis

Extraintestinal Manifestations (same for both)

SystemManifestations
MSKPeripheral arthritis (most common EIM), sacroiliitis/ankylosing spondylitis (HLA-B27)
SkinErythema nodosum (parallels disease activity), pyoderma gangrenosum (UC > Crohn, independent of activity)
EyesUveitis/episcleritis
HepatobiliaryPrimary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC) — strongly associated with UC (70% of PSC patients have UC). ERCP: beading pattern
🔑 Serologic Markers: p-ANCA positive in UC (60-70%). ASCA positive in Crohn (60-70%). Not diagnostic alone but helpful in indeterminate cases. Calprotectin and lactoferrin are fecal markers that distinguish IBD from IBS.

Diagnosis

Colonoscopy with biopsy is gold standard for both. CT enterography or MR enterography for small bowel Crohn evaluation. Capsule endoscopy if CT/MR negative but clinical suspicion remains.

TREATMENT
Step-Up Therapy
SeverityTreatment
Mild5-ASA (mesalamine) — especially UC. Budesonide for ileal Crohn
ModerateCorticosteroids (prednisone) for flares (not maintenance!). Thiopurines (azathioprine/6-MP). Methotrexate (Crohn)
Severe/RefractoryBiologics: anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab — first-line for moderate-severe), anti-integrin (vedolizumab — gut-selective), anti-IL-12/23 (ustekinumab), JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib — UC)
⚠️ Toxic Megacolon: Life-threatening complication of UC (or C. diff). Transverse colon >6 cm + systemic toxicity (fever, tachycardia, leukocytosis). Treatment: NPO, NG decompression, IV steroids, broad-spectrum antibiotics. If no improvement in 48-72h → emergent colectomy. AVOID colonoscopy, opioids, anticholinergics (increase dilation risk).
🔑 CRC Surveillance in UC: Start colonoscopy 8-10 years after diagnosis, then every 1-2 years with random biopsies. PSC patients: annual colonoscopy from diagnosis (higher CRC risk).
