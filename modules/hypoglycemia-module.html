MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Hypoglycemia is defined by Whipple's Triad: (1) symptoms consistent with hypoglycemia, (2) low plasma glucose (<70 mg/dL symptomatic; <54 mg/dL clinically significant), (3) symptom resolution with glucose correction.

🔑 Counter-Regulatory Hormones: When glucose drops: glucagon (first response) → epinephrine → cortisol → growth hormone. In diabetes, glucagon response is often impaired, and beta-blocker use masks adrenergic symptoms.

Etiologies

CategoryCausesMechanism
Medication-inducedInsulin, sulfonylureas (#1 cause overall in diabetics)Excessive insulin or insulin secretagogue effect
InsulinomaPancreatic β-cell tumorAutonomous insulin secretion; high insulin + high C-peptide + low glucose
FactitiousExogenous insulin injection, surreptitious sulfonylurea useExogenous insulin: high insulin, LOW C-peptide. Sulfonylurea: high insulin, high C-peptide + positive drug screen
Adrenal insufficiencyCortisol deficiencyLoss of counter-regulatory hormone
Hepatic failureCirrhosis, fulminant hepatitisImpaired gluconeogenesis and glycogen storage
AlcoholBinge drinking (especially fasting)NAD+ consumed by alcohol metabolism → blocks gluconeogenesis
SepsisSevere infectionIncreased glucose utilization, hepatic dysfunction
⚠️ Hypoglycemia Unawareness: Recurrent hypoglycemia downregulates the adrenergic response → patients lose warning symptoms (tremor, sweating) and progress directly to neuroglycopenic symptoms (confusion, seizure, coma). Common in type 1 DM with tight control. Avoid hypoglycemia for 2-3 weeks to restore awareness.

Diagnostic Workup (for non-diabetic hypoglycemia)

During a hypoglycemic episode, simultaneously measure: glucose, insulin, C-peptide, proinsulin, β-hydroxybutyrate, and sulfonylurea screen.

DiagnosisInsulinC-PeptideSulfonylurea Screen
Insulinoma↑ HIGH↑ HIGHNegative
Exogenous insulin↑ HIGH↓ LOWNegative
Sulfonylurea use↑ HIGH↑ HIGHPositive
Non-insulin mediated↓ LOW↓ LOWNegative
🔑 C-peptide is the key. C-peptide is co-secreted with endogenous insulin in a 1:1 ratio. HIGH insulin + HIGH C-peptide = endogenous source (insulinoma or sulfonylurea). HIGH insulin + LOW C-peptide = exogenous insulin administration.
TREATMENT
Management

Acute Treatment

SettingTreatment
Conscious, able to swallow15-20g fast-acting carbohydrate (juice, glucose tablets). Recheck in 15 min
Unconscious or unable to swallowIV dextrose (D50 25-50mL) OR Glucagon 1mg IM/SC (if no IV access)
Sulfonylurea-inducedIV dextrose + octreotide (inhibits insulin secretion). Admit for observation (long half-life of sulfonylureas)
⚠️ Sulfonylurea Hypoglycemia: Requires prolonged monitoring (24-72h) because sulfonylureas have long half-lives (glipizide, glyburide). Dextrose bolus alone causes MORE insulin secretion → rebound hypoglycemia. Add octreotide to suppress insulin release.
