MECHANISM
Overview & Classification

Acute leukemia = malignant proliferation of immature hematopoietic cells (blasts) in the bone marrow, with ≥20% blasts on bone marrow biopsy (WHO criteria).

FeatureALL (Lymphoblastic)AML (Myeloid)
Peak ageChildren (2-5 years) — most common pediatric cancerAdults (median 68 years)

MorphologySmall blasts, scant cytoplasm, no granulesLarger blasts, Auer rods (fused azurophilic granules)

🔑 Auer rods are PATHOGNOMONIC for AML. They are fused azurophilic granules seen in myeloblasts. Most associated with APL (M3) but can occur in other AML subtypes. TdT (terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase) is a marker of immature lymphocytes — positive in ALL, negative in AML.
CLINICAL
Clinical Presentation

Bone Marrow Failure Symptoms

Blasts crowd out normal hematopoiesis → pancytopenia: anemia (fatigue, pallor), thrombocytopenia (bleeding, petechiae, epistaxis), neutropenia (infections, fever).

Organ Infiltration

Lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, bone pain (children often present with limping or refusal to walk), CNS involvement (headache, cranial nerve palsies — especially in ALL), gum hypertrophy (AML, especially M4/M5 monocytic subtypes), mediastinal mass (T-cell ALL), testicular enlargement (ALL relapse sanctuary site).

⚠️ Leukostasis: WBC >100,000 → blast cells clog microvasculature → respiratory distress, CNS symptoms, DIC. Emergency: leukapheresis + hydroxyurea to rapidly reduce WBC. Most common in AML (larger, stickier blasts).

Tumor Lysis Syndrome (TLS)

Rapid cell death releases intracellular contents: hyperuricemia, hyperkalemia, hyperphosphatemia, hypocalcemia. Prevention: aggressive IV fluids + allopurinol or rasburicase (recombinant urate oxidase). Monitor electrolytes q6-8h. Can cause acute kidney injury, cardiac arrhythmias.

HIGH YIELD
AML Subtypes: Focus on APL

Acute Promyelocytic Leukemia (APL/M3)

⚠️ Oncologic EMERGENCY. APL = t(15;17) → PML-RARA fusion. Presents with DIC (life-threatening bleeding). The promyelocytes release tissue factor → consumption coagulopathy.

Treatment: All-trans retinoic acid (ATRA) + arsenic trioxide (ATO). ATRA differentiates the arrested promyelocytes into mature neutrophils. This is a landmark of targeted therapy — cure rates >90%.

🔑 Differentiation syndrome (formerly ATRA syndrome): fever, dyspnea, weight gain, pulmonary infiltrates, pleural/pericardial effusion when promyelocytes differentiate. Treatment: dexamethasone. Can be fatal if untreated.

TREATMENT
ALL: Treatment & Prognosis

ALL Treatment Phases

Induction → Consolidation → Maintenance (2-3 years total). Induction: vincristine + prednisone + L-asparaginase ± daunorubicin. CNS prophylaxis: intrathecal methotrexate (CNS is a sanctuary site).

Prognostic Factors in ALL

🔑 Philadelphia chromosome t(9;22) = BCR-ABL fusion → constitutive tyrosine kinase activity. Found in 25% of adult ALL (poor prognosis) and 95% of CML. Treatment: add imatinib (TKI) to chemotherapy.

Pediatric ALL cure rate: ~90% — one of the great success stories in oncology. Adult ALL cure rate: ~40-50%.
