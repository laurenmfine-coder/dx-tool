🔑 BNP/NT-proBNP: Elevated in HF (ventricular wall stress → BNP release). BNP >100 or NT-proBNP >300 suggests HF. Used for diagnosis AND monitoring. BNP is falsely LOW in obesity and falsely HIGH in renal failure, A-fib, and older age.

⚠️ Acute Decompensated HF (Flash Pulmonary Edema): Severe dyspnea, SpO2 dropping, pink frothy sputum, crackles bilaterally. Treatment: sit upright, oxygen/NIV (BiPAP preferred), IV furosemide, nitroglycerin (afterload reduction), morphine cautiously. If cardiogenic shock: inotropes (dobutamine/milrinone) ± mechanical support (IABP, Impella).
🔑 S3 vs S4 Gallop: S3 = rapid ventricular filling (volume overload, systolic HF) — pathologic in adults >40. S4 = atrial contraction against stiff ventricle (diastolic dysfunction, HTN, HCM). S4 disappears in A-fib (no organized atrial contraction).

Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)

HFrEF: The Four Pillars

Drug ClassExamplesMortality BenefitKey Notes
ACEi/ARB or ARNISacubitril-valsartan (ARNI) preferred over ACEi/ARB✅ YesARNI: PARADIGM-HF trial. Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB. 36h washout before switching ACEi → ARNI
Beta-blockerCarvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol (ONLY these 3)✅ YesDo NOT start in acute decompensation. Only these 3 specific BBs shown to reduce mortality
MRASpironolactone, eplerenone✅ YesRALES trial. Monitor K+ and Cr. Contraindicated if K+ >5 or GFR <30
SGLT2 inhibitorDapagliflozin, empagliflozin✅ YesDAPA-HF, EMPEROR-Reduced. Benefits even in non-diabetics. Newest pillar
🔑 Diuretics (furosemide) relieve SYMPTOMS but do NOT reduce mortality. Only the four pillars above have mortality benefit in HFrEF. Hydralazine + isosorbide dinitrate is used in patients who cannot tolerate ACEi/ARB/ARNI (especially beneficial in Black patients — A-HeFT trial).

HFpEF Treatment

No proven mortality-reducing therapy until recently. SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin — EMPEROR-Preserved trial) now show benefit. Otherwise: diuretics for congestion, BP control, treat underlying cause. Rate control for A-fib.
