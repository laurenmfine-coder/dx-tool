MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Anaphylaxis is a severe, potentially fatal systemic IgE-mediated (Type I) hypersensitivity reaction. Mechanism: allergen crosslinks IgE on mast cells/basophils → massive degranulation → release of histamine, tryptase, leukotrienes, prostaglandins, and PAF → vasodilation, increased vascular permeability, bronchospasm, and mucus secretion.

🔑 Key Concept: Anaphylaxis can also be non-IgE mediated (anaphylactoid): direct mast cell activation by radiocontrast, opioids, vancomycin (Red Man Syndrome), or complement activation. Clinically identical to IgE-mediated anaphylaxis. Exercise-induced anaphylaxis and cold-induced anaphylaxis also occur.

Mediators and Effects

MediatorEffectClinical Manifestation
HistamineVasodilation, increased permeability, bronchospasmFlushing, urticaria, hypotension, wheezing
TryptaseMast cell activation markerElevated serum tryptase confirms mast cell degranulation (peak 60-90 min, draw within 3 hrs)
Leukotrienes C4/D4Potent bronchoconstriction, mucusSevere bronchospasm, refractory wheezing
PAFPlatelet activation, vasodilationCardiovascular collapse, DIC in severe cases
⚠️ Biphasic Reactions: 5-20% of patients experience a second phase 4-12 hours (up to 72 hours) after initial resolution. Risk factors: severe initial reaction, delayed epinephrine, oral allergen trigger. All patients should be observed for minimum 4-6 hours (some guidelines suggest 12-24 hours for severe cases).
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Presentation

Diagnostic Criteria (any ONE of three)

1. Acute onset involving skin/mucosal tissue + respiratory compromise OR hypotension

2. Two or more of the following rapidly after exposure: skin/mucosal involvement, respiratory compromise, hypotension, persistent GI symptoms

3. Hypotension after exposure to a known allergen

SystemManifestationsFrequency
Skin/MucosalUrticaria, flushing, angioedema, pruritus80-90%

🔑 Board Pearl: Anaphylaxis can occur WITHOUT skin findings in 10-20% of cases — especially with drug and venom triggers. Don't exclude anaphylaxis based on absence of urticaria. Cardiovascular collapse may be the only manifestation.
TREATMENT
Management

Immediate Treatment Algorithm

PriorityInterventionDetails
FIRSTEpinephrine IM0.3-0.5 mg (1:1000) IM in anterolateral thigh. Pediatric: 0.01 mg/kg (max 0.3 mg). Repeat every 5-15 min PRN. NO SUBSTITUTE — antihistamines are NOT first-line.
SecondPosition + O₂Supine with legs elevated (if hypotensive). High-flow O₂. IV access × 2 large-bore.
ThirdIV fluidsNS bolus 1-2 L rapidly for hypotension. May need 5-10 L in severe distributive shock.
AdjunctsH1 + H2 blockers, steroids, albuterolDiphenhydramine 25-50 mg IV, ranitidine/famotidine IV, methylprednisolone 125 mg IV (prevents biphasic), albuterol nebulizer for bronchospasm.
RefractoryEpinephrine infusion, glucagonEpi drip 1-10 mcg/min for refractory hypotension. Glucagon 1-5 mg IV for patients on beta-blockers (bypasses beta-receptor).
⚠️ Fatal Errors: (1) Delaying epinephrine — mortality rises dramatically with each minute of delay. (2) Using antihistamines instead of epinephrine. (3) Giving epinephrine IV push (causes arrhythmias — IM only unless in cardiac arrest or on infusion pump). (4) Discharging too early without observation for biphasic reaction.

Discharge Plan

Prescribe epinephrine auto-injector (2 devices), create anaphylaxis action plan, refer to allergist for trigger identification, consider medical alert bracelet. Educate on biphasic reaction risk.
