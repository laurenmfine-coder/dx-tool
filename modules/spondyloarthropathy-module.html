Overview

Spondyloarthropathies (SpA) are a group of HLA-B27-associated inflammatory disorders affecting the axial skeleton, entheses (tendon/ligament insertions), and peripheral joints. Unlike RA, they are seronegative (RF and anti-CCP negative).

🔑 Shared Features (Seronegative SpA): Inflammatory back pain (worse at rest, better with activity, morning stiffness >30 min), sacroiliitis, enthesitis, dactylitis ("sausage digits"), HLA-B27 association, anterior uveitis, RF negative. Often affects young men age 15-40.

CLINICAL
Ankylosing Spondylitis

Clinical Features

Inflammatory back pain in a young male (<40): insidious onset, duration >3 months, worse at night/rest, improves with exercise, morning stiffness >30 minutes. Progressive spinal fusion → loss of lumbar lordosis → thoracic kyphosis → fixed flexion deformity.

Physical Exam

Schober test: Measures lumbar flexion. Mark 10 cm above L5 → patient bends forward → <5 cm expansion = positive (limited flexion). Reduced chest expansion (<5 cm) from costovertebral ankylosis.

Extra-Articular Manifestations

SystemManifestation
EyeAnterior uveitis (acute, unilateral, painful) — most common extra-articular feature (25-40%)
CardiacAortic regurgitation, conduction abnormalities (AV block)
PulmonaryApical pulmonary fibrosis (restrictive pattern from chest wall fusion)
RenalIgA nephropathy (secondary)
NeurologicCauda equina syndrome (rare, late). Spinal fractures with minimal trauma (chalk stick fractures)
⚠️ Imaging: MRI sacroiliac joints = gold standard for early disease (bone marrow edema). X-ray shows sacroiliitis (bilateral, symmetric) and syndesmophytes → bamboo spine in advanced disease. X-ray changes lag years behind symptoms.

Psoriatic Arthritis (5 Patterns)

1. Asymmetric oligoarthritis (most common). 2. Symmetric polyarthritis (RA-like). 3. DIP-predominant. 4. Spondylitis. 5. Arthritis mutilans (severe destructive → "pencil-in-cup" deformity). Nail changes (pitting, onycholysis) correlate with DIP arthritis.
