MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

CO binds hemoglobin with 200-250× greater affinity than O2 → forms carboxyhemoglobin (COHb) → displaces O2 + shifts oxyhemoglobin dissociation curve LEFT → impaired O2 delivery to tissues. Also directly inhibits cytochrome oxidase (mitochondrial toxicity).

FeatureDetails
SourcesHouse fires (#1), car exhaust, faulty furnaces/heaters, generators in enclosed spaces, charcoal grills indoors
Key deceptionCO is odorless, colorless. Pulse oximetry reads FALSELY NORMAL (cannot distinguish COHb from OxyHb). SpO2 is unreliable!
Seasonal patternWinter (heaters, enclosed spaces). Post-disaster (generators after power outages)
CLINICAL
Presentation & Diagnosis

Symptoms by COHb Level

COHb %Symptoms
<10%Normal (smokers: baseline 3-10%)
10-20%Headache (#1 symptom), dizziness, nausea
20-40%Confusion, visual changes, syncope, chest pain
40-60%Seizures, coma, arrhythmias
>60%Death
⚠️ Cherry-red skin is a LATE/POSTMORTEM finding. Do not rely on it clinically. Diagnosis: co-oximetry (ABG with co-oximeter) — directly measures COHb. Standard pulse ox is UNRELIABLE. Always check co-oximetry in house fire victims, winter headaches, multiple family members with same symptoms.
🔑 Clues to CO Poisoning: Headache that improves when leaving the house, multiple family members/pets ill simultaneously, winter, post-storm/generator use, fire exposure. "Headache + family members sick" = CO until proven otherwise.
TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
100% O2 via NRB (first-line)Displaces CO from hemoglobin. COHb half-life: room air = 4-6h, 100% NRB = 60-90 min, hyperbaric = 20-30 min
Hyperbaric O2 (HBO)Indications: LOC/coma, seizures, focal neuro deficits, COHb >25% (some say >15% in pregnant), cardiac ischemia, persistent symptoms despite NRB
🔑 Delayed Neuropsychiatric Syndrome (DNS): 2-40 days post-exposure. Cognitive deficits, personality changes, parkinsonism, memory loss. Occurs in 15-40% of significant exposures. HBO therapy may reduce DNS risk (controversial but one rationale for HBO). Follow-up cognitive testing recommended.
⚠️ Pregnancy: Fetal hemoglobin binds CO with even greater affinity than adult Hb → fetal COHb levels higher and clear more slowly. Lower threshold for HBO in pregnant patients. CO poisoning in pregnancy is an obstetric emergency.
