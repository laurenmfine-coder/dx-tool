🔧 Hypospadias

One of the Most Common Congenital Anomalies in Males

📚 Overview

Hypospadias is a congenital condition where the urethral meatus opens on the ventral (underside) surface of the penis, proximal to its normal position at the tip of the glans.

Epidemiology

ParameterValue
Incidence1 in 200-300 male births
TrendIncreasing (possibly environmental factors)
Familial8% if father affected, 14% if sibling affected
Associated anomaliesCryptorchidism (10%), inguinal hernia (10%)
⚠️ CRITICAL: DO NOT CIRCUMCISE a baby with hypospadias! The foreskin is needed for surgical repair (can be used as graft material).

🧬 Embryology

Normal Development: The penile urethra forms when the urogenital (urethral) folds fuse in the midline on the ventral surface of the penis, proceeding from proximal to distal during weeks 8-14.

Normal Urethral Fold Fusion

TESTOSTERONE (from fetal Leydig cells) ↓ 5α-reductase (in genital skin) ↓ DHT (dihydrotestosterone) ↓ Urethral folds fuse ventrally (PROXIMAL → DISTAL direction) ↓ Complete penile urethra with meatus at glans tip

Hypospadias: Fusion Failure

URETHRAL FOLD FUSION STOPS PREMATURELY ↓ ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐ │ WHERE FUSION STOPS = MEATUS LOCATION │ │ │ │ Proximal failure = More severe hypospadias │ │ Distal failure = Milder hypospadias │ └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘ ↓ Associated findings: • Ventral curvature (CHORDEE) - from fibrotic tissue • Hooded foreskin (dorsal only) • Abnormal glans (flattened, grooved)

Etiology

CategoryExamples
GeneticPolygenic, AR mutations, WT1, SF1
Hormonal↓ Testosterone, ↓ DHT, androgen receptor defects
EnvironmentalEndocrine disruptors (phthalates, pesticides)
PlacentalPlacental insufficiency, preeclampsia
IdiopathicMost cases - no identifiable cause

📍 Classification by Meatus Location

DISTAL (Anterior) - 70%

  • Glanular: On glans, just proximal to normal position
  • Coronal: At corona (junction of glans and shaft)
  • Subcoronal: Just proximal to corona

Usually minimal chordee; simpler repair; best outcomes

MIDDLE (Penile Shaft) - 15-20%

  • Distal shaft
  • Midshaft
  • Proximal shaft

Variable chordee; moderate complexity

PROXIMAL (Posterior) - 10-15%

  • Penoscrotal: At junction of penis and scrotum
  • Scrotal: On scrotum
  • Perineal: Behind scrotum (most severe)

Significant chordee; may have bifid scrotum; consider DSD workup

When to Evaluate for DSD (Disorder of Sex Development):
  • Proximal hypospadias (penoscrotal, scrotal, perineal)
  • Hypospadias + bilateral cryptorchidism
  • Hypospadias + unilateral cryptorchidism with nonpalpable gonad
  • Micropenis

Get karyotype, hormonal evaluation!

🔍 Clinical Features

Classic Triad

  1. Ectopic meatus - Opens ventrally, proximal to normal
  2. Chordee - Ventral penile curvature (fibrous bands)
  3. Hooded foreskin - Dorsal foreskin only, absent ventrally

Additional Findings

  • Glans may be flattened with ventral groove
  • Meatal stenosis possible
  • Abnormal urinary stream (sprays, deflected)
  • May have associated cryptorchidism or inguinal hernia

🔪 Surgical Management

Goals of Repair

  1. Create a slit-like meatus at the glans tip
  2. Straighten the penis (correct chordee)
  3. Create a neourethra from meatus to tip
  4. Achieve good cosmesis
  5. Allow normal voiding (standing, forward stream)
  6. Allow future sexual function

Timing

Optimal age: 6-18 months
  • Anesthesia safe
  • Before toilet training and gender awareness
  • Adequate penile size for surgery
  • Healing optimized

Surgical Techniques

SeverityCommon Techniques
DistalMAGPI, Snodgrass (TIP), Mathieu flip-flap
MiddleSnodgrass (TIP), Onlay flap
ProximalStaged repairs, Bracka (staged graft), Koyanagi
TIP (Tubularized Incised Plate) = Snodgrass Procedure
  • Most popular for distal/middle hypospadias
  • Uses native urethral plate
  • Single stage, good cosmesis

📋 Key Clinical Pearls

⚠️ DO NOT CIRCUMCISE! The foreskin may be needed for repair. If circumcised, buccal mucosa or other grafts will be required.
Hypospadias + Undescended Testis:
  • Unilateral UDT + hypospadias: DSD workup if gonad nonpalpable
  • Bilateral UDT + hypospadias: ALWAYS evaluate for DSD!
  • Get karyotype before any surgery
Outcomes:
  • Distal repairs: >90% success rate
  • Proximal repairs: More complications, may need multiple surgeries
  • Most boys achieve normal voiding and sexual function
  • Fertility generally preserved

🧠 Quick Quiz

A newborn is noted to have the urethral meatus on the ventral shaft of the penis with a hooded foreskin. What should you advise the parents regarding circumcision?

A. Proceed with routine circumcision
B. Do NOT circumcise - foreskin needed for repair
C. Circumcision will fix the hypospadias
D. Wait until age 5 for circumcision
Correct: B
The foreskin is preserved in hypospadias because it can be used as graft material during surgical repair. Circumcision should NOT be performed. If inadvertently circumcised, alternative tissue sources (buccal mucosa) will be needed.
