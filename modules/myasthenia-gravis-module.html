MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Myasthenia gravis (MG): autoimmune antibodies against nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (AChR) at the neuromuscular junction → impaired neuromuscular transmission → fatigable weakness.

FeatureDetails
AntibodiesAnti-AChR (80-85% — seropositive). Anti-MuSK (5-10% — more severe, bulbar predominant). Seronegative (5-10%)
ThymusThymic abnormalities in 75%: thymic hyperplasia (65%, younger patients) or thymoma (10%, older patients). Thymectomy beneficial even without thymoma
BimodalYoung women (20-30s), older men (60-70s)
PatternFATIGABLE weakness — worse with repetitive use, improves with rest. Fluctuating. Worse at END of day
🔑 Ocular vs Generalized MG: Ocular MG (50% at presentation): ptosis + diplopia only. 50% progress to generalized within 2 years. Generalized: ocular + bulbar (dysphagia, dysarthria, nasal voice) + limb (proximal) + respiratory weakness.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis
TestDetails
Anti-AChR antibodiesMost specific test. Positive in 80-85% of generalized MG, 50% of ocular MG
Anti-MuSK antibodiesCheck if AChR negative. More severe, bulbar predominant, may not respond to AChEi
Edrophonium (Tensilon) testIV anticholinesterase → brief improvement (30 sec). Risk: bradycardia, bronchospasm. Rarely used now
Ice pack testIce on ptotic eyelid × 2 min → improvement. Safe bedside test (cold inhibits AChE → more ACh available)
Repetitive nerve stimulation (RNS)Decremental response (>10% amplitude decrease) with repetitive stimulation at 2-3 Hz. Most commonly used electrodiagnostic test
Single-fiber EMGMost SENSITIVE test. Increased jitter. Used when other tests are negative
CT chestALL patients — look for thymoma
⚠️ MG vs Lambert-Eaton Myasthenic Syndrome (LEMS):
MG: post-synaptic (AChR antibodies), weakness WORSENS with use, associated with thymoma
LEMS: pre-synaptic (VGCC antibodies — voltage-gated calcium channels), weakness IMPROVES with use (facilitating), associated with small cell lung cancer. RNS shows INCREMENTAL response
TREATMENT
Management & Crisis
TreatmentDetails
Pyridostigmine (Mestinon)Symptomatic: AChE inhibitor → more ACh at NMJ. First-line for symptom control. Side effects: cholinergic (DUMBBELSS — diarrhea, urination, miosis, bronchospasm, bradycardia, emesis, lacrimation, salivation, sweating)
ImmunosuppressionPrednisone (may worsen initially), azathioprine, mycophenolate, rituximab. For inadequate response to pyridostigmine
ThymectomyRecommended for ALL thymoma patients + generalized MG (even without thymoma — MGTX trial showed benefit)
Crisis treatmentIVIG or plasmapheresis (PLEX) for myasthenic crisis or pre-thymectomy optimization
⚠️ MYASTHENIC CRISIS: Respiratory failure from MG exacerbation. Triggers: infection (#1), surgery, medication changes, stress. Monitor FVC/NIF. Intubate early. Treat with IVIG or PLEX. Resume/increase immunotherapy. Distinguish from CHOLINERGIC CRISIS (excess pyridostigmine → DUMBBELSS + weakness). Both cause weakness — edrophonium test historically used to differentiate.
🔑 Drugs That Worsen MG: Aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones, macrolides, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, magnesium, D-penicillamine, telithromycin, botulinum toxin, succinylcholine (avoid). HIGH-YIELD drug interaction list.
