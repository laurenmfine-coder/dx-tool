📖 Teaching Point

CPAM = hamartomatous overgrowth of terminal bronchioles. Embryologically, it represents abnormal branching morphogenesis during the pseudoglandular stage (weeks 5-17). The lesion communicates with the normal bronchial tree and receives pulmonary arterial blood supply. Classification (Stocker): Type 0 (acinar dysplasia, fatal) → Type 1 (large cysts, best) → Type 2 (small cysts) → Type 3 (microcystic/solid) → Type 4 (peripheral, pneumothorax risk).

📖 Teaching Point

Prenatal CPAM management: (1) Serial ultrasound with CVR tracking; (2) CVR <1.6 and no hydrops → observe, most regress or stabilize by term; (3) CVR >1.6 or hydrops developing → intervention options: maternal betamethasone (for microcystic/Type 3 — can shrink lesion), thoracoamniotic shunt (for macrocystic/Type 1 — drains dominant cyst), EXIT procedure, or fetal surgery (open resection — rare, last resort). Spontaneous regression occurs in 15-20% of prenatally diagnosed CPAMs.

📖 Teaching Point

5 stages of lung development: (1) EMBRYONIC (weeks 3-7): lung bud from foregut, primary bronchi. (2) PSEUDOGLANDULAR (5-17): all conducting airways form (23 generations of branching), no gas exchange. (3) CANALICULAR (16-26): vascularization, respiratory bronchioles, blood-air barrier begins. VIABILITY begins here (~24 weeks). (4) SACCULAR (24-38): terminal sacs, type II pneumocytes mature, surfactant production. (5) ALVEOLAR (36 weeks - 8 years): true alveoli form and multiply.

📖 Teaching Point

Bronchogenic cysts: 65% mediastinal (subcarinal most common), 35% intrapulmonary. Key features: (1) Single, well-defined, fluid-filled cyst; (2) Wall contains cartilage + respiratory epithelium + smooth muscle; (3) Usually does NOT communicate with the airway; (4) Can cause compression (dysphagia, stridor, recurrent pneumonia); (5) Risk of infection if they communicate; (6) Small malignancy risk → resection recommended even if asymptomatic.

📖 Teaching Point

Congenital lung lesion management summary: CPAM — elective resection recommended (infection risk + malignancy risk), even if asymptomatic; timing debated (3-6 months vs observation). SEQUESTRATION — resection for symptomatic cases (recurrent pneumonia). BRONCHOGENIC CYST — resection recommended (compression + infection + small malignancy risk). CLE — lobectomy if causing respiratory distress; observation if mild. Prenatal observation: many lesions regress or shrink by term.
