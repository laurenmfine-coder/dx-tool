🌬️ COPD & Asthma Exacerbations

COPD vs Asthma: Key Differences

Feature COPD Asthma
Age of Onset >40 years Childhood/young adult
Smoking History Almost always Variable
Symptoms Persistent, progressive dyspnea Episodic, variable, nocturnal
Reversibility Partially reversible (<12%) Fully reversible (≥12% & 200mL)
Inflammation Neutrophilic Eosinophilic
Allergies/Atopy Uncommon Common (rhinitis, eczema)

COPD Exacerbation (AECOPD)

Definition & Triggers

Acute worsening of respiratory symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation
Triggers: Viral infection (most common), bacterial infection, air pollution, PE, cardiac events, non-adherence

Cardinal Symptoms

• ↑ Dyspnea
• ↑ Sputum volume
• ↑ Sputum purulence
Anthonisen criteria: Mild (1 symptom), Moderate (2), Severe (3)

Management: "COPD" Mnemonic

C Corticosteroids
Prednisone 40mg × 5 days. Shortens recovery, improves FEV1.
O Oxygen
Target SpO2 88-92%. Avoid over-oxygenation (↓hypoxic drive, V/Q mismatch).
P Pump (Bronchodilators)
SABA (albuterol) + SAMA (ipratropium) via nebulizer. Duoneb.
D Delivery (Non-Invasive Ventilation)
BiPAP for respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35, pCO2 >45). ↓Intubation, ↓mortality.

Antibiotics in AECOPD

When to Use:
  • • ↑ Sputum purulence + (↑dyspnea OR ↑volume)
  • • Requiring mechanical ventilation
  • • Severe exacerbation
Agents (5-7 days):
  • • Azithromycin (or doxycycline)
  • • Amoxicillin-clavulanate
  • • Respiratory fluoroquinolone (if risk factors)
Common bugs: H. influenzae, M. catarrhalis, S. pneumoniae. Consider Pseudomonas if severe COPD, recent hospitalization, frequent exacerbations.

Asthma Exacerbation

Triggers

  • • Viral URI (rhinovirus most common)
  • • Allergen exposure
  • • Exercise
  • • Cold air
  • • NSAIDs/Aspirin (Samter's triad)
  • • β-blockers
  • • Non-adherence to controller meds

Severity Assessment

Mild-Moderate: Talks in sentences, not agitated, RR <30, SpO2 >90%, PEF >50%
Severe: Talks in words, agitated, RR >30, accessory muscles, SpO2 <90%, PEF <50%
Life-threatening: Silent chest, cyanosis, bradycardia, altered LOC, PEF <25%

Acute Management

1. Oxygen
Target SpO2 93-95% (higher than COPD)
2. SABA
Albuterol q20min × 3, then q1-4h. Continuous neb if severe.
3. Ipratropium
Add to SABA for severe exacerbation (first few hours only).
4. Systemic Corticosteroids
Prednisone 40-60mg or IV methylpred. Give early! 5-7 days, no taper needed.

⚠️ Severe/Refractory Asthma

Add-on Therapies:
  • • Magnesium sulfate 2g IV (smooth muscle relaxation)
  • • Epinephrine IM/SQ (anaphylaxis or severe)
  • • Terbutaline SQ
  • • Heliox (↓airway resistance - limited evidence)
Intubation Considerations:
  • • Delayed sequence intubation (ketamine)
  • • Largest ETT possible
  • • Low RR, long expiratory time
  • • Watch for auto-PEEP/breath stacking
  • • Permissive hypercapnia OK

Discharge Considerations

COPD

  • ✓ SABA needed ≤ q4h
  • ✓ Able to ambulate
  • ✓ Able to eat/sleep without dyspnea
  • ✓ Stable for 12-24h
  • ✓ ABG/SpO2 at baseline
  • ✓ Inhaler technique reviewed
  • ✓ Action plan provided

Asthma

  • ✓ PEF >70% predicted or personal best
  • ✓ SpO2 >94% on room air
  • ✓ Minimal symptoms
  • ✓ Rx: Oral steroids (complete 5-7 day course)
  • ✓ Rx: ICS (start or increase)
  • ✓ Inhaler technique reviewed
  • ✓ Asthma action plan provided
  • ✓ Follow-up within 1-4 weeks
