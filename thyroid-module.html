🔥 Hyperthyroidism

Symptoms (Hypermetabolic)

  • • Weight loss despite ↑appetite
  • • Heat intolerance, sweating
  • • Tachycardia, palpitations, AFib
  • • Tremor, anxiety, insomnia
  • • Diarrhea, ↑frequency
  • • Menstrual irregularities

Differential by RAIU

↑ RAIU (making hormone)
Graves' (diffuse), Toxic adenoma (focal), Toxic MNG
↓ RAIU (releasing stored)
Thyroiditis (painful or silent), Exogenous T4, Struma ovarii

❄️ Hypothyroidism

Symptoms (Hypometabolic)

  • • Weight gain, fatigue
  • • Cold intolerance
  • • Bradycardia
  • • Constipation
  • • Dry skin, hair loss
  • • Delayed DTRs (relaxation phase)
  • • Menorrhagia
  • • Depression, cognitive slowing

Causes

Hashimoto's (most common US)
Anti-TPO, anti-thyroglobulin antibodies → Lymphocytic infiltration → Destruction
Iodine Deficiency (most common worldwide)
Iatrogenic
Post-RAI, post-thyroidectomy, lithium, amiodarone

Treatment

Levothyroxine (T4): Start low (25-50 mcg), go slow (especially elderly, CAD)
• Take on empty stomach, 30-60 min before breakfast
• Check TSH in 6-8 weeks after dose change
• Goal TSH: 0.5-2.5 (lower end if young, higher if elderly)

⚡ Thyroid Storm

Trigger: Infection, surgery, trauma, iodine load in hyperthyroid patient
Features: Fever (>104°F), tachycardia, AMS, GI (N/V/D), cardiovascular collapse
Treatment (order matters!):
  1. 1. Beta-blocker (propranolol - also blocks T4→T3)
  2. 2. PTU (blocks synthesis AND T4→T3)
  3. 3. Iodine 1 hour AFTER PTU (blocks release)
  4. 4. Steroids (blocks T4→T3, treats adrenal insufficiency)
  5. 5. Supportive (cooling, fluids)

🧊 Myxedema Coma

Trigger: Infection, cold exposure, sedatives in hypothyroid patient
Features: Hypothermia, bradycardia, AMS → coma, hypoventilation, hypoglycemia, hyponatremia
Treatment:
  • • IV Levothyroxine (large loading dose)
  • • IV Hydrocortisone (until adrenal insufficiency ruled out)
  • • Supportive: warming, ventilation, glucose
  • • Treat precipitant (infection)
