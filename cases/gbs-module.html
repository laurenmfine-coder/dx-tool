MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS): acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (AIDP — most common subtype). Molecular mimicry: antibodies against infectious agents cross-react with peripheral nerve gangliosides → demyelination of peripheral nerves.

FeatureDetails
Preceding illness1-4 weeks before: URI, GI illness (Campylobacter jejuni #1 trigger — 30%), CMV, EBV, Mycoplasma, Zika, influenza, COVID-19, surgery
TargetPeripheral nerves (LMN pattern). Spares CNS
PatternASCENDING symmetric weakness (legs → arms → respiratory → cranial nerves). Progresses over days-weeks. Nadir: 2-4 weeks
Key distinctionAREFLEXIA or hyporeflexia (LMN). Sensation may be preserved or mildly reduced. Autonomic dysfunction common (tachycardia, BP lability, urinary retention)
⚠️ Respiratory Failure: 30% need mechanical ventilation. Monitor FVC (forced vital capacity) and NIF (negative inspiratory force) serially. Intubate if FVC <20 mL/kg or NIF < -30 cmH2O (20/30 rule). Do NOT rely on SpO2 — it drops LATE.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis
TestFindings
LP (CSF)Albuminocytologic dissociation: elevated protein + normal cell count. Classic finding. May be normal in first week
Nerve conduction studies/EMGDemyelination pattern: prolonged distal latencies, conduction block, slowed conduction velocity. Most sensitive test
Anti-ganglioside antibodiesAnti-GM1 (AMAN variant), anti-GQ1b (Miller Fisher variant)
🔑 Miller Fisher Variant: Triad of ophthalmoplegia + ataxia + areflexia (DESCENDING pattern — starts with cranial nerves). Anti-GQ1b antibodies. Generally better prognosis than classic GBS.

Differential Diagnosis

Must distinguish from: myasthenia gravis (fatigable weakness, no sensory), transverse myelitis (UMN signs, sensory level), poliomyelitis (asymmetric, anterior horn cells), botulism (DESCENDING paralysis, starts with cranial nerves, dilated pupils), tick paralysis (ascending but resolves with tick removal).

TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
IVIGFirst-line: 0.4 g/kg/day × 5 days. Equally effective as plasmapheresis. Easier to administer
Plasmapheresis (PLEX)Alternative first-line: 5 exchanges over 2 weeks. Removes circulating antibodies. Best if started within 2 weeks
Supportive careICU monitoring, respiratory support (serial FVC/NIF), DVT prophylaxis, pain management (neuropathic pain common), PT/OT
⚠️ Steroids are NOT effective in GBS (unlike most autoimmune diseases). This is high-yield. IVIG + steroids = no additional benefit over IVIG alone. Do NOT combine IVIG + plasmapheresis (IVIG antibodies removed by plex).
🔑 Prognosis: ~80% recover fully or near-fully over weeks-months. 5% mortality (respiratory failure, autonomic dysfunction). 5-10% have significant residual disability. Recovery is SLOW — may take 6-12 months. Poor prognostic factors: older age, rapid onset, need for ventilation, Campylobacter trigger, axonal variant.
