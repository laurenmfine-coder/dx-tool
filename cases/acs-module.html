Pathophysiology & Classification

ACS results from atherosclerotic plaque rupture or erosion → thrombus formation → myocardial ischemia/infarction.

TypeST ChangesTroponinPathology
Unstable Angina (UA)ST depression or T-wave inversion (or normal)NegativePartial occlusion, no necrosis
NSTEMIST depression or T-wave inversionElevatedPartial occlusion, subendocardial infarct
STEMIST elevation ≥1 mm in ≥2 contiguous leadsElevatedComplete occlusion, transmural infarct
🔑 Troponin is the KEY differentiator between UA and NSTEMI — both have similar EKG findings but troponin elevation = myocardial necrosis = NSTEMI. High-sensitivity troponin detected within 1-3 hours.

CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Complications
⚠️ STEMI = Emergent Reperfusion:
• PCI (preferred): Door-to-balloon ≤90 min (≤120 min if transfer needed)
• Fibrinolytics: If PCI unavailable within 120 min. Door-to-needle ≤30 min. Contraindications: active bleeding, recent surgery, stroke <3 months, aortic dissection
• Right ventricular infarct: Inferior STEMI + hypotension + clear lungs + JVD. Give IV fluids (preload dependent). AVOID nitroglycerin, morphine, diuretics

TREATMENT
Management

Initial ACS Management (MONA-B)

Morphine (if pain refractory — use cautiously), Oxygen (only if SpO2 <90%), Nitroglycerin (SL then IV — contraindicated in RV infarct, PDE5 inhibitor use within 24-48h), Aspirin 325 mg chewed (MOST IMPORTANT initial therapy), Beta-blocker (within 24h if stable — contraindicated in cardiogenic shock, severe bradycardia, cocaine).

STEMINSTEMI/UA
Emergent PCI (preferred) or fibrinolyticsAnti-ischemic therapy + risk stratification
Dual antiplatelet: ASA + P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor preferred)DAPT + anticoagulation (heparin/enoxaparin)
Heparin (UFH or LMWH)Early invasive (PCI within 24-72h) for high-risk features
🔑 Long-term Post-ACS: DAPT (ASA + P2Y12) × 12 months. High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80 mg). ACEi (LV dysfunction). Beta-blocker. Lifestyle modification. Cardiac rehab.
