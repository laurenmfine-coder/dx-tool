MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Drug allergy involves immunologically mediated adverse drug reactions classified by Gell-Coombs type. Type I (IgE-mediated): penicillin anaphylaxis. Type II (cytotoxic): drug-induced hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia. Type III (immune complex): serum sickness. Type IV (T-cell mediated): contact dermatitis, SJS/TEN, DRESS.

🔑 Key Concept: Penicillin allergy is the most commonly reported drug allergy (~10% of patients) but 90% of labeled penicillin-allergic patients are NOT truly allergic when formally tested. De-labeling through skin testing saves costs and improves antibiotic stewardship.

TREATMENT
Management

Approach to Drug Allergy

Immediate: Stop the offending drug. For anaphylaxis: epinephrine IM. For SJS/TEN: transfer to burn unit, IV immunoglobulin or cyclosporine (debated). For DRESS: systemic corticosteroids.

🔑 Penicillin Skin Testing: Major determinant (penicilloyl-polylysine/PRE-PEN) + minor determinant mix + penicillin G. Negative predictive value >98%. If negative → safe to give penicillin. If positive → desensitization protocol (graded dose challenge in ICU over 4-12 hours) when penicillin is essential (e.g., neurosyphilis, enterococcal endocarditis).

Drug Desensitization

Temporary induction of tolerance by administering increasing doses over hours. Only works while drug is continued — allergy returns when drug is stopped. Indications: when no suitable alternative exists. Contraindicated in SJS/TEN, DRESS, vasculitis.
