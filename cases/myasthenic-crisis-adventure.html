🔑 Key Learning Points

  • 20/30/40 Rule: FVC < 20 mL/kg, NIF > -30 cmH2O, or PaCO2 > 40 indicates need for intubation
  • Avoid succinylcholine: MG patients are resistant (need higher doses) and have prolonged paralysis
  • Reduce NMBA doses: Use lower doses of non-depolarizing agents (increased sensitivity)
  • First-line treatment: PLEX or IVIG - faster onset than steroids which can initially worsen weakness
  • Avoid triggers: Macrolides, fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides, beta-blockers, magnesium
  • Hold cholinesterase inhibitors: During crisis to avoid cholinergic crisis and excessive secretions

📚 Myasthenic vs Cholinergic Crisis

Myasthenic Crisis: Weakness from inadequate treatment or precipitant - pupils normal/dilated, dry secretions
Cholinergic Crisis: From excessive anticholinesterase - SLUDGE symptoms (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI distress, Emesis), miosis, bradycardia
