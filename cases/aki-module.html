CLINICAL
Key Etiologies

ATN (Acute Tubular Necrosis) — #1 cause of intrinsic AKI

Ischemic ATN: prolonged pre-renal state → tubular cell death. Surgery, sepsis, hemorrhage.
Nephrotoxic ATN: aminoglycosides, contrast dye, cisplatin, myoglobin (rhabdomyolysis), NSAIDs + ACEi combination.

AIN (Acute Interstitial Nephritis)

Drug hypersensitivity reaction. Classic triad: fever + rash + eosinophilia (not always present). Common drugs: penicillins, cephalosporins, sulfonamides, NSAIDs, PPIs, rifampin. Urine: WBC casts, eosinophils (Hansel stain). Treatment: stop offending drug ± steroids.

⚠️ Contrast-Induced Nephropathy: Cr rise 24-72h after IV contrast. Risk factors: CKD, DM, CHF, dehydration. Prevention: IV hydration with isotonic saline before/after contrast. NAC benefit uncertain. Avoid nephrotoxins. Use iso-osmolar contrast.
TREATMENT
Management & Dialysis

Initial Management

TypeTreatment
Pre-renalVolume resuscitation (IV fluids). Treat underlying cause. Hold ACEi/ARBs, diuretics, NSAIDs
Intrinsic (ATN)Supportive — maintain euvolemia, avoid nephrotoxins, adjust drug dosing. ATN self-resolves in 1-3 weeks
Post-renalRelieve obstruction: Foley catheter (BPH), ureteral stent, nephrostomy tube
⚠️ Indications for EMERGENT Dialysis (AEIOU):
A — Acidosis (severe metabolic, pH <7.1, refractory to bicarb)
E — Electrolytes (refractory hyperkalemia, K+ >6.5 with EKG changes)
I — Intoxication (methanol, ethylene glycol, lithium, salicylate)
O — Overload (pulmonary edema refractory to diuretics)
U — Uremia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)
