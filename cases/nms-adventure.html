🔑 Key Learning Points

  • NMS tetrad: Hyperthermia, rigidity (lead-pipe), altered mental status, autonomic instability
  • Timeline: Develops over days to weeks after starting/increasing antipsychotic
  • CK elevation: Often markedly elevated (>1000 U/L); monitor for rhabdomyolysis
  • First step: STOP the offending agent immediately
  • Treatment: Supportive care + cooling + dantrolene (muscle relaxant) ± bromocriptine/amantadine (dopamine agonists)
  • Complications: Rhabdomyolysis, AKI, aspiration, DIC, respiratory failure
  • Restarting antipsychotics: Wait at least 2 weeks; use a different class with lower D2 affinity

⚠️ High-Risk Medications for NMS

Highest risk: High-potency typical antipsychotics (haloperidol, fluphenazine)
Moderate risk: Atypical antipsychotics, metoclopramide (high doses)
Risk factors: Dehydration, agitation, recent dose increase, polypharmacy, prior NMS episode
