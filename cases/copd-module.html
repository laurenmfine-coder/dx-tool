MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

COPD = chronic airflow limitation that is NOT fully reversible. Two phenotypes:

FeatureEmphysema ("Pink Puffer")Chronic Bronchitis ("Blue Bloater")
PathologyAlveolar wall destruction → enlarged airspaces, loss of elastic recoilMucus hypersecretion, airway inflammation, productive cough ≥3 months/year × 2 consecutive years
AppearanceThin, barrel chest, pursed lip breathing, tachypneicOverweight, cyanotic, peripheral edema (cor pulmonale)
Key featureMaintains oxygenation via increased work of breathingHypoxic + hypercapnic, polycythemia
🔑 Smoking = #1 cause (85-90%). Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency in young non-smokers with panacinar emphysema (lower lobes). Gold standard diagnosis: spirometry showing FEV1/FVC <0.70 post-bronchodilator (defines obstruction).
CLINICAL
Acute Exacerbation (AECOPD)
⚠️ AECOPD = worsening dyspnea, cough, and/or sputum beyond normal variation.
#1 trigger: viral URI. Bacterial: H. influenzae (#1), M. catarrhalis, S. pneumoniae. Purulent sputum suggests bacterial.

Severity Assessment

MildModerateSevere
Increased dyspnea, increased sputum+ wheezing, RR >24, HR >95AMS, accessory muscles, cyanosis, SpO2 <90%, acidosis (pH <7.35)
OutpatientED/inpatientICU ± intubation
🔑 ABG in COPD: Chronic CO2 retention → metabolic compensation (elevated bicarb). Acute-on-chronic: pH drops (uncompensated). Target SpO2 88-92% (NOT 100%) — excessive O2 suppresses hypoxic drive → worsens hypercapnia. Use Venturi mask for precise FiO2.
TREATMENT
Management

AECOPD Treatment

ComponentDetails
BronchodilatorsNebulized albuterol (SABA) + ipratropium (SAMA). First-line
Systemic steroidsPrednisone 40 mg × 5 days (REDUCE trial). Reduces treatment failure and shortens recovery
AntibioticsIf purulent sputum, mechanical ventilation, or severe exacerbation. Azithromycin, doxycycline, or amoxicillin-clavulanate
NIV (BiPAP)For respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35, PaCO2 >45). Reduces intubation and mortality. First-line for moderate-severe exacerbation
Supplemental O2Target SpO2 88-92%. Venturi mask preferred for precise delivery
🔑 Only TWO therapies improve MORTALITY in stable COPD:
1. Supplemental O2 (if PaO2 ≤55 or SpO2 ≤88% at rest) — NOTT and MRC trials
2. Smoking cessation
Inhalers reduce exacerbations and symptoms but do NOT reduce mortality.
