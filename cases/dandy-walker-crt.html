📖 Teaching Point

Dandy-Walker spectrum: DANDY-WALKER MALFORMATION (DWM) = full triad. DANDY-WALKER VARIANT = partial vermian hypoplasia, variable posterior fossa size. MEGA CISTERNA MAGNA = enlarged cisterna, normal vermis, normal 4th ventricle (benign variant). Blake's pouch cyst = persistent Blake's pouch (failure of Magendie foramen opening). These exist on a continuum, and the terminology is debated.

📖 Teaching Point

Cerebellar embryology: The cerebellum develops from the RHOMBENCEPHALON (hindbrain). Specifically, the rhombic lips (dorsal alar plates of the metencephalon) produce granule cell precursors that migrate to form the cerebellar cortex. The vermis forms last as the two rhombic lips fuse in the midline. Disruption of this process → vermian aplasia/hypoplasia. The foramina of Magendie (midline) and Luschka (lateral) open by 12-14 weeks to allow CSF outflow.

📖 Teaching Point

DWM association pyramid: MOST COMMON = agenesis of corpus callosum (25%). Other CNS = heterotopia, polymicrogyria, occipital encephalocele. CARDIAC = 20-30% (VSD, ASD, TOF). CHROMOSOMAL = 15-30% (T13, T18, triploidy, microdeletions). Environmental risk factors: first trimester rubella, alcohol, isotretinoin, warfarin. Prognosis: highly variable — depends on associated anomalies more than the posterior fossa cyst itself.

📖 Teaching Point

DWM hydrocephalus management: VP shunt is the mainstay. Special consideration: if the cystic 4th ventricle doesn't communicate with the lateral ventricles, a VP shunt alone may not decompress the posterior fossa → may need BOTH a VP shunt AND cyst-peritoneal shunt. Endoscopic third ventriculostomy (ETV) is an alternative in select cases. Posterior fossa shunting alone risks upward transtentorial herniation.

📖 Teaching Point

DWM outcomes: ISOLATED DWM (no other anomalies) — IQ often normal to borderline, mild ataxia, good quality of life. DWM WITH ASSOCIATED CNS ANOMALIES — significantly worse, often moderate-severe intellectual disability. DWM WITH CHROMOSOMAL ABNORMALITIES — prognosis determined by the chromosomal condition. Overall mortality: 15-25% (mostly from associated anomalies, not the DWM itself). Truncal ataxia is expected given vermian involvement (vermis coordinates axial/truncal balance).

🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
With early intervention services, the child makes steady developmental progress. At age 5, she attends mainstream school with support services and has mild balance difficulties.

Key Takeaways

✓DWM triad: cystic 4th ventricle + vermian agenesis/hypoplasia + enlarged posterior fossa
✓Cerebellum develops from rhombic lips; vermis fuses last (15-16 weeks)
✓DWM vs Mega cisterna magna: vermis normal in MCM, abnormal in DWM
✓DWM vs Chiari II: posterior fossa enlarged (DWM) vs small (Chiari II)
✓Associated anomalies: corpus callosum agenesis (25%), cardiac (20-30%)
✓Hydrocephalus in 75-90% — VP shunt is mainstay
✓Prognosis depends on associated anomalies, not cyst size
