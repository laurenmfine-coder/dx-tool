Scene 2: The Hunter Criteria

You suspect serotonin syndrome. The Hunter Criteria are the most accurate diagnostic tool (sensitivity 84%, specificity 97%).

🎯 Hunter Criteria for Serotonin Syndrome

Patient must have taken a serotonergic agent AND have ONE of:

  • Spontaneous clonus
  • Inducible clonus + (agitation OR diaphoresis)
  • Ocular clonus + (agitation OR diaphoresis)
  • Tremor + hyperreflexia
  • Hypertonia + temp >38°C + (ocular clonus OR inducible clonus)

Classic Triad (present in this patient)

🧠 CNS: Agitation, confusion, anxiety
💓 Autonomic: Hyperthermia, tachycardia, diaphoresis, mydriasis
🦵 Neuromuscular: Tremor, hyperreflexia, CLONUS, rigidity (LOWER > UPPER)

C. Bradykinesia
D. Resting tremor
Scene 3: Serotonin Syndrome vs NMS

You need to distinguish serotonin syndrome from other hyperthermic syndromes, especially NMS. This is a common clinical question!

Feature Serotonin Syndrome NMS
Onset Rapid (hours) Gradual (days-weeks)
Causative drugs Serotonergic (SSRIs, tramadol, triptans) Dopamine antagonists (antipsychotics)
Muscle tone Hyperreflexia, CLONUS, LE > UE "Lead-pipe" rigidity, bradykinesia
Pupils DILATED (mydriasis) Normal
Bowel sounds HYPERactive (GI motility increased) Normal or decreased
Resolution 24-72 hours with treatment Days to weeks

Scene 4: Management

The patient's temperature is now 39.8°C and she's becoming more agitated. You need to act quickly.

💊 Treatment Approach

  1. STOP all serotonergic agents - most important!
  2. Supportive care: IV fluids, cooling, cardiac monitoring
  3. Benzodiazepines: For agitation AND helps with hyperthermia/rigidity
  4. Cyproheptadine: Serotonin antagonist (12mg load, then 2mg q2h)
  5. Severe cases: Intubation, paralysis, cooling for temp >41°C

A. External cooling
B. Acetaminophen and NSAIDs
C. Benzodiazepines
D. IV fluids

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Triad: Mental status changes + Autonomic instability + Neuromuscular abnormalities
  • CLONUS is key: Most specific finding, especially in lower extremities
  • vs NMS: SS has clonus/hyperreflexia; NMS has lead-pipe rigidity/bradykinesia
  • Rapid onset: Usually within 24h of drug change (NMS takes days-weeks)
  • Treatment: Stop drug + benzos + cyproheptadine + cooling
  • Avoid antipyretics: Hyperthermia is from muscle activity, not hypothalamic reset
  • Common culprits: SSRI + tramadol, SSRI + triptan, MAOI + anything serotonergic
