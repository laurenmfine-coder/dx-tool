Cushing's Syndrome

Excess cortisol (any source)

Symptoms
Central obesity, moon facies, buffalo hump, purple striae, HTN, hyperglycemia, proximal weakness, osteoporosis, easy bruising
Causes (by ACTH level)
ACTH-Independent (↓)
• Exogenous steroids (#1)
• Adrenal adenoma/cancer
ACTH-Dependent (↑)
• Pituitary adenoma (Cushing's Disease)
• Ectopic ACTH (small cell lung CA)
Workup
1. Confirm hypercortisolism: 24h urine cortisol, late-night salivary cortisol, or dex suppression test
2. Check ACTH level
3. High-dose dex test / CRH stim / petrosal sinus sampling to localize

Adrenal Insufficiency

Cortisol deficiency

Symptoms
Fatigue, weakness, weight loss, hypotension, nausea, salt craving, hyperpigmentation (primary only - ↑ACTH stimulates melanocytes)
Types
Primary (Addison's)
↑ACTH, ↓cortisol
↓Aldosterone too
Autoimmune, TB, hemorrhage
Secondary/Tertiary
↓ACTH, ↓cortisol
Aldosterone preserved
Chronic steroids (#1), pituitary dz
Diagnosis
Morning cortisol (low), ACTH stimulation test (no response = primary)
Adrenal Crisis
Hypotension, shock, hyponatremia, hyperkalemia. Give IV hydrocortisone ASAP!

Primary Hyperaldosteronism

Excess aldosterone independent of renin

Mechanism
Aldosterone → ENaC activation → Na⁺ retention → K⁺/H⁺ excretion
Result: HTN + Hypokalemia + Metabolic alkalosis
Labs
↑Aldosterone, ↓Renin, ↑Aldo/Renin ratio (ARR >30)
HTN + hypoK + metabolic alkalosis = screen for this!
Causes
• Conn's syndrome (aldosterone-producing adenoma) - unilateral
• Bilateral adrenal hyperplasia

Pheochromocytoma

Catecholamine-secreting tumor of adrenal medulla

Symptoms (Rule of 10s)
Paroxysmal: HTN, headache, sweating, palpitations, pallor
"Spells" triggered by stress, surgery, certain foods/drugs
Rule of 10s
10% bilateral, 10% extra-adrenal (paraganglioma), 10% malignant, 10% familial (MEN2, VHL, NF1, SDH)
Diagnosis
24h urine metanephrines/catecholamines or plasma metanephrines
CT/MRI to localize, MIBG scan if needed
Treatment
α-blockade FIRST (phenoxybenzamine) → then β-blockade → Surgery
Never β-block first! (unopposed α → HTN crisis)
