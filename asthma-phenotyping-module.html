Asthma Phenotyping & Biologics

Precision Medicine in Type 2 Inflammation

Type 2 High vs Type 2 Low

Type 2 High Biomarkers:

  • Blood eosinophils ≥150/μL (or ≥300 for some biologics)
  • FeNO ≥20 ppb
  • Sputum eosinophils ≥2%
  • IgE elevated (allergic)

Type 2 Low Asthma:

  • Neutrophilic or paucigranulocytic
  • Often smoking-related, obesity-related
  • May respond less to ICS
  • Consider macrolide, LAMA, bronchial thermoplasty

💡 Type 2 high = ~50% of severe asthma, excellent biologic candidates

Type 2 Cytokine Cascade

Epithelial alarmins (TSLP, IL-33, IL-25):

  • Released by damaged epithelium
  • Activate ILC2s and dendritic cells

Downstream pathway:

  1. TSLP → promotes Th2 differentiation
  2. IL-4 → IgE class switching, mucus
  3. IL-13 → airway remodeling, mucus, AHR
  4. IL-5 → eosinophil production/survival

💡 Biologics target specific steps: IL-5 (mepo/benra), IL-4/13 (dupilumab), IgE (omalizumab), TSLP (tezepelumab)

Biologic Selection

Anti-IgE (Omalizumab):

  • Best for: Allergic asthma with elevated IgE (30-1300 IU/mL)
  • Positive skin tests/specific IgE required

Anti-IL-5/IL-5R (Mepolizumab, Benralizumab, Reslizumab):

  • Best for: Eosinophilic asthma (≥150-300/μL)
  • Benralizumab also works in lower eosinophil counts via ADCC

Anti-IL-4Rα (Dupilumab):

  • Blocks IL-4 AND IL-13
  • Best for: Type 2 high OR with comorbid atopic dermatitis/CRSwNP

Anti-TSLP (Tezepelumab):

  • Works in Type 2 high AND low
  • Broadest mechanism - upstream blockade

Phenotype-Based Approach

Allergic eosinophilic: Omalizumab, anti-IL-5, dupilumab

Non-allergic eosinophilic: Anti-IL-5, dupilumab

Aspirin-exacerbated (AERD): Anti-IL-5, dupilumab (+ aspirin desensitization)

Overlap with CRSwNP: Dupilumab, mepolizumab

Overlap with atopic dermatitis: Dupilumab

Type 2 low/Non-responders: Tezepelumab, bronchial thermoplasty

💡 If one biologic fails, can switch to another with different mechanism

🎯 Key Points

  • Type 2 high: eosinophils ≥150, FeNO ≥20, elevated IgE
  • Type 2 cytokines: IL-4 (IgE), IL-5 (eosinophils), IL-13 (remodeling)
  • Biologic choice based on: IgE level, eosinophil count, comorbidities
  • Dupilumab blocks IL-4/IL-13 - good for multiple Type 2 comorbidities
  • Tezepelumab (anti-TSLP) works across phenotypes
