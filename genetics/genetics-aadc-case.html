Mei is a 4-month-old girl referred for profound hypotonia since birth and episodic involuntary upward eye deviation lasting 1–2 minutes, occurring 3–5 times daily. Episodes are not associated with EEG changes. She has poor head control, has never smiled, and feeds poorly with significant reflux. Parents note she sweats excessively even in a cool room, has nasal congestion without URI symptoms, and her eyelids droop bilaterally (ptosis). Temperature is often 35.8°C. She was full-term with an uncomplicated delivery. Parents are first cousins of Chinese Taiwanese descent. Brain MRI: normal. EEG: normal. Standard metabolic panel: normal. The referring pediatrician charted "probable hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy vs early cerebral palsy."
