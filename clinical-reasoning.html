Welcome!

This tool helps you practice hypothesis-driven clinical reasoning. Unlike checklist-based approaches, you'll learn to:

  • Generate a differential diagnosis based on limited initial information
  • Select targeted history questions that distinguish between diagnoses
  • Perform focused physical exams that test your hypotheses
  • Prioritize high-yield questions in time-limited encounters

How it works: Each case starts with only a door note. You'll generate your differential, then select which questions to ask and which exam maneuvers to perform. After each stage, you'll receive feedback on your choices and learn which items were high-yield vs. low-priority.

Learning Objectives

After completing these cases, you will be able to:

  1. Generate a prioritized differential diagnosis based on chief complaint and vitals
  2. Identify "must-not-miss" diagnoses for common presentations
  3. Select hypothesis-driven history questions efficiently
  4. Perform targeted physical exams that test specific diagnoses
  5. Distinguish high-yield from low-priority clinical actions

Key Skills Practiced:

  • Clinical reasoning under time pressure
  • Prioritization of differential diagnoses
  • Focused vs. comprehensive history taking
  • Targeted physical examination
  • OSCE preparation
