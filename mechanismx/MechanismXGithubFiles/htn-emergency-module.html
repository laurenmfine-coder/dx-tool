🔴 Hypertensive Emergencies

Emergency vs Urgency

Hypertensive EMERGENCY

BP >180/120 + Evidence of end-organ damage
End-Organ Damage:
  • 🧠 Neuro: Encephalopathy, stroke, ICH
  • ❤️ Cardiac: ACS, acute HF, aortic dissection
  • 🫘 Renal: AKI, hematuria
  • 👁️ Eyes: Papilledema, hemorrhages
  • 🤰 OB: Eclampsia, HELLP
→ ICU admission, IV antihypertensives

Hypertensive URGENCY

BP >180/120 + NO end-organ damage
Features:
  • • May have headache, anxiety, nosebleed
  • • Often non-adherence to meds
  • • No acute target organ injury
  • • Normal neuro exam
  • • Stable renal function
→ Oral antihypertensives, lower over 24-48h, outpatient f/u
⚠️ The NUMBER doesn't define emergency - END-ORGAN DAMAGE does!

Blood Pressure Reduction Goals

General Principle: "Low and Slow"
25%
Reduction in first hour
160/100
Target over 2-6 hours
Normal
Over 24-48 hours
Why not fast? Cerebral autoregulation shifts rightward in chronic HTN. Rapid ↓BP → watershed ischemia, stroke, MI, AKI.

Specific Emergencies & Treatment

🫀

Aortic Dissection

MOST AGGRESSIVE TARGET
Goals:
  • • SBP <120 mmHg
  • • HR <60 bpm
  • • Within 20 minutes
Treatment:
  • • β-blocker FIRST (esmolol, labetalol)
  • • Then add vasodilator if needed (nitroprusside, nicardipine)
  • • ⚠️ NEVER vasodilator alone → reflex tachy → ↑shear stress
🧠

Acute Ischemic Stroke

If receiving tPA:
  • • Must be <185/110 before tPA
  • • Maintain <180/105 for 24h after
If NOT receiving tPA:
  • • Permissive HTN up to 220/120
  • • Only treat if >220/120 or other indication
  • • Maintain cerebral perfusion!
Agents: Labetalol, nicardipine
🩸

Intracerebral Hemorrhage (ICH)

Goals:
  • • SBP 150-220 → Target <140
  • • SBP >220 → Aggressive IV reduction
  • • Goal: Reduce hematoma expansion
Evidence:
  • • INTERACT2/ATACH-2 trials
  • • Intensive control (<140) safe but unclear benefit
Agents: Nicardipine, labetalol, clevidipine
💔

Acute Coronary Syndrome

Goals: Reduce myocardial O2 demand, control HR & BP
Agents: Nitroglycerin (preload reduction, coronary dilation), β-blockers (↓HR, ↓contractility). Avoid hydralazine (reflex tachy).
🫁

Acute Pulmonary Edema / Heart Failure

Goals: Reduce afterload & preload rapidly
Agents: Nitroglycerin or nitroprusside (vasodilators), loop diuretics (furosemide). Consider clevidipine. BiPAP for respiratory support.
🤰

Preeclampsia / Eclampsia

Goals:
  • • SBP <160, DBP <110
  • • Prevent seizures & maternal stroke
Treatment:
  • • Labetalol (1st line IV)
  • • Hydralazine IV
  • • Nifedipine PO
  • • Magnesium sulfate (seizure prophylaxis)
⚠️ Avoid: ACEi, ARBs, nitroprusside (fetal toxicity)
⚡

Sympathetic Crisis

(Cocaine, amphetamines, pheochromocytoma, MAOi + tyramine)
Key: α-adrenergic excess → vasoconstriction
Agents: Benzodiazepines first (↓central sympathetic), phentolamine, nicardipine
⚠️ Avoid β-blockers alone → unopposed α-stimulation → worse HTN!
