🧠 Altered Mental Status

Altered Mental Status (AMS)

Definition:
Change in level of consciousness (alertness) and/or content of consciousness (cognition, orientation, behavior)
Delirium
Acute, fluctuating attention/awareness
Obtundation/Stupor
Decreased arousal, responds to stimuli
Coma
Unarousable, no purposeful response

Differential: "AEIOU TIPS"

A
Alcohol / Acidosis
Intoxication, withdrawal, DKA, uremia, lactic acidosis
E
Endocrine / Electrolytes / Encephalopathy
Hypo/hyperglycemia, thyroid, adrenal, Na+, Ca2+, hepatic encephalopathy, Wernicke
I
Infection
Meningitis, encephalitis, sepsis, UTI (elderly), pneumonia, abscess
O
Oxygen / Overdose
Hypoxia, hypercapnia, CO poisoning, drug overdose
U
Uremia
Renal failure, uremic encephalopathy
T
Trauma / Temperature
Head injury, subdural, epidural, hypothermia, hyperthermia
I
Infection (CNS)
Meningitis, encephalitis, brain abscess
P
Psychiatric / Porphyria
Psychosis, catatonia (diagnosis of exclusion!)
S
Stroke / Seizure / Shock
CVA, postictal, status epilepticus, hypovolemic/cardiogenic/septic shock

🔍 Physical Exam Clues

Pupils

Pinpoint
Opioids, pontine stroke, organophosphates
Dilated
Sympathomimetics, anticholinergics, anoxia
Unequal
Herniation, CN III palsy, structural lesion
Non-reactive
Severe anoxia, brainstem lesion

Vital Sign Patterns

Cushing Triad
HTN + Bradycardia + Irregular respirations → ↑ICP
Fever + Neck Stiffness
Meningitis - do LP!
Hyperthermia + Rigidity
NMS, serotonin syndrome, malignant hyperthermia
Hypothermia
Myxedema coma, sepsis, exposure, overdose

Other Key Signs

Asterixis: Hepatic encephalopathy, uremia, CO2 narcosis
Fruity breath: DKA
Fetor hepaticus: Liver failure
Track marks: IVDU
Battle sign/Raccoon eyes: Basilar skull fracture
Decorticate/Decerebrate: Severe brain injury

📋 Diagnostic Workup

Immediate (Everyone)

  • ✓ Fingerstick glucose
  • ✓ CBC, BMP, LFTs
  • ✓ Urinalysis
  • ✓ Blood gas (ABG/VBG)
  • ✓ Lactate
  • ✓ Urine/serum drug screen
  • ✓ ECG
  • ✓ Ammonia (if liver disease)

As Indicated

  • • CT head (trauma, focal findings, ↑ICP concern)
  • • LP (if meningitis/encephalitis suspected - CT first if concern for mass)
  • • TSH, cortisol
  • • Blood cultures
  • • CXR
  • • EEG (if seizure/status suspected)
  • • MRI brain (encephalitis, posterior stroke)
  • • CO level, methemoglobin

Delirium: Special Focus

Features (CAM Criteria)

  1. 1. Acute onset, fluctuating course
  2. 2. Inattention
  3. 3. Disorganized thinking
  4. 4. Altered level of consciousness
Diagnosis requires: 1 + 2 + (3 OR 4)

Types

Hyperactive: Agitation, hallucinations, combative (easier to recognize)
Hypoactive: Withdrawn, quiet, sleepy (often missed!)
Mixed: Fluctuates between both

Common Causes: "DELIRIUM"

Drugs (anticholinergics, benzos, opioids, steroids)
Electrolytes (Na, Ca, glucose)
Lack of drugs (withdrawal)
Infection (UTI, pneumonia, sepsis)
Reduced sensory input (ICU, restraints)
Intracranial (stroke, bleed, seizure)
Urinary/fecal retention
Myocardial (MI, CHF, hypoxia)

💊 Delirium Management

Non-Pharmacologic (FIRST LINE)

  • ✓ Treat underlying cause!
  • ✓ Reorient frequently
  • ✓ Sleep hygiene (lights off at night)
  • ✓ Early mobilization
  • ✓ Glasses, hearing aids
  • ✓ Minimize lines, restraints, catheters
  • ✓ Avoid deliriogenic meds
  • ✓ Family presence

Pharmacologic (If Severe Agitation)

Antipsychotics:
  • • Haloperidol 0.5-2mg IV/IM
  • • Quetiapine 25-50mg PO
  • • Olanzapine 2.5-5mg IM
⚠️ Avoid in Parkinson's, Lewy body (use quetiapine if must)
⚠️ Check QTc before haloperidol
If withdrawal suspected:
  • • Alcohol: Benzodiazepines (CIWA protocol)
