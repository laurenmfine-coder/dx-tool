💔 Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

ADHF: Decompensation Triggers

"FAILURES" Mnemonic
Forgetting medications (non-adherence)
Arrhythmia / Anemia
Ischemia / Infarction
Lifestyle (salt/fluid excess)
Upregulation (thyroid, pregnancy)
Renal failure
Embolism (PE)
Stenosis (valvular) / Sepsis
Always look for the trigger! Treatment of underlying cause is essential for recovery.

Clinical Profiles: Warm/Cold × Wet/Dry

Warm & Wet Most common (~70%)
Perfusion: Adequate (warm extremities)
Congestion: Present (edema, JVD, crackles)
Tx: Diuretics ± vasodilators
Cold & Wet Cardiogenic shock
Perfusion: Poor (cool, clammy, ↓UOP)
Congestion: Present
Tx: Inotropes ± vasopressors, diuretics cautiously
Warm & Dry Compensated
Perfusion: Adequate
Congestion: Absent
Tx: Optimize oral meds, may not need admission
Cold & Dry Over-diuresed or low CO
Perfusion: Poor
Congestion: Absent
Tx: Careful volume, ± inotropes

💊 Acute Management

"LMNOP" for Acute Pulmonary Edema
L
Lasix (Diuretic)
M
Morphine*
N
Nitrates
O
Oxygen
P
Position (upright)
*Morphine controversial - may increase mortality. Use cautiously for anxiety/air hunger.

💧 Diuretics

IV Furosemide: 40mg if naive, or home dose × 1-2.5 IV
Goal: 1-2L negative/day, UOP 100-150 mL/hr initially
If resistant:
  • • Increase dose (max 200-400mg IV)
  • • Continuous infusion
  • • Add thiazide (metolazone) - "sequential nephron blockade"
  • • Consider ultrafiltration

🩸 Vasodilators

Nitroglycerin: ↓Preload primarily. Good for HTN, flash pulmonary edema, ischemia.
Nitroprusside: ↓Preload + afterload. For severe HTN, MR, AR.
⚠️ Avoid if SBP <90 or severe AS

⚡ Inotropes (for Cold profiles / Cardiogenic Shock)

Dobutamine
β1 > β2. ↑CO, ↓SVR. May cause hypotension, tachycardia.
Milrinone
PDE3 inhibitor. ↑CO, ↓SVR, ↓PVR. Good for RV failure. Renally cleared.
Dopamine
Dose-dependent. High dose: α effects. More arrhythmogenic.
Add norepinephrine if SBP <90 despite inotropes. Mechanical support (IABP, Impella, ECMO) for refractory shock.

🫁 Respiratory Support

Supplemental O2
Start with NC, titrate to SpO2 >90%
BiPAP/CPAP
Strong evidence! ↓Work of breathing, ↓preload, ↓intubation rates. Start CPAP 5-10 or BiPAP 10/5.
Intubation
If failing NIV, AMS, or hemodynamic collapse. Avoid excessive PPV → ↓venous return.

⚠️ Special Situations

Flash Pulmonary Edema

Sudden onset, often with severe HTN. Think: renal artery stenosis, acute MR, severe HTN.
Tx: Aggressive afterload reduction (NTG, nitroprusside), BiPAP, diuretics

Cardiogenic Shock

SBP <90, CI <2.2, PCWP >15. Cold & wet profile. High mortality.
Tx: Inotropes, vasopressors, revascularization if ACS, mechanical support

RV Failure

Prominent JVD, hepatomegaly, peripheral edema. May be RV infarct, PE, or pulm HTN.
Tx: Preload sensitive! Careful with diuresis. Milrinone preferred. Treat underlying cause.

HFpEF Exacerbation

EF ≥50%. Stiff ventricle, diastolic dysfunction. Often elderly, HTN, AF.
Tx: Diuretics, rate/rhythm control, BP control. More sensitive to volume shifts.

✅ Before Discharge

Clinical Stability

  • ☐ On stable oral diuretic regimen ≥24h
  • ☐ No IV inotropes/vasodilators ≥24h
  • ☐ Ambulatory without symptomatic hypotension
  • ☐ No dyspnea at rest
  • ☐ Heart rate controlled

Medications Optimized

  • ☐ ACEi/ARB/ARNI at target or max tolerated
  • ☐ β-blocker initiated (if not contraindicated)
  • ☐ MRA if indicated
  • ☐ SGLT2i if indicated
  • ☐ Diuretic dose adjusted
Follow-up: Clinic within 7 days, labs (BMP) within 1-2 weeks, cardiac rehab referral, patient education on daily weights, sodium restriction, fluid limits.
