Hemolysis: Intravascular vs Extravascular

Intravascular Hemolysis

RBCs lyse IN bloodstream

• Free Hgb in plasma → ↓Haptoglobin (binds free Hgb)
• Hemoglobinuria (red/brown urine)
• ↑↑LDH
Causes: ABO mismatch, PNH, mechanical valves, DIC, TTP/HUS

Extravascular Hemolysis

RBCs destroyed in spleen/liver

• Hgb processed by macrophages → ↑Indirect bilirubin
• Splenomegaly common
• Haptoglobin mildly ↓ or normal
Causes: Autoimmune, Hereditary spherocytosis, Sickle cell, Thalassemia major

B12 vs Folate Deficiency

Vitamin B12 Deficiency

Causes: Pernicious anemia (anti-IF), gastric surgery, terminal ileal disease, vegan diet, metformin
Unique feature: Neurological symptoms (subacute combined degeneration - posterior columns + lateral corticospinal tracts)
Labs: ↑Methylmalonic acid, ↑Homocysteine
Smear: Hypersegmented neutrophils, macro-ovalocytes

Folate Deficiency

Causes: Poor diet (alcoholics), malabsorption, ↑demand (pregnancy, hemolysis), drugs (methotrexate, phenytoin, TMP)
Unique feature: NO neurological symptoms
Labs: Normal MMA, ↑Homocysteine
Smear: Same as B12 (hypersegmented PMNs)
Clinical Pearl: If you give folate to B12-deficient patient, anemia improves but neuro damage continues! Always check B12 before treating presumed folate deficiency.
