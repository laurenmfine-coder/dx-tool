Appendicitis

Presentation
Periumbilical pain → RLQ, anorexia, N/V, low-grade fever. McBurney's point. Psoas sign, Obturator sign, Rovsing's sign.
Workup
CT abdomen/pelvis (gold standard). US first in children/pregnancy. Alvarado score. WBC often elevated.
Treatment
NPO, IV fluids, antibiotics. Appendectomy (lap vs open). Non-operative management possible in uncomplicated cases.

Acute Cholecystitis

Presentation
RUQ pain >6h (vs biliary colic <6h), fever, Murphy's sign. Pain after fatty meals. "4 F's": Female, Forty, Fat, Fertile.
Workup
RUQ US: Gallstones, wall thickening >4mm, pericholecystic fluid, sonographic Murphy's. HIDA if US equivocal.
Treatment
NPO, IVF, IV antibiotics (cover gram-neg + anaerobes). Lap cholecystectomy within 72h. Perc drain if high-risk.

Acute Diverticulitis

Presentation
LLQ pain, fever, altered bowel habits. "Left-sided appendicitis." Older patients. Complications: abscess, perforation, fistula.
Workup
CT abdomen/pelvis with contrast: Wall thickening, pericolic fat stranding, ± abscess. Avoid colonoscopy in acute phase.
Treatment
Uncomplicated: Outpatient, clear liquids, ± antibiotics. Complicated: NPO, IV antibiotics, IR drainage if abscess. Surgery if perforation.

Small Bowel Obstruction

Presentation
Colicky abdominal pain, N/V, obstipation, distension. Prior surgery (adhesions #1 cause). High-pitched bowel sounds early.
Workup
XR: Dilated loops, air-fluid levels. CT with contrast: Transition point, strangulation signs (mesenteric haziness, poor enhancement).
Treatment
NPO, NGT decompression, IVF. Partial: Trial of conservative management. Complete/strangulation: Urgent surgery.

Physical Exam Signs

Murphy's: Inspiratory arrest with RUQ palpation → Cholecystitis
McBurney's: Tenderness 2/3 from umbilicus to ASIS → Appendicitis
Rovsing's: LLQ palpation → RLQ pain → Appendicitis
Psoas: Pain with hip extension → Retrocecal appendix
Obturator: Pain with hip flexion/internal rotation → Pelvic appendix
Cullen's/Grey Turner: Periumbilical/flank bruising → Hemorrhagic pancreatitis
