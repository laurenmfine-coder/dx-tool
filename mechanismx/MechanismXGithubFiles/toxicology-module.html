⚠️ High-Yield Overdoses

Acetaminophen

Toxic dose: >150 mg/kg
Mechanism: NAPQI depletes glutathione → hepatotoxicity
Stages:
  • 0-24h: N/V or asymptomatic
  • 24-72h: ↑LFTs, RUQ pain
  • 72-96h: Peak hepatotoxicity
  • >96h: Recovery or ALF
Treatment:
  • • Check level at 4h post-ingestion
  • • Use Rumack-Matthew nomogram
  • • NAC most effective if <8h, but give even late
  • • IV NAC: 150mg/kg load → 50mg/kg over 4h → 100mg/kg over 16h

Salicylates

Classic ABG: Mixed resp alkalosis + AG metabolic acidosis
Early: Tinnitus, N/V, tachypnea
Late: AMS, hyperthermia, pulmonary edema, seizures
Treatment:
  • • Activated charcoal if early
  • • IV sodium bicarbonate (alkalinize urine, pH 7.5-8)
  • • Hemodialysis: level >100, AMS, renal failure, pulm edema

TCAs (Tricyclic Antidepressants)

Deadly triad: Seizures, hypotension, arrhythmias
ECG: QRS >100ms, R in aVR >3mm, rightward axis
Mechanism: Na channel blockade, anticholinergic, α-blockade
Treatment:
  • • NaHCO3 for QRS >100ms (goal pH 7.50-7.55)
  • • Benzos for seizures
  • • Lipid emulsion if refractory
  • • Avoid: Class IA/IC antiarrhythmics, physostigmine

Toxic Alcohols

Methanol: Visual changes ("snowfield"), blindness → formic acid
Ethylene glycol: Flank pain, AKI, Ca oxalate crystals
Clue: ↑Osmol gap + ↑Anion gap acidosis
Treatment:
  • • Fomepizole (blocks alcohol dehydrogenase)
  • • Hemodialysis
  • • Methanol: + Folate
  • • Ethylene glycol: + Thiamine, pyridoxine

GI Decontamination

Activated Charcoal
Best within 1-2h. 1g/kg.
NOT for: Acids/alkalis, alcohols, metals (Fe, Li), hydrocarbons
Whole Bowel Irrigation
PEG solution until clear.
For: SR meds, body packers, iron, lithium
Gastric Lavage
Rarely indicated.
Only within 1h of life-threatening ingestion
