Understanding Acid-Base Physiology

Master the mechanisms of pH regulation and disorder compensation

The Henderson-Hasselbalch Relationship

pH = 6.1 + log([HCO₃⁻] / 0.03 × PCO₂)
pH depends on the RATIO of bicarbonate (metabolic) to CO₂ (respiratory)

🫘 Kidneys Control HCO₃⁻

  • • Reabsorb filtered HCO₃⁻ (proximal tubule)
  • • Generate new HCO₃⁻ (distal tubule)
  • • Excrete H⁺ as NH₄⁺ and titratable acid
  • • Takes days to fully compensate

🫁 Lungs Control CO₂

  • • Ventilation eliminates CO₂
  • • Chemoreceptors sense pH and PCO₂
  • • Adjust respiratory rate and depth
  • • Takes minutes to hours to compensate

The Four Primary Disorders

🔴
Metabolic Acidosis
pH↓ HCO₃⁻↓ (Primary)
Either acid gain OR bicarbonate loss
🔵
Metabolic Alkalosis
pH↑ HCO₃⁻↑ (Primary)
Either acid loss OR bicarbonate gain
🟠
Respiratory Acidosis
pH↓ PCO₂↑ (Primary)
Hypoventilation → CO₂ retention
🟢
Respiratory Alkalosis
pH↑ PCO₂↓ (Primary)
Hyperventilation → CO₂ blow-off

Anion Gap: The Mechanism Detective

AG = Na⁺ - (Cl⁻ + HCO₃⁻) = 12 ± 2
Represents unmeasured anions (albumin, phosphate, sulfate, organic acids)

High AG Acidosis (GOLDMARK)

Unmeasured acid added → HCO₃⁻ consumed buffering

  • Glycols (ethylene, propylene)
  • Oxoproline (acetaminophen)
  • Lactate
  • D-lactate
  • Methanol
  • Aspirin (salicylates)
  • Renal failure (uremia)
  • Ketoacidosis

Normal AG (Hyperchloremic) Acidosis

HCO₃⁻ lost → Cl⁻ rises to maintain electroneutrality

  • • GI losses: Diarrhea, fistulas, ileostomy
  • • Renal losses: RTA types 1, 2, 4
  • • Iatrogenic: NS resuscitation, TPN
  • • Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors

Delta-Delta (Δ/Δ): Finding Hidden Disorders

In pure AG acidosis, for every 1 mEq/L rise in AG, HCO₃⁻ should fall by ~1 mEq/L
Δ/Δ = (AG - 12) / (24 - HCO₃⁻)
Δ/Δ = 1-2
Pure AG acidosis
Δ/Δ < 1
+ Non-AG acidosis
Δ/Δ > 2
+ Metabolic alkalosis

Compensation: The Body's Response

The body NEVER overcompensates. If pH is normal with abnormal values, suspect a mixed disorder.

Metabolic Acidosis → Respiratory compensation
Expected PCO₂ = 1.5 × HCO₃⁻ + 8 (±2)
Winter's Formula - Hyperventilation to blow off CO₂
Metabolic Alkalosis → Respiratory compensation
Expected PCO₂ = 0.7 × HCO₃⁻ + 21 (±2)
Hypoventilation to retain CO₂ (limited by hypoxia drive)
Respiratory Acidosis → Metabolic compensation
Acute: ↑1 HCO₃⁻ per 10 ↑PCO₂
Chronic: ↑3.5 HCO₃⁻ per 10 ↑PCO₂
Kidneys retain HCO₃⁻ (takes 3-5 days for full compensation)
Respiratory Alkalosis → Metabolic compensation
Acute: ↓2 HCO₃⁻ per 10 ↓PCO₂
Chronic: ↓5 HCO₃⁻ per 10 ↓PCO₂
Kidneys excrete HCO₃⁻
