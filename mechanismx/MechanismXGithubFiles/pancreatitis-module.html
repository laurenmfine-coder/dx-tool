🔥 Pancreatitis

Acute Pancreatitis: Diagnosis

Requires 2 of 3 criteria:
🤕
Abdominal Pain
Epigastric, radiating to back, sudden onset
🔬
Lipase >3× ULN
More specific than amylase
📷
Imaging Findings
CT/MRI/US showing pancreatitis
Pearl: Lipase level does NOT correlate with severity. A patient with lipase 500 can be sicker than one with lipase 5000.

Etiology: "I GET SMASHED"

I
Idiopathic
~20% (consider microlithiasis, SOD)
G
Gallstones (#1)
~40%. Check RUQ US, LFTs, CBD dilation
E
Ethanol (#2)
~30%. Usually after years of heavy use
T
Trauma
Blunt abdominal, post-ERCP
S
Steroids
And other drugs (see below)
M
Mumps/Malignancy
Viral, pancreatic/ampullary cancer
A
Autoimmune
IgG4-related disease
S
Scorpion stings
Rare but classic board question
H
Hyperlipidemia/Hypercalcemia/Hypothermia
TG >1000 mg/dL increases risk
E
ERCP
Most common iatrogenic cause
D
Drugs
Valproic acid, azathioprine, didanosine, thiazides

Severity Assessment

BISAP Score (Bedside Index of Severity)

B
BUN >25
I
Impaired mental status
S
SIRS criteria (≥2)
A
Age >60
P
Pleural effusion
Score ≥3 = High risk of mortality. Easy to calculate at admission.
Mild
• No organ failure
• No local/systemic complications
• Resolves in first week
Moderate
• Transient organ failure (<48h)
• OR local complications
• OR exacerbation of comorbidity
Severe
• Persistent organ failure (>48h)
• Single or multiple organ
• Mortality ~30%

💊 Management

Aggressive IV Fluids

• Lactated Ringer's preferred (less SIRS than NS)
• 250-500 mL/hr initially (if no CHF/CKD)
• Goal: UOP >0.5 mL/kg/hr, ↓BUN, ↓Hct
Most impactful in first 12-24 hours

Pain Control

• Opioids (hydromorphone, fentanyl)
• No evidence morphine worsens sphincter spasm
• NSAIDs as adjunct

Nutrition

• Early enteral feeding (within 24-48h if tolerated)
• Start with clear liquids → low-fat diet
• NPO only if severe nausea/vomiting/ileus
• TPN rarely needed (only if prolonged ileus)
"Bowel rest" is outdated - feed the gut!

What NOT to Do

• No prophylactic antibiotics
• No routine CT on admission
• No early ERCP unless cholangitis/obstruction

Gallstone Pancreatitis: Special Considerations

ERCP Indications:
  • • Concurrent cholangitis (urgent within 24h)
  • • Persistent biliary obstruction
  • • NOT for predicted mild gallstone pancreatitis
Cholecystectomy:
  • • Same admission for mild cases
  • • Delay 6 weeks if necrotizing
  • • ↓ Recurrence from 30% to <5%

⚠️ Complications

Local Complications
Acute Peripancreatic Fluid Collection
<4 weeks. No wall. Usually resolves.
Pancreatic Pseudocyst
>4 weeks. Encapsulated. Drain if symptomatic, infected, or >6cm.
Acute Necrotic Collection
<4 weeks. Contains necrotic tissue. No wall.
Walled-Off Necrosis (WON)
>4 weeks. Encapsulated necrosis. High risk of infection.
Systemic Complications
Infected Necrosis
Suspect if: Fever, ↑WBC, failure to improve after 7-10 days, gas in necrosis on CT.
Tx: Antibiotics (carbapenems) + Drainage (step-up approach)
Organ Failure
Respiratory (ARDS), Renal (ATN), Cardiovascular (shock).
Modified Marshall Score used to define.
Other
• Splenic vein thrombosis → Gastric varices
• Pseudoaneurysm (splenic a.) → Hemorrhage
• Abdominal compartment syndrome

Chronic Pancreatitis

Features

  • • Chronic epigastric pain (may be painless)
  • • Steatorrhea (fat malabsorption)
  • • Diabetes (endocrine failure)
  • • Weight loss
  • • Pancreatic calcifications on imaging

Causes

• Alcohol (most common in Western countries)
• Idiopathic
• Genetic (CFTR, PRSS1, SPINK1)
• Autoimmune (IgG4-related)
• Recurrent acute pancreatitis
Management
Pain control, alcohol/smoking cessation, pancreatic enzyme replacement (with meals), fat-soluble vitamins (ADEK), diabetes management
