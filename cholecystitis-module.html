⚙️ Cholecystitis/Cholangitis

Pathophysiology | Surgery

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Cholecystitis = cystic duct obstruction; Cholangitis = CBD obstruction + infection
  • Murphy's sign for cholecystitis; Charcot's triad for cholangitis
  • Reynolds' pentad = surgical emergency
  • Cholangitis requires biliary decompression (ERCP), not just antibiotics

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Acute Cholecystitis

Gallstone obstruction of cystic duct leads to gallbladder inflammation.

Pathway

Gallstone impacts cystic duct→Bile stasis in gallbladder→Mucosal injury + bacterial overgrowth→Gallbladder wall inflammation→RUQ pain, Murphy's sign

Ascending Cholangitis

Biliary obstruction with infection is a surgical emergency.

Pathway

CBD obstruction (stone, stricture, tumor)→Bile stasis + bacterial colonization→Ascending infection→Bacteremia/sepsis→Charcot's triad → Reynolds' pentad

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Murphy's sign

Mechanism: Inflamed gallbladder contacts parietal peritoneum during inspiration

Significance: Inspiratory arrest during RUQ palpation - specific for cholecystitis

Charcot's triad (fever, RUQ pain, jaundice)

Mechanism: Biliary obstruction + infection

Significance: Classic for acute cholangitis

Reynolds' pentad (+ AMS, hypotension)

Mechanism: Sepsis from cholangitis

Significance: Indicates severe cholangitis - emergent decompression needed
