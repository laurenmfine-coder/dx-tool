🩻 NEW MODULE

RadDx

Learn imaging the way clinicians actually use it — not as pattern recognition, but as a reasoning tool that shapes diagnosis and management.

🧠

Order & Justify

Given a clinical scenario, which imaging study do you order and why? Modality selection, contraindications, urgency, and cost-effectiveness.

"68F with acute-onset worst headache of life. What do you order first and why? What are you looking for?"
🔍

Predict & Interpret

Before seeing results, what do you expect to find? Then systematically interpret the image — what's normal, what's abnormal, and what's the diagnosis?

"Before I show you the CT, what findings would confirm SAH? What would change your mind?"
⚡

Reason & Act

How do these results change your differential, urgency, and management? What's your next step — and what if the results were different?

"CT shows blood in the basal cisterns. How does this change your plan? What's the next imaging study and why?"
🔗

Powered by Radiopaedia Integration

RadDx links to Radiopaedia.org case pages for authentic imaging examples. Rather than showing pre-labeled images (which gives away the answer), we link to their staged case presentations — history first, then images, then diagnosis — so you practice the full reasoning process. Imaging questions in RadDx are designed so you work through the clinical thinking before seeing any images.

Core Principles

🚨 Don't Delay for Imaging

Some diagnoses are clinical — imaging can wait
Tension Pneumothorax Compartment Syndrome Testicular Torsion Meningitis (Abx first)

👶 ALARA in Pediatrics

As Low As Reasonably Achievable — ultrasound first
US → Appendicitis US → Intussusception US → Pyloric Stenosis CXR → NRD

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Minimize radiation, maximize safety
US → Ectopic US → Appendicitis MRI over CT No gadolinium
