The Ceiling

Textbook cases
have a ceiling.

The foundation cases — the classic presentations, the textbook constellations — are essential. You cannot recognize an atypical presentation if you've never seen the typical one.

But medicine's hardest problems don't present from the textbook. The patient who had AADC but also had fevers that made the oculogyric crises look like febrile seizures. The HAE patient who'd been on an ACE inhibitor for two years and everyone assumed that was the cause. The Lynch syndrome family whose cancers spanned four specialties and no one connected them.

These cases don't exist in any textbook. They exist only in the memory of the physicians who saw them.
