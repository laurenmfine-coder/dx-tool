The Breathless Banker

A 58-year-old executive with progressive dyspnea

A 58-year-old male investment banker presents to the ED with progressive shortness of breath over the past 3 weeks. He reports orthopnea (sleeps on 3 pillows) and wakes up gasping at night. He's also noticed swelling in his ankles. His medical history includes hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He takes lisinopril and atorvastatin. He drinks 2-3 glasses of wine nightly and quit smoking 10 years ago.

❓ Question 1 of 3

Based on this presentation, which physical exam finding would you most expect?

✅ Correct! Bibasilar crackles and elevated JVP

This presentation is classic for acute decompensated heart failure (ADHF):

  • Orthopnea + PND = left-sided failure (pulmonary congestion)
  • Peripheral edema = right-sided failure (systemic congestion)
  • Bibasilar crackles = pulmonary edema
  • Elevated JVP = volume overload / right heart pressure

The tachycardia and hypoxia indicate the body's compensatory response to reduced cardiac output and pulmonary congestion.
