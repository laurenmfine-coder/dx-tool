The Question You'll Face

Why does hyperkalemia cause cardiac toxicity, and why does calcium help?

After this module, you'll understand: How K+ affects the resting membrane potential, why cardiac myocytes are vulnerable, and how calcium stabilizes the membrane without changing potassium levels.

Why This Could Save a Life

Emergency Scenario

"It's 3 AM. A 45-year-old man arrives by EMS with severe chest pain radiating to his back. His BP is 180/110 in the right arm and 140/90 in the left arm. The ED attending looks at you: 'What are you thinking?'"

The Life-or-Death Question

How do you recognize aortic dissection before it's too late?

This knowledge matters because: Missing an aortic dissection is often fatal. The classic triad (tearing pain + blood pressure differential + widened mediastinum) can save your patient's life.

The Clinical Puzzle

Your Patient

"Your patient has pneumonia and you start antibiotics. Two days later, her sodium drops from 140 to 125. She's euvolemic on exam. The intern suggests fluid restriction. Is that right? Why did this happen?"

The Diagnostic Challenge

How do you approach hyponatremia systematically?

Understanding this means: You'll recognize SIADH from pneumonia, know to check urine studies before treating, and avoid the dangerous overcorrection that causes osmotic demyelination.
