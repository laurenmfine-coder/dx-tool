MECHANISM
Etiology
🔑 I GET SMASHED: Idiopathic, Gallstones (#1 — 40%), Ethanol (#2 — 30%), Trauma, Steroids, Mumps, Autoimmune, Scorpion stings, Hypertriglyceridemia/Hypercalcemia/Hypothermia, ERCP, Drugs (azathioprine, valproic acid, didanosine, thiazides)

Mechanism: premature activation of trypsinogen → trypsin within pancreas → autodigestion → inflammation → local/systemic complications. Gallstone pancreatitis: stone impacts at ampulla of Vater → pancreatic duct obstruction → ductal hypertension → enzyme activation.

SeverityDefinition
MildNo organ failure, no local complications. Self-limited (80%)
Moderately severeTransient organ failure (<48h) OR local complications
SeverePersistent organ failure (>48h). Mortality 30-50%
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Complications

Diagnosis (2 of 3 criteria)

1. Characteristic epigastric pain radiating to back. 2. Lipase ≥3× upper limit of normal (lipase preferred over amylase — more sensitive AND specific). 3. CT findings of pancreatitis.

⚠️ Complications Timeline:
• Early (0-2 weeks): SIRS, organ failure (pulmonary #1 — ARDS), peripancreatic fluid collections
• Late (>2-4 weeks): Pancreatic pseudocyst (encapsulated fluid WITHOUT necrosis), walled-off necrosis (WON — encapsulated WITH necrosis). Infected necrosis: suspect if fever/leukocytosis at week 2-3, gas bubbles on CT → FNA culture → antibiotics (carbapenems) → necrosectomy if needed
🔑 Ranson Criteria: Predict severity. At admission (GA LAW): Glucose >200, Age >55, LDH >350, AST >250, WBC >16K. At 48h (C HOBBS): Calcium <8, Hct drop >10%, O2 (PaO2 <60), BUN increase >5, Base deficit >4, Sequestration (fluid) >6L. Score ≥3 = severe.
TREATMENT
Management
InterventionDetails
IV fluidsAggressive early resuscitation: goal-directed (LR preferred over NS). 1.5-2 mL/kg/hr initially. Monitor UOP, lactate, BUN
Pain controlIV opioids (hydromorphone, fentanyl). Morphine is safe (meperidine myth debunked — no evidence morphine worsens pancreatitis via sphincter of Oddi spasm)
NutritionEARLY oral feeding as tolerated (within 24h). If unable: enteral (nasojejunal tube) preferred over TPN. Enteral nutrition maintains gut barrier and reduces infection
Gallstone pancreatitisERCP within 24h if cholangitis. Same-admission cholecystectomy once resolved to prevent recurrence
AntibioticsNOT routine. Only for confirmed infected necrosis or cholangitis. Carbapenems penetrate pancreatic tissue
