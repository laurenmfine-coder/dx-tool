MECHANISM
Classification & Neurobiology

Substance use disorders (SUD) involve dysregulation of the brain's reward circuitry (mesolimbic dopamine pathway: VTA → nucleus accumbens). All addictive substances increase dopamine release in the nucleus accumbens, producing reinforcement.

🔑 DSM-5 Criteria (≥2 of 11 in 12 months): Impaired control (4): larger amounts, desire to cut down, time spent, craving. Social impairment (3): role failure, social problems, giving up activities. Risky use (2): hazardous situations, continued despite knowledge of harm. Pharmacological (2): tolerance, withdrawal. Mild: 2-3, Moderate: 4-5, Severe: ≥6 criteria.

Medication-Assisted Treatment

Alcohol Use Disorder

AgentMechanismKey Points
NaltrexoneOpioid antagonist → reduces reward from drinkingOral or monthly IM (Vivitrol). Reduces heavy drinking days. Contraindicated with opioid use
AcamprosateModulates glutamate/GABA → reduces cravingPromotes abstinence. Safe in liver disease (renally excreted). Take TID
DisulfiramInhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase → acetaldehyde accumulationAversive conditioning. If patient drinks: flushing, nausea, headache, tachycardia. Requires high motivation/compliance

Opioid Use Disorder

🔑 Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) saves lives:
Methadone: Full mu-agonist, long half-life. Dispensed at federally regulated clinics. Prevents withdrawal and craving. Risk of QTc prolongation.
Buprenorphine: Partial mu-agonist (ceiling effect → safer overdose profile). Office-based prescribing (DATA waiver no longer required as of 2023). Often combined with naloxone (Suboxone) to deter IV misuse.
Naltrexone: Mu-antagonist. Extended-release IM monthly (Vivitrol). Patient must be opioid-free 7-14 days first (precipitates withdrawal if opioids in system).

Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome

Wernicke encephalopathy (acute, reversible): Confusion + Ophthalmoplegia + Ataxia. Due to thiamine (B1) deficiency from chronic alcohol use. Treatment: IV thiamine BEFORE glucose (glucose metabolism depletes remaining thiamine → precipitates Wernicke). Korsakoff syndrome (chronic, often irreversible): Anterograde amnesia + confabulation (patient fills memory gaps with fabricated stories). Results from untreated/undertreated Wernicke.
