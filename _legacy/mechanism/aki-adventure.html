Bicarb of 18 is a mild non-anion gap metabolic acidosis from renal failure — it does not need direct correction. Treating the underlying AKI (volume repletion, removing nephrotoxins) will correct the acidosis as kidney function recovers. Sodium bicarbonate can cause volume overload and does not address the root cause.

Renal biopsy is not indicated here. The UA is bland (no active sediment suggesting glomerulonephritis or interstitial nephritis), FENa is prerenal, and there's a clear precipitant (NSAID + ACEi). RESOLUTION
48 Hours Later
After 2L NS and stopping nephrotoxins: urine output recovered to 60 mL/hr. Cr trending down: 5.2 → 3.8 → 2.6. K+ normalized to 4.4 after treatment. Patient feeling much better, eating and drinking.
OUTCOME
🏆 Excellent Clinical Reasoning
Excellent AKI management! Key discharge points: (1) NEVER use NSAIDs again — document allergy/intolerance. (2) Restart lisinopril only after Cr returns to within 30% of baseline — ACEi/ARB provides long-term renal protection but must be held during acute injury. (3) Hold metformin until Cr <1.5 (or eGFR >30). (4) Recheck BMP in 5-7 days. (5) Nephrology follow-up — this AKI episode may accelerate CKD progression.
