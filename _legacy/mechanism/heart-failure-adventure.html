DECISION 1
Acute Management
✅
Correct!
Correct! Triple therapy for acute pulmonary edema: (1) BiPAP improves oxygenation, reduces work of breathing, and decreases preload/afterload — reduces intubation rates. (2) IV furosemide (start at 1-2.5× home dose, minimum 40mg). (3) IV nitroglycerin for afterload reduction and venodilation (BP 168/98 supports this). DO NOT give fluids.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
BiPAP should be tried first — it's remarkably effective in cardiogenic pulmonary edema (reduces intubation rates by ~60%). Reserve intubation for patients failing BiPAP, altered mental status, or unable to protect airway. Positive pressure ventilation + diuresis + vasodilators often rapidly improve oxygenation.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
This patient has 15 pounds of excess fluid! IV fluids will worsen pulmonary edema and could be fatal. The elevated JVP, crackles, and peripheral edema all indicate severe volume overload. She needs aggressive diuresis and afterload reduction, not volume.

✅
Correct!
Correct! Continue aggressive diuresis (consider furosemide drip 10-20mg/hr for consistent output). Optimize guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT): ACEi/ARB (or ARNI), beta-blocker (carvedilol/metoprolol succinate/bisoprolol), MRA (spironolactone), and SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin/empagliflozin). The SGLT2i is the newest pillar — reduces HF hospitalizations by 26%.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Mild creatinine elevation during decompensated HF diuresis is expected and acceptable ('permissive worsening'). The creatinine rise reflects improved venous decongestion more than kidney injury. Stopping diuresis because of small Cr bumps leads to persistent congestion, which actually worsens renal outcomes long-term.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone) are reserved for cardiogenic shock with low cardiac output syndrome (cold extremities, low BP, poor perfusion). This patient is 'warm and wet' — adequate perfusion but volume overloaded. Inotropes increase mortality in stable HFrEF and are only for hemodynamically compromised patients.

Consider cardiac rehab referral and implantable cardioverter-defibrillator (ICD) evaluation for EF ≤35% on optimal GDMT ≥3 months.
