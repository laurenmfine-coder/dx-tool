MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Organophosphates (OPs) irreversibly inhibit acetylcholinesterase (AChE) → acetylcholine accumulates at muscarinic AND nicotinic receptors → cholinergic crisis. Found in pesticides (malathion, parathion), nerve agents (sarin, VX).

ReceptorLocationEffects of ACh Excess
MuscarinicParasympathetic end-organsDUMBBELSS: Diarrhea, Urination, Miosis, Bronchospasm/Bronchorrhea, Bradycardia, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation, Sweating
NicotinicNMJ, sympathetic gangliaFasciculations, muscle weakness, paralysis, tachycardia (ganglionic), hypertension
CNSBrainSeizures, coma, respiratory depression
⚠️ Death in OP poisoning: Respiratory failure from bronchospasm + bronchorrhea (secretion flooding) + respiratory muscle paralysis + central respiratory depression. The combination is lethal.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis
🔑 Garlic/petroleum odor on patient's clothing or breath. Agricultural worker, pesticide exposure, chemical attack scenario. Lab: RBC cholinesterase (butyrylcholinesterase) levels depressed — confirmatory but don't wait for results to treat.

Carbamates vs Organophosphates

FeatureOrganophosphatesCarbamates
AChE inhibitionIrreversible (ages over hours)Reversible (spontaneous recovery)
DurationProlonged (days-weeks)Shorter (hours)
CNS penetrationYes (seizures common)Less (CNS symptoms less severe)
PralidoximeEssential (before aging)Generally not needed (AChE recovers spontaneously)

Intermediate Syndrome

24-96h after exposure: proximal muscle weakness, cranial nerve palsies, respiratory failure. Occurs AFTER cholinergic crisis resolves. Due to persistent NMJ dysfunction. May require prolonged ventilatory support.

TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
DecontaminationRemove clothing, wash skin with soap/water. Protect healthcare workers (PPE!). OP can be absorbed through skin
Atropine (first-line)Competitive muscarinic antagonist. Treats: bronchorrhea, bronchospasm, bradycardia, salivation. Does NOT treat nicotinic effects (weakness, fasciculations). Dose: 2-4 mg IV, double q5 min until secretions clear (may need massive doses — 10-100+ mg). Endpoint: DRYING OF SECRETIONS (not heart rate or pupil size)
Pralidoxime (2-PAM)Reactivates AChE by cleaving OP from enzyme. Must give BEFORE aging (irreversible binding — varies by agent, hours to days). Treats NICOTINIC effects. Dose: 1-2g IV over 15-30 min. Continue for 24-48h
BenzodiazepinesFor seizure control. Diazepam preferred (also used for fasciculations)
🔑 Atropine endpoint: Drying of pulmonary secretions. NOT heart rate and NOT pupil size. Bronchorrhea/bronchospasm is the lethal muscarinic effect — clear the lungs first. Pupil dilation and tachycardia are poor indicators of adequate atropinization.
