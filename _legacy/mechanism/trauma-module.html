CLINICAL
Key Trauma Injuries

FAST Exam

4 windows: RUQ (Morrison pouch — most sensitive), LUQ, suprapubic, subxiphoid. Positive FAST + unstable → OR.

⚠️ Surgical Emergencies:
• Tension PTX: tracheal deviation AWAY, absent breath sounds, JVD. Clinical diagnosis → needle decompression BEFORE imaging
• Cardiac tamponade: Beck triad (hypotension + JVD + muffled hearts). Pericardiocentesis/thoracotomy
• Massive hemothorax: >1500 mL or >200 mL/hr → thoracotomy
• Flail chest: ≥3 ribs in ≥2 places → underlying pulmonary contusion is the real danger

Abdominal Trauma

OrganKey Points
SpleenMost injured in BLUNT trauma. Kehr sign. Stable → observe. Unstable → splenectomy + vaccines (pneumococcal, meningococcal, Hib)
LiverMost injured in PENETRATING trauma. Most managed non-operatively if stable
