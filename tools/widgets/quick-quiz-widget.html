Renal / Electrolytes
A patient with DKA has a serum glucose of 450 mg/dL and measured Na+ of 128 mEq/L. What is the corrected sodium?
Correct! Use the correction formula: Na + 1.6 × [(glucose - 100) / 100]

128 + 1.6 × [(450 - 100) / 100] = 128 + 1.6 × 3.5 = 128 + 5.6 ≈ 133-134 mEq/L

This matters because the "true" sodium is higher than measured — important for management decisions.
