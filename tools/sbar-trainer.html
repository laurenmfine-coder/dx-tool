        S

        Situation

        "I'm calling about... The reason I'm calling is..."

        Good Situation statements include:
        • Your name and role
        • Patient name and location
        • The specific concern (one sentence)
        Example: "This is Sarah, the RN caring for Mrs. Mitchell in 4215. I'm concerned about a change in her mental status."
        B

        Background

        "The patient was admitted for... Their baseline is..."

        Background should include:
        • Admission diagnosis/reason
        • Relevant medical history
        • What's "normal" for this patient
        • Recent changes/treatments
        Example: "She's post-op day 2 from hip replacement. She was oriented and stable earlier today."
        A

        Assessment

        "I think the problem is... I'm concerned about..."

        Assessment is YOUR clinical judgment:
        • State your assessment/concern clearly
        • Include objective data (vitals, labs)
        • Compare to baseline
        • Say what you're worried about
        Example: "Her BP dropped from 128 to 98, she's tachycardic at 108, and has a new fever. I'm concerned about sepsis."
        R

        Recommendation

        "I think we need... I'm requesting..."

        Recommendation should be specific:
        • State what you want to happen
        • Be specific (come evaluate, order labs, etc.)
        • Include urgency level
        • Ask for read-back if giving/receiving orders
        Example: "I'd like you to come evaluate her. Can we also get a CBC, lactate, and blood cultures?"

          💡 SBAR Tips

          S - Situation: Be concise. State the problem in one sentence. "I'm calling about [patient] because [concern]."
          B - Background: Only include RELEVANT history. What does this person need to know to understand the current situation?
          A - Assessment: This is YOUR clinical judgment. Don't be afraid to say what you think is happening. "I'm concerned this could be..."
          R - Recommendation: Be specific about what you need. "I'd like you to come evaluate" or "Can we get [specific orders]?"
          Pro tip: Have relevant data ready (vitals, recent labs, current medications) before you make the call.
