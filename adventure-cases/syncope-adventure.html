🧬 Syncope Clinical AdventureCARDIOLOGY

🎮 Case Presentation

A 17-year-old collapses during basketball practice. Teammates report he was running when he suddenly fell. He regained consciousness within 30 seconds. No seizure activity. Family history: uncle died suddenly at age 32.

Decision Point: Risk Stratification

⚠️ Exertional syncope + family history of sudden death = HIGH RISK for cardiac cause. This is NOT vasovagal until proven otherwise.

Cardiac vs Non-Cardiac Syncope

Cardiac (high-risk)Non-Cardiac (low-risk)
ExertionalPositional (standing up quickly)
During exerciseAfter prolonged standing
Family history of sudden death <50Prodrome (warmth, nausea, vision changes)
Abnormal ECGKnown trigger (blood draw, pain)
Palpitations before eventYoung, otherwise healthy

Differential: Cardiac Causes

ConditionECG Clues
Hypertrophic cardiomyopathyLVH, septal Q waves, systolic murmur increases with Valsalva/standing
Long QT syndromeQTc >470ms
BrugadaCoved ST elevation V1-V3
WPWShort PR, delta wave, wide QRS
ARVCEpsilon waves, T inversion V1-V3
🔑 HCM is the #1 cause of sudden cardiac death in young athletes. Workup: ECG (everyone), echo if concern for structural disease, exercise stress test, consider genetic testing if family history.
