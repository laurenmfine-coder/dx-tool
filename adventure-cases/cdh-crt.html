🧬 Congenital Diaphragmatic Hernia CRTEMBRYOLOGY

Clinical Scenario

A newborn delivered at term develops severe respiratory distress immediately after birth. Left-sided breath sounds are absent. The abdomen appears scaphoid.

Key Findings

FindingSignificance
Immediate respiratory distressPulmonary hypoplasia from compression by herniated abdominal contents
Scaphoid abdomenBowel in chest, not abdomen — classic CDH sign
Absent breath sounds (left)90% are left-sided Bochdalek hernias (posterolateral defect)
CXR: bowel loops in thoraxConfirms diagnosis; mediastinal shift to contralateral side
🔑 Board Pearl: Do NOT bag-mask ventilate aggressively — insufflates stomach/bowel in chest → worsens compression. Intubate immediately. Place OG tube for gastric decompression. Stabilize → surgical repair is NOT emergent (stabilize pulmonary hypertension first).

Embryology

Bochdalek hernia (posterolateral, 90%): failure of pleuroperitoneal membrane closure at ~8-10 weeks. Left > right (liver protects right side). Morgagni hernia (anterior/retrosternal, rare): usually right-sided, often asymptomatic.

⚠️ Complications: Pulmonary hypoplasia (both lungs affected), persistent pulmonary hypertension (PPHN), intestinal malrotation (always present with CDH).
