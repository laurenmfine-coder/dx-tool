🫘 Glomerular Disease

Nephritic vs Nephrotic

Nephritic

"Inflammatory"
  • • Hematuria + RBC casts
  • • Proteinuria (<3.5g/day)
  • • Hypertension
  • • Oliguria, ↑Cr
  • • Mild edema

Nephrotic

"Leaky podocytes"
  • • Proteinuria >3.5g/day
  • • Hypoalbuminemia
  • • Severe edema
  • • Hyperlipidemia
  • • Lipiduria (fatty casts)
Pearl: Many diseases can have features of both (nephritic-nephrotic). Classification helps guide thinking but isn't absolute.

Nephritic Diseases

Post-Streptococcal GN (PSGN)

Most common GN in children
↓C3
Presentation: 1-3 weeks after pharyngitis or 3-6 weeks after skin infection. "Cola-colored urine," edema, HTN.
Diagnosis: ↑ASO (pharyngitis) or anti-DNase B (skin). ↓C3. Biopsy: "starry sky" (subepithelial humps).
Tx: Supportive. Self-limited in children (weeks). Adults may have worse prognosis.

IgA Nephropathy (Berger's Disease)

Most common GN worldwide
Normal C3
Presentation: Episodic gross hematuria during/within days of URI or GI infection ("synpharyngitic"). Young males.
Diagnosis: Biopsy: Mesangial IgA deposits on IF. ↑Serum IgA in 50%.
Tx: ACEi/ARB. Steroids if progressive. Variable course - some progress to ESRD.

Rapidly Progressive GN (RPGN)

⚠️ Nephrology emergency - rapid loss of kidney function
Biopsy: Crescents (proliferated parietal epithelial cells) in >50% of glomeruli
Type I: Anti-GBM
Linear IF. Goodpasture's if + lung hemorrhage. Anti-GBM Ab.
Type II: Immune Complex
Granular IF. Lupus, PSGN, IgA, MPGN, endocarditis.
Type III: Pauci-immune
No/little IF. ANCA vasculitis (GPA, MPA, EGPA).
Tx: Urgent! Steroids + cyclophosphamide or rituximab. Plasmapheresis for anti-GBM/severe ANCA.

Lupus Nephritis

50% of SLE patients develop renal involvement
↓C3, ↓C4
Classes: I-VI based on biopsy. Class III/IV (proliferative) are most severe.
Biopsy: "Full house" IF (IgG, IgA, IgM, C3, C1q). Wire loops.
Labs: ↓C3/C4, +ANA, +anti-dsDNA (correlates with activity), +anti-Smith.
Tx: Based on class. III/IV: Steroids + MMF or cyclophosphamide. V: May add calcineurin inhibitor.

Nephrotic Diseases

Minimal Change Disease (MCD)

#1 cause of nephrotic syndrome in CHILDREN
Presentation: Children 2-6 years. Sudden onset nephrotic syndrome, often after URI. Selective proteinuria (albumin).
Biopsy: Normal on LM. EM: Podocyte foot process effacement. IF negative.
Tx: Excellent response to steroids (90%). Relapse common but steroid-responsive.

Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS)

#1 cause of nephrotic syndrome in ADULTS (especially African Americans)
Causes: Primary (idiopathic), HIV, heroin, obesity, sickle cell, reflux nephropathy, genetic (APOL1).
Biopsy: Focal (some glomeruli) + Segmental (part of glomerulus) sclerosis. EM: foot process effacement.
Tx: Steroids (less responsive than MCD). May need calcineurin inhibitors. High recurrence post-transplant.

Membranous Nephropathy

#1 cause of nephrotic syndrome in WHITE adults
Associations: Primary (anti-PLA2R antibody in 70%). Secondary: HBV, SLE, NSAIDs, penicillamine, solid tumors (lung, colon).
Biopsy: Thickened GBM, "spike and dome" on silver stain. Subepithelial deposits. Granular IgG/C3 on IF.
⚠️ High risk of venous thromboembolism (renal vein thrombosis!)
Tx: Conservative if stable. Rituximab or calcineurin inhibitors if progressive. Screen for malignancy in older adults.

Diabetic Nephropathy

#1 cause of ESRD in the US
Progression: Hyperfiltration → Microalbuminuria (30-300 mg/day) → Overt proteinuria → Nephrotic syndrome → ESRD.
Biopsy: Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules, GBM thickening, mesangial expansion.
Tx: ACEi/ARB (even if normotensive), SGLT2 inhibitors, tight glucose control, BP control, finerenone.

Amyloidosis

Types: AL (primary, plasma cell dyscrasia), AA (secondary, chronic inflammation like RA).
Biopsy: Congo red stain → Apple-green birefringence under polarized light. Amyloid deposits in glomeruli.
Tx: Treat underlying cause. AL: Chemo (bortezomib). AA: Control inflammation.

Nephrotic Syndrome Complications

Thromboembolism
Loss of antithrombin III. Hypercoagulable state. Renal vein thrombosis (especially membranous). DVT/PE.
Infection
Loss of immunoglobulins. Encapsulated organisms (S. pneumoniae). Peritonitis in children.
Hyperlipidemia
Liver increases lipoprotein synthesis. ↑LDL, ↑Total cholesterol. Accelerated atherosclerosis.
Other
Vitamin D deficiency (loss of binding protein), hypothyroidism (loss of thyroid-binding globulin), AKI.
