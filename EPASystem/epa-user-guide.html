EPA & Competency Tracking System

Welcome to the DxSuite EPA and Competency Tracking System. This guide will help you understand how your clinical reasoning practice contributes to your progression toward entrustment for residency.

💡 Quick Start

Every case you complete in DxSuite automatically contributes to your EPA and competency progress. Check your EPA Dashboard regularly to see your advancement toward residency readiness.

Entrustment Levels

The Modified Chen Entrustment Scale describes your readiness to perform clinical activities with progressively less supervision.

Level Label Supervisor Says What It Means
1b Observation "Watch me do this" You observe the activity being performed
2a Coactivity "Let's do this together" You perform with direct guidance throughout
2b Direct Supervision "I'll watch you" You perform while supervisor is present and ready to assist
3a Indirect - Full Review "I'll double-check all findings" You perform independently; supervisor reviews everything
3b Entrustable "I'll double-check key findings" Ready for residency - perform independently with spot-checks

🎯 Goal for Graduation

Medical students should achieve Level 3b (Entrustable) in all 13 Core EPAs before entering residency. This means you can perform these activities independently with only key findings reviewed.

What are EPAs?

Entrustable Professional Activities (EPAs) are units of clinical work that can be entrusted to a learner once they have demonstrated sufficient competence. Unlike traditional competencies that describe general abilities, EPAs describe specific tasks that physicians perform.

The AAMC defined 13 Core EPAs that all medical students should be able to perform upon entering residency, regardless of specialty choice.

Competency Framework

The 2024 Foundational Competencies for Undergraduate Medical Education provides the framework underlying EPA development. This unified framework was developed by LCME, AAMC, AACOM, and ACGME.

The 6 Competency Domains

1️⃣ Professionalism

Integrity, respect, ethical reasoning, accountability (10 subcompetencies)

2️⃣ Patient Care

Compassionate, evidence-informed, equitable care (11 subcompetencies)

3️⃣ Medical Knowledge

Foundational sciences, clinical reasoning, evidence appraisal (5 subcompetencies)

4️⃣ Practice-Based Learning

Feedback integration, self-assessment, lifelong learning (5 subcompetencies)

5️⃣ Interpersonal Skills

Communication, collaboration, patient education (6 subcompetencies)

6️⃣ Systems-Based Practice

Social determinants, safety, quality improvement (8 subcompetencies)

The Four Tenets of Osteopathic Medicine

  1. Body Unity: The body is a unit; the person is a unit of body, mind, and spirit
  2. Self-Regulation: The body is capable of self-regulation, self-healing, and health maintenance
  3. Structure-Function: Structure and function are reciprocally interrelated
  4. Rational Treatment: Rational treatment is based on understanding body unity, self-regulation, and structure-function interrelationship

Developmental Feedback

After completing cases, you receive feedback aligned with AAMC EPA Developmental Schematics. Feedback is categorized as:

  • Behaviors Requiring Correction: Identifies specific issues to address immediately
  • Developing Behaviors: Acknowledges progress while suggesting refinements
  • Entrustable Behaviors: Confirms readiness for the next level of independence

Portfolio Exports

Generate reports for advising meetings, entrustment committees, and accreditation documentation:

  • Student EPA Report: Individual progress across all 13 EPAs
  • Competency Summary: Performance by domain and subcompetency
  • Class Aggregate: Cohort-level data for curriculum planning
  • LCME Curriculum Map: Maps activities to accreditation elements
  • DO OMM Integration: Osteopathic-specific documentation

Frequently Asked Questions

How is my entrustment level determined?

Your level is calculated based on case performance, self-assessment accuracy, and pattern of improvement over time. Multiple successful cases at a given level are required before advancement.

What if my level goes down?

Entrustment levels can decrease if performance indicates the need for more supervision. This is normal and helps ensure accurate tracking of your readiness.

How many cases do I need to become entrustable?

There's no fixed number. Progress depends on consistent demonstration of competence. Most students achieve entrustment after 20-50 cases per EPA with consistent performance.

Can I see which specific competencies I'm developing in each case?

Yes! Each case displays EPA and competency tags. After completion, your feedback includes competency-specific comments.
