8:44 AM
🧑‍🎓
When the coronary artery is occluded, the myocardium becomes ischemic. The cells start dying due to lack of oxygen, and when they die, they release their intracellular contents including troponin into the bloodstream.
8:45 AM
👩‍⚕️
Good foundation! Let me add some nuance to your understanding.
📚 Teaching Point:
The Cascade:
1. Plaque rupture → thrombogenic core exposed
2. Platelet aggregation → occlusive thrombus forms
3. Complete occlusion → transmural ischemia (minutes)
4. ATP depletion → ion pump failure → membrane instability
5. Cell death → troponin release (hours)

Key insight: Troponin rises 3-4 hours after symptom onset. A negative troponin at presentation doesn't rule out STEMI - that's why the ECG is your friend in the first few hours!
🤔 Clinical application:
Given this timeline, why do we say "time is muscle" in STEMI? What's the target door-to-balloon time and why?
