About Kawasaki Disease

Kawasaki disease is an acute systemic vasculitis primarily affecting children under 5 years of age, characterized by prolonged fever and mucocutaneous inflammation. It is the leading cause of acquired heart disease in children in developed countries, with coronary artery aneurysms being the most serious complication.

Pathophysiology

The exact etiology remains unknown, but Kawasaki disease involves immune-mediated inflammation of medium-sized arteries, particularly coronary arteries. The inflammatory process leads to endothelial damage, smooth muscle proliferation, and potential aneurysm formation through activation of T-cells, macrophages, and cytokine release.

Clinical Reasoning

Diagnosis relies on clinical criteria including fever >5 days plus at least 4 of 5 mucocutaneous features: conjunctival injection, oral changes, rash, extremity changes, and lymphadenopathy. Early recognition and treatment with IVIG and aspirin within 10 days of fever onset significantly reduces the risk of coronary artery complications from 25% to less than 5%.
