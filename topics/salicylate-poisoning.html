About Salicylate Poisoning

Salicylate poisoning occurs from overdose of aspirin or other salicylate-containing medications, causing a complex toxidrome affecting multiple organ systems. It presents with altered mental status, tinnitus, hyperthermia, and mixed acid-base disorders, with severity correlating to serum salicylate levels and clinical presentation.

Pathophysiology

Salicylates uncouple oxidative phosphorylation in mitochondria, leading to increased oxygen consumption, heat production, and metabolic acidosis. They also stimulate the respiratory center causing initial respiratory alkalosis, interfere with cellular glucose utilization despite normal serum glucose, and disrupt the Krebs cycle leading to lactate and ketoacid accumulation.

Clinical Reasoning

Diagnosis requires high clinical suspicion as symptoms can mimic other conditions like sepsis or diabetic ketoacidosis, with the classic triad of altered mental status, hyperthermia, and mixed acid-base disorder being key. Serum salicylate levels guide treatment intensity, but clinical presentation is more important than levels alone, as tissue toxicity can persist even with declining serum concentrations due to delayed cellular clearance.
