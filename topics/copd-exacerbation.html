About Copd Exacerbation

COPD exacerbation is an acute worsening of respiratory symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variations in a patient with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. These episodes are characterized by increased dyspnea, cough, and sputum production that require immediate medical intervention and often hospitalization.

Pathophysiology

Exacerbations occur due to increased airway inflammation, often triggered by bacterial or viral respiratory infections, air pollution, or other environmental factors. The inflammatory cascade leads to further bronchoconstriction, increased mucus production, and worsening ventilation-perfusion mismatch, resulting in hypoxemia and potential hypercapnia.

Clinical Reasoning

Early recognition involves assessing the severity of symptoms, arterial blood gas analysis, and chest imaging to rule out complications like pneumonia or pneumothorax. Treatment typically includes bronchodilators, systemic corticosteroids, oxygen therapy, and antibiotics if bacterial infection is suspected, with careful monitoring for respiratory failure requiring non-invasive or mechanical ventilation.
