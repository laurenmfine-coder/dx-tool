About Asthma Exacerbation

Asthma exacerbation is an acute worsening of asthma symptoms characterized by increased airway inflammation, bronchospasm, and mucus production leading to severe breathing difficulty. It represents a medical emergency requiring prompt recognition and treatment to prevent respiratory failure and potential death.

Pathophysiology

During an exacerbation, inflammatory mediators cause intense bronchial smooth muscle contraction, mucosal edema, and excessive mucus secretion, resulting in severe airway obstruction. The combination of reduced airway caliber and increased airway resistance leads to impaired gas exchange, ventilation-perfusion mismatch, and potential respiratory acidosis.

Clinical Reasoning

Early recognition involves assessing peak flow measurements, oxygen saturation, and clinical signs such as accessory muscle use, inability to speak in full sentences, and altered mental status. Treatment prioritizes rapid bronchodilation with beta-agonists, systemic corticosteroids to reduce inflammation, and oxygen therapy while monitoring for signs of impending respiratory failure requiring mechanical ventilation.
