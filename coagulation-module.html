The Coagulation Cascade

Understanding the cascade helps you predict which labs are affected and which drugs work where

The Cascade (Simplified)

Intrinsic Pathway

Measured by PTT

XII → XIIa
↓
XI → XIa
↓
IX → IXa
↓
VIII + IXa
↓

Heparin works here

Measured by PT/INR

Tissue Factor
↓
VII → VIIa
↓
→ Common Pathway
Warfarin affects this first

Common Pathway

Both PT & PTT affected

↓
V + Xa + Ca²⁺
↓

↓

↓

DOACs work here (Xa or IIa)

Lab Patterns & Differential

Pattern PT/INR PTT Think...
Isolated ↑PT ↑ N Factor VII deficiency, early warfarin, early liver disease
Isolated ↑PTT N ↑ Heparin, Hemophilia A/B, vWD, Factor XI/XII def, Lupus anticoagulant
Both ↑PT & ↑PTT ↑ ↑ Common pathway (X, V, II, I), DIC, liver failure, supratherapeutic warfarin, vitamin K def
Normal PT & PTT but bleeding N N Platelet disorder, Factor XIII def, mild vWD, fibrinolysis

The Mixing Study: Factor Deficiency vs Inhibitor

When PTT is elevated, mix patient plasma 1:1 with normal plasma:

PTT Corrects

= Factor DEFICIENCY

Normal plasma provides the missing factor

Think: Hemophilia, other factor deficiencies

PTT Does NOT Correct

= INHIBITOR present

Something is blocking the cascade

Think: Lupus anticoagulant, Factor VIII inhibitor, heparin

Anticoagulant Mechanisms & Monitoring

Heparin (UFH)

Mechanism: Potentiates Antithrombin III → Inhibits IIa, Xa, IXa
Monitoring: PTT (target 1.5-2.5x normal)
Reversal: Protamine sulfate

LMWH (Enoxaparin)

Mechanism: Mainly anti-Xa (less anti-IIa than UFH)
Monitoring: Usually none; Anti-Xa if needed
Reversal: Protamine (partial)

Warfarin

Mechanism: Blocks vitamin K epoxide reductase → ↓II, VII, IX, X, Protein C & S
Monitoring: PT/INR
Reversal: Vitamin K, FFP, PCC

DOACs

Xa inhibitors: Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Edoxaban
IIa inhibitor: Dabigatran
Reversal: Andexanet alfa (Xa), Idarucizumab (dabigatran)
⚠️ Warfarin Skin Necrosis: Protein C has shortest half-life → drops first → transient hypercoagulability → skin necrosis. Bridge with heparin!

DIC: When Everything Goes Wrong

Trigger → Massive coagulation activation → Consume all factors & platelets → Bleeding + Thrombosis

Causes (STOP Making Thrombin)

  • • Sepsis
  • • Trauma / burns
  • • Obstetric (abruption, amniotic embolism)
  • • Pancreatitis
  • • Malignancy (esp. APL, adenocarcinoma)
  • • Transfusion reaction

Lab Findings

  • • ↑PT, ↑PTT (consuming factors)
  • • ↓Platelets (consumed)
  • • ↓Fibrinogen (consumed)
  • • ↑D-dimer (fibrin breakdown)
  • • Schistocytes on smear (microthrombi shear RBCs)
Treatment: Treat underlying cause! Supportive: platelets, FFP, cryoprecipitate (fibrinogen) as needed.
