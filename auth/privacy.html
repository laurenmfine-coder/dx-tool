Privacy & Data Practices

Last updated: April 2026

ReasonDx is a clinical reasoning education platform. To help you learn — and to make the platform better — we collect information about how you interact with it. This page explains what we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. We've tried to write this in plain English.

1. What we collect

Account information

If you create an account or give us your email, we store your email address, any name or institution you provide, your profession profile (medical student, resident, pharmacy student, etc.), and any cohort or program you've been invited to.

Learning activity

As you work through the platform, we record which cases you start and finish, how long you spend in each phase of a case, the answers you select, the questions you ask the AI attending, and which reasoning tools you use (fingerprint analysis, bias analysis, differential builder, and others).

Free-text responses

When you type responses to the AI attending, questions during patient interviews, or reasoning explanations, we store the text of those responses alongside the rest of your case data. The next section explains what we do with that text.

Session and technical data

We record an anonymous session ID, the pages you visit, the approximate time you spend on each page, your browser and device type, your general geographic region (from IP address — not precise location), and referrer information if you arrived from another site.

2. How we analyze your reasoning

This is the section most platforms don't explain clearly. ReasonDx analyzes patterns in your reasoning to give you feedback and help you improve. We think you should know exactly what that means.

Passive linguistic analysis

When you type free-text responses, we automatically analyze them for:

  • Hedging and confidence language — words like "might," "probably," "definitely," which we use to estimate how confident you are in your reasoning.
  • Question type — whether your questions to the AI attending are open-ended, closed, leading, or clarifying.
  • Response latency — how quickly you reply, which we use as one signal of cognitive load.
  • Empathy and rapport signals — communication patterns in how you talk to simulated patients.
  • Emotional tone — the affective content of your language (calm, concerned, alarmed), which research suggests can influence diagnostic accuracy.
  • Cue utilization — whether you followed up on clinical cues the patient mentioned.

Bias detection

We look for patterns in your decisions that research has linked to common cognitive biases in clinical reasoning — anchoring, premature closure, availability bias, confirmation bias, framing effects, and base rate neglect. Our feedback is meant as a learning tool, not a clinical judgment about you.

Clinical reasoning fingerprint

Across multiple cases, we build a picture of your reasoning patterns — where you tend to pause, which diagnostic frameworks you favor, how your accuracy varies across types of cases, and how your confidence tracks with correctness. This is what powers the fingerprint analysis you can view on your dashboard.

Plain version: when you write answers to the AI attending, we don't just store the text — we analyze it for signals about how you're thinking. That's the core of what makes ReasonDx work as an education platform, but it's also the thing most likely to surprise you, so we want to be upfront about it.

3. How we use your data

  • To deliver the platform — saving your progress, showing your dashboard, giving you personalized feedback.
  • To improve the platform — seeing which cases are working, where students get stuck, which features are used, and fixing problems.
  • To send you relevant messages — if you've given us your email, we may send occasional platform updates, reminders, or educational content. You can unsubscribe anytime.
  • To support institutions you're affiliated with — if you join a program or cohort, we share aggregate performance summaries with that program's faculty (not individual reasoning text unless explicitly part of a coursework submission).

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share identifiable learning data with advertisers.

4. Research and platform improvement

ReasonDx is an education platform, and we also intend to contribute to the field of clinical reasoning education through research. We distinguish between two types of research use, and they're handled very differently:

General platform improvement (covered by this notice)

We may analyze aggregated, de-identified patterns from platform use to improve how the platform works — which case types need better scaffolding, which reasoning tools actually help, how different professions learn. This is covered by your agreement to these terms.

Formal research studies (separate consent required)

If we want to use your data in a formal research study intended for publication, that requires a separate process: Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval of the specific study, and a separate consent form that you would sign explicitly for that study. Your decision to use ReasonDx does not enroll you in any research study. If you're ever approached about a formal study, it will be clearly labeled as such and will require its own, specific consent — which you're free to decline without affecting your use of the platform.

In short: general platform improvement happens under this notice; any formal research study will come with its own explicit consent form.

5. Third parties

We use a small number of third-party services to run ReasonDx:

  • Supabase — stores your account data and learning activity.
  • Cloudflare — hosts our backend functions and workers, including email delivery and the AI attending infrastructure.
  • Anthropic — provides the AI model that powers the AI attending. Your free-text responses are sent to Anthropic's API to generate replies. Anthropic does not use data sent through the API to train its models by default; see Anthropic's privacy policy.
  • Google Analytics — aggregate traffic analytics. You can opt out via browser settings or a Google Analytics opt-out extension.
  • Loops — email delivery for platform messages, if you've given us your email.
  • Stripe — payment processing for paid plans, if applicable. We do not see or store your full payment card details.
  • Vercel — website hosting and edge delivery.

Each of these providers has their own privacy policy. We only share with them what's needed for them to do their job.

6. How long we keep data

We keep your account and learning data for as long as your account is active, and for a reasonable period after — typically up to 2 years — to support longitudinal feedback and account recovery. You can request earlier deletion at any time (see your rights below). Aggregated, de-identified analytics may be kept longer because they can no longer be traced to you.

7. Your choices and rights

Using ReasonDx without an account

You can browse cases and use many features without creating an account. In that case, we only have anonymous session data (no email, no name) stored locally in your browser and associated with an anonymous session ID on our servers.

Requesting your data

You can ask us for a copy of the data we hold about you. We'll send it to you in a portable format within a reasonable time.

Requesting deletion

You can ask us to delete your account and associated data. We'll honor the request, with two caveats: (1) we may retain aggregated, de-identified data that can no longer be linked to you, and (2) if you're part of a formal research study with its own consent, that study's data handling is governed by its specific IRB protocol.

Correcting your information

You can update your profile details directly in your account settings, or ask us to make corrections.

Opting out of optional communications

Every email we send you includes an unsubscribe link. Unsubscribing from platform messages doesn't delete your account.

If you're in the EU/UK or California

You have specific rights under GDPR (EU/UK) and the CCPA (California). We honor these rights globally as a matter of policy — data access, deletion, correction, and portability are available to all users regardless of location. Contact us to exercise them.

8. Security

We use industry-standard security practices — encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS), encrypted-at-rest databases, access controls on our backend, and regular review of our infrastructure. No system is perfectly secure, and we'll notify affected users if a breach involving personal data ever occurs.

9. Contact us

Questions, requests, or concerns

Email reasondxeducation@gmail.com for anything related to your data — requesting a copy, correction, deletion, or just a question about how something works. We aim to respond within 10 business days.
