Tetracyclines

30S Ribosome Inhibitors - Broad Spectrum Classics

30S Ribosome Inhibitor Bacteriostatic Broad Spectrum Chelates Cations

📋 Drug Class Overview

Doxycycline

Most commonly used. Once/twice daily. Food doesn't affect absorption much. Fecal/hepatic elimination. Safe in renal failure!

Minocycline

Best CNS penetration. Used for acne, MRSA. Vestibular toxicity (dizziness). Can cause blue-gray skin discoloration.

Tetracycline

Original drug. QID dosing. Must take on empty stomach. Rarely used now due to inconvenience.

Tigecycline

Glycylcycline (modified tetracycline). IV only. Broader spectrum including MRSA, VRE, MDR Gram-negatives. NOT for bloodstream infections.

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Tetracyclines inhibit protein synthesis by binding to the 30S ribosomal subunit:

  1. Enter bacteria via porin channels and active transport
  2. Bind to 30S ribosome at the A (aminoacyl) site
  3. Block attachment of aminoacyl-tRNA to the mRNA-ribosome complex
  4. Prevent addition of new amino acids to the growing peptide chain
  5. Result: Bacteriostatic
Tetracyclines bind 30S ribosome (like aminoglycosides), but are bacterioSTATIC (unlike aminoglycosides which are bactericidal). Remember: Aminoglycosides cause IRREVERSIBLE binding and membrane damage → cidal. Tetracyclines have REVERSIBLE binding → static.

Cation chelation is CRITICAL: Tetracyclines bind divalent/trivalent cations (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺, Al³⁺) forming insoluble complexes. This causes: (1) ↓ absorption with dairy, antacids, iron supplements - separate by 2 hours; (2) Deposition in teeth and bones (binds calcium).

⚠️ Adverse Effects

Major Toxicities

  • Teeth discoloration: Binds calcium in developing teeth → yellow-brown staining. CONTRAINDICATED in children <8 years and pregnancy (after 1st trimester).
  • Bone growth inhibition: Deposits in bone, may retard growth in fetus/children.
  • Photosensitivity: Severe sunburn with UV exposure. Warn patients!
  • GI upset: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea. Esophageal ulceration if taken lying down without water.
  • Vestibular toxicity: Dizziness, vertigo (especially minocycline)
  • Hepatotoxicity: Rare, usually with high IV doses or pregnancy
  • Pseudotumor cerebri: Benign intracranial hypertension (rare, especially with vitamin A)
  • Fanconi syndrome: With expired tetracycline (degradation products are nephrotoxic)

Drug Interactions

  • Antacids, dairy, iron: ↓ absorption (chelation)
  • Warfarin: May ↑ INR (displaced from protein binding)
  • Oral contraceptives: May ↓ effectiveness (controversial, but warn patients)
  • Isotretinoin: Both cause pseudotumor cerebri - avoid combination
  • Digoxin: ↑ levels (kills gut flora that metabolize digoxin)

🦠 Tigecycline - The Glycylcycline

Tigecycline is a modified tetracycline with broader spectrum:

  • Active against MRSA, VRE, ESBL-producers, Acinetobacter
  • Evades common tetracycline resistance mechanisms (efflux pumps, ribosomal protection)
  • IV only, large volume of distribution
  • NOT for bloodstream infections - FDA warning of increased mortality
  • Used for complicated skin/intra-abdominal infections, CAP
  • Significant nausea/vomiting

🛡️ Resistance Mechanisms

  • Efflux pumps: Most common. Tet(A), Tet(B), etc. pump drug out of cell
  • Ribosomal protection proteins: Tet(M), Tet(O) dislodge tetracycline from ribosome
  • Enzymatic inactivation: Rare (Tet(X) oxygenase)

Tigecycline overcomes many of these mechanisms but resistance is emerging.

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Mechanism: Bind 30S ribosome → block aminoacyl-tRNA attachment → bacterioSTATIC
  • Spectrum: Very broad! Rickettsia (RMSF - DOC), Borrelia (Lyme), Chlamydia, Mycoplasma, Vibrio, MRSA, malaria prophylaxis
  • Doxycycline: Most used. BID, OK with food, safe in renal failure, NO cation interaction issues
  • Cation chelation: Avoid dairy, antacids, iron (↓ absorption). Binds calcium → teeth/bone deposition.
  • CONTRAINDICATED: Pregnancy (2nd/3rd trimester), children <8 years (teeth staining). EXCEPTION: RMSF - use regardless of age!
  • Toxicities: Photosensitivity, GI upset, esophageal ulcers, vestibular (minocycline)
  • Tigecycline: Glycylcycline, IV only, MRSA/VRE/MDR-GNR activity, NOT for bacteremia
