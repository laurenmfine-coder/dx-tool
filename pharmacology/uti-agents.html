UTI-Specific Agents

Nitrofurantoin & Fosfomycin — First-Line for Uncomplicated Cystitis

📋 Overview

Nitrofurantoin and fosfomycin are first-line agents for uncomplicated cystitis (acute lower UTI). They concentrate in urine, have minimal systemic absorption, and preserve broader-spectrum antibiotics.

IDSA Guidelines: Nitrofurantoin, TMP-SMX, and fosfomycin are preferred for uncomplicated cystitis. Fluoroquinolones should be reserved for complicated UTIs due to resistance concerns and side effects.
💊 Nitrofurantoin (Macrobid, Macrodantin)

Nitrofurantoin

  • Mechanism: Reduced by bacterial enzymes → reactive intermediates damage DNA, ribosomes, and other macromolecules
  • Spectrum: E. coli (most UTIs), Enterococcus, Staph saprophyticus, Klebsiella. NOT Proteus, Pseudomonas, Serratia.
  • Dosing: Macrobid 100mg BID × 5 days (uncomplicated cystitis)
  • Advantage: Low resistance rates, concentrates in urine, minimal collateral damage to gut flora
⚠️ Nitrofurantoin Contraindications
  • CrCl less than 30 mL/min — inadequate urine concentration, increased toxicity
  • Pyelonephritis — doesn't achieve adequate tissue levels
  • G6PD deficiency — risk of hemolytic anemia
  • Pregnancy at term (38-42 weeks) — risk of neonatal hemolysis

Toxicities

  • Pulmonary toxicity: Acute (hypersensitivity pneumonitis) or chronic (pulmonary fibrosis with long-term use)
  • Peripheral neuropathy: Especially with renal impairment or prolonged use
  • Hepatotoxicity: Rare, usually with chronic use
  • GI upset: Take with food (also improves absorption)
💊 Fosfomycin (Monurol)

Fosfomycin

  • Mechanism: Inhibits MurA (first step in peptidoglycan synthesis) — unique target
  • Spectrum: E. coli (including many ESBL), Enterococcus (including VRE), most Gram-negatives
  • Dosing: Single 3g oral dose — that's it!
  • Advantage: Single dose = excellent compliance; unique mechanism = low cross-resistance
Single Dose Convenience: Fosfomycin 3g × 1 dose is the entire treatment course for uncomplicated cystitis. Ideal for patients with compliance concerns.

Considerations

  • Efficacy: Slightly less effective than 5-day nitrofurantoin, but convenience often outweighs this
  • Resistance: Generally low; may be active against some MDR organisms
  • Toxicity: GI upset (diarrhea), headache — generally well-tolerated
  • NOT for pyelonephritis — same limitation as nitrofurantoin

🎯 When to Use
  • Uncomplicated cystitis — first-line options
  • Recurrent UTI prophylaxis — nitrofurantoin low-dose nightly
  • MDR organisms — fosfomycin often retains activity
  • NOT for: Pyelonephritis, complicated UTI, prostatitis (poor tissue penetration)
📝 High-Yield Summary
  • Nitrofurantoin: 100mg BID × 5 days; avoid if CrCl less than 30 or pyelonephritis
  • Nitrofurantoin toxicity: Pulmonary fibrosis (chronic), peripheral neuropathy
  • Fosfomycin: Single 3g dose; excellent compliance
  • Both: First-line for uncomplicated cystitis; NOT for pyelonephritis
  • Advantage: Minimal resistance, preserves broad-spectrum antibiotics
