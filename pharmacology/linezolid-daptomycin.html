Linezolid & Daptomycin

Last-Line Agents for Resistant Gram-Positives

🎯 Overview

These agents are reserved for serious infections caused by resistant Gram-positive organisms:

  • MRSA (especially when vancomycin fails or isn't tolerated)
  • VRE (vancomycin-resistant enterococci)
  • VISA/VRSA (vancomycin-intermediate/resistant S. aureus)

🔵 Linezolid (Zyvox)

Mechanism of Action

Oxazolidinone class - unique mechanism!

  • Binds to 23S rRNA of the 50S ribosomal subunit
  • Prevents formation of the 70S initiation complex
  • Blocks protein synthesis at the EARLIEST step (before first peptide bond)
  • Bacteriostatic against most organisms
Unique target! Unlike other protein synthesis inhibitors (which block elongation), linezolid blocks INITIATION of protein synthesis. This novel mechanism means no cross-resistance with other antibiotics.

Spectrum & Clinical Uses

OrganismActivityNotes
MRSA✓ ExcellentAlternative to vancomycin. BETTER for pneumonia!
VRE✓ ExcellentOne of few options for VRE infections
Streptococci✓ ExcellentIncluding penicillin-resistant pneumococci
MSSA✓ ActiveBut β-lactams preferred for MSSA
Gram-negatives✗ NoneNo activity!

Key Clinical Indications

  • MRSA pneumonia: Linezolid may be SUPERIOR to vancomycin (better lung penetration)
  • VRE infections: Bacteremia, UTI, soft tissue
  • Skin/soft tissue infections: MRSA complicated infections
  • Osteomyelitis: Good bone penetration, oral option for step-down
100% oral bioavailability! IV and PO doses are the same. This makes linezolid excellent for oral step-down therapy (e.g., finishing osteomyelitis treatment at home).

⚠️ Linezolid Toxicities

Major Toxicities - Often Limit Long-Term Use

  • Myelosuppression: THROMBOCYTOPENIA (most common, dose-related), anemia, neutropenia. Monitor CBC weekly! Usually reversible.
  • Peripheral neuropathy: With prolonged use (>2 weeks). May be IRREVERSIBLE!
  • Optic neuropathy: Rare but serious. Vision changes = stop drug!
  • Lactic acidosis: Rare, mitochondrial toxicity
  • Serotonin syndrome: MAO inhibitor activity (see below)

MAO Inhibition - Critical Drug Interactions!

Linezolid is a reversible MAO inhibitor. This causes two major concerns:

InteractionEffectManagement
Serotonergic drugs SEROTONIN SYNDROME Avoid SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs, meperidine, tramadol if possible
Tyramine-rich foods Hypertensive crisis Limit aged cheese, wine, cured meats (less strict than irreversible MAOIs)
Sympathomimetics ↑ BP Use with caution
Board favorite: Patient on linezolid develops confusion, hyperthermia, tremor, and rigidity → think SEROTONIN SYNDROME from drug interaction (probably with an antidepressant).

🟢 Daptomycin (Cubicin)

Mechanism of Action

Lipopeptide antibiotic - unique mechanism!

  • Inserts into bacterial cell membrane in a Ca²⁺-dependent manner
  • Forms pores → rapid depolarization → ion leakage
  • Leads to cell death WITHOUT cell lysis (no inflammatory LPS release)
  • Bactericidal and concentration-dependent

Spectrum & Clinical Uses

OrganismActivityNotes
MRSA✓ BactericidalExcellent for bacteremia, endocarditis
VRE✓ ActiveHigher doses needed (8-10 mg/kg)
VISA/VRSA✓ Usually activeOptions limited for these
Streptococci✓ ActiveIncluding penicillin-resistant
Gram-negatives✗ NoneCannot penetrate outer membrane

Key Clinical Indications

  • S. aureus bacteremia: Drug of choice alternative to vancomycin
  • Right-sided endocarditis: FDA-approved
  • Complicated skin/soft tissue infections
  • VRE infections: Use higher doses (8-10 mg/kg)

⚠️ DO NOT USE FOR PNEUMONIA!

Daptomycin is INACTIVATED BY PULMONARY SURFACTANT. Surfactant sequesters the drug and prevents membrane insertion. For MRSA pneumonia, use vancomycin or linezolid instead!

⚠️ Daptomycin Toxicities

Major Toxicity: Myopathy

  • Skeletal muscle toxicity: Myalgias, weakness, elevated CK
  • Monitor CK weekly (or more often if symptoms)
  • Hold statins while on daptomycin (additive myopathy risk)
  • Usually reversible if caught early

Other Considerations

  • Eosinophilic pneumonia: Rare but reported (ironic given it doesn't work for pneumonia)
  • Dose adjustment: Required in renal impairment (renally eliminated)
  • IV only: Not absorbed orally

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Both: Reserved for MRSA, VRE, resistant Gram-positives. No Gram-negative activity.
  • Linezolid: Oxazolidinone, blocks 70S initiation complex. 100% oral bioavailable!
  • Linezolid toxicity: Thrombocytopenia (#1), peripheral neuropathy, serotonin syndrome (MAO inhibitor)
  • Linezolid advantage: WORKS for pneumonia (better than vanco for MRSA pneumonia)
  • Daptomycin: Lipopeptide, membrane depolarization. Bactericidal, concentration-dependent.
  • Daptomycin toxicity: Myopathy - monitor CK, hold statins
  • Daptomycin limitation: NOT FOR PNEUMONIA (inactivated by surfactant)!
