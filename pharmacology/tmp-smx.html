TMP-SMX (Bactrim)

Synergistic Folate Synthesis Inhibitors - PCP Prophylaxis Champion

Folate Synthesis Inhibitor Bactericidal (Combined) Broad Spectrum

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Sequential blockade of folate synthesis pathway at two different steps:

PABA (para-aminobenzoic acid)
        ↓
[Dihydropteroate Synthase] ← SULFAMETHOXAZOLE blocks
        ↓
Dihydrofolic Acid (DHF)
        ↓
[Dihydrofolate Reductase] ← TRIMETHOPRIM blocks
        ↓
Tetrahydrofolic Acid (THF) → DNA synthesis
Why doesn't TMP-SMX harm humans? Humans get folate from diet (we don't synthesize it), and human DHFR has much lower affinity for trimethoprim than bacterial DHFR (selective toxicity). Bacteria MUST synthesize folate - they can't take it up from the environment.

⚠️ Adverse Effects

Major Toxicities

  • Hypersensitivity (sulfa allergy): Rash, fever, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, TEN. More common in HIV patients!
  • Hyperkalemia: TMP blocks ENaC (like amiloride) → ↓ K⁺ secretion. Monitor K⁺, especially with ACEi/ARB.
  • Bone marrow suppression: Megaloblastic anemia (folate deficiency), leukopenia, thrombocytopenia. Give leucovorin (folinic acid) if severe.
  • Increased creatinine: TMP inhibits creatinine secretion → ↑ Cr (not true renal injury). Doesn't affect GFR.
  • Crystalluria: SMX can precipitate in urine (acidic pH). Maintain hydration.
  • Kernicterus: SMX displaces bilirubin from albumin → avoid in neonates and late pregnancy
  • Photosensitivity: Sun sensitivity with sulfonamides

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin: ↑ INR (SMX inhibits CYP2C9, displaces from albumin) - major interaction!
  • Methotrexate: ↑ toxicity (both inhibit folate pathway) - bone marrow suppression
  • Phenytoin: ↑ levels (CYP2C9 inhibition)
  • ACEi/ARB/K⁺-sparing diuretics: Additive hyperkalemia risk
  • Sulfonylureas: ↑ hypoglycemia risk
TMP causes hyperkalemia by blocking ENaC channels in the collecting duct (same mechanism as amiloride/triamterene). This is especially dangerous when combined with ACE inhibitors, ARBs, or in patients with renal impairment.

💊 Sulfa Allergy Considerations

Sulfonamide antibiotics (like SMX) can cause hypersensitivity reactions. Important distinctions:

  • Sulfonamide ANTIBIOTICS (SMX, sulfadiazine) have the arylamine group that causes allergies
  • Non-antibiotic sulfonamides (furosemide, thiazides, sulfonylureas, celecoxib) have different structure - cross-reactivity is RARE
  • Most patients labeled "sulfa allergic" can safely receive non-antibiotic sulfonamides
In HIV patients who need PCP prophylaxis but have mild sulfa allergy, consider desensitization protocol. Alternatives: dapsone, atovaquone, or pentamidine (but TMP-SMX is most effective and covers Toxoplasma).

⚠️ Adverse Effects

Major Toxicities

  • Hypersensitivity (sulfa allergy): Rash, fever, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, TEN. More common in HIV patients! Cross-reactivity with other sulfonamides.
  • Bone marrow suppression: Megaloblastic anemia (folate deficiency), leukopenia, thrombocytopenia. Give folinic acid (leucovorin) for severe cases.
  • Hyperkalemia: Trimethoprim blocks ENaC channel in collecting duct (like amiloride). Monitor K⁺!
  • Nephrotoxicity: Crystalluria (keep hydrated), interstitial nephritis, increased creatinine (TMP blocks tubular secretion)
  • Kernicterus: Sulfonamides displace bilirubin from albumin. Avoid in neonates and late pregnancy.
  • Photosensitivity: Sun exposure → rash
  • GI upset: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin: ↑ INR (SMX inhibits CYP2C9, displaces from albumin)
  • Methotrexate: ↑ toxicity (both affect folate; decreased renal excretion)
  • Phenytoin: ↑ levels (CYP2C9 inhibition)
  • ACE inhibitors/ARBs/K⁺-sparing diuretics: Additive hyperkalemia risk
  • Sulfonylureas: ↑ hypoglycemia risk
Trimethoprim causes hyperkalemia by blocking the epithelial sodium channel (ENaC) in the collecting duct - same mechanism as potassium-sparing diuretics like amiloride. This is a commonly tested side effect!

🛡️ Resistance Mechanisms

  • Altered DHFR: Mutations in dihydrofolate reductase reduce TMP binding
  • Altered dihydropteroate synthase: Mutations reduce sulfonamide binding
  • Overproduction of PABA: Overwhelms competitive inhibition by sulfonamide
  • Decreased permeability: Reduced drug uptake

Resistance to TMP-SMX is increasing in E. coli - check local antibiograms before using for UTI!

💊 Dosing Forms

  • Single Strength (SS): TMP 80mg / SMX 400mg
  • Double Strength (DS): TMP 160mg / SMX 800mg
  • IV formulation: Available for severe infections (same dosing as oral)
  • Ratio: Always 1:5 (TMP:SMX)
