Fluoroquinolones

DNA Gyrase/Topoisomerase IV Inhibitors - Broad Spectrum with Serious Warnings

DNA Synthesis Inhibitor Bactericidal Concentration-Dependent Oral = IV bioavailability

📋 Drug Class Overview

Ciprofloxacin

Best Pseudomonas coverage. GI/GU infections, anthrax. Poor strep coverage!

Levofloxacin

"Respiratory quinolone" - good strep + atypicals. CAP, sinusitis, UTI.

Moxifloxacin

Best anaerobic coverage. CAP, skin infections. NO renal excretion → not for UTI!

Delafloxacin

Newest. MRSA activity! Acidic pH enhances activity (good for abscesses).

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

Fluoroquinolones inhibit bacterial DNA synthesis by targeting two essential enzymes:

  • DNA Gyrase (Topoisomerase II): Primary target in Gram-NEGATIVES. Relieves positive supercoiling ahead of replication fork.
  • Topoisomerase IV: Primary target in Gram-POSITIVES. Separates daughter chromosomes after replication.

By inhibiting these enzymes, FQs cause double-stranded DNA breaks → cell death (bactericidal).

Fluoroquinolones target DNA GYRASE (Gram-negatives) and Topoisomerase IV (Gram-positives). Remember: "Gyrase = Gram-negative" (both start with G). They cause DNA breaks → bactericidal.

⚠️ Critical Drug Interaction

Divalent/trivalent cations (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Al³⁺, Fe²⁺) chelate fluoroquinolones → ↓ absorption. Separate from antacids, dairy, supplements by 2 hours before or 6 hours after!

⚠️ Black Box Warnings & Toxicities

🚨 FDA Black Box Warnings

Tendinitis/Tendon Rupture

Especially Achilles tendon. Risk ↑ with age >60, corticosteroids, kidney/heart/lung transplant. Can occur during or after treatment. STOP drug if tendon pain!

Peripheral Neuropathy

May be irreversible! Paresthesias, pain, weakness. Can occur early. Risk ↑ in diabetics.

CNS Effects

Seizures, psychosis, confusion, anxiety, nightmares. Risk ↑ with renal impairment, NSAIDs, theophylline.

Aortic Aneurysm/Dissection

Avoid in patients with aortic aneurysm, Marfan syndrome, Ehlers-Danlos, or older age with HTN.

Other Important Toxicities

  • QT prolongation: Especially moxifloxacin! Avoid with other QT-prolonging drugs.
  • C. difficile: All antibiotics, but FQs are high risk
  • Cartilage damage: Avoid in children/pregnancy (animal studies show cartilage toxicity)
  • Photosensitivity: Sun exposure → severe sunburn
  • Hypoglycemia/hyperglycemia: In diabetics on sulfonylureas
Fluoroquinolone toxicities to remember: "Tendon ACHes" = Tendinopathy, Aortic problems, Cartilage damage (kids), CNS effects, Hypoglycemia. They damage collagen-containing tissues!

🛡️ Resistance Mechanisms

  • Target mutations: Mutations in DNA gyrase (gyrA, gyrB) or topoisomerase IV (parC, parE). Stepwise accumulation of mutations → increasing resistance.
  • Efflux pumps: Pump drug out of cell (e.g., NorA in S. aureus)
  • Plasmid-mediated (qnr genes): Low-level resistance, often with other resistance genes

Resistance is increasing! Particularly in E. coli UTIs, Neisseria gonorrhoeae (FQs no longer recommended for gonorrhea), and Salmonella.

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Mechanism: Inhibit DNA gyrase (Gram-neg) and topoisomerase IV (Gram-pos) → DNA breaks → bactericidal
  • PK advantage: Oral bioavailability ≈ IV! Excellent tissue penetration.
  • Spectrum: Cipro = best Pseudomonas (only oral option!), poor strep. Levo/Moxi = "respiratory" with strep + atypicals.
  • Moxi: Best anaerobe coverage, but NOT for UTI (hepatic elimination)
  • Black Box: Tendon rupture, neuropathy, CNS effects, aortic aneurysm
  • Avoid: Children (cartilage), pregnancy, with QT-prolonging drugs, if safer alternatives exist
  • Interaction: Cations (antacids, dairy) ↓ absorption - separate dosing!
