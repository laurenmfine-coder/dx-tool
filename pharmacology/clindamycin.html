Clindamycin

Anaerobe & Gram-Positive Coverage — C. diff Risk

Lincosamide50S RibosomeAnaerobesBone Penetration

📋 Overview

🔬 Mechanism

Binds 50S ribosomal subunit → inhibits protein synthesis (bacteriostatic). Same binding site as macrolides — potential cross-resistance.

📊 Spectrum

Gram-positives: Staph (including MRSA), Strep. Anaerobes: Bacteroides, Clostridium (not C. diff!). NO Gram-negative aerobes.

Board Pearl: Clindamycin has excellent bone penetration — used for osteomyelitis. Also suppresses toxin production — useful in necrotizing fasciitis, toxic shock.
💊 Clinical Uses

Major Indications

  • Skin/soft tissue infections — MRSA coverage, abscesses, cellulitis
  • Osteomyelitis — excellent bone penetration
  • Dental infections — anaerobic coverage
  • Aspiration pneumonia — anaerobic coverage
  • Pelvic/intra-abdominal infections — with Gram-negative coverage
  • Necrotizing fasciitis / Toxic shock — toxin suppression (add to beta-lactam)
  • Babesiosis — with quinine/atovaquone
  • Toxoplasmosis — alternative (with pyrimethamine)
  • PCP (alternative) — with primaquine
⚠️ Adverse Effects
🦠 C. difficile Colitis — Major Risk!
  • Clindamycin is classically associated with C. diff (though any antibiotic can cause it)
  • Disrupts normal gut flora → C. diff overgrowth
  • Watch for: watery diarrhea, abdominal pain, fever after starting clindamycin
  • If suspected: stop clindamycin, test for C. diff, treat with vancomycin PO or fidaxomicin

Other Side Effects

  • Diarrhea (common, even without C. diff)
  • Rash
  • Elevated LFTs
  • Metallic taste
🎯 Special Considerations

🦴 Osteomyelitis

Excellent bone penetration makes it ideal for bone/joint infections, especially MRSA osteomyelitis.

☠️ Toxin Suppression

Inhibits protein synthesis → reduces toxin production. Add to beta-lactam for necrotizing fasciitis, streptococcal/staphylococcal toxic shock.

🤰 Pregnancy

Category B — generally safe. Alternative to metronidazole for anaerobic coverage in pregnancy.

D-test: Inducible clindamycin resistance in MRSA. If erythromycin-resistant but clindamycin-sensitive, do D-test. If positive (D-shaped zone), clindamycin resistance can be induced — avoid using.
📝 High-Yield Summary
  • Mechanism: 50S ribosomal subunit inhibitor (bacteriostatic)
  • Spectrum: Gram-positives (including MRSA), anaerobes (not C. diff)
  • Key uses: SSTI, osteomyelitis, dental infections, aspiration PNA, toxin suppression
  • Major toxicity: C. difficile colitis — classic association
  • Special: Excellent bone penetration, suppresses toxin production
  • D-test: Check for inducible resistance before using for erythro-R MRSA
