Antivirals

Targeting Viral Replication at Every Step

🎯 Overview: Viral Life Cycle Targets

Antivirals work by targeting specific steps in viral replication:

  • Attachment/Entry: Enfuvirtide, maraviroc
  • Uncoating: Amantadine (obsolete for influenza)
  • Nucleic acid synthesis: Acyclovir, ganciclovir, antiretrovirals
  • Protein processing: Protease inhibitors
  • Assembly/Release: Neuraminidase inhibitors (oseltamivir)

🦠 Anti-Herpesvirus Agents

Acyclovir / Valacyclovir

MechanismGuanosine analog → requires VIRAL THYMIDINE KINASE (TK) for activation → incorporated into viral DNA → chain termination
SpectrumHSV-1, HSV-2, VZV. Less active against EBV, minimal CMV activity.
ValacyclovirProdrug of acyclovir - better oral bioavailability (55% vs 20%)
UsesGenital herpes (treatment & suppression), herpes labialis, varicella, zoster, HSV encephalitis (IV)
ToxicityCrystalline nephropathy (hydrate!), neurotoxicity (high doses/renal failure)
ResistanceTK mutations (can't activate drug) - seen in immunocompromised. Use foscarnet.
Why is acyclovir selective? It requires VIRAL thymidine kinase for initial phosphorylation. Human cells lack this enzyme, so acyclovir is only activated in infected cells. This is why it's so well-tolerated!

Ganciclovir / Valganciclovir

MechanismSimilar to acyclovir, but activated by CMV kinase (UL97) - doesn't need viral TK
SpectrumCMV (drug of choice), also active against HSV, VZV
ValganciclovirOral prodrug - 60% bioavailability (vs 5% for ganciclovir)
UsesCMV retinitis (HIV), CMV prophylaxis (transplant), CMV colitis/esophagitis
ToxicityBONE MARROW SUPPRESSION (neutropenia, thrombocytopenia) - dose-limiting!
⚠️ Ganciclovir causes significant myelosuppression! Monitor CBC regularly. Avoid with other myelotoxic drugs (zidovudine, TMP-SMX). This toxicity is why we don't use ganciclovir for HSV/VZV when acyclovir works.

Foscarnet

MechanismPyrophosphate analog → directly inhibits viral DNA polymerase (no activation needed)
SpectrumCMV, HSV, VZV - including acyclovir/ganciclovir-resistant strains
UsesAcyclovir-resistant HSV, ganciclovir-resistant CMV, second-line CMV
ToxicityNEPHROTOXICITY (dose-limiting), electrolyte abnormalities (↓Ca, ↓Mg, ↓K, ↓PO₃)
Foscarnet is the go-to for resistant herpesvirus infections because it doesn't require viral kinase activation. But watch those electrolytes and kidneys!

Cidofovir

MechanismCytosine analog - activated by CELLULAR kinases (not viral)
SpectrumBroad: CMV, HSV, VZV, adenovirus, HPV, poxviruses
UsesCMV retinitis (resistant), acyclovir-resistant HSV
ToxicitySEVERE NEPHROTOXICITY - must give with probenecid and IV saline

🦠 Anti-Influenza Agents

Neuraminidase Inhibitors

Drugs: Oseltamivir (Tamiflu - oral), Zanamivir (Relenza - inhaled), Peramivir (IV)

MechanismInhibit neuraminidase → prevent release of new virions from infected cells
SpectrumInfluenza A and B
UsesTreatment (within 48h of symptoms) and prophylaxis of influenza
EfficacyShortens illness by ~1-2 days if started early. Greater benefit in high-risk.
ToxicityOseltamivir: GI upset, rare neuropsychiatric (adolescents). Zanamivir: bronchospasm (avoid in asthma/COPD)
Start within 48 hours! Neuraminidase inhibitors work best when started early. But in hospitalized/high-risk patients, start even if >48h from symptom onset - may still provide benefit.

Baloxavir (Xofluza)

MechanismInhibits cap-dependent endonuclease → blocks viral mRNA synthesis
SpectrumInfluenza A and B
AdvantageSINGLE DOSE treatment
ConcernResistance emerges quickly, especially in children
Adamantanes (amantadine, rimantadine) are OBSOLETE for influenza! Widespread resistance (>99% of circulating strains). They block M2 protein (uncoating) but are no longer recommended. Amantadine is still used for Parkinson's disease.

🦠 Hepatitis Antivirals

Hepatitis C - Direct-Acting Antivirals (DAAs)

CURE RATES >95%! Revolutionary breakthrough in HCV treatment.

ClassSuffixTargetExamples
NS3/4A Protease Inhibitors-previrViral proteaseGlecaprevir, voxilaprevir
NS5A Inhibitors-asvirNS5A protein (replication)Ledipasvir, velpatasvir
NS5B Polymerase Inhibitors-buvirRNA polymeraseSofosbuvir

Common Regimens (8-12 weeks)

  • Epclusa (sofosbuvir/velpatasvir): Pan-genotypic, most commonly used
  • Mavyret (glecaprevir/pibrentasvir): Pan-genotypic, 8 weeks for treatment-naïve
  • Harvoni (ledipasvir/sofosbuvir): Genotype 1
HCV is now CURABLE! DAAs achieve sustained virologic response (SVR) in >95% of patients with minimal side effects. Treatment is typically 8-12 weeks of oral therapy. This is one of the greatest advances in infectious disease!

Quick Summary of ART Classes

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Acyclovir: HSV/VZV. Requires VIRAL TK for activation → selective toxicity. Nephrotoxicity (crystalluria).
  • Ganciclovir: CMV drug of choice. MYELOSUPPRESSION is dose-limiting.
  • Foscarnet: For resistant herpesvirus. Nephrotoxicity + electrolyte abnormalities.
  • Oseltamivir: Neuraminidase inhibitor for influenza. Start within 48h. GI upset.
  • HCV DAAs: >95% CURE RATE! Sofosbuvir-based regimens. 8-12 weeks oral therapy.
  • HBV: Entecavir or tenofovir first-line. Don't stop abruptly (flare risk).
  • HIV ART: 2 NRTIs + integrase inhibitor. Lifelong therapy. Goal = undetectable VL.
