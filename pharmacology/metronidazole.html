Metronidazole & Anaerobic Agents

Covering the Oxygen-Haters

🎯 Metronidazole (Flagyl)

Mechanism of Action

Metronidazole is a prodrug that requires activation by anaerobic organisms:

  1. Enters cell by passive diffusion
  2. Reduced by ferredoxin (or similar) in anaerobes → toxic free radicals
  3. Free radicals damage DNA → strand breaks → cell death
Why only anaerobes? The reduction step requires a low-redox environment. Aerobic organisms re-oxidize the drug before it can damage DNA. This selective activation makes metronidazole specifically toxic to anaerobes!

⚠️ Metronidazole Toxicities & Interactions

Major Toxicities

  • Disulfiram-like reaction: Severe nausea/vomiting with alcohol. Avoid alcohol during and 48h after therapy!
  • Metallic taste: Very common, unpleasant
  • Peripheral neuropathy: With prolonged use. May be irreversible!
  • CNS effects: Seizures, encephalopathy (rare, more with high doses)
  • Dark urine: Harmless (metabolite)

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin: ↑ INR (inhibits warfarin metabolism) - monitor closely!
  • Lithium: ↑ lithium levels - monitor
  • Alcohol: Disulfiram reaction (acetaldehyde accumulation)
"Metro makes me metallic and nauseous with wine" - metallic taste + disulfiram reaction are the classic side effects to remember!

💊 Other Anaerobic Coverage Options

Clindamycin

MechanismBinds 50S ribosome → inhibits protein synthesis (bacteriostatic)
SpectrumGram-positives (including MRSA), anaerobes ABOVE the diaphragm
GapPoor B. fragilis coverage (below diaphragm anaerobes)
UsesSkin infections, aspiration pneumonia, dental infections, toxin suppression (GAS)
Key toxicityC. DIFFICILE! - one of the highest-risk antibiotics for CDI
Clindamycin covers anaerobes ABOVE the diaphragm (mouth, lung). For intra-abdominal (BELOW diaphragm), use metronidazole - it covers B. fragilis better.

β-Lactam/β-Lactamase Inhibitor Combinations

These provide excellent anaerobic coverage as part of broad-spectrum activity:

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam: Excellent anaerobic coverage + Pseudomonas
  • Ampicillin-sulbactam: Good anaerobic coverage, no Pseudomonas
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate: Oral option, good anaerobic coverage

Carbapenems

Broadest spectrum including excellent anaerobic coverage:

  • Meropenem, imipenem, ertapenem: All cover B. fragilis and most anaerobes
  • Usually don't need to add metronidazole when using carbapenems

Moxifloxacin

The only fluoroquinolone with good anaerobic coverage:

  • Covers most anaerobes including B. fragilis
  • NOT for UTI (hepatic elimination, doesn't concentrate in urine)
  • QT prolongation risk

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Metronidazole mechanism: Prodrug activated by anaerobes → free radicals → DNA damage
  • Spectrum: Anaerobes (B. fragilis!), protozoa (Giardia, Entamoeba, Trichomonas), H. pylori
  • Key uses: BV, trichomoniasis, giardiasis, amebiasis, intra-abdominal infections, H. pylori
  • C. diff: Metronidazole is SECOND-LINE. Oral vancomycin or fidaxomicin preferred.
  • Toxicities: Disulfiram reaction (NO ALCOHOL!), metallic taste, peripheral neuropathy, ↑ INR with warfarin
  • Clindamycin: Above-diaphragm anaerobes + MRSA. HIGH CDI RISK!
  • Pip-tazo/carbapenems: Excellent anaerobic coverage built-in - often don't need to add metronidazole
