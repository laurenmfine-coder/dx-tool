Anti-Tuberculosis Drugs

RIPE Regimen — First-Line TB Treatment

RifampinIsoniazidPyrazinamideEthambutol

📋 Overview

TB requires multi-drug therapy for 6+ months to prevent resistance. Standard treatment uses four first-line drugs (RIPE) for initial 2 months, then two drugs (RI) for 4 months continuation.

🧠 RIPE Mnemonic

Rifampin + Isoniazid + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol

Why 4 drugs? Prevents emergence of resistance. Each drug has different mechanism — combined effect kills bacteria in different metabolic states.
💊 First-Line Drugs

🔴 Rifampin (R)

  • Mechanism: Inhibits bacterial DNA-dependent RNA polymerase
  • Key toxicity: CYP450 inducer (↓ warfarin, OCP, HIV meds, etc.)
  • Side effects: Orange/red body fluids (urine, tears, sweat), hepatotoxicity, flu-like syndrome
  • Pearl: Warn patients about orange discoloration — harmless but alarming!

🟡 Isoniazid (INH) (I)

  • Mechanism: Inhibits mycolic acid synthesis (cell wall)
  • Key toxicities: Hepatotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy (B6 deficiency), drug-induced lupus
  • Prevention: Give pyridoxine (B6) with INH to prevent neuropathy
  • Pearl: INH is metabolized by N-acetyltransferase — slow acetylators have more toxicity

🟢 Pyrazinamide (PZA) (P)

  • Mechanism: Disrupts membrane function; active in acidic environment (inside macrophages)
  • Key toxicities: Hepatotoxicity (most hepatotoxic of RIPE), hyperuricemia (gout)
  • Only used first 2 months — that's why it's most hepatotoxic phase
  • Pearl: PZA = Podagra (gout) — inhibits uric acid excretion

🔵 Ethambutol (E)

  • Mechanism: Inhibits arabinosyltransferase (cell wall arabinogalactan synthesis)
  • Key toxicity: Optic neuritis — red-green color blindness, decreased visual acuity
  • Monitoring: Baseline and monthly visual acuity and color vision tests
  • Pearl: Ethambutol = Eyes — remember the toxicity!

⚠️ Hepatotoxicity — All Three!
  • INH, Rifampin, and PZA are all hepatotoxic
  • Monitor LFTs at baseline and if symptoms develop
  • Stop drugs if ALT greater than 3× ULN with symptoms OR greater than 5× ULN asymptomatic
  • Risk factors: alcohol, viral hepatitis, older age
📅 Standard Treatment Regimen

Drug-Susceptible Pulmonary TB

  • Intensive phase (2 months): RIPE (all 4 drugs)
  • Continuation phase (4 months): RI (rifampin + INH only)
  • Total: 6 months minimum
  • Directly Observed Therapy (DOT): Recommended to ensure adherence

Latent TB (LTBI)

  • INH × 9 months — traditional regimen
  • Rifampin × 4 months — shorter, good for INH resistance
  • INH + Rifapentine × 3 months (12 weekly doses) — 3HP regimen
🔄 Drug Interactions
Rifampin — Potent CYP450 Inducer
  • Decreases levels of: warfarin, OCPs, HIV protease inhibitors, methadone, azoles, steroids
  • HIV patients: Use rifabutin instead (less CYP induction)
  • Women on OCPs: Need alternative contraception
📝 High-Yield Summary
  • RIPE: Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol
  • Rifampin: RNA polymerase inhibitor; CYP inducer; orange fluids
  • INH: Mycolic acid; hepatotoxicity + neuropathy (give B6)
  • PZA: Most hepatotoxic; hyperuricemia/gout
  • Ethambutol: Optic neuritis — monitor vision
  • Regimen: 2 months RIPE → 4 months RI (total 6 months)
