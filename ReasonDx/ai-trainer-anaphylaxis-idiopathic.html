❓ Idiopathic Anaphylaxis

Managing recurrent anaphylaxis without identifiable trigger
AnaphylaxisIdiopathicMastocytosis ~25 min
📋 Clinical Scenario
Patient Presentation

40-year-old woman with 4 episodes of anaphylaxis over 2 years. Extensive allergy testing negative. Reactions occur at random times with no food, drug, or environmental correlation. Baseline tryptase normal.

🎯 Key Learning Points
💡 Clinical Pearls
  • Diagnosis of exclusion after thorough workup
  • Rule out mastocytosis (baseline tryptase, consider bone marrow)
  • Consider hidden allergens: alpha-gal, galactooligosaccharides, seminal fluid
  • Prophylaxis with daily antihistamines ± prednisone for frequent episodes
  • Omalizumab effective for refractory idiopathic anaphylaxis
