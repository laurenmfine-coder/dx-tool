⚠️ Severe Drug Rash Evaluation

Recognizing and managing DRESS, SJS/TEN
DRESSSJS-TENSCARs ~25 min
📋 Clinical Scenario
Patient Presentation

55-year-old man started on allopurinol 6 weeks ago. Now presents with fever, diffuse morbilliform rash, facial edema, and elevated liver enzymes. Labs show eosinophilia (15%) and atypical lymphocytes.

🎯 Key Learning Points
💡 Clinical Pearls
  • DRESS: rash + fever + eosinophilia + organ involvement, 2-8 weeks after drug
  • SJS/TEN: mucosal involvement + skin detachment, 1-3 weeks after drug
  • Immediately stop culprit drug
  • DRESS: systemic steroids; SJS/TEN: supportive care, consider IVIG
  • Document allergy prominently - rechallenge contraindicated
