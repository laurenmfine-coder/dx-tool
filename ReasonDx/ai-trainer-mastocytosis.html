🔶 Systemic Mastocytosis Workup

Evaluating elevated tryptase and mast cell disease
MastocytosisTryptaseKIT ~30 min
📋 Clinical Scenario
Patient Presentation

48-year-old man with recurrent flushing, abdominal cramping, and 2 episodes of hypotension after bee stings. Baseline tryptase 28 ng/mL (normal <11.4). No skin lesions.

🎯 Key Learning Points
💡 Clinical Pearls
  • Baseline tryptase >20 ng/mL suggests mastocytosis (or HαT)
  • WHO criteria: major (BM aggregates) + 1 minor OR 3 minor criteria
  • Minor: abnormal mast cell morphology, CD25+, KIT D816V, tryptase >20
  • Bone marrow biopsy needed for definitive diagnosis
  • Higher anaphylaxis risk - carry 2 epinephrine autoinjectors
