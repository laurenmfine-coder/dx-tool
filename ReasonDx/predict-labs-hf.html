Case: Acute Decompensated Heart Failure
Cardiology

Chief Complaint: 68-year-old man with worsening shortness of breath and leg swelling for 1 week

History: History of ischemic cardiomyopathy (EF 25%), hypertension, diabetes. Reports progressive dyspnea, now occurring at rest. Three-pillow orthopnea, PND. Weight gain of 12 lbs over past week. Admits to eating salty foods at holiday party and ran out of furosemide 5 days ago.

Vitals: BP 148/92, HR 102, RR 24, SpO2 91% on RA, Temp 36.8°C

Physical Exam: Labored breathing, JVP elevated to angle of jaw, bilateral crackles to mid-lung, S3 gallop, 3+ pitting edema to knees bilaterally, cool extremities.

BNP--
Your Prediction

Actual
1,850 pg/mL
Why very high: BNP is released in response to ventricular wall stress (stretch). With EF 25% and acute volume overload, wall stress is markedly elevated. BNP >400 strongly suggests HF; >1000 indicates severe decompensation.
Sodium--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
131 mEq/L
Why low: Dilutional hyponatremia. In HF, low cardiac output triggers ADH release (water retention) + RAAS activation. Total body Na is HIGH, but water retention is proportionally greater. Hyponatremia is a poor prognostic marker in HF.
Creatinine--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
1.9 mg/dL
Why elevated: Cardiorenal syndrome. Low cardiac output = reduced renal perfusion. Venous congestion also impairs kidney function. Some rise is expected during aggressive diuresis (acceptable "permissive azotemia").
Troponin--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
0.08 ng/mL
Why mildly elevated: Chronic myocardial strain can cause mild troponin leak without acute MI. Also, high sensitivity troponins detect smaller amounts. Check for dynamic changes — stable mild elevation is "demand ischemia," not NSTEMI.

Your Prediction
--
Actual
11.2 g/dL
Why low: Anemia of chronic disease is common in HF. Causes: EPO resistance, chronic inflammation, hemodilution, iron deficiency from GI congestion. Anemia worsens HF symptoms and is an independent mortality predictor.

🎯 Key Takeaways
  • BNP correlates with severity: >1000 = severe HF
  • Hyponatremia = bad prognosis: Reflects neurohormonal activation
  • Cardiorenal syndrome: Low output + venous congestion impair kidneys
  • Troponin ≠ MI: Demand ischemia causes mild, stable elevation
  • Anemia is common: Multifactorial, worsens symptoms
