💊 NSAID Hypersensitivity

Classifying and managing NSAID reactions
NSAIDCross-reactivityCOX-1 ~20 min
📋 Clinical Scenario
Patient Presentation

38-year-old woman developed facial swelling and hives 2 hours after taking ibuprofen for headache. Has had similar reactions to aspirin and naproxen in the past. Tolerates acetaminophen without issue.

🎯 Key Learning Points
💡 Clinical Pearls
  • Cross-reactive = reacts to multiple NSAIDs (COX-1 mediated)
  • Single-drug = allergic to one NSAID only (IgE or T-cell)
  • NSAID-exacerbated respiratory disease (AERD) = asthma + polyps + NSAID
  • COX-2 selective agents (celecoxib) often tolerated in cross-reactive patients
  • Avoid all NSAIDs in AERD unless desensitized
