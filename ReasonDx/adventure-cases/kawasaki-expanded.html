Stage 1 of 8
The Persistent Fever
Mia, 3-year-old girl, has had high fever for 5 days (38.9-40°C) unresponsive to antipyretics. On exam: bilateral non-purulent conjunctival injection, cracked red lips, strawberry tongue, polymorphous rash on trunk, and swollen hands.
🔬 Pathophysiology
Unknown trigger → Medium-vessel vasculitis → Coronary artery inflammation → ANEURYSMS (15-25% untreated) → Peak age: 6mo-5yr
Does Mia meet criteria for Kawasaki Disease?
ANo - needs 7 days of fever
BNo - only 3 of 5 criteria
CYes - fever ≥5 days plus ≥4 of 5 features
DCannot diagnose without echo
Classic KD Criteria: Fever ≥5 days + ≥4 of 5: Conjunctivitis, Oral changes, Rash, Extremity changes, Lymphadenopathy. "CRASH and Burn" mnemonic. Mia has 4 criteria = CLASSIC KAWASAKI
Stage 2 of 8
Laboratory Workup
Labs: WBC 18K, Platelets 450K, ESR 85, CRP 12.5. Sterile pyuria on UA. ALT mildly elevated.
🎯 KD Lab Findings (Supportive, not diagnostic)
Elevated WBC, ESR/CRP (nearly universal), Thrombocytosis (week 2-3), Sterile pyuria, Elevated transaminases, Anemia
What is the MOST important next step?
ABlood cultures to rule out sepsis
BEchocardiogram to evaluate coronary arteries
CLumbar puncture for meningitis
DWait for platelets >500K
Echo timing: At diagnosis (baseline), 1-2 weeks, 4-6 weeks. Coronary Z-scores: Small: 2.5-5, Medium: 5-10, Giant: ≥10 or ≥8mm
Stage 3 of 8
First-Line Treatment
Echo shows mild coronary dilation (Z-score 2.8). Mia remains febrile at 39.2°C.
🔬 Why IVIG Works
IVIG 2g/kg → Anti-inflammatory modulation → ↓Vasculitis → Reduces aneurysm risk from 25% → 4-5%
What is standard first-line treatment?
AHigh-dose corticosteroids
BIVIG 1g/kg/day x 2 days + aspirin
CIVIG 2g/kg single infusion + aspirin
DInfliximab + aspirin
IVIG 2g/kg SINGLE dose over 10-12 hours + Aspirin: High-dose initially → Low-dose (3-5mg/kg) when afebrile. Treat within 10 days of fever onset!
Stage 4 of 8
Treatment Response
36 hours post-IVIG, fever has resolved. Mia is much improved. Parents ask: "How long does she need aspirin?"
How long should Mia remain on aspirin?
AStop when fever resolves
BHigh-dose for 2 weeks, then stop
CLow-dose when afebrile, continue minimum 6-8 weeks
DLow-dose for life
Duration by coronary status: No changes: 6-8 weeks. Transient dilation: until normalized. Small aneurysm: until regression. Giant aneurysm: indefinitely + anticoagulation.
Stage 5 of 8
Refractory Disease
Another patient, James, received IVIG yesterday but fever persists at 39.8°C (36 hours post-IVIG). CRP remains elevated.
⚠️ IVIG-Resistant KD
Definition: Fever ≥36 hours after IVIG. Risk factors: Age <12mo, male, low albumin, high CRP, coronary changes at presentation.
Treatment for IVIG-resistant KD?
ASecond IVIG 2g/kg +/- corticosteroids
BPlasmapheresis
CIncrease aspirin to 150mg/kg/day
DObserve 48 more hours
Refractory treatment: Repeat IVIG 2g/kg +/- IV methylprednisolone. Third-line: Infliximab, cyclosporine, anakinra. Don't delay - higher aneurysm risk!
Stage 6 of 8
Incomplete Kawasaki
8-month-old Emma has 7 days of fever with only conjunctival injection and lip cracking (2 of 5 criteria). Labs: CRP 8.5, ESR 72, albumin 2.9.
Approach to suspected incomplete KD?
ACannot diagnose without 4+ criteria
BAHA algorithm: ≥3 supplemental lab criteria OR positive echo → treat
CEmpiric antibiotics first
DOnly treat if aneurysm present
Supplemental lab criteria (need ≥3): Albumin ≤3.0, Anemia, ALT elevation, Platelets ≥450K (day 7+), WBC ≥15K, Sterile pyuria. Infants <6 months have HIGHEST aneurysm risk!
Stage 7 of 8
Long-term Follow-up
6 weeks later, Mia's echo shows normalized coronary arteries (Z-score 1.8). Parents ask about vaccines.
Which vaccines require special consideration after KD treatment?
AAll vaccines delayed 1 year
BMMR and varicella delayed 11 months after IVIG
CFlu vaccine contraindicated on aspirin
DNo modifications needed
Live vaccines (MMR, Varicella): Delay 11 months post-IVIG (antibody interference). Flu vaccine: RECOMMENDED while on aspirin (Reye syndrome prevention)!
Stage 8 of 8
Case Synthesis
📝 Kawasaki Disease Summary
DIAGNOSIS: Fever ≥5 days + ≥4 of 5 criteria ("CRASH and Burn") TREATMENT: IVIG 2g/kg single dose + Aspirin (within 10 days) REFRACTORY: Repeat IVIG +/- steroids +/- infliximab INCOMPLETE KD: Use AHA algorithm (≥3 supplemental labs or positive echo) FOLLOW-UP: Echo series, aspirin duration based on coronary status VACCINES: Delay MMR/varicella 11mo; GIVE flu vaccine
Which statement is INCORRECT?
AKD is the leading cause of acquired heart disease in children
BDesquamation occurs in convalescent phase (week 2-3)
CA negative echo rules out Kawasaki Disease
DExtreme irritability is common (aseptic meningitis)
Echo does NOT diagnose KD! It's a CLINICAL diagnosis. Normal echo doesn't rule out KD - it just means no coronary complications yet. When in doubt, TREAT!
