Scene 2: Understanding the Pathophysiology

This patient has secondary adrenal insufficiency from chronic steroid use, now in crisis. Let's understand why.

🔬 Why Chronic Steroids Cause Adrenal Suppression

  • Exogenous steroids suppress ACTH release from pituitary
  • Adrenal glands atrophy without ACTH stimulation
  • Takes 2-3 weeks of daily steroids (≥5mg prednisone) to suppress HPA axis
  • Recovery takes weeks to MONTHS after stopping
  • During stress, atrophied adrenals can't mount cortisol response → CRISIS

Scene 3: Emergency Treatment

You strongly suspect adrenal crisis. BP is now 68/40 despite 2L NS. What's your immediate management?

🚨 Adrenal Crisis Treatment Protocol

  1. Hydrocortisone 100mg IV bolus (has mineralocorticoid activity)
  2. Aggressive IV saline (often need 3-4L in first 24h)
  3. Dextrose for hypoglycemia
  4. Treat precipitant (infection, etc.)
  5. Then: Hydrocortisone 50-100mg IV q6-8h, taper as patient stabilizes

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Classic triad: Hypotension + Hyponatremia + Hypoglycemia (+ hyperkalemia in primary)
  • Precipitants: Infection, trauma, surgery, stopping steroids, any stress
  • TREAT FIRST, test later: Don't wait for labs - hydrocortisone 100mg IV STAT
  • Hydrocortisone preferred: Has mineralocorticoid activity (vs dexamethasone)
  • Chronic steroids: Taper slowly; HPA recovery takes up to 1 year
  • Stress dosing: All patients on chronic steroids need sick-day rules
  • Hyperpigmentation = primary AI only (elevated ACTH → MSH)
