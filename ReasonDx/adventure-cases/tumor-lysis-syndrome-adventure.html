Scene 2: Understanding the Pathophysiology

TLS occurs when rapid cell death releases intracellular contents into the bloodstream. Let's trace the cascade:

🔬 The TLS Cascade

  • Cell lysis → Release of K⁺, phosphorus, nucleic acids
  • ↑ Potassium → Cardiac arrhythmias (peaked T waves → wide QRS → VF)
  • ↑ Phosphorus → Binds calcium → ↓ Calcium (tetany, seizures, arrhythmias)
  • Nucleic acids → Purines → Uric acid → Crystallizes in renal tubules → AKI
  • Calcium-phosphate precipitation → Also causes AKI + soft tissue calcification

Cairo-Bishop Criteria for TLS

Laboratory TLS: ≥2 of the following within 3 days before or 7 days after chemo:

  • Uric acid ≥8 mg/dL or 25% ↑ from baseline
  • Potassium ≥6 mEq/L or 25% ↑ from baseline
  • Phosphorus ≥4.5 mg/dL (adults) or 25% ↑
  • Calcium ≤7 mg/dL or 25% ↓ from baseline

Clinical TLS: Laboratory TLS + ≥1 of: AKI, seizures, cardiac arrhythmia, death

Scene 3: Immediate Management

The ECG shows peaked T waves, prolonged PR, and widened QRS. You need to act fast. The K⁺ is 7.2.

⚡ Hyperkalemia Emergency Protocol

  1. Stabilize the heart: Calcium gluconate 1-2g IV (works in minutes)
  2. Shift K⁺ into cells: Insulin 10U + D50 (AND/OR albuterol nebulizer)
  3. Remove K⁺ from body: Loop diuretic, sodium polystyrene (Kayexalate), or DIALYSIS

Scene 4: Prevention & Risk Stratification

Your attending asks: "How could we have prevented this?" Let's review TLS risk stratification and prophylaxis.

High-Risk Malignancies for TLS

  • Very high risk: Burkitt lymphoma, ALL with WBC >100K, AML with WBC >100K
  • High risk: DLBCL, ALL/AML with lower WBC, high tumor burden solid tumors
  • Risk factors: high LDH, bulky disease, pre-existing renal impairment, dehydration

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • TLS tetrad: ↑K⁺, ↑PO₄, ↑uric acid, ↓Ca²⁺ (+ AKI)
  • Most dangerous: Hyperkalemia (immediate cardiac risk)
  • Don't give calcium for hypocalcemia if phosphorus is high (precipitation!)
  • Rasburicase: Enzymatically breaks down uric acid; for high-risk or established TLS
  • Allopurinol: Prevents NEW uric acid formation; for intermediate risk
  • Fluids: Essential for ALL risk levels (target UOP 100 mL/hr)
  • Dialysis: For refractory hyperkalemia, volume overload, or severe AKI
