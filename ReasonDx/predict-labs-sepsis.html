Case: Sepsis
Critical Care

Chief Complaint: 72-year-old woman brought from nursing home with fever and confusion

History: History of diabetes, hypertension, and recurrent UTIs. Staff noticed increasing confusion over 24 hours. Had fever to 102°F and decreased oral intake. Indwelling Foley catheter in place.

Vitals: BP 88/52, HR 108, RR 24, SpO2 94% on RA, Temp 38.9°C

Physical Exam: Lethargic, oriented only to name, warm extremities, suprapubic tenderness, cloudy urine in Foley bag, no neck stiffness.

🎯 Key Takeaways
