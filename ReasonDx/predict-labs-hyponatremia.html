Case: SIADH
Nephrology

Chief Complaint: 71-year-old man with confusion and unsteady gait for 3 days

History: Recently diagnosed small cell lung cancer (limited stage, started chemotherapy 2 weeks ago). Wife notes progressive confusion, poor concentration, and a fall yesterday. He's been drinking water "to stay hydrated during chemo." No vomiting, diarrhea, or edema. Medications: no diuretics.

Vitals: BP 132/78, HR 76, RR 14, SpO2 97% on RA, Temp 37.1°C

Physical Exam: Alert but disoriented to date. Euvolemic — no JVD, moist mucous membranes, normal skin turgor, no peripheral edema. Lungs clear. No asterixis.

Initial Labs: Sodium 118 mEq/L (critical), Glucose 95 mg/dL

📊 Actual Results
Serum Osmolality--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
248 mOsm/kg
Why low: Serum osmolality = 2×Na + Glucose/18 + BUN/2.8. With Na of 118, serum is hypotonic. This confirms TRUE hypotonic hyponatremia (not pseudohyponatremia from lipids/proteins, not hypertonic from glucose). First step: Always check serum osm to confirm hypotonic!
Urine Osmolality--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
520 mOsm/kg
Why concentrated (the "inappropriate" part): Normally, with low serum Na, ADH should be suppressed and urine should be maximally DILUTE (<100). In SIADH, ADH is released despite low osmolality → concentrated urine when it should be dilute. Urine osm >100 = ADH is active = "inappropriate."
Urine Sodium--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
68 mEq/L
Why high: In SIADH, volume expansion triggers natriuresis (sodium wasting). The body tries to shed volume by excreting Na. Key DDx point: In hypovolemic hyponatremia, kidneys RETAIN sodium (UNa <20). High UNa (>40) with euvolemia = SIADH pattern.
Serum Uric Acid--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
2.8 mg/dL
Why low: Volume expansion in SIADH increases uric acid clearance (same reason UNa is high). Low uric acid (<4) supports SIADH over hypovolemia. Pearl: This is a helpful confirmatory test when volume status is ambiguous!
TSH--
Your Prediction
--
Actual
1.8 mIU/L
Why normal: TSH is checked to rule out hypothyroidism as a cause of hyponatremia (hypothyroid patients have impaired free water excretion). Similarly, cortisol should be checked to rule out adrenal insufficiency. In this case, the cause is paraneoplastic SIADH from SCLC.

🎯 Key Takeaways
  • SIADH criteria: Hypotonic, euvolemic, UOsm >100, UNa >40
  • The "inappropriate": ADH active when it should be off
  • UNa is KEY: >40 in SIADH, <20 in hypovolemia
  • Low uric acid: Supports SIADH (volume expansion)
  • Rule out: Hypothyroid and adrenal insufficiency
  • SCLC = classic SIADH: Ectopic ADH production
