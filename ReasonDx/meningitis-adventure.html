🧬 Meningitis Clinical AdventureINFECTIOUS DISEASE

🎮 Case Presentation

A 19-year-old college freshman presents to the ED with severe headache, fever (39.5°C), neck stiffness, and photophobia. His roommate had similar symptoms 3 days ago. He has a petechial rash on his trunk and lower extremities.

Decision Point 1: Initial Workup

Critical Pearl: With bacterial meningitis suspected, do NOT delay antibiotics for LP. If LP will be delayed (CT needed first for papilledema/focal deficits), give empiric antibiotics IMMEDIATELY then do LP. Blood cultures before antibiotics if possible.

Empiric Treatment by Age
