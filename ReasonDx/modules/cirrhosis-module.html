CauseKey Features
Alcohol#1 in Western countries. Spectrum: steatosis → hepatitis → cirrhosis. AST:ALT >2:1 (mitochondrial injury). Elevated GGT
Hepatitis C#1 cause worldwide for transplant. Chronic → cirrhosis (20-30% over 20-30 years). Now curable with DAAs (direct-acting antivirals, >95% SVR)
Hepatitis BDNA virus, can integrate. Chronic if acquired perinatally (90%) vs adult (5%). Tenofovir/entecavir
MASLD (formerly NAFLD)Most common liver disease globally. Metabolic syndrome. May progress to MASH (steatohepatitis) → cirrhosis. Resmetirom (THR-β agonist) approved for MASH with fibrosis
Autoimmune hepatitisYoung women. ANA/anti-smooth muscle Ab. Responds to steroids + azathioprine
OtherHemochromatosis (iron), Wilson disease (copper), PBC (anti-mitochondrial Ab), PSC (IBD association, p-ANCA), α1-antitrypsin deficiency
🔑 AST:ALT ratio: >2:1 = alcoholic hepatitis. <1 = viral/MASLD. ALT is more specific to liver (ALT = Liver). Both >1000 = acute viral, ischemic ("shock liver"), acetaminophen, or autoimmune.

Complications of Cirrhosis
ComplicationPathophysiologyManagement
AscitesPortal HTN + low albumin → fluid shiftsSodium restriction + spironolactone (+ furosemide). SAAG ≥1.1 = portal HTN. Paracentesis for tense/refractory. SBP prophylaxis
SBPBacterial translocation → infected ascitesPMN >250 in ascitic fluid = SBP. Ceftriaxone (or cefotaxime). IV albumin day 1 and 3 (prevents hepatorenal syndrome)
Hepatic encephalopathyAmmonia crosses BBB → astrocyte swellingLactulose (first-line — traps NH3 as NH4+ in gut) + rifaximin (adjunct — reduces ammonia-producing bacteria). Identify/treat precipitant (infection, GIB, constipation, meds)
Hepatorenal syndromeRenal vasoconstriction from splanchnic vasodilationIV albumin + midodrine + octreotide (or norepinephrine). TIPS. Definitive: liver transplant. FENa <1%, bland sediment
Variceal bleedingPortal HTN → collateral vesselsOctreotide + EGD band ligation + ceftriaxone. Prophylaxis: NSBB
TREATMENT
Transplant & Scoring
🔑 MELD Score (Model for End-Stage Liver Disease): Uses bilirubin, creatinine, INR. Predicts 3-month mortality. Used for transplant allocation — higher MELD = sicker = higher priority. MELD-Na adds sodium (hyponatremia = worse prognosis).

Transplant Indications

MELD ≥15, hepatocellular carcinoma (Milan criteria: single ≤5 cm or ≤3 nodules each ≤3 cm), acute liver failure, refractory ascites/HRS/variceal bleeding, hepatopulmonary/portopulmonary syndrome.

HCC Screening

All cirrhotics: ultrasound ± AFP every 6 months. HCC is most common primary liver malignancy. AFP >400 in cirrhotic = HCC until proven otherwise. Diagnosis: multiphase CT or MRI (arterial enhancement + washout).
