MECHANISM
Domains of Development

Child development progresses through four interconnected domains: gross motor, fine motor/adaptive, language, and social/emotional. Milestones follow a predictable cephalocaudal (head-to-toe) and proximal-to-distal pattern.

Key Developmental Principles

PrincipleExplanationExample
CephalocaudalDevelopment proceeds head → feetHead control (2 mo) → sitting (6 mo) → standing (9 mo) → walking (12 mo)
Proximal-DistalCentral → peripheral controlBatting objects (3 mo) → reaching (4 mo) → raking grasp (6 mo) → pincer grasp (9-10 mo)
Primitive ReflexesPresent at birth, disappear as cortical control developsMoro (0-4 mo), rooting (0-4 mo), palmar grasp (0-6 mo), Babinski (0-12 mo). Persistence = red flag.
🔑 Key Concept: The persistence of primitive reflexes beyond expected age suggests upper motor neuron pathology. Moro reflex beyond 6 months, obligate ATNR beyond 6 months, or Babinski beyond 2 years warrants neurologic evaluation. Conversely, absence of primitive reflexes at birth suggests CNS depression or peripheral nerve injury.

Screening Tools

ASQ-3 (Ages and Stages Questionnaire): Parent-completed, screens all domains at 2, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 24, 30 months. M-CHAT-R (Modified Checklist for Autism): Autism-specific screening at 18 and 24 months. PEDS (Parents' Evaluation of Developmental Status): Parent concern-based screening. AAP recommends developmental surveillance at EVERY well-child visit and formal screening at 9, 18, and 30 months.

⚠️ Developmental Regression: Loss of previously acquired skills at ANY age is a medical emergency warranting urgent evaluation. Consider: Rett syndrome (girls, regression at 6-18 months with hand-wringing), late-onset metabolic disorders (Tay-Sachs, metachromatic leukodystrophy), Landau-Kleffner syndrome (language regression + seizures), disintegrative disorder, and brain tumors.
