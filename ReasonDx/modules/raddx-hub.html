Teaching Approach

What Makes RadDx Different

  • Reasoning First: Every scenario starts with a clinical vignette — you decide what to order before seeing any images. This builds the ordering reflex that matters in practice.
  • Hypothesis-Driven: Before viewing results, you predict what you expect to see. This primes pattern recognition and reduces anchoring bias.
  • Management Integration: Findings aren't endpoints — they change differentials, disposition, and treatment. Every scenario asks "now what?" after interpretation.
  • Modality Reasoning: Not just "get a CT" — learn WHY a specific modality (with or without contrast, specific protocol) is the right choice for the clinical question being asked.
  • Don't-Image Decisions: Some of the most important imaging decisions are knowing when NOT to image — compartment syndrome, tension pneumothorax, and clinical diagnoses that imaging delays.

How Imaging Integrates Across the Platform

  • Adventure Cases: Imaging decision nodes embedded at key clinical moments — order the wrong study and watch the patient's condition evolve while you wait.
  • Module MCQs: Questions test modality selection, expected findings, and management changes — not just pattern recognition from labeled images.
  • Deep Dives: Pathophysiology modules explain WHY imaging findings appear — consolidation on CXR because of alveolar filling, not just "white-out on X-ray."
  • Radiopaedia Links: Curated case links to Radiopaedia's staged-reveal format where you work through history → images → diagnosis yourself.

About Radiopaedia Integration

RadDx links to Radiopaedia.org case studies, which use a staged reveal format — you see clinical history first, then images, then the diagnosis. This avoids the problem of labeled images giving away the answer. We link to case pages (not static images) so you practice the full reasoning arc. Radiopaedia content is available under Creative Commons licensing and represents one of the world's largest open-access radiology education resources.
