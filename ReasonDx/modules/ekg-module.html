Systematic Approach

EKG Reading Framework: Rate → Rhythm → Axis → Intervals → ST/T Changes

StepNormalHow to Assess
Rate60-100 bpm300 ÷ (# large boxes between R-R). Or count R waves in 6-sec strip × 10
RhythmRegular, sinus (upright P in II, each P followed by QRS)Regular R-R intervals + P before every QRS + QRS after every P
Axis-30° to +90° (normal)Lead I and aVF both positive = normal. Lead I positive + aVF negative = LAD. Lead I negative + aVF positive = RAD
IntervalsPR: 120-200 ms. QRS: <120 ms. QTc: <440 ms (M), <460 ms (F)PR >200 = heart block. QRS >120 = bundle branch block. QTc prolonged = torsades risk
ST/T changesIsoelectric ST, upright T (most leads)Elevation = STEMI/pericarditis. Depression = ischemia/reciprocal. T-wave inversion = ischemia/strain
CLINICAL
Key EKG Patterns
PatternEKG FindingsClinical Significance
STEMIST elevation ≥1 mm in ≥2 contiguous leads + reciprocal depressionAcute MI — emergent PCI
PericarditisDiffuse ST elevation (concave up, "smiley face") + PR depression. No reciprocal changesDistinguish from STEMI: diffuse vs territorial, concave vs convex, PR depression
Complete heart block (3°)P waves march independently of QRS (AV dissociation). Regular P-P, regular R-R, no relationship between themMay need emergent pacing. Atropine for temporizing
A-fibNo P waves, irregularly irregular R-R, fibrillatory baselineRate/rhythm control + anticoagulation by CHA₂DS₂-VASc
WPW (Wolff-Parkinson-White)Short PR (<120 ms) + delta wave + wide QRSAccessory pathway (Bundle of Kent). Risk of sudden death if A-fib develops
BrugadaCoved ST elevation in V1-V3 + pseudo-RBBBRisk of VF/sudden death. ICD indicated for high risk
Long QTQTc >500 msRisk of torsades de pointes. Avoid QT-prolonging drugs. Causes: congenital, drugs, hypoK, hypoMg, hypoCa
🔑 Bundle Branch Blocks:
• RBBB: rsR' ("M-shaped") in V1-V2, wide S in I/V6. "MaRRoW" — M in Right leads (V1), W in left leads (V6)
• LBBB: Broad notched R in I, aVL, V5-V6. Absent r in V1. "WiLLiaM" — W in Left leads (V1), M in left leads (V6). New LBBB + chest pain = STEMI equivalent → emergent PCI
TREATMENT
Life-Threatening EKG Findings
⚠️ EKG Emergencies — Act Immediately:
1. STEMI: activate cath lab. Door-to-balloon ≤90 min
2. New LBBB + chest pain: treat as STEMI equivalent
3. Ventricular fibrillation (VF): immediate defibrillation
4. Pulseless VT: immediate defibrillation
5. Complete heart block with bradycardia: atropine → transcutaneous pacing → transvenous pacing
6. Torsades de pointes: IV magnesium + overdrive pacing. AVOID amiodarone
7. Hyperkalemia (peaked T, wide QRS, sine wave): IV calcium gluconate (stabilize membrane) → insulin + glucose, albuterol, kayexalate

Hyperkalemia EKG Progression

Peaked narrow T waves (earliest) → PR prolongation → P wave loss → QRS widening → sine wave → VF/asystole. IV calcium gluconate is the FIRST intervention (cardioprotective — stabilizes myocardial membrane within minutes). Does NOT lower K+ — add shifting agents (insulin/glucose, bicarb, albuterol) and elimination (kayexalate, dialysis).
