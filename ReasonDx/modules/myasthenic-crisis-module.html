MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Myasthenia gravis = autoimmune destruction of postsynaptic acetylcholine receptors (AChR) at the neuromuscular junction. Myasthenic crisis = respiratory failure requiring intubation from MG exacerbation.

Antibody% of MGKey Features
Anti-AChR85%Most common. Associated with thymic abnormalities (thymoma 10-15%, thymic hyperplasia 65%)
Anti-MuSK5-8%More severe bulbar weakness. Poor response to cholinesterase inhibitors. Female predominance
Seronegative5-10%Clinical MG with negative antibodies. May have low-affinity AChR or LRP4 antibodies
🔑 Fatigable Weakness: Hallmark of MG. Weakness worsens with repeated/sustained muscle use and improves with rest. Pattern: ptosis, diplopia → facial weakness, dysarthria, dysphagia → limb weakness → respiratory muscles. Descending pattern (opposite of GBS).
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Crisis

Diagnosis

TestDetails
Anti-AChR antibodiesMost specific. Positive in 85% of generalized MG
Anti-MuSK antibodiesCheck if AChR negative
Edrophonium (Tensilon) testShort-acting AChE inhibitor → transient improvement. Rarely used now (risk of cholinergic crisis). Ice pack test safer
CT chestALL MG patients — screen for thymoma
Repetitive nerve stimulation (RNS)Decremental response (>10% decrement) at low-frequency stimulation
⚠️ Crisis Triggers: Infection (#1 trigger), surgery, medications (aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones, beta-blockers, magnesium, neuromuscular blockers), tapering immunosuppressants, pregnancy, emotional stress.

Myasthenic vs Cholinergic Crisis: Both cause weakness. Myasthenic = under-treated (improves with edrophonium). Cholinergic = over-treated with AChE inhibitors (DUMBBELSS: diarrhea, urination, miosis, bradycardia, bronchospasm, emesis, lacrimation, salivation, sweating).
TREATMENT
Management

Chronic MG Management

ClassDrugNotes
AChE inhibitorPyridostigmine (first-line symptomatic)Improves NMJ transmission. Does NOT change disease course
ImmunosuppressantPrednisone, azathioprine, mycophenolateDisease-modifying. Prednisone for acute exacerbations
ThymectomyRecommended for thymoma (all) and non-thymoma generalized MG age 18-65MGTX trial: thymectomy improved outcomes even without thymoma
Targeted therapyEculizumab, efgartigimod (FcRn inhibitor)For refractory generalized AChR+ MG

Myasthenic Crisis

🔑 Crisis Treatment: IVIG or plasmapheresis (both effective, rapid onset). Hold/reduce pyridostigmine during crisis (may worsen secretions). Intubate if FVC <15-20 mL/kg. Avoid medications that worsen MG. Treat the trigger (usually infection).
