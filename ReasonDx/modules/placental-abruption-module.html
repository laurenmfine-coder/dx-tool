MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Placental abruption: premature separation of normally implanted placenta from the uterine wall BEFORE delivery. Bleeding between placenta and uterine wall → retroplacental hematoma → compromised fetal blood supply.

Risk FactorDetails

CLINICAL
Presentation & Diagnosis
⚠️ Classic Presentation: Painful vaginal bleeding + rigid/tender uterus ("board-like") + fetal distress. However, 20% of abruptions are concealed (no external bleeding — blood trapped behind placenta). Suspect if: tetanic uterine contractions, fetal distress without visible bleeding, or sudden uterine tenderness.

🔑 Abruption is a CLINICAL diagnosis. US has only ~25% sensitivity for abruption (retroplacental clot may not be visible). Do NOT wait for US confirmation if clinical suspicion is high. Couvelaire uterus: blood infiltrates myometrium → purplish discoloration.
TREATMENT
Management
ScenarioManagement
Severe + fetal distress (any GA)EMERGENT cesarean delivery. Simultaneous resuscitation: large-bore IVs, blood products, correct DIC
Mild + near term (≥37 wk)Deliver (vaginal if feasible and fetal status reassuring)
Mild + preterm + stableExpectant management in hospital with continuous fetal monitoring. Betamethasone if <34 wk. Deliver for deterioration
Fetal demiseVaginal delivery preferred (avoid surgery in DIC). Correct coagulopathy aggressively
🔑 DIC Complication: Abruption releases tissue thromboplastin → consumptive coagulopathy → DIC. Monitor fibrinogen (most sensitive — drops early), platelets, PT/PTT, D-dimer. Replace: cryoprecipitate (fibrinogen), FFP, platelets. Fibrinogen <200 predicts severe hemorrhage.
