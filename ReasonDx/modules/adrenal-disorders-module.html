MECHANISM
Adrenal Cortex Review
🔑 Adrenal cortex layers (outside in): GFR = Salt, Sugar, Sex. Zona Glomerulosa → mineralocorticoids (aldosterone). Zona Fasciculata → glucocorticoids (cortisol). Zona Reticularis → androgens (DHEA, androstenedione).

The HPA axis: Hypothalamus (CRH) → Anterior pituitary (ACTH) → Adrenal cortex (cortisol) → Negative feedback on both hypothalamus and pituitary.

EXCESS CORTISOL
Cushing Syndrome

Causes (ACTH-dependent vs ACTH-independent)

ACTH-Dependent (80%)ACTH-Independent (20%)
Cushing DISEASE (70%): pituitary adenoma secreting ACTHAdrenal adenoma (most common ACTH-independent)
Ectopic ACTH (15%): small cell lung cancer, carcinoidAdrenal carcinoma (large, often >6 cm)
⚠️ #1 cause of Cushing syndrome overall: EXOGENOUS glucocorticoids (iatrogenic). Always ask about medication use first!

Clinical Features

Central obesity, moon facies, dorsocervical fat pad (buffalo hump), abdominal striae (purple, wide), proximal muscle weakness, skin thinning/easy bruising, hyperglycemia, hypertension, osteoporosis, psychiatric symptoms, menstrual irregularity, immunosuppression.

Workup Algorithm

1. Confirm hypercortisolism (any 2 of 3): 24h urine free cortisol (>3x ULN), late-night salivary cortisol (elevated, loss of diurnal variation), low-dose dexamethasone suppression test (1 mg overnight — cortisol should suppress to <1.8; failure = Cushing). 2. Determine ACTH: ACTH low → adrenal source (CT adrenals). ACTH normal/high → pituitary vs ectopic. 3. High-dose dexamethasone suppression: Pituitary adenoma suppresses (>50% decline). Ectopic does NOT suppress. 4. MRI pituitary or inferior petrosal sinus sampling if ambiguous.

CORTISOL DEFICIENCY
Addison Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency)

Etiology

Developed world: autoimmune adrenalitis (80%) — antibodies against 21-hydroxylase. Developing world: TB (most common worldwide). Other: adrenal hemorrhage (Waterhouse-Friderichsen in meningococcal sepsis), metastatic disease, infiltrative (sarcoidosis, amyloid), congenital adrenal hyperplasia.

Clinical Features

Fatigue, weakness, weight loss, hypotension (orthostatic), salt craving, nausea/vomiting/abdominal pain, hyperpigmentation (ACTH elevated → POMC cleavage → MSH → pigmentation of skin creases, buccal mucosa, scars).

🔑 Hyperpigmentation distinguishes PRIMARY from SECONDARY/TERTIARY adrenal insufficiency. Primary = high ACTH → hyperpigmentation. Secondary (pituitary) or tertiary (hypothalamic) = low ACTH → NO hyperpigmentation.

Labs

Low cortisol, high ACTH (primary), hyperkalemia (low aldosterone), hyponatremia (cortisol loss → ADH release), hypoglycemia, eosinophilia. Confirm with ACTH stimulation test (cosyntropin test): give 250 mcg cosyntropin → cortisol should rise to ≥18-20 mcg/dL at 30-60 min. Failure to rise = adrenal insufficiency.

Adrenal Crisis

⚠️ Medical emergency. Triggered by stress (surgery, infection, trauma) in patients with undiagnosed or undertreated adrenal insufficiency, or abrupt discontinuation of chronic steroids. Presents: hypotension refractory to fluids/pressors, altered mental status, fever, abdominal pain. Treatment: IV hydrocortisone 100 mg bolus then 50 mg q8h + IV NS. Do NOT wait for lab confirmation — treat empirically.
