MAOIsInhibit monoamine oxidase → increased serotonin, NE, dopaminePhenelzine, tranylcypromine, selegiline (patch)TYRAMINE CRISIS: hypertensive emergency from aged cheeses, cured meats, red wine. Serotonin syndrome risk. Requires dietary restrictions
OthersVariousBupropion (NDRI), mirtazapine (NaSSA), trazodone (5-HT2 antagonist), vilazodone, vortioxetineBupropion: seizures, no sexual SE. Mirtazapine: sedation, weight gain. Trazodone: priapism, sedation
⚠️ TCA Overdose: Leading cause of death from antidepressant overdose. Lethal mechanisms: 1) Cardiac sodium channel blockade → QRS widening → ventricular arrhythmias. 2) Anticholinergic toxicity. 3) Seizures. Treatment: IV sodium bicarbonate (narrows QRS, alkalinizes urine). QRS >100 ms = seizure risk. QRS >160 ms = arrhythmia risk.

TREATMENT
Special Considerations

Drug Interactions

🔑 MAOI Washout Periods: MAOI → other serotonergic drug: 2-week washout. Fluoxetine → MAOI: 5-week washout (long half-life). Other SSRIs → MAOI: 2-week washout. Combining MAOIs with serotonergic drugs = serotonin syndrome risk.

Antidepressant Discontinuation Syndrome

FINISH mnemonic: Flu-like symptoms, Insomnia, Nausea, Imbalance (dizziness), Sensory disturbances (brain zaps, electric shock sensations), Hyperarousal. Worst with: paroxetine (shortest half-life SSRI), venlafaxine. Best avoided with: fluoxetine (long half-life). Prevention: gradual taper over 2-4 weeks.
