Classification
DepthAppearanceSensationHealing
Superficial (1st)Red, dry, no blisters (sunburn)Painful3-5 days, no scarring
Superficial partial (2nd)Red, moist, blisters, blanchesVery painful7-14 days, minimal scarring
Deep partial (2nd)White/red mottled, may not blanchDecreased14-21+ days. May need grafting
Full thickness (3rd)White/brown/charred, leathery (eschar)PAINLESSNo spontaneous healing → excision + graft
Subdermal (4th)Into muscle/tendon/bonePainlessAmputation or flap
🔑 Paradox: Full-thickness burns are PAINLESS (nerve destruction) while superficial partial burns are MOST painful. Painless white/leathery wound = WORSE burn than painful blisters.
CLINICAL
Assessment & Complications

Rule of 9's (Adults)

Head 9%, each arm 9%, anterior trunk 18%, posterior trunk 18%, each leg 18%, perineum 1%. Palm ≈ 1% TBSA.

⚠️ Burn Center Transfer: Partial thickness >10% TBSA, any full-thickness, face/hands/feet/genitalia/joints, chemical/electrical, inhalation injury, circumferential burns.

Inhalation Injury

Facial burns, singed nasal hair, carbonaceous sputum, hoarseness, stridor. Early intubation — airway edema progresses rapidly.

Complications

ComplicationDetails
Compartment syndromeCircumferential burns → escharotomy (NOT fasciotomy)
Curling ulcerStress ulcer of duodenum. Prophylaxis: PPI/H2 blocker
Infection/Sepsis#1 cause of death after resuscitation. Pseudomonas, S. aureus
Hypermetabolism2-3x metabolic rate. High-calorie, high-protein nutrition
TREATMENT
Resuscitation & Wound Care
🔑 Parkland Formula: 4 mL × kg × %TBSA = total LR in 24h. HALF in first 8h (from burn time). Monitor: UOP 0.5-1 mL/kg/hr. Only count partial + full thickness.

Wound Management

DepthTreatment
SuperficialCool water, aloe, NSAIDs
Superficial partialSilver sulfadiazine (avoid face — staining; use bacitracin). Non-adherent dressings
Deep partial/FullEarly excision + split-thickness skin graft within 72h

Special Burns

Electrical: External underestimates internal damage. ECG, CK, rhabdomyolysis risk. Chemical: Copious water irrigation. HF acid → calcium gluconate. CO poisoning: 100% O2 NRB (SpO2 unreliable). COHb level. Hyperbaric for severe (COHb >25%, neuro symptoms, pregnancy).
