📊 Systematic ABG Approach

5-Step ABG Interpretation

StepQuestionHow to Determine
1. Primary disorderAcidemia (pH <7.35) or Alkalemia (pH >7.45)?Look at pH first
2. Respiratory or metabolic?Is the primary process respiratory (pCO2) or metabolic (HCO3)?In acidosis: low HCO3 = metabolic, high pCO2 = respiratory. In alkalosis: high HCO3 = metabolic, low pCO2 = respiratory.
3. Anion gap (if metabolic acidosis)AG = Na - (Cl + HCO3). Normal = 12 ± 4Elevated AG → MUDPILES causes. Normal AG → hyperchloremic (GI loss, RTA)
4. Compensation adequate?Is the body compensating appropriately?Use compensation formulas (Winter formula for metabolic acidosis)
5. Delta-delta (if AGMA)Delta AG / Delta HCO3Ratio 1-2 = pure AGMA. >2 = concurrent metabolic alkalosis. <1 = concurrent non-AG metabolic acidosis.

Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis: MUDPILES

  • Methanol
  • Uremia (renal failure)
  • Diabetic ketoacidosis (and alcoholic/starvation ketoacidosis)
  • Propylene glycol, Paraldehyde
  • Isoniazid, Iron
  • Lactic acidosis (Type A: tissue hypoxia; Type B: medications like metformin)
  • Ethylene glycol
  • Salicylates (aspirin toxicity — unique: causes AGMA + primary respiratory alkalosis)

🔢 Compensation & Mixed Disorders

Compensation Formulas

Primary DisorderExpected CompensationFormula
Metabolic acidosisRespiratory (↓pCO2)Winter: pCO2 = 1.5 × [HCO3] + 8 (± 2)
Metabolic alkalosisRespiratory (↑pCO2)pCO2 = 0.7 × [HCO3] + 21 (± 2). pCO2 rarely rises >55.
Acute respiratory acidosisMetabolic (↑HCO3)HCO3 rises 1 per 10 rise in pCO2
Chronic respiratory acidosisMetabolic (↑HCO3)HCO3 rises 3.5 per 10 rise in pCO2
Acute respiratory alkalosisMetabolic (↓HCO3)HCO3 drops 2 per 10 drop in pCO2
Chronic respiratory alkalosisMetabolic (↓HCO3)HCO3 drops 5 per 10 drop in pCO2

Delta-Delta Ratio

  • When to use: Only in anion gap metabolic acidosis (AGMA)
  • Formula: (AG - 12) / (24 - HCO3) = Delta AG / Delta HCO3
  • Ratio 1-2: Pure AGMA (all excess acid accounted for by AG rise)
  • Ratio >2: Concurrent metabolic ALKALOSIS (HCO3 higher than expected — some other process raising it)
  • Ratio <1: Concurrent non-AG metabolic acidosis (HCO3 lower than expected — additional non-AG acid present)

💎 Salicylate Toxicity = Mixed Disorder

ASA overdose uniquely causes BOTH primary respiratory alkalosis (direct medullary stimulation) AND anion gap metabolic acidosis (uncoupling oxidative phosphorylation). Finding both on ABG in a patient with altered mental status or tinnitus should prompt immediate salicylate level.
