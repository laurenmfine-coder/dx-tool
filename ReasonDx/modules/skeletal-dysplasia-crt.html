📖 Teaching Point

Two types of bone formation: ENDOCHONDRAL = cartilage model → replaced by bone (long bones, vertebral bodies, skull base). Growth occurs at the physis (growth plate) — zone of reserve → proliferative → hypertrophic → calcification → ossification. INTRAMEMBRANOUS = mesenchyme directly → bone without cartilage intermediate (skull vault, clavicle, facial bones). Achondroplasia FGFR3 mutation constitutively activates the receptor → inhibits chondrocyte proliferation at the growth plate → short endochondral bones, normal intramembranous bones.

📖 Teaching Point

Growth plate zones (epiphysis → diaphysis): 1) Reserve zone: resting chondrocytes, cartilage storage. 2) Proliferative zone: rapid cell division, organized columns (the 'engine' of growth). 3) Hypertrophic zone: cells enlarge 5-10x, produce type X collagen, signal for vascular invasion. 4) Calcification zone: matrix calcifies, chondrocytes undergo apoptosis. 5) Ossification zone: osteoblasts deposit bone on calcified cartilage scaffolding. FGFR3 is a NEGATIVE regulator of bone growth — it normally puts the brakes on proliferation. Achondroplasia = brakes stuck on.

📖 Teaching Point

OI classification (simplified): Type I = mild, autosomal dominant, blue sclerae, fractures after birth, normal stature. Type II = lethal (perinatal), crumpled long bones, dark blue sclerae. Type III = severe deforming, progressively deforming, normal sclerae, very short stature. Type IV = moderate, normal sclerae, variable height. Key distinguishing features: Blue sclerae (thin collagen allows choroid to show through), wormian bones (extra skull ossicles), dentinogenesis imperfecta (abnormal teeth), hearing loss (ossicle fractures). Bisphosphonates (pamidronate) reduce fracture rate.

📖 Teaching Point

Three axes of limb development: 1) Proximal-distal (shoulder→hand): AER → FGFs. AER removal → limb truncation. 2) Anterior-posterior (thumb→pinky): ZPA → Sonic hedgehog (SHH). Ectopic ZPA → mirror-image duplication. 3) Dorsal-ventral (nail→palm): dorsal ectoderm → WNT7a (dorsal), ventral ectoderm → EN1 (ventral). Limb bud appears at week 4, upper before lower. Mesoderm: lateral plate (bones, connective tissue) + somites (muscle via myoblast migration). Neural crest: does NOT contribute to limbs.

📖 Teaching Point

FGFR3 mutation severity spectrum: FGFR3 gain-of-function mutations exist on a severity spectrum. Mildest: Hypochondroplasia (mild short stature, minimal dysmorphism). Moderate: Achondroplasia (classic short-limbed dwarfism, compatible with life). Severe: Thanatophoric dysplasia (lethal, severe shortening, narrow chest). All are autosomal dominant gain-of-function. Homozygous achondroplasia (two copies) = lethal (essentially thanatophoric phenotype). Treatment advances: Vosoritide (C-type natriuretic peptide analog) is used for achondroplasia — counteracts FGFR3 overactivation, increases growth velocity.

🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
✓Two bone formation types: Endochondral (cartilage→bone, long bones) vs Intramembranous (direct, skull vault)
✓Achondroplasia: FGFR3 gain-of-function → inhibits growth plate proliferative zone → short but strong bones
✓OI: Type I collagen defect → normal-length but fragile bones, blue sclerae, fractures
✓Growth plate zones: Reserve → Proliferative → Hypertrophic → Calcification → Ossification
✓Limb patterning: AER (P-D, FGF), ZPA (A-P, SHH), dorsal ectoderm (D-V, WNT7a)
✓Thanatophoric dysplasia: more severe FGFR3 mutation → narrow chest → pulmonary hypoplasia → lethal
