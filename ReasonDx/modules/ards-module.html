Berlin Definition
CriterionDetails
TimingWithin 1 week of known insult or worsening respiratory symptoms
ImagingBilateral opacities NOT fully explained by effusions, atelectasis, or nodules
OriginNOT fully explained by heart failure or fluid overload (need echo to exclude)
OxygenationMild: P/F 200-300, Moderate: P/F 100-200, Severe: P/F ≤100 (on PEEP ≥5)
🔑 Pathophysiology: Diffuse alveolar damage → disrupted alveolar-capillary barrier → protein-rich pulmonary edema → surfactant dysfunction → atelectasis → severe hypoxemia + decreased compliance. Non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema (PCWP ≤18 mmHg distinguishes from cardiogenic).

Common Causes

Direct (pulmonary): Pneumonia (#1 overall), aspiration, pulmonary contusion, inhalation injury, near-drowning. Indirect (extrapulmonary): Sepsis (#1 extrapulmonary), pancreatitis, trauma, massive transfusion, burns, DIC.

CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Severity
⚠️ ARDS Severity (P/F Ratio on PEEP ≥5):
• Mild: P/F 200-300 (mortality ~27%)
• Moderate: P/F 100-200 (mortality ~32%)
• Severe: P/F ≤100 (mortality ~45%)

TREATMENT
Management

Lung-Protective Ventilation (ARDSNet)

🔑 ARDSNet Protocol:
• Low tidal volume: 6 mL/kg ideal body weight (reduces volutrauma). Target plateau pressure ≤30 cmH₂O
• PEEP: Optimize to improve oxygenation while avoiding barotrauma. Use PEEP/FiO2 table
• Permissive hypercapnia: Accept higher pCO2 to keep volumes low (pH >7.25 acceptable)
• Conservative fluid strategy: Keep dry (FACTT trial — better outcomes vs liberal)

Additional Therapies

InterventionIndication
Prone positioningP/F <150 (severe). PROSEVA trial: 16h/day proning reduced mortality by ~50%. Most impactful intervention after lung-protective ventilation
Neuromuscular blockadeEarly severe ARDS (first 48h). Cisatracurium. Prevents ventilator dyssynchrony
ECMORefractory hypoxemia despite optimal management
