🎤 MMI & Interview Practice

The MMI Format

Multiple Mini Interviews use 6-10 stations, each 8 minutes long. You read a prompt (2 min), then discuss with an interviewer (6 min). Each station is independent — a bad station doesn't ruin the others. Schools use MMIs because they're harder to fake than traditional interviews and better predict clinical performance.

🎯 The STAR-E Framework for MMI Responses

S — Stakeholders: Identify everyone affected by the situation. Don't just think about the obvious person — who else is impacted?
T — Tensions: Name the competing values or priorities. Most MMI prompts have no "right answer" — they want to see you recognize the tensions.
A — Actions: Propose what you would do. Be specific and practical, not abstract. "I would have a conversation with..." is better than "communication is important."
R — Reasoning: Explain WHY you chose this approach. Connect to principles (autonomy, beneficence, fairness, honesty).
E — Explore alternatives: Acknowledge what you might be wrong about. "However, someone could argue..." shows maturity and intellectual humility.

🚫 Common MMI Mistakes

→ Jumping to a conclusion without exploring multiple perspectives
→ Being preachy or moralistic instead of thoughtful and nuanced
→ Forgetting to consider the patient's perspective and autonomy
→ Using medical jargon or trying to sound like a doctor already
→ Not managing time (spending 4 min on background, 2 min on your actual answer)
→ Giving a "safe" answer that says nothing — interviewers want to see genuine thinking
→ Forgetting to acknowledge uncertainty — "I'm not sure, but here's how I'd approach it" is STRONG
