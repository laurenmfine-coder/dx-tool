🗺️ Learning Paths

🔭 Explorer Path

For early premeds with prereqs in progress. Learn why doctors think differently — and start building that thinking yourself.

🧬 Biology → Clinical Connections

🫀
Why Hearts Fail
Connect cardiac muscle physiology to why patients get short of breath climbing stairs. From sarcomeres to symptoms.
Prereq: Bio — cardiac muscle, action potentials
🦠
Infections: Why They Win
How bacteria evade the immune system. Why antibiotics work (and why resistance is terrifying). Microbiology meets the ICU.
Prereq: Bio — microbiology, immune system basics
🧠
The Brain Under Attack
From neurons to stroke: how a blood clot in an artery destroys brain tissue in minutes. Why time = brain.
Prereq: Bio — neuroscience, cell biology

⚗️ Chemistry → Clinical Connections

🩸
Blood Gas Basics
Acid-base chemistry is the foundation of emergency medicine. Learn why pH matters when a patient can't breathe.
Prereq: Chem — acid-base, buffers, Henderson-Hasselbalch
💊
How Drugs Work
Receptor binding, enzyme inhibition, pharmacokinetics — your organic chemistry knowledge IS pharmacology.
Prereq: Chem/OChem — molecular interactions, kinetics

🎯 Clinical Reasoning Foundations

🤔
How Doctors Think
Introduction to clinical reasoning: pattern recognition, hypothesis-driven thinking, Bayesian reasoning, and cognitive biases.
Prereq: None — start here
🔍
The Differential Diagnosis
Why physicians generate a list of possibilities, not one answer. The art and science of narrowing the differential.
Prereq: How Doctors Think module

🏗️ Foundation Path

For post-prereqs students actively shadowing or preparing for the MCAT. Build mechanism-based thinking and connect physiology to pathophysiology.

🔬 Mechanism-Based Thinking

🫁
Respiratory Mechanisms
V/Q mismatch, shunting, dead space — why some patients desaturate and others don't. ReasonDx: Respiratory module.
Prereq: Physiology — gas exchange, ventilation
🩺
Cardiovascular Mechanisms
Preload, afterload, contractility → heart failure. Atherosclerosis → ACS. From Starling curves to the cath lab.
Prereq: Physiology — cardiac output, hemodynamics
🧪
Renal Mechanisms
GFR, tubular function, electrolyte handling. Why kidneys are the body's master regulators — and what breaks.
Prereq: Physiology — renal physiology, acid-base
🧬
Endocrine Mechanisms
Feedback loops in action: thyroid, adrenal, glucose regulation. Why diabetes is a disease of failed homeostasis.
Prereq: Biochemistry — hormones, receptor signaling

📊 MCAT Clinical Bridge

📈
Biostatistics in Medicine
Sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NNT — the statistics that drive every medical decision. MCAT meets real clinical reasoning.
Prereq: Stats — basic probability, distributions
⚖️
Ethics in Clinical Practice
Autonomy, beneficence, justice, non-maleficence — how these play out in real patient scenarios, not just textbooks.
Prereq: MCAT — behavioral sciences section
🧠
Psych/Soc in Medicine
Health disparities, social determinants, behavioral change models — the psych/soc MCAT content you'll actually use.
Prereq: MCAT — psychology, sociology foundations

🌉 Bridge Path

For gap year, post-bac, or accepted students. Case-based clinical previews that build clerkship-ready frameworks. Get a head start on M1/M2 thinking.

🏥 Case-Based Clinical Previews

💔
Chest Pain: 5 Diagnoses
Work through a chest pain case and learn to differentiate ACS, PE, aortic dissection, GERD, and MSK pain. Your first clinical reasoning case.
Links to: RadDx Cardiac Imaging, Cardiology CRT
🤕
Abdominal Pain Framework
Localize by quadrant, characterize by pattern, and narrow by demographics. The approach you'll use every day in clerkships.
Links to: RadDx Abdominal Imaging, GI CRT
😵
Altered Mental Status
The undifferentiated confused patient: metabolic, infectious, structural, toxic, psychiatric. Learn the systematic approach.
Links to: RadDx Neuroimaging, Neuro CRT
🤰
The Pregnant Patient
How pregnancy changes EVERYTHING about clinical reasoning — physiology, imaging, medications, differentials all shift.
Links to: RadDx OB/GYN Imaging

📋 Clerkship-Ready Skills

📝
The Patient Presentation
Learn the format every medical student must master: 1-liner, HPI, ROS, PMH, meds, exam, assessment, plan. Practice now.
Prereq: Explorer or Foundation path
📊
Reading Labs Like a Clinician
CBC, BMP, LFTs, coags, urinalysis — what every lab value means clinically and when to worry.
Prereq: Foundation — mechanism modules
🗣️
Breaking Bad News
The SPIKES protocol for difficult conversations. One of the hardest and most important skills in medicine.
Prereq: None — essential for all levels
