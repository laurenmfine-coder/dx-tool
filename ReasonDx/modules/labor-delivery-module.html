Stages of Labor
StageDefinitionKey Points
1st StageOnset of regular contractions → complete cervical dilation (10 cm)Latent phase: 0-6 cm (may take hours). Active phase: 6-10 cm (~1 cm/hr). Arrest: no cervical change for ≥6h with adequate contractions (or ≥4h with oxytocin + ROM)
2nd StageFull dilation → delivery of babyPushing stage. Arrest: >3h nullipara with epidural (>2h without). >2h multipara with epidural
3rd StageDelivery of baby → delivery of placentaUsually <30 min. Active management: oxytocin after delivery, controlled cord traction. Retained placenta if >30 min → manual extraction
🔑 Cardinal Movements of Labor: Engagement → Descent → Flexion → Internal Rotation → Extension → External Rotation → Expulsion. Most common presentation: LOA (left occiput anterior).

TREATMENT
Interventions

Labor Induction

Cervical ripening (if unfavorable cervix, Bishop score <6): misoprostol (PGE1) or dinoprostone (PGE2) or mechanical (Foley bulb). Oxytocin for augmentation/induction. Monitor for tachysystole (>5 contractions/10 min).

Cesarean Delivery Indications

Failure to progress (most common), non-reassuring FHR (category III), malpresentation (breech, transverse), placenta previa, active HSV outbreak, prior classical C-section, cord prolapse.

⚠️ Shoulder Dystocia: Anterior shoulder impacted behind pubic symphysis after head delivers. Risk: macrosomia, GDM, prolonged 2nd stage. Maneuvers: McRoberts (flexion/abduction of thighs — FIRST), suprapubic pressure (NOT fundal!), Rubin (rotation of anterior shoulder), Wood's corkscrew, delivery of posterior arm. Complication: Erb palsy (C5-C6 brachial plexus injury → waiter's tip position).
🔑 Postpartum Hemorrhage: Blood loss >1000 mL or signs of hypovolemia. #1 cause: uterine atony (treat with uterine massage + oxytocin → methylergonovine → carboprost [PGF2α] → misoprostol → tamponade → surgery). Risk factors: overdistended uterus, prolonged labor, magnesium use, chorioamnionitis.
