MECHANISM
Virology & Diagnosis

HIV is a retrovirus (RNA → DNA via reverse transcriptase) that targets CD4+ T lymphocytes. Without treatment: acute HIV → clinical latency (years) → AIDS (CD4 <200 or AIDS-defining illness).

Diagnostic Algorithm

4th-generation HIV test (recommended screening): detects both HIV-1/2 antibodies AND p24 antigen. Can detect infection 2-4 weeks after exposure (window period). If positive → HIV-1/HIV-2 differentiation assay. If indeterminate → HIV RNA (viral load) PCR.

🔑 Acute HIV (Acute Retroviral Syndrome): 2-4 weeks after exposure. Mono-like illness: fever, pharyngitis, lymphadenopathy, maculopapular rash, myalgias. High viral load, antibodies may be NEGATIVE. The 4th-gen test can detect p24 antigen even when antibodies haven't formed.

Other Key OIs

Kaposi sarcoma (HHV-8): violaceous skin lesions, any CD4. Oral hairy leukoplakia (EBV): white lesions on lateral tongue, cannot scrape off (vs thrush which scrapes off). Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML) (JC virus): white matter demyelination, no treatment except ART.

TREATMENT
Antiretroviral Therapy

ART Principles

START ART IN ALL HIV+ patients regardless of CD4 count (since 2015 guidelines). Earlier treatment = better outcomes. Standard regimen: 2 NRTIs + 1 INSTI (integrase strand transfer inhibitor).

Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS)

Paradoxical worsening of OI symptoms after starting ART (usually within 4-8 weeks). Occurs because recovering immune system mounts inflammatory response against existing (often subclinical) infections. Common with TB, MAC, Crypto, CMV. Treatment: continue ART + treat the OI + steroids if severe.

PREVENTION
PrEP & Post-Exposure Prophylaxis

Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)

For HIV-negative individuals at substantial risk. Options: daily oral TDF/FTC (Truvada) or TAF/FTC (Descovy), or injectable cabotegravir (Apretude) q2 months. Reduces HIV acquisition by >99% when adherent. Screen: HIV test, renal function, hepatitis B (TDF/FTC also treats HBV — stopping can cause HBV flare).

Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP)

Start within 72 hours of exposure (sooner = better). 3-drug regimen (TDF/FTC + dolutegravir or raltegravir) for 28 days. Indicated for: occupational exposure (needlestick), sexual assault, high-risk sexual exposure. Test for HIV at baseline, 4-6 weeks, and 3 months.
