MECHANISM
Causes

Normal serum K+: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L. Hyperkalemia (>5.0) becomes dangerous at >6.0 and life-threatening at >6.5.

MechanismCauses
Decreased excretionAKI/CKD (#1 cause), ACEi/ARBs, K+-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, amiloride), NSAIDs, calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, cyclosporine), type IV RTA, adrenal insufficiency
Transcellular shift (ICF → ECF)Acidosis (H+/K+ exchange), insulin deficiency (DKA), beta-blockers, cell lysis (rhabdomyolysis, tumor lysis, hemolysis), succinylcholine, digoxin toxicity
Increased intakeRare — oral/IV K+ supplementation, salt substitutes (KCl)
PseudohyperkalemiaHemolyzed sample, prolonged tourniquet, fist clenching, extreme leukocytosis/thrombocytosis. Always repeat if unexpected
🔑 Acidosis-K+ relationship: For every 0.1 decrease in pH, K+ rises ~0.6 mEq/L (H+ enters cells → K+ exits to maintain electroneutrality). Correcting acidosis will lower K+.
CLINICAL
EKG Changes & Assessment

EKG Progression (CRITICAL)

K+ LevelEKG Changes
5.5-6.5Peaked, narrow T waves (earliest sign)
6.5-7.0PR prolongation, P wave flattening
7.0-8.0QRS widening, loss of P waves
>8.0Sine wave pattern → VF → asystole
⚠️ ANY EKG changes with hyperkalemia = EMERGENCY. EKG changes can progress to cardiac arrest rapidly. Peaked T waves may be the only warning before lethal arrhythmia. Always get EKG immediately when hyperkalemia detected.
TREATMENT
Management (3-Step Approach)
StepAgentMechanismOnset
1. Stabilize membraneIV calcium gluconate 10% (10 mL over 2-3 min)Raises threshold potential — does NOT lower K+1-3 min, lasts 30-60 min
2. Shift K+ into cellsInsulin (10 units regular) + D50 (25g dextrose)Insulin drives K+ into cells via Na/K-ATPase15-30 min
Albuterol nebulizer (10-20 mg)Beta-2 stimulation → Na/K-ATPase activation15-30 min
Sodium bicarbonate (if acidotic)Corrects acidosis → K+ shifts back into cells15-30 min
3. Remove K+ from bodyLoop diuretics (furosemide)Renal K+ excretion (if kidneys functional)30-60 min
Sodium polystyrene sulfonate (Kayexalate)GI K+ exchange (limited evidence, slow)2-6 hours
HemodialysisDefinitive removal (most effective)Immediate during dialysis
🔑 Calcium gluconate is FIRST — stabilizes cardiac membrane within minutes. Does NOT lower K+. Then shift K+ intracellularly (insulin/glucose = most reliable). Then remove K+ from body. If K+ >6.5 with EKG changes or refractory to medical therapy → emergent dialysis.
