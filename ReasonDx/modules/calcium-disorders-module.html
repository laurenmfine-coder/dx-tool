MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Calcium homeostasis involves PTH (raises Ca²⁺), Vitamin D (raises Ca²⁺), and calcitonin (lowers Ca²⁺, minor role). Normal total calcium: 8.5-10.5 mg/dL. Always correct for albumin: corrected Ca = measured Ca + 0.8 × (4.0 - albumin).

🔑 PTH Actions: ↑ Bone resorption (releases Ca²⁺/PO₄), ↑ Renal Ca²⁺ reabsorption + ↓ PO₄ reabsorption (phosphaturia), ↑ 1α-hydroxylase → active vitamin D → ↑ intestinal Ca²⁺ absorption. Net effect: ↑Ca²⁺, ↓PO₄.

CLINICAL
Hypocalcemia

Etiologies

CauseMechanism
HypoparathyroidismPost-surgical (#1, thyroidectomy), autoimmune, DiGeorge syndrome
Vitamin D deficiencyDecreased intake/synthesis, malabsorption, CKD (↓1α-hydroxylase)
HypomagnesemiaMg²⁺ needed for PTH secretion and action → functional hypoparathyroidism
Acute pancreatitisSaponification — calcium binds to necrotic fat
HyperphosphatemiaCKD, tumor lysis, rhabdomyolysis → calcium-phosphate precipitation

Signs of Hypocalcemia

Chvostek sign: Facial nerve tap → ipsilateral facial twitching. Trousseau sign: BP cuff inflation → carpopedal spasm (more specific). Severe: Tetany, laryngospasm, seizures, prolonged QT → torsades de pointes.

🔑 Correct Mg²⁺ first! Hypocalcemia will be refractory to calcium replacement if hypomagnesemia is not corrected. Magnesium is required for both PTH secretion and PTH action on target organs.

Management

InterventionOnsetMechanism

Calcitonin4-6 hoursInhibits osteoclasts. Rapid but transient (tachyphylaxis in 48h)
Bisphosphonate (zoledronic acid)2-4 daysInhibits osteoclasts. Most effective long-term. Avoid in renal failure
DenosumabDaysAnti-RANKL. Use if bisphosphonate contraindicated (renal failure)
DialysisImmediateFor refractory cases or renal failure

Hypocalcemia Treatment

Symptomatic/severe: IV calcium gluconate 1-2g over 10-20 min (cardiac monitoring). Chronic: Oral calcium + vitamin D. Always correct magnesium simultaneously.
