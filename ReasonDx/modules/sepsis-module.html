MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection (Sepsis-3 definition, 2016). Septic shock is a subset with circulatory and cellular/metabolic dysfunction associated with higher mortality.

🔑 Key Concept: Sepsis-3 uses the SOFA score (Sequential Organ Failure Assessment) to define organ dysfunction. An acute increase of ≥2 SOFA points from baseline = sepsis. qSOFA (bedside screening): altered mental status + respiratory rate ≥22 + SBP ≤100. Meeting ≥2 qSOFA criteria should prompt further evaluation — but qSOFA is a screening tool, not a diagnostic criterion.
CLINICAL
Definitions & Diagnosis

Sepsis-3 Definitions (2016)

ConditionDefinitionMortality
SepsisInfection + acute SOFA increase ≥2 points~10%
Septic ShockSepsis + vasopressors needed to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg + lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation~40%

Key Biomarkers

MarkerRoleNotes
LactateTissue hypoperfusion marker, prognosticGoal: normalize lactate or decrease by ≥20% in 2-4 hours. Lactate >4 mmol/L = severe. Can be elevated without hypotension.
ProcalcitoninBacterial infection marker, guide antibiotic durationRises 4-6 hrs after bacterial infection. More specific for bacterial vs viral than CRP. Serial levels guide de-escalation.
Blood CulturesIdentify pathogen, guide therapy2 sets (aerobic + anaerobic) from 2 sites BEFORE antibiotics. Do NOT delay antibiotics for cultures.
⚠️ Lactate Clearance: Serial lactate measurement is a key resuscitation target. Failure to clear lactate (decrease by ≥20% in 2-4 hours) despite fluids and vasopressors is associated with significantly higher mortality and should prompt reassessment of the resuscitation strategy.
TREATMENT
Hour-1 Bundle & Management

Surviving Sepsis Campaign Hour-1 Bundle

ActionTargetDetails
Measure LactateRemeasure if >2 mmol/LGoal-directed resuscitation based on lactate clearance
Blood CulturesBefore antibiotics2 sets from 2 sites. Do NOT delay antibiotics if cultures delayed.
Broad-Spectrum AntibioticsWithin 1 hourEach hour of delay increases mortality ~7.6%. Cover based on suspected source. De-escalate when cultures return.
IV Fluid Bolus30 mL/kg crystalloid if hypotensive or lactate ≥4Balanced crystalloids (LR) may be preferred over NS. Reassess volume responsiveness dynamically.
VasopressorsMAP ≥65 mmHgStart if hypotensive despite fluids. Norepinephrine is FIRST-LINE. Add vasopressin (0.03 U/min) as second agent.

Vasopressor Hierarchy

1st line: Norepinephrine (α1 > β1). 2nd line: Vasopressin 0.03 U/min (V1 receptor). 3rd line: Epinephrine (if cardiac dysfunction). NOT first-line: Dopamine (more arrhythmias than norepinephrine — avoid except in bradycardia).

🔑 Board Pearl: Corticosteroids (hydrocortisone 200 mg/day) are indicated in refractory septic shock (requiring escalating vasopressors despite adequate fluids). The ADRENAL and APROCCHSS trials support use for vasopressor-dependent shock. Do NOT use for sepsis without shock — no benefit.
⚠️ Source Control: Antibiotics alone may be insufficient. Identify and manage the source: drain abscesses, remove infected devices, debride necrotic tissue, relieve obstructions. Source control within the first 6-12 hours is critical for survival.
