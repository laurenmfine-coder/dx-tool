🚨 MH Crisis Protocol (MHAUS)

1
STOP triggers: Discontinue volatile agents, turn off vaporizers
2
Call for help: MH cart, additional personnel, notify surgeon
3
Dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg IV push, repeat q5min until symptoms resolve (max 10 mg/kg)
4
Hyperventilate with 100% O2 at high flows (≥10 L/min)
5
Treat hyperkalemia: Calcium, insulin/glucose, bicarbonate
6
Active cooling: Cold IV saline, surface cooling, body cavity lavage

🔑 Key Learning Points

  • Triggers: Volatile anesthetics (sevoflurane, desflurane, isoflurane) and succinylcholine
  • Earliest sign: Unexplained rise in ETCO2 despite increased ventilation
  • Pathophysiology: RYR1 mutation → uncontrolled Ca²⁺ release → sustained muscle contraction → hypermetabolism
  • Masseter rigidity: Often the first clinical sign after succinylcholine
  • Dantrolene: Only specific treatment - blocks ryanodine receptor, reduces Ca²⁺ release
  • Safe agents: Propofol, opioids, benzodiazepines, non-depolarizing NMBAs, nitrous oxide
  • Mortality: Reduced from >70% to <5% with early dantrolene

⚠️ Post-Crisis Care

ICU monitoring: Continue dantrolene 1 mg/kg q4-6h × 24-48h for recrudescence (25% risk)
Referral: MHAUS hotline (1-800-644-9737), genetic testing, family screening
Documentation: MedAlert bracelet, anesthesia alert in medical record
