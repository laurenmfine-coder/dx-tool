MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Abnormal placental implantation → defective spiral artery remodeling → placental ischemia → release of anti-angiogenic factors (sFlt-1, sEng) → widespread endothelial dysfunction → hypertension + proteinuria + multi-organ damage.

ConditionDefinition
Gestational HTNBP ≥140/90 after 20 weeks WITHOUT proteinuria or end-organ damage
PreeclampsiaBP ≥140/90 after 20 weeks + proteinuria (≥300mg/24h) OR end-organ damage
Severe preeclampsiaBP ≥160/110 OR symptoms: headache, visual changes, RUQ pain, plt <100K, Cr >1.1, elevated LFTs, pulmonary edema
EclampsiaPreeclampsia + new-onset SEIZURES (can occur antepartum, intrapartum, or postpartum)
HELLP syndromeHemolysis (schistocytes, elevated LDH), Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets. Variant of severe preeclampsia
🔑 Risk Factors: Nulliparity, extremes of age, obesity, chronic HTN, DM, renal disease, antiphospholipid syndrome, multiple gestation, prior preeclampsia, family history. Prevention: low-dose aspirin (81mg) starting 12-16 weeks in high-risk patients.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis & Complications
⚠️ HELLP = Emergency: Can occur without severe HTN or proteinuria. Right upper quadrant pain (hepatic capsule distension/subcapsular hematoma). Complications: hepatic rupture, DIC, placental abruption, stroke, renal failure. Delivery is definitive treatment regardless of gestational age.

Complications

Placental abruption, IUGR, eclamptic seizures, stroke, pulmonary edema, hepatic rupture (HELLP), DIC, renal failure, fetal demise.

TREATMENT
Management
ScenarioManagement
Mild preeclampsia <37 wksExpectant management with close monitoring (BP, labs, fetal testing 2×/week). Deliver at 37 weeks
Severe preeclampsia ≥34 wksDELIVER (after stabilization)
Severe preeclampsia <34 wksStabilize (IV MgSO4 + antihypertensives) → corticosteroids for fetal lung maturity → deliver at 34 wks or sooner if deteriorating
EclampsiaIV magnesium sulfate (FIRST-LINE for seizure treatment AND prophylaxis). NOT diazepam or phenytoin. Then deliver
🔑 Magnesium Sulfate: First-line for eclamptic seizures AND seizure prophylaxis in severe preeclampsia. Loading: 4-6g IV over 20 min, then 1-2g/hr maintenance. Monitor: reflexes (first sign of toxicity = loss of DTRs), respiratory rate (>12), urine output (>30 mL/hr). Antidote for Mg toxicity: IV calcium gluconate.

Antihypertensives: IV labetalol, IV hydralazine, or PO nifedipine for BP ≥160/110. Goal: <160/110 (not normal — placental perfusion depends on higher BP).
