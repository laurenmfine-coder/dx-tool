MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Hypertensive emergency = severely elevated BP (typically >180/120) WITH acute end-organ damage (brain, heart, kidneys, eyes, aorta). Hypertensive urgency = severe BP WITHOUT end-organ damage — managed with oral medications and outpatient follow-up.

🔑 The distinction is END-ORGAN DAMAGE, not the BP number. A patient with BP 200/120 and no symptoms may be urgency. A patient with BP 170/110 and pulmonary edema is an emergency. Always assess for target organ damage.

End-Organ Damage Patterns

Target OrganManifestationKey Findings
BrainHypertensive encephalopathy, hemorrhagic stroke, PRESHeadache, AMS, seizures, focal deficits
HeartAcute HF/pulmonary edema, ACS, aortic dissectionDyspnea, chest pain, S3 gallop, pulmonary crackles
KidneysAcute renal failureElevated creatinine, hematuria, proteinuria
EyesHypertensive retinopathyPapilledema, flame hemorrhages, cotton-wool spots
VasculatureAortic dissectionTearing chest/back pain, BP differential between arms
PregnancyEclampsia, HELLP syndromeSeizures, elevated LFTs, thrombocytopenia
⚠️ BP Reduction Speed: Reduce MAP by no more than 25% in the first hour, then to 160/100 over 2-6 hours. Overly aggressive reduction causes watershed infarcts (brain, kidney). Exception: aortic dissection — target SBP <120 within 20 minutes.
CLINICAL
Evaluation

Workup for Suspected Hypertensive Emergency

CBC, BMP (creatinine, K+), UA (protein, RBCs), troponin, BNP, LDH/haptoglobin (MAHA screen), chest X-ray, ECG, head CT (if neuro symptoms), fundoscopy.

🔑 Malignant Hypertension: Severe HTN + microangiopathic hemolytic anemia (schistocytes) + AKI + papilledema. The endothelial damage from extreme BP causes fibrinoid necrosis of arterioles, leading to MAHA — can mimic TTP/HUS on labs.
TREATMENT
Management

IV Antihypertensives by Indication

ScenarioPreferred AgentRationale
General hypertensive emergencyNicardipine or clevidipine (IV CCB)Predictable, titratable, safe in most contexts
Aortic dissectionIV esmolol (then add nitroprusside if needed)β-blocker FIRST to reduce shear stress (dP/dt). Target SBP <120, HR <60
Acute pulmonary edemaIV nitroglycerin or nitroprusside + furosemideVenous and arterial dilation reduces preload and afterload
EclampsiaIV labetalol or hydralazine + magnesium sulfate (for seizures)Safe in pregnancy. Avoid ACE-I/ARBs (teratogenic) and nitroprusside (cyanide risk to fetus)
Pheochromocytoma crisisPhentolamine (α-blocker)α-blockade before β-blockade — unopposed α-stimulation with β-blocker causes worsening HTN
Sympathomimetic (cocaine)Benzodiazepines + nicardipine/phentolamineAvoid β-blockers (unopposed α-stimulation). Benzodiazepines first-line
⚠️ Aortic Dissection Protocol: β-blocker MUST be started FIRST before any vasodilator. Vasodilators alone cause reflex tachycardia → increased aortic shear stress → propagation of dissection. Esmolol IV is ideal (short-acting, titratable).
