MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

The thyroid produces T4 (90%, prohormone) and T3 (10%, active hormone). Regulation: Hypothalamus (TRH) → Pituitary (TSH) → Thyroid (T3/T4) → negative feedback.

🔑 Radioiodine Uptake is the Key Differentiator: HIGH uptake = thyroid is overproducing (Graves, toxic nodule). LOW uptake = thyroid is leaking preformed hormone (thyroiditis) or patient is taking exogenous thyroid hormone. This single test distinguishes the major categories.

Thyroid Emergencies

EmergencyThyroid StormMyxedema Coma

⚠️ In thyroid storm: Give PTU BEFORE iodine — iodine without blocking synthesis first provides substrate for more hormone production (Jod-Basedow effect). Wait 1 hour after PTU to give iodine (Wolff-Chaikoff effect blocks further organification).
TREATMENT
Management

Hyperthyroidism Treatment

OptionDetails
ThionamidesMethimazole (first-line, except 1st trimester) or PTU (preferred in 1st trimester — less teratogenic, and in thyroid storm — blocks T4→T3 conversion)
Radioactive iodine (RAI)Definitive treatment in US. Destroys thyroid tissue. Contraindicated in pregnancy. Usually results in hypothyroidism
SurgeryLarge goiter, compressive symptoms, suspicious nodule, or failed medical therapy
β-blockersSymptomatic relief (propranolol preferred — also blocks T4→T3 conversion)

Hypothyroidism Treatment

Levothyroxine (T4) is the standard replacement. Start 1.6 µg/kg/day in young healthy patients; start low (12.5-25 µg) in elderly/cardiac patients. Monitor TSH in 6-8 weeks.

🔑 Subclinical Thyroid Disease: Subclinical hypothyroidism (elevated TSH, normal T4): treat if TSH >10, symptoms present, or pregnancy. Subclinical hyperthyroidism (low TSH, normal T4): treat if TSH <0.1, age >65, cardiovascular risk, or osteoporosis risk.
