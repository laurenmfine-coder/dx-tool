💧 Urinary Incontinence

Stress, Urge, Overflow & Mixed - Classification & Management

📚 Overview

Urinary incontinence is the involuntary loss of urine. It affects quality of life significantly and is often underreported. Understanding the TYPE of incontinence is crucial for appropriate treatment.

Epidemiology

ParameterValue
Prevalence (women)25-45% (increases with age)
Prevalence (men)5-15% (increases with age)
Nursing home residents50-70%
Most common type (women)Stress or mixed
Most common type (men)Urge (often BPH-related)

📋 Types of Incontinence

STRESS URINARY INCONTINENCE (SUI)

DefinitionInvoluntary leakage with increased abdominal pressure
TriggersCough, sneeze, laugh, exercise, lifting
MechanismUrethral sphincter weakness or urethral hypermobility
Risk factorsVaginal delivery, menopause, obesity, pelvic surgery, chronic cough
Key history"I leak when I cough or exercise"

URGE URINARY INCONTINENCE (UUI) / OVERACTIVE BLADDER

DefinitionInvoluntary leakage with sudden urge to void
TriggersHearing running water, cold, key-in-door, caffeine
MechanismDetrusor overactivity (involuntary contractions)
Risk factorsNeurologic disease, idiopathic, aging, UTI, bladder irritants
Key history"When I gotta go, I GOTTA GO - and sometimes don't make it"

OVERFLOW INCONTINENCE

DefinitionLeakage due to overdistended bladder
PatternConstant dribbling, incomplete emptying, weak stream
MechanismBladder outlet obstruction OR detrusor underactivity
CausesBPH, urethral stricture, neurogenic bladder, medications
Key findingElevated post-void residual (PVR)

MIXED INCONTINENCE

DefinitionCombination of stress AND urge incontinence
PrevalenceVery common, especially in older women
ManagementTreat the predominant/most bothersome symptom first

FUNCTIONAL INCONTINENCE

DefinitionLeakage due to inability to reach toilet in time
CausesMobility issues, cognitive impairment, environmental barriers
KeyBladder function may be normal; problem is external
Quick Differentiation:
  • Stress: Leak WITH activity (cough, sneeze) - no urge
  • Urge: Leak AFTER sudden urge - can't make it to bathroom
  • Overflow: Constant dribbling, weak stream, elevated PVR

🔍 Evaluation

History - Key Questions

  • When do you leak? (triggers)
  • Do you feel an urge before leaking?
  • How often do you void? (frequency)
  • Do you wake at night to void? (nocturia)
  • Is your stream strong or weak?
  • Do you feel like you empty completely?
  • How much fluid do you drink? Caffeine? Alcohol?
  • Medications (diuretics, anticholinergics)

Voiding Diary

3-Day Voiding Diary - Gold standard for characterizing symptoms:
  • Time of each void
  • Volume voided
  • Leakage episodes and triggers
  • Fluid intake
  • Urgency rating

Physical Exam

  • Abdominal exam (distended bladder?)
  • Pelvic exam (atrophy, prolapse, pelvic floor strength)
  • Cough stress test: Visualize leakage with cough (full bladder)
  • Rectal exam (prostate in men, rectal tone)
  • Neurologic exam (perineal sensation, reflexes)

Testing

TestPurpose
UrinalysisRule out UTI, hematuria
Post-void residual (PVR)Elevated in overflow (>200mL concerning)
UrodynamicsComplex cases; before surgery; mixed symptoms
CystoscopyHematuria, recurrent UTI, suspected pathology

💊 Treatment

Stress Incontinence Treatment

LineTreatmentDetails
1stBehavioral/ConservativePelvic floor exercises (Kegels), weight loss, fluid management
2ndDevicesPessary, urethral inserts
3rdSurgeryMidurethral sling (gold standard), Burch colposuspension, bulking agents

Urge Incontinence Treatment

LineTreatmentDetails
1stBehavioralBladder training, timed voiding, fluid management, avoid irritants (caffeine)
2ndMedicationsAntimuscarinics (oxybutynin, tolterodine) or β3-agonist (mirabegron)
3rdAdvancedBotox injection, sacral neuromodulation (InterStim), PTNS
⚠️ Anticholinergic Side Effects:
  • Dry mouth, constipation, blurred vision
  • Cognitive impairment (especially elderly) - avoid in dementia
  • Urinary retention (check PVR)
  • Contraindicated in narrow-angle glaucoma
Mirabegron (β3-agonist) avoids anticholinergic effects but watch for hypertension.

Overflow Incontinence Treatment

  • Treat underlying cause: BPH treatment, discontinue causative meds
  • Catheterization: Intermittent (preferred) or indwelling if needed
  • Surgery: TURP for BPH, urethral dilation for stricture

Pelvic Floor Muscle Training (Kegels)

Proper Technique:
  • Contract pelvic floor muscles (like stopping urine stream)
  • Hold for 5-10 seconds, relax for same duration
  • 10-15 repetitions, 3 sets per day
  • Takes 6-12 weeks to see improvement
  • Physical therapy referral helpful for proper technique

📋 Special Considerations

Transient Causes of Incontinence

DIAPPERS Mnemonic:
  • Delirium
  • Infection (UTI)
  • Atrophic vaginitis
  • Pharmaceuticals (diuretics, sedatives, anticholinergics)
  • Psychological (depression)
  • Excessive urine output (CHF, hyperglycemia)
  • Restricted mobility
  • Stool impaction
Treat reversible causes first!

🧠 Quick Quiz

A 65-year-old woman reports leaking urine when she coughs, sneezes, or exercises. She denies urinary urgency. What type of incontinence does she have?

A. Stress incontinence
B. Urge incontinence
C. Overflow incontinence
D. Functional incontinence
Correct: A - Stress Incontinence
Leakage with increased abdominal pressure (cough, sneeze, exercise) WITHOUT urgency is classic for stress urinary incontinence. This is caused by urethral sphincter weakness or urethral hypermobility.
