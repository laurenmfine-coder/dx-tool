⚙️ Guillain-Barré Syndrome

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Neurology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Post-infectious autoimmune peripheral neuropathy
  • 20/30/40 rule for intubation
  • IVIG or plasmapheresis equally effective
  • Autonomic instability can be life-threatening

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Molecular Mimicry

Post-infectious autoimmune attack on peripheral nerve myelin or axons.

Pathway

Preceding infection (Campylobacter, CMV, EBV)→Antibodies cross-react with gangliosides→Complement activation→Macrophage-mediated demyelination→Ascending weakness

Respiratory Failure Pathway

Progressive weakness can affect respiratory muscles.

Pathway

Ascending paralysis→Intercostal weakness→Diaphragmatic involvement→Reduced vital capacity→Respiratory failure (20-30% need ventilation)

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Areflexia

Mechanism: Disruption of reflex arc at peripheral nerve level

Clinical Significance: Key distinguishing feature from central causes of weakness

Ascending weakness

Mechanism: Longer nerves affected first (length-dependent)

Clinical Significance: Classic pattern: starts distally, moves proximally

Autonomic dysfunction

Mechanism: Involvement of autonomic nerve fibers

Clinical Significance: Arrhythmias, BP lability - cause of mortality
