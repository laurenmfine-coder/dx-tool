📖 Teaching Point

The corpus callosum is the largest white matter commissure, connecting the two cerebral hemispheres. It develops from ANTERIOR to POSTERIOR (genu → body → splenium → rostrum) during weeks 12-20. The rostrum forms LAST despite being the most anterior part. In ACC, the callosal fibers that would normally cross form longitudinal bundles called PROBST BUNDLES that run parallel along the medial walls of the ventricles.

📖 Teaching Point

Key concept: Because the CC develops genu → body → splenium → rostrum, partial ACC (hypogenesis) typically shows an ABSENT POSTERIOR portion with a PRESENT ANTERIOR portion. If you see the reverse (absent genu with present splenium), it's likely a SECONDARY DESTRUCTION (e.g., from a vascular event or infection), not a primary developmental failure. This distinction helps determine etiology.

📖 Teaching Point

ACC associations: ISOLATED (~50%) — often good prognosis, may be incidental. SYNDROMIC (~50%) — Aicardi syndrome, Andermann syndrome, FG syndrome, Walker-Warburg, many metabolic disorders (pyruvate dehydrogenase deficiency, nonketotic hyperglycinemia). CHROMOSOMAL (15-20%) — T8, T13, T18. CNS associations — Dandy-Walker (25% of DWM has ACC), gray matter heterotopia, polymicrogyria, interhemispheric cysts.

📖 Teaching Point

Probst bundles are pathognomonic of ACC on MRI. They indent the medial walls of the lateral ventricles, giving them a crescent shape on coronal images. Despite not crossing, these bundles show that the brain generates the callosal neurons — they just can't find the midline crossing point. Interestingly, some individuals with ACC develop alternative interhemispheric connections through the anterior commissure, which can partially compensate for the absent CC.

📖 Teaching Point

ACC cognitive profile: The CC normally allows rapid interhemispheric transfer for complex tasks. Without it, individuals struggle with: (1) bimanual coordination tasks; (2) transfer of tactile learning from one hand to the other; (3) comprehension of non-literal language (sarcasm, humor, metaphor); (4) complex social inference; (5) novel problem-solving requiring integration of multiple information sources. Compensation through the anterior commissure and other pathways can be remarkably effective.

🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
The child continues to develop well with targeted speech therapy and social skills support. At age 10, he is in mainstream school with accommodations.

Key Takeaways

✓CC develops anterior → posterior: genu → body → splenium → rostrum (rostrum LAST)
✓Partial ACC typically affects POSTERIOR portions (development stops before completion)
✓Classic MRI: absent CSP, parallel ventricles, colpocephaly, elevated 3rd ventricle, Probst bundles
✓Probst bundles = callosal axons that run longitudinally instead of crossing
✓Isolated ACC (~50%): often good prognosis with subtle cognitive deficits
