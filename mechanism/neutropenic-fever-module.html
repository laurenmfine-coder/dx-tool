🦠 Neutropenic Fever

An oncologic emergency requiring immediate empiric antibiotics. Without neutrophils, infection can progress rapidly with minimal signs.

⏱️ 18 min📊 Oncologic Emergency🎯 Oncology/ID

📋 Definition

Neutropenia

ANC <500 cells/μL
OR ANC <1000 expected to fall to <500

Fever

Single oral temp ≥38.3°C (101°F)
OR ≥38.0°C (100.4°F) sustained for 1 hour

⚠️ Antibiotics Within 60 Minutes!

Neutropenic fever is a medical emergency. Start empiric antibiotics within 60 minutes of presentation. Delayed treatment significantly increases mortality.

🔬 Pathophysiology

🎯 Why So Dangerous?

Neutrophils are the first responders to bacterial infection. Without them:
• Infection spreads rapidly with minimal immune response
• Classic signs (pus, erythema, swelling) may be ABSENT
• Bacteremia can progress to septic shock within hours
• GI mucositis allows gut bacteria translocation

💊 Treatment

🔑 Empiric Monotherapy
  • Cefepime (most common choice)
  • Pip-tazo
  • Meropenem (if recent β-lactam exposure)
  • Must cover Pseudomonas!
➕ Add Vancomycin If:
  • Hemodynamic instability
  • Skin/soft tissue infection
  • Catheter-related infection suspected
  • Known MRSA colonization
  • Severe mucositis

Duration

  • Continue until ANC ≥500 AND afebrile for ≥48 hours
  • If source identified: treat for appropriate duration
  • Persistent fever >4-7 days: consider antifungal coverage
