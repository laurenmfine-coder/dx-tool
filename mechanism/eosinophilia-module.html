Eosinophilia Workup

From Allergy to Malignancy

Definition & Classification

Absolute Eosinophil Count (AEC):

  • Mild: 500-1500/μL
  • Moderate: 1500-5000/μL
  • Severe: >5000/μL
  • Hypereosinophilia: >1500/μL on 2 occasions OR tissue eosinophilia with symptoms

Hypereosinophilic Syndrome (HES):

  • AEC >1500/μL for ≥6 months
  • Evidence of organ involvement
  • No secondary cause identified

Eosinophil Biology

Key cytokines (eosinophilopoietins):

  • IL-5: Primary driver of eosinophil production, activation, survival
  • IL-3: Stimulates bone marrow production
  • GM-CSF: Enhances survival and activation

Tissue damage mechanisms:

  • Major Basic Protein (MBP) → epithelial damage
  • Eosinophil Cationic Protein (ECP) → neurotoxicity
  • Eosinophil Peroxidase → oxidative damage
  • Cytokine/chemokine release

💡 IL-5 is THE therapeutic target: mepolizumab, benralizumab block this pathway

Differential Diagnosis (CHINA)

C - Connective tissue/Collagen vascular: EGPA (Churg-Strauss), eosinophilic fasciitis

H - Helminth/Parasitic: Strongyloides, Toxocara, Ascaris, Filaria, Trichinella

I - Idiopathic/HES: Primary clonal (FIP1L1-PDGFRA+), lymphocyte-variant, idiopathic

N - Neoplasm: Hodgkin lymphoma, T-cell lymphoma, mastocytosis, leukemia

A - Allergy/Atopy: Asthma, allergic rhinitis, atopic dermatitis, ABPA, drug reactions

💡 Always check Strongyloides serology before starting steroids - can cause fatal hyperinfection

Systematic Workup

Initial:

  • CBC with differential, smear
  • CMP, LDH, B12 (elevated in clonal disease)
  • IgE level, tryptase
  • Stool O&P × 3, Strongyloides serology

If persistent/severe:

  • CT chest/abdomen (organ involvement)
  • Echo (cardiac eosinophilia - Löffler's)
  • Bone marrow biopsy + flow cytometry
  • FIP1L1-PDGFRA by FISH
  • T-cell receptor rearrangement

Organ-specific:

  • PFTs + HRCT for eosinophilic lung disease
  • EGD/colonoscopy for EoE/GI involvement

🎯 Key Points

  • IL-5 is the key eosinophilopoietin - therapeutic target
  • CHINA mnemonic: Connective tissue, Helminth, Idiopathic, Neoplasm, Allergy
  • Always rule out Strongyloides before steroids
  • FIP1L1-PDGFRA mutation = myeloproliferative HES, responds to imatinib
  • Cardiac involvement (Löffler's) is most dangerous complication
