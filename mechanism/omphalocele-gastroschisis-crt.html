📖 Teaching Point

Key distinguishing features: OMPHALOCELE — AT umbilicus, membrane-covered, contains liver ± bowel, associated with chromosomal anomalies (Trisomy 13, 18, 21), Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome, cardiac defects. GASTROSCHISIS — RIGHT of umbilicus, NO membrane, bowel only (no liver), associated with young maternal age, smoking, vasoactive drugs (NSAIDs, pseudoephedrine); chromosomal anomalies are RARE.

📖 Teaching Point

OMPHALOCELE embryology: Failure of the 4 body wall folds (cephalic, caudal, 2 lateral) to close → persistent communication between abdominal cavity and umbilical cord → contents remain in the herniated position. GASTROSCHISIS embryology: Leading theory is involution of the right umbilical vein → ischemic weakness in the paraumbilical body wall → rupture, typically to the RIGHT of the cord insertion. This explains why gastroschisis is always right-sided.

📖 Teaching Point

Association comparison: OMPHALOCELE — chromosomal (30-50%), cardiac (30-50%), Beckwith-Wiedemann, pentalogy of Cantrell. Prognosis depends on associated anomalies more than the defect itself. GASTROSCHISIS — chromosomal anomalies RARE (<1%), intestinal atresia (10-15%) from vascular compromise, and short gut from bowel damage. Prognosis is generally better because it's usually an isolated defect.

📖 Teaching Point

Gastroschisis bowel management: The fibrous peel on the bowel is caused by prolonged exposure to amniotic fluid (chemical peritonitis from fetal urine and meconium). This thickening impairs motility and may take weeks to resolve postoperatively. Staged reduction using a silo allows gravity-assisted return of bowel to the abdomen over 3-7 days, avoiding abdominal compartment syndrome.

📖 Teaching Point

Gastroschisis outcomes: Overall survival >90%. Main morbidity is prolonged hospitalization (average 30-50 days) due to slow return of bowel function. The damaged serosa causes chronic dysmotility. TPN-associated liver disease is a concern with prolonged IV nutrition. Intestinal atresia (10-15%) occurs from mesenteric vascular compromise and worsens prognosis. 'Closing' gastroschisis (atretic bowel with very small defect) has the worst outcomes.

Stage 6 of 6
🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
Both families are followed long-term. The gastroschisis baby eventually feeds normally by 3 months. The omphalocele baby's course is dominated by the Trisomy 18 prognosis.

Key Takeaways

✓Omphalocele: AT umbilicus, membrane-covered, liver ± bowel, high genetic risk (30-50%)
✓Gastroschisis: RIGHT of umbilicus, NO membrane, bowel only, low genetic risk (<1%)
✓Omphalocele = failure of midgut return; Gastroschisis = vascular disruption (R umbilical vein)
✓Omphalocele prognosis depends on associated anomalies; Gastroschisis >90% survival
✓Gastroschisis: sterile bag/silo, aggressive fluids, staged reduction
✓Prolonged dysmotility is the major gastroschisis morbidity (damaged serosa)
✓Both are distinguishable on prenatal ultrasound
