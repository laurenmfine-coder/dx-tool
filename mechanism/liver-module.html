Cirrhosis Complications

Portal Hypertension

  • • Varices: Portosystemic shunts → GI bleed
  • • Ascites: ↑Hydrostatic + ↓Albumin
  • • Splenomegaly: → Thrombocytopenia

Synthetic Failure

  • • Coagulopathy: ↓Factors → ↑INR
  • • Hypoalbuminemia: → Edema
  • • Hypoglycemia: ↓Gluconeogenesis

Hepatic Encephalopathy

Mechanism: ↓NH₃ clearance → Brain toxicity
Triggers: GI bleed, infection, constipation
Signs: Asterixis, confusion → coma
Tx: Lactulose, Rifaximin

Hepatorenal Syndrome

Mechanism: Splanchnic vasodilation → Renal vasoconstriction
Labs: FeNa <1%, bland sediment
Tx: Albumin + vasoconstrictors, transplant

SBP (Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis)

Mechanism: Bacterial translocation into low-protein ascites
Dx: Ascites PMN >250/mm³
Bugs: E. coli, Klebsiella, Strep
Tx: Ceftriaxone + Albumin. Ppx: Fluoroquinolone

⚠️ Acute Liver Failure

Definition: Coagulopathy (INR ≥1.5) + Encephalopathy in patient WITHOUT pre-existing liver disease

Causes

  • • Acetaminophen (most common US)
  • • Viral hepatitis (HAV, HBV)
  • • Drug-induced (idiosyncratic)
  • • Autoimmune hepatitis
  • • Wilson's disease, Budd-Chiari
  • • Ischemic ("shock liver")
  • • AFLP, HELLP (pregnancy)

Management

  • • NAC for acetaminophen (and possibly others)
  • • Monitor for cerebral edema
  • • Correct coagulopathy only if bleeding
  • • Watch for hypoglycemia
  • • Early transplant evaluation
