MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Chronic alcohol enhances GABA (inhibitory) and inhibits NMDA/glutamate (excitatory). Abrupt cessation → loss of inhibition + unopposed excitation → CNS hyperexcitability → seizures, autonomic storm, delirium tremens.

Timeline

Time After Last DrinkManifestation
6-12 hoursMinor withdrawal: tremor, anxiety, insomnia, tachycardia, diaphoresis, GI upset
12-24 hoursAlcoholic hallucinosis: visual/auditory/tactile hallucinations with CLEAR sensorium (patient knows they're not real)
24-48 hoursWithdrawal seizures: generalized tonic-clonic, brief, often in clusters. Risk of status epilepticus
48-96 hoursDelirium tremens (DT): delirium + autonomic storm (severe tachycardia, hypertension, hyperthermia, diaphoresis) + hallucinations with IMPAIRED sensorium. Mortality: 5-15% untreated
CLINICAL
Assessment & Risk Factors
⚠️ Delirium Tremens Risk Factors: Prior DT or withdrawal seizures (#1), prolonged heavy drinking, older age, concurrent illness, elevated BAL on admission, prior detoxification episodes (kindling effect), CIWA-Ar score >20
🔑 CIWA-Ar (Clinical Institute Withdrawal Assessment): Validated scoring tool (0-67). Measures: nausea, tremor, anxiety, agitation, diaphoresis, tactile/auditory/visual disturbances, headache, orientation. Score guides treatment:
• <10: Mild (may not need meds)
• 10-18: Moderate (treatment recommended)
• >20: Severe (aggressive treatment, high DT risk)

Key Distinction

Alcoholic hallucinosis: hallucinations with CLEAR sensorium (oriented, conversational). DT: hallucinations with IMPAIRED sensorium (confused, disoriented, agitated). DT is far more dangerous.

TREATMENT
Management
TreatmentDetails
Benzodiazepines (first-line)Long-acting: diazepam, chlordiazepoxide (self-tapering pharmacokinetics). Short-acting: lorazepam (preferred in hepatic dysfunction — no active metabolites, LLOT: Lorazepam for Liver, Oxazepam, Temazepam). Symptom-triggered dosing (CIWA-based) preferred over fixed schedule
Thiamine (B1)Give BEFORE glucose (glucose metabolism consumes thiamine → can precipitate Wernicke encephalopathy). Wernicke triad: confusion + ataxia + ophthalmoplegia. Give thiamine 500 mg IV × 3 days
Folate, Mg²⁺, electrolytesAlcoholics are deficient. Hypomagnesemia common → can cause refractory hypokalemia and seizures
PhenobarbitalAdjunct or alternative for severe/refractory withdrawal. Direct GABA agonist (works even if BZD-resistant)
🔑 Alcohol and benzo withdrawal CAN KILL. Unlike opioid withdrawal (uncomfortable but not fatal), alcohol/benzo withdrawal can cause seizures, DT, autonomic storm, and death. This is why benzodiazepines are the cornerstone of treatment — cross-tolerance with alcohol at GABA receptor.
