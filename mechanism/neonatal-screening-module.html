MECHANISM
Universal Newborn Screening

Newborn screening (NBS) identifies conditions that are treatable but not clinically apparent at birth. The RUSP (Recommended Uniform Screening Panel) includes 35 core conditions and 26 secondary conditions. Screening uses dried blood spot (heel stick) collected 24-48 hours after birth.

CLINICAL
Key Conditions in Detail

Congenital Hypothyroidism

Most common preventable cause of intellectual disability worldwide. Screening measures TSH (primary) \u00b1 T4. Elevated TSH = start levothyroxine within 2 weeks of life. Untreated: cretinism (irreversible intellectual disability, growth failure, coarse features).

Phenylketonuria (PKU)

Autosomal recessive deficiency of phenylalanine hydroxylase. Phenylalanine accumulates \u2192 neurotoxicity. Treatment: lifelong low-phenylalanine diet. Musty/mousy body odor is a classic exam finding. Maternal PKU: uncontrolled phenylalanine during pregnancy causes microcephaly, cardiac defects, and intellectual disability in the fetus (even if fetus is unaffected carrier).

Galactosemia

Galactose-1-phosphate uridylyltransferase (GALT) deficiency. Presents in first days of life after milk feeding: jaundice, hepatomegaly, E. coli sepsis, cataracts. Treatment: eliminate ALL galactose (no breast milk or regular formula \u2192 soy formula). Can be fatal if missed.

\u26a0\ufe0f Galactosemia and breastfeeding: Unlike most metabolic conditions, galactosemia is an ABSOLUTE contraindication to breastfeeding. Breast milk contains lactose (glucose + galactose). This is a frequently tested board concept.

SCID (Severe Combined Immunodeficiency)

Screened by TREC (T-cell receptor excision circles) assay on dried blood spot. Low/absent TRECs indicate T-cell lymphopenia. Early detection allows hematopoietic stem cell transplant before fatal infections develop. Without treatment, death occurs within 1-2 years from opportunistic infections.

TREATMENT
Follow-Up & Management

Positive Screen Protocol

A positive newborn screen is NOT diagnostic \u2014 it requires confirmatory testing. False positives are common (especially for CF, CAH). Confirmatory tests vary by condition: sweat chloride for CF, serum amino acids for PKU, hemoglobin electrophoresis for sickle cell, TSH/free T4 for hypothyroidism.

\U0001f511 Critical congenital heart disease screening: Pulse oximetry measures pre-ductal (right hand) and post-ductal (either foot) saturations. A positive screen is any reading <90%, any reading <95% on 3 measurements, or >3% difference between pre- and post-ductal readings. Positive screen \u2192 echocardiography.
