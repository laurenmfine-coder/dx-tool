DECISION 1
Initial Workup
✅
Correct!
Correct! With multiple risk factors and high clinical suspicion (Wells score ≥7), skip D-dimer and go straight to CTPA. D-dimer is only useful to rule OUT PE in LOW probability patients. Start empiric anticoagulation while awaiting CT.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
D-dimer is useful only in LOW pretest probability. This patient has Wells ≥7 (recent surgery +1.5, HR >100 +1.5, immobilization +1.5, PE most likely diagnosis +3). A negative D-dimer cannot reliably exclude PE here. You've delayed diagnosis.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
While CXR and EKG are part of the workup, they should not delay CTPA in high-probability PE. CXR is often normal in PE. The classic S1Q3T3 on EKG is seen in <20% of PE cases.

Correct! This is a submassive PE (hemodynamically stable but with RV strain markers: elevated troponin, BNP, and RV/LV ratio >0.9). Heparin drip, ICU admission, and close monitoring for hemodynamic deterioration. Systemic thrombolytics are reserved for massive PE (with hemodynamic instability).

❌
Suboptimal Choice
This is submassive PE with RV strain — she needs ICU-level monitoring. ~5% of submassive PE patients decompensate. IV heparin (not Lovenox) is preferred initially for titratability in case she needs escalation to thrombolytics or catheter-directed therapy.
DECISION 3
ICU Day 1
Six hours later, the patient becomes more tachycardic (HR 135), BP drops to 78/50, and she is confused. Repeat echo shows severely dilated RV with septal bowing. Despite 2L NS bolus, she remains hypotensive.
✅
Correct!
Correct! She has now progressed to massive PE (hemodynamic instability despite resuscitation). Alteplase 100mg IV over 2 hours is the standard regimen. The benefit of clot lysis now outweighs the bleeding risk. If tPA is contraindicated, catheter-directed therapy or surgical embolectomy are alternatives.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Caution with fluids in massive PE! The RV is already dilated and failing. Excess volume worsens RV distension, shifts the septum further leftward, and compromises LV filling (interventricular dependence). Small fluid bolus is reasonable but the root cause (clot burden) must be addressed.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Heparin prevents clot propagation but does NOT lyse existing clot. With hemodynamic collapse, she needs active clot removal — either systemic thrombolytics, catheter-directed therapy, or surgical embolectomy. Delay increases mortality.
RESOLUTION
Resolution
tPA is administered. Within 90 minutes, HR decreases to 95, BP improves to 110/72, and SpO2 rises to 96% on 2L NC. Repeat echo shows improved RV function.
OUTCOME
🏆 Excellent Clinical Reasoning
Excellent management! Long-term plan: transition to DOAC (rivaroxaban or apixaban) for minimum 3 months. Given the provoked nature (surgery + OCP), discontinue OCPs and consider IUD. Hypercoagulability workup (Factor V Leiden, prothrombin mutation, antiphospholipid) after completing anticoagulation.
