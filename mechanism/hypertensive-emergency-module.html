🔑 The PRESENCE of end-organ damage defines emergency, not the absolute BP number. A patient with BP 200/110 + acute pulmonary edema = emergency. A patient with BP 220/120 + no symptoms = urgency. End-organ damage includes: encephalopathy, stroke, ACS, acute HF/pulmonary edema, aortic dissection, AKI, eclampsia, retinal hemorrhages/papilledema.

⚠️ PRES (Posterior Reversible Encephalopathy Syndrome): Vasogenic edema in posterior circulation from loss of cerebral autoregulation. Headache, visual changes, seizures, AMS. Reversible with BP control. MRI shows bilateral parieto-occipital white matter edema.
TREATMENT
Management

BP Reduction Goals

🔑 Do NOT lower BP too fast! Reduce MAP by no more than 25% in the first hour, then to 160/100 over next 2-6 hours, then gradually to normal over 24-48h. Too-rapid reduction → cerebral/coronary/renal hypoperfusion (watershed infarcts).

Exceptions (lower aggressively):
• Aortic dissection: SBP <120 + HR <60 within minutes
• Eclampsia: SBP <160 within 30-60 min
• Ischemic stroke with thrombolytic candidacy: <185/110 before tPA
