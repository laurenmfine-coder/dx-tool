🔬 Sepsis-3 Definitions (2016)

The modern definitions emphasize organ dysfunction rather than inflammation.

⚠️

Sepsis

Definition: Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host response to infection.

Operationally: ≥2 point increase in SOFA score due to infection.

The old SIRS criteria (temp, HR, RR, WBC) are no longer used to define sepsis.

🚨

Septic Shock

High Yield

Sepsis PLUS both:

  • Vasopressors needed to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation

Mortality ~40% — true medical emergency.

💡
qSOFA: Bedside Screening Tool

RR ≥22 + Altered mental status + SBP ≤100. Score ≥2 suggests high risk but should NOT delay treatment — it's for risk stratification, not diagnosis.

SOFA Score Components
Resp (P/F) + Coag (Plt) + Liver (Bili) + CV (MAP/pressors) + CNS (GCS) + Renal (Cr/UOP)
Each organ system scored 0-4 points. Total range 0-24. ≥2 point increase = sepsis.

🧬 The Inflammatory Cascade

Sepsis represents a dysregulated host response with both pro-inflammatory and immunosuppressive phases.

🔄

🎯
Glycocalyx Breakdown

The endothelial glycocalyx normally prevents capillary leak. In sepsis, inflammatory mediators degrade this layer → third-spacing, edema, and poor tissue perfusion despite adequate volume.

⚠️
Lactate ≠ Just Hypoperfusion

In sepsis, elevated lactate results from both tissue hypoxia AND accelerated aerobic glycolysis (epinephrine-driven). Lactate clearance is a better marker than absolute value.

💔 Classification of Shock

All shock results in inadequate tissue perfusion, but the underlying mechanism differs.

🔥 Distributive

Vasodilation & maldistribution of flow

Examples: Sepsis, anaphylaxis, neurogenic

CVP: ↓/N CO: ↑→↓ SVR: ↓↓
💧 Hypovolemic

Volume loss (hemorrhage, dehydration)

Examples: Hemorrhage, GI losses, burns

CVP: ↓↓ CO: ↓↓ SVR: ↑↑
💔 Cardiogenic

Pump failure

Examples: MI, cardiomyopathy, arrhythmia

CVP: ↑↑ CO: ↓↓ SVR: ↑↑
🚧 Obstructive

Mechanical obstruction to flow

Examples: PE, tamponade, tension PTX

CVP: ↑ CO: ↓↓ SVR: ↑↑
⚠️
Mixed Shock is Common!

A septic patient may also be hypovolemic (third-spacing) or develop cardiogenic shock (sepsis-induced cardiomyopathy). Always reassess hemodynamics after initial resuscitation.

💉 Hour-1 Sepsis Bundle

Time-critical interventions that must begin immediately upon recognition.

🩸 Measure Lactate

If lactate >2 mmol/L, remeasure within 2-4 hours to guide resuscitation. Target: lactate normalization or ≥10% decrease.

🧫 Obtain Blood Cultures

Before antibiotics if possible, but do NOT delay antibiotics >45 min. Two sets from different sites.

💊 Administer Broad-Spectrum Antibiotics

Within 1 hour of sepsis recognition. Each hour of delay increases mortality ~7%. Cover likely pathogens based on source.

💧 Begin Fluid Resuscitation

30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4. Reassess volume responsiveness after bolus — avoid fluid overload!

🎯

Resuscitation Targets

  • MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Lactate normalizing
  • Urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr
  • Capillary refill <3 seconds
  • Mental status improving
💡
Fluid Responsiveness

Static measures (CVP) poorly predict response. Use dynamic measures: passive leg raise, pulse pressure variation, IVC collapsibility. Only ~50% of ICU patients are fluid responsive!

💊 Vasopressor Selection

When fluids fail to restore adequate perfusion.

Agent Receptors Primary Effect When to Use
Norepinephrine α1 >> β1 ↑ SVR, mild ↑ CO First-line for septic shock
Vasopressin V1 ↑ SVR (vasoconstriction) Second-line adjunct to norepi
Epinephrine β1 > α1 ↑ CO and ↑ SVR Refractory shock, anaphylaxis
Dobutamine β1 > β2 ↑ CO (inotropy) Cardiogenic component
Phenylephrine α1 pure ↑ SVR only Avoid in sepsis (↓ CO)
🎯
Surviving Sepsis Guidelines

Norepinephrine first → Add vasopressin if norepi >0.25 mcg/kg/min → Add epinephrine for refractory shock. Consider stress-dose hydrocortisone (200mg/day) if vasopressor-dependent.
