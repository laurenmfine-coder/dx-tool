MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

PE = thrombus (usually from deep leg veins) embolizes to pulmonary vasculature → V/Q mismatch → hypoxemia + increased pulmonary vascular resistance → right heart strain.

Virchow Triad (DVT/PE Risk Factors)

FactorExamples
StasisImmobilization, long travel, surgery, paralysis, CHF
Endothelial injurySurgery, trauma, central lines, prior DVT
HypercoagulabilityFactor V Leiden (#1 inherited), malignancy, OCP/HRT, pregnancy, antiphospholipid syndrome, protein C/S deficiency
🔑 Factor V Leiden = most common inherited thrombophilia. Resistance to activated protein C. Heterozygous: 5-10× VTE risk. Homozygous: 50-100× risk.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis

Clinical Presentation

Acute dyspnea (#1 symptom), pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, tachypnea, hemoptysis, syncope (massive PE with RV failure). EKG: sinus tachycardia (most common), S1Q3T3 (classic but insensitive), right heart strain pattern.

Diagnostic Algorithm

StepAction
1. Pre-test probabilityWells score: DVT symptoms, HR >100, immobilization/surgery, prior VTE, hemoptysis, malignancy, PE most likely diagnosis
2. Low probabilityD-dimer (ELISA). Negative D-dimer RULES OUT PE (high NPV). Age-adjusted: threshold = age × 10 for >50 years
3. High probability or + D-dimerCT pulmonary angiography (CTPA) — gold standard imaging
4. CTPA unavailableV/Q scan (perfusion defects without ventilation defects). Best if normal CXR
⚠️ Massive PE: Hemodynamic instability (SBP <90 or vasopressor requirement) + RV dysfunction. Mortality >50% without treatment. → Systemic thrombolytics (alteplase) or catheter-directed therapy or surgical embolectomy.
TREATMENT
Management
SeverityTreatment
Low-risk (hemodynamically stable, no RV strain)Anticoagulation: DOAC (rivaroxaban or apixaban — no bridging needed) OR LMWH bridge → warfarin (INR 2-3)
Submassive (stable but RV dysfunction on echo/CT + elevated troponin)Anticoagulation + close monitoring. Consider catheter-directed therapy or thrombolytics if deteriorating
Massive (hemodynamic instability)Systemic thrombolytics (alteplase). If contraindicated: catheter-directed therapy or surgical embolectomy

Duration of Anticoagulation

ScenarioDuration
Provoked (surgery, trauma, OCP)3 months
Unprovoked (first episode)≥3 months, consider indefinite (risk-benefit)
Recurrent unprovoked or cancer-associatedIndefinite
🔑 IVC filter: Only if anticoagulation is absolutely contraindicated (active hemorrhage) AND acute VTE. Retrievable preferred. Does NOT replace anticoagulation — resume AC as soon as safe.
