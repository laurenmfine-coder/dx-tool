Stages of Labor & Delivery

Normal labor progression, cardinal movements, arrest disorders, and indications for operative delivery.

📊 Stages of Labor

Four Stages

StageDefinitionDuration (Nulliparous)Key Events
First — LatentOnset of contractions to 6 cm dilationUp to 20 hoursIrregular contractions, slow cervical change; may send home if not in active labor
First — Active6 cm to complete (10 cm) dilation~0.5-0.7 cm/hr (varies)Regular contractions q2-3min, active cervical change; admit if not already
SecondComplete dilation to delivery of infant<3 hrs (nullip), <2 hrs (multip)Maternal pushing effort, descent of fetal head, cardinal movements
ThirdDelivery of infant to delivery of placentaUp to 30 minutesPlacental separation and delivery; manage with gentle traction + oxytocin
Fourth (unofficial)First 1-2 hours after placental delivery1-2 hoursMonitor for hemorrhage, uterine atony, vital signs; fundal massage

Cardinal Movements of Labor

  • 1. Engagement: Biparietal diameter passes pelvic inlet (station 0)
  • 2. Descent: Continuous movement through pelvis driven by contractions and pushing
  • 3. Flexion: Chin to chest — smallest diameter (suboccipitobregmatic) presents
  • 4. Internal rotation: Occiput rotates from transverse to anterior (OA position)
  • 5. Extension: Head extends under symphysis pubis, delivering face
  • 6. External rotation (restitution): Head rotates to align with shoulders
  • 7. Expulsion: Anterior then posterior shoulder delivers, followed by body

💎 Bishop Score

Assesses cervical readiness for induction. Components: Dilation (0-3), Effacement (0-3), Station (-3 to +2 → scored 0-3), Consistency (firm to soft), Position (posterior to anterior). Score ≥8 = favorable cervix, likely successful induction. Score <6 = unfavorable, consider cervical ripening first.

⚠️ Abnormal Labor & Operative Delivery

Arrest Disorders

DisorderDefinitionManagement
Protracted active phase<1 cm/hr dilation in active laborAssess 3 Ps: Power (adequate contractions?), Passenger (fetal size/position?), Passage (pelvic adequacy?). Consider amniotomy, oxytocin augmentation.
Arrest of dilationNo cervical change for ≥6 hours with adequate contractions (or ≥4 hrs with oxytocin + amniotomy)If truly arrested with adequate contractions → cesarean delivery
Arrest of descentNo descent for ≥3 hours (nullip pushing) or ≥2 hours (multip)Evaluate position (occiput posterior?); consider operative vaginal delivery or cesarean

Indications for Cesarean Delivery

  • Emergent: Cord prolapse, placental abruption with fetal distress, uterine rupture, non-reassuring fetal status unresponsive to resuscitation
  • Urgent: Failed induction, arrest of labor, failed operative vaginal delivery
  • Scheduled: Prior classical (vertical) uterine incision, placenta previa, malpresentation (transverse lie, breech in some protocols), active herpes outbreak, HIV with high viral load

⚠️ Shoulder Dystocia = Emergency

Head delivers but anterior shoulder impacted behind pubic symphysis. McRoberts maneuver FIRST (hyperflexion of maternal thighs). Then suprapubic pressure (NOT fundal pressure — can worsen). If these fail: episiotomy, Rubin/Woods corkscrew maneuvers, delivery of posterior arm. Complication: brachial plexus injury (Erb palsy).
