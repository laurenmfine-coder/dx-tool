Urticaria & Angioedema Mechanisms

Mast Cell Activation & Bradykinin Pathways

Mast Cell-Mediated Urticaria

Pathway: Mast cell activation → histamine + other mediators → vasodilation, increased permeability

Triggers:

  • IgE-mediated: Foods, drugs, latex, insect venom
  • Direct activation: Opioids, radiocontrast, certain drugs
  • Autoimmune: IgG anti-FcεRI or anti-IgE autoantibodies
  • Physical: Dermatographism, cold, cholinergic, solar

💡 Chronic spontaneous urticaria: ~40% have autoimmune component (autoantibodies)

Histamine vs Bradykinin

Histamine-Mediated (mast cell):

  • Urticaria with pruritus
  • Responds to antihistamines
  • Rapid onset/resolution
  • Associated with systemic symptoms

Bradykinin-Mediated:

  • Angioedema WITHOUT urticaria
  • Does NOT respond to antihistamines, steroids, epinephrine
  • Slower onset, longer duration (24-72 hours)
  • ACE-inhibitor induced, HAE, acquired angioedema

💡 Key distinction: If antihistamines don't work and no urticaria → think bradykinin

Hereditary Angioedema (HAE)

Types:

  • Type 1 (85%): Low C4, low C1-INH level and function
  • Type 2 (15%): Low C4, normal C1-INH level, low function
  • HAE-nC1INH: Normal C1-INH, FXII or other mutations

Pathophysiology:

  1. C1-INH deficiency → uncontrolled contact system activation
  2. Factor XII activates kallikrein
  3. Kallikrein cleaves HMWK → bradykinin
  4. Bradykinin → B2 receptors → vasodilation, edema

💡 C4 is low during AND between attacks - excellent screening test

Chronic Urticaria Workup

Definition: Urticaria ≥6 weeks

Limited workup needed:

  • CBC (eosinophilia, basophilia)
  • CMP (thyroid, hepatic causes)
  • ESR/CRP (systemic inflammation)

Extended if indicated:

  • Anti-TPO (thyroid autoimmunity)
  • ANA (lupus-associated)
  • Autologous serum skin test (autoimmune urticaria)
  • Skin biopsy if urticarial vasculitis suspected

💡 >80% of chronic urticaria cases remain idiopathic

🎯 Key Points

  • Histamine urticaria: pruritic, antihistamine-responsive, wheals
  • Bradykinin angioedema: NO urticaria, NO response to antihistamines/epi
  • HAE: C1-INH deficiency → bradykinin excess; C4 always low
  • Chronic urticaria: ~40% autoimmune, >80% idiopathic
  • Key distinction: isolated angioedema without urticaria = bradykinin pathway
