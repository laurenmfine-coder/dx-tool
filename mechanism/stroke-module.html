CLINICAL
Diagnosis & tPA Criteria
⚠️ "Time is Brain": 1.9 million neurons die per minute in untreated stroke.
IV tPA (alteplase): within 4.5 hours of symptom onset (3h for some exclusions). Door-to-needle ≤60 min.
Mechanical thrombectomy: large vessel occlusion (LVO), up to 24 hours in select patients (DAWN, DEFUSE-3 trials — perfusion mismatch on imaging).

tPA Contraindications (Key)

Active internal bleeding, recent surgery/trauma (14 days), prior ICH, BP >185/110 (must lower first), platelets <100K, INR >1.7, glucose <50 (hypoglycemia mimics stroke), stroke/head trauma within 3 months.

🔑 Stroke Mimics to Exclude: Hypoglycemia (check glucose FIRST — most important), seizure with Todd paralysis, complicated migraine, brain tumor, conversion disorder. Always check glucose before tPA.

Imaging

Non-contrast CT first (rule out hemorrhage — takes 5 minutes). CT normal in early ischemic stroke. CTA for vessel occlusion. MRI (DWI) most sensitive for early ischemia but takes longer.

SAHSecure aneurysm (coiling preferred > clipping) within 24h. Nimodipine 21 days (prevent vasospasm). Monitor for hydrocephalus, rebleeding, vasospasm (days 4-14)Screen first-degree relatives if ≥2 family members affected
🔑 SAH Vasospasm: Peak days 4-14 post-bleed. Presents as new neurologic deficit. Nimodipine (CCB) is neuroprotective (does NOT prevent vasospasm but improves outcomes). Treatment of vasospasm: "triple-H" therapy (hypertension, hemodilution, hypervolemia) + intra-arterial vasodilators.
