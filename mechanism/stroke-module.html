Stroke

Ischemic vs Hemorrhagic: Time-Critical Diagnosis and Management
⏱️ ~50 minutes 📚 18 concepts ❓ 8 MCQs
1 Stroke Classification
🔴 Ischemic vs Hemorrhagic

Ischemic stroke (87%): Arterial occlusion → tissue infarction

  • Large vessel atherosclerosis: Carotid, vertebral, intracranial arteries
  • Cardioembolism: AFib, valve disease, LV thrombus, PFO
  • Small vessel (lacunar): Lipohyalinosis of penetrating arteries
  • Other: Dissection, vasculitis, hypercoagulable states
  • Cryptogenic: No identified cause (~25%)

Hemorrhagic stroke (13%):

  • Intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH): Bleeding into brain parenchyma - HTN, amyloid angiopathy, AVM
  • Subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH): Bleeding into subarachnoid space - usually aneurysm rupture
⚠️ CRITICAL: Cannot Distinguish Clinically
You CANNOT reliably differentiate ischemic from hemorrhagic stroke by clinical exam alone. Headache, vomiting, and decreased consciousness suggest hemorrhage but are not diagnostic. ALWAYS get CT head before any treatment decisions.
2 Ischemic Stroke Pathophysiology
⏰ The Ischemic Cascade

When cerebral blood flow drops below ~20 mL/100g/min:

  • Seconds-minutes: ATP depletion → Na/K pump failure → cellular depolarization
  • Minutes: Glutamate release → excitotoxicity → Ca²⁺ influx
  • Minutes-hours: Free radical formation, inflammation, apoptosis
  • Hours-days: Edema peaks (day 3-5), potential herniation
🎯 Core vs Penumbra

Ischemic core: Irreversibly damaged tissue (CBF <10 mL/100g/min)

Penumbra: At-risk but salvageable tissue surrounding the core - THIS IS THE TREATMENT TARGET

Goal of acute stroke therapy: Save the penumbra before it becomes core.

3 Vascular Territory Syndromes
🗺️ Anterior Circulation (Carotid)

MCA (most common):

  • Contralateral face/arm > leg weakness and sensory loss
  • Aphasia (dominant hemisphere) or neglect (non-dominant)
  • Gaze preference toward lesion

ACA:

  • Contralateral leg > arm weakness
  • Personality changes, abulia (if bilateral)
  • Urinary incontinence
🗺️ Posterior Circulation (Vertebrobasilar)

PCA:

  • Contralateral homonymous hemianopia
  • Memory impairment (if bilateral)
  • Alexia without agraphia (dominant)

Brainstem/Cerebellum:

  • Crossed signs (ipsilateral face, contralateral body)
  • Vertigo, diplopia, dysarthria, dysphagia
  • Ataxia, nystagmus
  • Decreased consciousness (basilar)
💎 Lacunar Syndromes (Small Vessel)
Pure motor: Face/arm/leg weakness, no sensory/visual/cognitive deficits (internal capsule)
Pure sensory: Face/arm/leg numbness (thalamus)
Ataxic hemiparesis: Weakness + ipsilateral ataxia (pons)
Dysarthria-clumsy hand: Slurred speech + hand weakness (pons)
4 Acute Stroke Assessment
🚨 Acute Stroke Protocol
1
ABCs + glucose - Hypoglycemia mimics stroke!
2
Establish time last known well (LKW) - Critical for treatment eligibility
3
NIHSS score - Quantify deficit severity (0-42)
4
CT head without contrast - Rule out hemorrhage (door-to-CT <25 min)
5
CTA head/neck - Identify large vessel occlusion for thrombectomy
6
Consider CT perfusion - If LKW >6 hours or wake-up stroke
📊 NIHSS: Key Components
Score RangeSeverityImplications
0-4MinorMay not benefit from thrombolysis; consider other causes
5-15ModerateGood thrombolysis candidates
16-20Moderate-SevereConsider thrombectomy if LVO
21-42SevereHigher hemorrhage risk; thrombectomy may still help
5 Acute Ischemic Stroke Treatment
💉 IV Thrombolysis (Alteplase/Tenecteplase)

Time window: Within 4.5 hours of LKW (3 hours if >80 y/o, on anticoagulants, or severe stroke + DM)

Dose: Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90mg), 10% bolus, 90% infusion over 1 hour

Key exclusions:

  • Hemorrhage on CT
  • BP >185/110 despite treatment
  • Platelets <100,000
  • INR >1.7 or therapeutic anticoagulation
  • Recent major surgery/trauma
  • Prior ICH

Target: Door-to-needle <60 minutes (ideally <45)

🔧 Mechanical Thrombectomy

Indication: Large vessel occlusion (ICA, M1, sometimes M2, basilar)

Time window:

  • 0-6 hours: Based on clinical criteria (NIHSS ≥6, no large core)
  • 6-24 hours: Based on perfusion imaging (DAWN/DEFUSE 3 criteria) - mismatch between clinical deficit and infarct core

Target: Door-to-groin puncture <90 minutes

NNT: ~2.5 to reduce disability by 1 level (remarkably effective!)

🩸 BP Management in Acute Stroke

Before thrombolysis: Must be <185/110 (use labetalol, nicardipine)

After thrombolysis: Maintain <180/105 for 24 hours

No thrombolysis planned: Permissive HTN (don't treat unless >220/120) - the brain needs perfusion pressure!

ICH: Target SBP <140 (reduces hematoma expansion)

6 Secondary Prevention
💊 Antithrombotic Therapy

Non-cardioembolic stroke:

  • Aspirin 81-325mg daily OR clopidogrel 75mg daily
  • Dual antiplatelet (ASA + clopidogrel) for 21 days if minor stroke/TIA (POINT, CHANCE trials)

Cardioembolic (AFib):

  • Anticoagulation with DOAC (preferred) or warfarin
  • Timing: Start 4-14 days after stroke depending on size/hemorrhagic transformation risk
🎯 Risk Factor Management
  • BP: Target <130/80 (after acute phase)
  • Statin: High-intensity (atorvastatin 80mg) regardless of LDL
  • Diabetes: Optimize control
  • Smoking cessation
  • Carotid stenosis: CEA if symptomatic 70-99% stenosis
