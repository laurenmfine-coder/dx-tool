⚙️ Kawasaki Disease

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Pediatrics

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Diagnosis: fever ≥5 days + 4 of 5 criteria (or 3 + coronary changes)
  • IVIG + aspirin within 10 days prevents coronary aneurysms
  • Most common cause of acquired heart disease in children (developed countries)
  • Echo at diagnosis, 2 weeks, and 6-8 weeks to monitor coronaries

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Vasculitis Cascade

Systemic inflammation targets medium-sized arteries, especially coronary arteries.

Pathway

Unknown trigger → immune activation→ Cytokine storm (IL-1, IL-6, TNF)→ Endothelial inflammation→ Coronary arteritis→ Aneurysm formation (if untreated)

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Persistent fever ≥5 days

Mechanism: Systemic inflammatory response

Clinical Significance: Required diagnostic criterion

Conjunctival injection (limbic sparing)

Mechanism: Vasculitis of conjunctival vessels

Clinical Significance: Non-purulent; spares area around iris

Coronary artery aneurysms

Mechanism: Destruction of internal elastic lamina

Clinical Significance: Most serious complication; prevented by early IVIG
