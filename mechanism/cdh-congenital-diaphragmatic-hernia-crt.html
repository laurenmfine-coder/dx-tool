📖 Teaching Point

Congenital diaphragmatic hernia (CDH) results from failure of the pleuroperitoneal folds to close (weeks 8-10). The most common type is left-sided Bochdalek hernia (80-85%) through the posterolateral diaphragm. Abdominal contents herniate into the thorax → pulmonary hypoplasia + pulmonary hypertension.

📖 Teaching Point

4 components of the diaphragm: (1) Septum transversum → central tendon (anterior); (2) Pleuroperitoneal membranes → close posterolateral canals; (3) Body wall mesoderm → muscular rim; (4) Esophageal mesentery → crura. LEFT pleuroperitoneal membrane closes LATER → left Bochdalek hernias are more common (85%). Motor innervation: phrenic nerve (C3-C5, 'C3-4-5 keeps the diaphragm alive').

📖 Teaching Point

CDH pulmonary hypertension is the primary determinant of mortality (not the hernia itself). The lung develops in a compressed state → fewer alveoli, fewer arteries, and the arteries that form have excess smooth muscle. This is why surgery is NOT emergent — stabilizing the pulmonary hypertension takes priority. ECMO may be needed for refractory cases (20-30% of CDH patients).

📖 Teaching Point

CDH surgical repair is NOT a surgical emergency. Paradigm shift: the old approach of immediate repair led to worse outcomes because it destabilized fragile pulmonary hemodynamics. Modern approach: (1) Gentle ventilation strategies; (2) Pulmonary hypertension management (iNO, sildenafil); (3) ECMO if refractory; (4) Surgical repair only after stabilization. Repair: primary closure if possible, or patch (Gore-Tex) for large defects.

📖 Teaching Point

CDH prognostic indicators: O/E LHR is strongest predictor. Other negative prognosticators: liver herniation into chest, right-sided hernia, associated anomalies (cardiac defects), need for ECMO, low Apgar scores. Overall CDH survival is ~70-80% at specialized centers. Fetal intervention (FETO — fetoscopic endoluminal tracheal occlusion) is being studied for severe cases (O/E LHR <25%).

Key Takeaways

✓CDH = failure of pleuroperitoneal membrane closure (weeks 8-10)
✓Left Bochdalek hernia = 85% (left closes later embryologically)
✓4 diaphragm components: septum transversum, pleuroperitoneal membranes, body wall, esophageal mesentery
✓Bag-mask ventilation is CONTRAINDICATED — intubate immediately
✓Scaphoid abdomen + absent breath sounds + mediastinal shift = CDH triad
✓Pulmonary hypertension (not the hernia) determines mortality
