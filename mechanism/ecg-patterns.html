🚨 Life-Threatening

STEMI: ST elevation ≥1mm limb/≥2mm precordial, reciprocal changes, hyperacute T waves
VT: Wide QRS >120ms, regular, fast 100-250, AV dissociation, concordance
VFib: Chaotic, no QRS. Shock immediately.
Complete Heart Block: P waves march through, no relation to QRS, slow escape
Torsades: Polymorphic VT, twisting axis, long QT. Tx: Magnesium
Hyperkalemia: Peaked T → Wide QRS → No P waves → Sine wave

⚡ Arrhythmias

AFib: Irregularly irregular, no P waves, fibrillatory baseline
Aflutter: Sawtooth waves ~300/min, regular rate (2:1, 3:1, 4:1)
SVT: Regular, narrow, 150-250. Pseudo-R' V1 or pseudo-S inferior
WPW: Short PR <120ms, delta wave, wide QRS
Mobitz I: Progressive PR prolongation → dropped QRS. Usually benign.
Mobitz II: Constant PR, sudden dropped QRS. Needs pacing.

📊 Other Patterns

LBBB: QRS >120ms, deep S in V1, tall R in I/V6. WiLLiaM
RBBB: QRS >120ms, RSR' "bunny ears" V1, wide S in I/V6. MaRRoW
PE: S1Q3T3, sinus tach, new RBBB, T inv V1-V4
Pericarditis: Diffuse ST elevation, PR depression, no reciprocal
Long QT: QTc >460 (M) / >470 (F). Risk of Torsades.
Brugada: Coved ST elevation V1-V2 + T inversion. ICD needed.
