⚙️ Hereditary Angioedema

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Allergy/Immunology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Bradykinin-mediated, NOT histamine-mediated
  • No urticaria, no response to epinephrine/antihistamines
  • Treatment: C1-INH concentrate, icatibant, ecallantide
  • Low C4 is screening test; confirm with C1-INH level/function

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Bradykinin Excess

C1-inhibitor deficiency leads to uncontrolled bradykinin production.

Pathway

C1-INH deficiency→Uninhibited kallikrein→Excess bradykinin generation→Vascular permeability→Non-histaminergic angioedema

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Angioedema without urticaria

Mechanism: Bradykinin (not histamine) mediated

Clinical Significance: KEY: no hives distinguishes from allergic angioedema

No response to epinephrine/antihistamines

Mechanism: Not mast cell/histamine mediated

Clinical Significance: Diagnostic clue; requires specific therapy

Recurrent abdominal pain

Mechanism: Intestinal wall edema

Clinical Significance: Can mimic acute abdomen; may cause unnecessary surgery
