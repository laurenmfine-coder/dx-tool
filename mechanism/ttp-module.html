🩸 Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP)

A hematologic emergency caused by ADAMTS13 deficiency leading to widespread microvascular thrombosis. Untreated mortality >90%, but with plasma exchange >80% survive.

⏱️ 20 min📊 Critical🎯 Hematology

📋 The Classic Pentad

📝 "FAT RN" - TTP Pentad

Fever, Anemia (microangiopathic hemolytic), Thrombocytopenia, Renal dysfunction, Neurologic symptoms

Note: Full pentad only present in ~5% of cases. Thrombocytopenia + MAHA is enough to suspect TTP!

🔬 Pathophysiology

1
ADAMTS13 Deficiency
Autoantibodies (acquired TTP) or genetic mutation (congenital) → severe deficiency of ADAMTS13 protease
2
Ultra-Large VWF Accumulation
ADAMTS13 normally cleaves ultra-large VWF multimers. Without it, these giant VWF strings remain on endothelium
3
Platelet Aggregation
Platelets bind to ultra-large VWF → form microthrombi in small vessels → consume platelets
4
MAHA + End-Organ Ischemia
RBCs shear against fibrin strands → schistocytes. Microthrombi cause CNS, renal, cardiac ischemia

🎯 Key Distinction: TTP vs HUS vs DIC

TTP: ADAMTS13 <10%, neurologic symptoms predominate, PT/PTT normal
HUS: Renal failure predominates, often post-diarrheal (Shiga toxin), ADAMTS13 normal
DIC: Coagulopathy present (↑PT/PTT, ↓fibrinogen), underlying cause (sepsis, malignancy)

🔎 Diagnosis

🔬 Lab Findings
  • Thrombocytopenia (often <30k)
  • MAHA: Schistocytes, ↑LDH, ↑indirect bili, ↓haptoglobin
  • Normal PT/PTT (unlike DIC)
  • ADAMTS13 <10% (confirm but don't wait)
  • ADAMTS13 inhibitor (autoantibody)
📊 PLASMIC Score
  • Platelet count <30k
  • Hemolysis (retic, LDH, bili)
  • No active cancer
  • No stem cell transplant
  • MCV <90
  • INR <1.5
  • Creatinine <2.0
  • Score ≥6: High probability TTP

💊 Treatment

⚠️ Do NOT Delay Treatment!

TTP is a clinical diagnosis. Start plasma exchange BEFORE ADAMTS13 results return. Mortality increases with each day of delay.

🔑 Plasma Exchange (TPE)
  • Removes autoantibodies + ultra-large VWF
  • Replaces ADAMTS13
  • Daily until platelets >150k x2 days
  • Reduces mortality from >90% to <20%
💊 Adjunctive Therapy
  • Steroids: Prednisone 1mg/kg (suppress autoantibodies)
  • Rituximab: For refractory/relapsing cases
  • Caplacizumab: Anti-VWF nanobody (reduces time to response)
  • NO platelet transfusion (fuels the fire)
