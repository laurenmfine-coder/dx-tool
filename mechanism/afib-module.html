⚙️ Atrial Fibrillation

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Cardiology

🎯 Learning Objectives

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Electrical Remodeling

AF causes structural and electrical changes that perpetuate the arrhythmia ("AF begets AF").

Pathway

Atrial stretch/fibrosis→ Shortened atrial refractory period→ Multiple re-entry circuits→ Loss of organized contraction→ Stasis → thrombus formation

Stroke Risk Mechanism

The left atrial appendage is the primary site of thrombus formation in AF.

Pathway

Loss of atrial contraction→ Blood stasis in LAA→ Thrombus formation→ Embolization to brain→ Cardioembolic stroke

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Irregularly irregular pulse

Mechanism: Chaotic atrial electrical activity with variable AV conduction

Clinical Significance: Pathognomonic for AF - no two R-R intervals are the same

Variable S1 intensity

Mechanism: Variable diastolic filling time affects mitral valve position

Clinical Significance: Helps distinguish AF from other irregular rhythms

Absent a-wave in JVP

Mechanism: No organized atrial contraction

Clinical Significance: Confirms loss of atrial kick
