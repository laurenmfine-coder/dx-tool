MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Progressive loss of dopaminergic neurons in the substantia nigra pars compacta → decreased dopamine in the nigrostriatal pathway → motor dysfunction. Pathological hallmark: Lewy bodies (α-synuclein inclusions).

🔑 Cardinal Features (TRAP):
Tremor — resting tremor (pill-rolling, 4-6 Hz), decreases with action
Rigidity — cogwheel rigidity (ratchety resistance to passive movement)
Akinesia/bradykinesia — slowness of movement (most disabling). Micrographia, hypomimia (masked face), shuffling gait
Postural instability — late feature, falls

Key Distinguishing Features

Asymmetric onset (one side first), resting tremor (vs essential tremor which is action/postural), excellent response to levodopa (gold standard diagnostic test).

Non-Motor Features

Depression (#1 psychiatric comorbidity), REM sleep behavior disorder (may precede motor symptoms by years), constipation, anosmia, orthostatic hypotension, cognitive decline/dementia (late). Non-motor symptoms often precede motor features.

TREATMENT
Pharmacotherapy
DrugMechanismKey Points
Carbidopa-LevodopaLevodopa = dopamine precursor. Carbidopa = peripheral dopa decarboxylase inhibitor (prevents peripheral conversion)MOST EFFECTIVE treatment. Motor complications with chronic use: wearing-off, on-off phenomenon, dyskinesias. Start when symptoms impair function
Dopamine agonistsPramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotineLess dyskinesia risk. Side effects: impulse control disorders (gambling, hypersexuality, shopping), daytime somnolence, hallucinations
MAO-B inhibitorsSelegiline, rasagiline, safinamideMild benefit. Can be used as monotherapy early or adjunct later
COMT inhibitorsEntacapone, tolcaponeExtend levodopa effect. Used for wearing-off. Tolcapone: hepatotoxicity risk
AmantadineNMDA antagonist + increases dopamine releaseUsed for levodopa-induced dyskinesias
Deep brain stimulationSTN or GPi stimulationFor motor fluctuations refractory to meds. Does NOT cure or slow progression
⚠️ Abrupt Withdrawal: Never abruptly stop dopaminergic medications → neuroleptic malignant-like syndrome (rigidity, fever, AMS, rhabdomyolysis). Taper gradually.
