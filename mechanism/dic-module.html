🩸 Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC)

A consumptive coagulopathy where the body simultaneously clots AND bleeds. Understanding the paradox is key to management.

⏱️ 20 min📊 Critical🎯 Hematology/Critical Care

📋 The DIC Paradox

🎯 Clotting AND Bleeding Simultaneously

DIC activates coagulation system systemically → consumes clotting factors AND platelets → leads to both microvascular thrombosis AND hemorrhage. The patient bleeds because they've clotted too much!

🔬 Pathophysiology

1
Triggering Event
Sepsis, trauma, malignancy, obstetric complications → release of tissue factor
2
Systemic Thrombin Generation
Tissue factor activates coagulation cascade → widespread thrombin generation
3
Microvascular Thrombosis
Fibrin deposition in microvasculature → end-organ ischemia, MAHA (schistocytes)
4
Consumptive Coagulopathy
Platelets and clotting factors consumed → ↑PT/PTT, ↓fibrinogen, ↓platelets → BLEEDING

Common Causes

🦠 Infection
  • Sepsis (most common cause)
  • Gram-negative bacteremia
  • Meningococcemia
🩺 Obstetric
  • Placental abruption
  • Amniotic fluid embolism
  • HELLP syndrome
🎗️ Malignancy
  • Acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL)
  • Mucin-secreting adenocarcinoma
⚡ Trauma/Other
  • Massive trauma
  • Burns
  • Snake envenomation

💊 Treatment

🎯 Treat the Underlying Cause!

DIC is always secondary. The most important intervention is treating the trigger (antibiotics for sepsis, delivery for obstetric causes, chemotherapy for APL). Supportive therapy alone won't stop DIC.

🩸 Blood Products (if bleeding)
  • Platelets: Target >50k if bleeding
  • FFP: For ↑PT/PTT with bleeding
  • Cryoprecipitate: For fibrinogen <100
  • PRBCs: For anemia
💊 Other Considerations
  • Heparin: Controversial - consider if thrombosis predominates
  • Antithrombin: Not routinely recommended
  • TXA: For severe bleeding (limited data)

📝 Key Takeaways

🎯 Must Know
  • Always secondary - find the cause
  • ↑PT/PTT, ↓fibrinogen, ↓platelets, ↑D-dimer
  • Clotting AND bleeding simultaneously
  • ISTH Score ≥5 = overt DIC
💊 Treatment Pearls
  • Treat underlying cause FIRST
  • Blood products only if actively bleeding
  • Different from TTP (PT/PTT abnormal)
  • Heparin controversial
