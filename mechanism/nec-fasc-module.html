🦠 Necrotizing Fasciitis

A rapidly progressive, life-threatening soft tissue infection. Mortality increases ~9% with each hour of surgical delay. Time is tissue.

⏱️ 15 min📊 Surgical Emergency🎯 Surgery/ID

🚨 Clinical Red Flags

⚠️ "Pain Out of Proportion" is the Key Finding!

Severe pain that seems disproportionate to the skin findings is the earliest and most reliable sign. The infection spreads along fascial planes BEFORE skin changes appear.

Early Signs
  • Pain out of proportion to exam
  • Tenderness beyond erythema
  • Systemic toxicity (fever, tachycardia)
  • Rapidly spreading cellulitis
Late Signs (Ominous)
  • Crepitus (gas in tissues)
  • Skin necrosis, bullae
  • Anesthesia (nerve destruction)
  • Hemorrhagic bullae

💊 Management

🔪 Surgery is the Treatment!

Antibiotics alone cannot cure nec fasc. Emergent surgical debridement is the definitive treatment. Each hour of delay increases mortality by ~9%. Do NOT wait for imaging!

🔪 Surgery
  • EMERGENT debridement
  • Wide excision to bleeding tissue
  • Repeat debridement q24-48h
  • "Finger test" intraoperatively
💊 Antibiotics
  • Vancomycin (MRSA coverage)
  • Pip-tazo or carbapenem (broad gram-neg/anaerobe)
  • Clindamycin (toxin suppression for GAS)
  • Continue until no further debridement needed
