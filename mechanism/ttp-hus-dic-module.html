Thrombotic Thrombocytopenic Purpura (TTP)

Pathophysiology

ADAMTS13 deficiency (acquired antibodies or congenital)
ADAMTS13 normally cleaves ultra-large vWF multimers → Without it, vWF multimers accumulate → Platelet microthrombi form → RBCs shear on fibrin strands = schistocytes

Classic Pentad (rarely complete)

🩸 Thrombocytopenia
💉 MAHA (schistocytes, ↑LDH, ↓haptoglobin)
🧠 Neurologic symptoms (confusion, HA, seizure)
🫘 Renal dysfunction (mild, Cr ~1.5-2)
🌡️ Fever

PLASMIC Score (Pre-test Probability)

Platelet count <30K (+1)
Lysis: Hemolysis (retic >2.5%, hapto undetectable, indirect bili >2) (+1)
Active cancer: No (+1)
Stem cell/organ transplant: No (+1)
MCV <90 (+1)
INR <1.5 (+1)
Creatinine <2.0 (+1)
0-4: Low risk (0-4% TTP)
5: Intermediate (5-25%)
6-7: High risk (72-82%)

⚠️ Treatment - EMERGENCY

First-Line:
  • • Plasma exchange (PLEX) - removes antibodies, replaces ADAMTS13
  • • Steroids (methylpred 1g/day or prednisone 1mg/kg)
  • • Caplacizumab (anti-vWF nanobody) - faster platelet recovery
Critical Points:
  • • Start PLEX empirically if high suspicion - don't wait for ADAMTS13!
  • • Do NOT transfuse platelets (fuel the fire → more microthrombi)
  • • Exception: Life-threatening bleeding
  • • Rituximab for refractory/relapsed

Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS)

Typical HUS (STEC-HUS)

90% of pediatric cases
Cause:
Shiga toxin-producing E. coli (STEC) - especially O157:H7
From undercooked beef, unpasteurized products, contaminated water
Presentation:
Bloody diarrhea → 5-10 days later → Triad: MAHA + Thrombocytopenia + AKI (prominent!)
Treatment:
Supportive! Fluids, RBC transfusion, dialysis if needed.
⚠️ Avoid antibiotics (↑toxin release → worse outcomes)

Atypical HUS (aHUS)

Complement-mediated
Cause:
Dysregulated alternative complement pathway
Genetic mutations (CFH, CFI, MCP, C3, CFB) or acquired (anti-CFH Ab)
Presentation:
NO diarrhea prodrome
Triad: MAHA + Thrombocytopenia + Severe AKI
Can be triggered by infection, pregnancy, drugs
Treatment:
Eculizumab (anti-C5 monoclonal Ab) - blocks terminal complement
Or ravulizumab (longer-acting)
Key Difference from TTP: HUS has prominent renal failure (Cr often >3-4), TTP has milder renal involvement but more neuro symptoms. Both have normal coags!

Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC)

Pathophysiology

Systemic activation of coagulation → Widespread microthrombi → Consumption of platelets & clotting factors → Bleeding
"Clotting AND bleeding at the same time"

Causes

  • • Sepsis (most common)
  • • Trauma / Burns
  • • Malignancy (APL, adenocarcinomas)
  • • Obstetric (abruption, amniotic embolism, HELLP)
  • • Transfusion reaction
  • • Snake envenomation

Lab Findings

↓ Platelets
Consumed
↑ PT/INR
↓ Clotting factors
↓ Fibrinogen
Consumed (may be normal early)
↑↑ D-dimer
Fibrinolysis
+ Schistocytes on smear, ↑LDH, ↓haptoglobin (MAHA)

Treatment

Primary:
  • • Treat underlying cause! (antibiotics for sepsis, delivery for obstetric)
  • • This is essential - DIC won't resolve otherwise
Supportive (if bleeding):
  • • Platelets (if <10K or <50K with bleeding)
  • • FFP (if PT/PTT elevated + bleeding)
  • • Cryoprecipitate (if fibrinogen <100)
  • • pRBCs as needed
