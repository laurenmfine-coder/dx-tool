Hypercalcemia of Malignancy

PTHrP, Osteolysis & Treatment

Mechanisms (HHM vs LOH)

Humoral Hypercalcemia of Malignancy (HHM) - 80%:

  • Tumor secretes PTHrP (parathyroid hormone-related peptide)
  • PTHrP mimics PTH → ↑ bone resorption, ↑ renal Ca reabsorption
  • Common: Squamous cell carcinoma (lung, H&N), renal cell, breast, ovarian

Local Osteolytic Hypercalcemia (LOH) - 20%:

  • Tumor directly invades bone → local cytokine release → osteoclast activation
  • Common: Multiple myeloma, breast cancer bone mets

💡 PTH will be LOW in both mechanisms (appropriately suppressed)

Lab Pattern Recognition

Primary Hyperparathyroidism: ↑Ca, ↑PTH, ↓Phos, normal PTHrP

HHM (PTHrP-mediated): ↑Ca, ↓PTH, ↓Phos, ↑PTHrP

LOH (local osteolysis): ↑Ca, ↓PTH, normal/↑Phos, normal PTHrP

Vitamin D toxicity: ↑Ca, ↓PTH, ↑1,25(OH)₂D

💡 Phosphorus helps: HHM has LOW phos (PTHrP effect), LOH has normal/high phos

Clinical Manifestations

Mnemonic - "Stones, Bones, Groans, Moans, Psychiatric Overtones":

  • Stones: Nephrolithiasis, nephrocalcinosis
  • Bones: Pain, osteoporosis, pathologic fractures
  • Groans: Constipation, nausea, pancreatitis
  • Moans: Fatigue, weakness
  • Psychiatric: Confusion, lethargy, coma

ECG changes: Shortened QT, Osborn waves, heart block

💡 Severity correlates with both level AND rate of rise

Treatment Algorithm

1. Volume resuscitation (FIRST):

  • NS 200-300 mL/hr initially
  • Goal: 2-4 L/day (monitor for overload)
  • Promotes calciuresis

2. Bisphosphonate (mainstay):

  • Zoledronic acid 4mg IV (most potent)
  • Pamidronate 60-90mg IV (if renal impairment)
  • Onset: 2-4 days, Duration: 2-4 weeks

3. Calcitonin (bridge):

  • 4 IU/kg SC/IM q12h
  • Rapid onset (hours) but tachyphylaxis in 48-72h

4. Denosumab (if refractory):

  • RANKL inhibitor, works even if bisphosphonate-resistant
  • Risk of severe hypocalcemia

Prognosis & Special Cases

Prognosis: Hypercalcemia of malignancy often indicates advanced disease; median survival ~3 months

Myeloma-specific:

  • Mechanism: LOH from local plasma cell invasion
  • Treatment also includes chemotherapy, bortezomib

Refractory cases:

  • Hemodialysis with low-calcium bath
  • Gallium nitrate (rarely used)
  • Treat underlying malignancy

💡 Best long-term treatment is treating the cancer itself

🎯 Key Points

  • HHM (80%): PTHrP-mediated, low phos, common in squamous cell
  • LOH (20%): Local bone destruction, normal phos, myeloma/bone mets
  • PTH is always suppressed in malignancy (distinguishes from 1° HPT)
  • Treatment: fluids → bisphosphonate → calcitonin bridge → denosumab if refractory
  • Poor prognosis marker - median survival ~3 months
