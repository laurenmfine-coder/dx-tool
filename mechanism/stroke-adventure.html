Correct! tPA (alteplase 0.9 mg/kg, max 90mg, 10% bolus + remainder over 60 min) is indicated within 4.5 hours for ischemic stroke without contraindications. BP must be brought below 185/110 first — use IV labetalol 10-20mg or nicardipine drip. Also order CTA head/neck to evaluate for large vessel occlusion (LVO) for potential thrombectomy.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
BP MUST be <185/110 before tPA administration. Giving tPA with uncontrolled hypertension dramatically increases the risk of symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage (sICH). Take 5-10 minutes to control BP with IV labetalol or nicardipine, then give tPA.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
tPA and thrombectomy are NOT mutually exclusive — they're complementary. Bridging therapy (tPA then thrombectomy) is standard for LVO within the tPA window. tPA starts working immediately while the thrombectomy team mobilizes. Do both.
DECISION 3
Post-tPA
BP lowered to 178/98 with labetalol, then tPA administered. CTA shows left MCA M1 occlusion. Interventional neuroradiology is available. 2 hours from symptom onset.
✅
Correct!
Correct! Mechanical thrombectomy is indicated for LVO (ICA or M1 MCA) within 24 hours (with favorable imaging) when NIHSS ≥6 and ASPECTS ≥6. Five landmark trials (MR CLEAN, ESCAPE, EXTEND-IA, SWIFT PRIME, REVASCAT) showed NNT of ~3-5 for functional independence.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Time is brain! tPA alone recanalizes M1 MCA occlusions in only ~30% of cases. Delaying thrombectomy to see if tPA works loses critical salvageable tissue. The evidence supports proceeding to thrombectomy as soon as possible after tPA.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
With a confirmed M1 occlusion and NIHSS 14, withholding thrombectomy is a missed opportunity. Thrombectomy achieves 90% recanalization vs 30% with tPA alone. This patient has a large ischemic penumbra that can be saved.
RESOLUTION
Recovery
Successful mechanical thrombectomy with TICI 2b-3 recanalization. At 24 hours, NIHSS has improved from 14 to 3 (mild residual word-finding difficulty). MRI shows limited infarct core.
OUTCOME

Excellent stroke management! Secondary prevention priorities: anticoagulation for AFib (the likely stroke etiology — start DOAC after 3-14 days depending on infarct size), statin, antihypertensive therapy, swallow evaluation, PT/OT/speech therapy. He was not on anticoagulants — this stroke was preventable.
