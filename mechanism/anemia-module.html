Anemia

Understanding the MCV-based approach — from iron metabolism to hemolysis, with the pathophysiology behind each category.

📚 8 Concepts ❓ 8 MCQs ⏱ ~40 min

🩸 The MCV-Based Approach

Defining Anemia

Anemia is reduced oxygen-carrying capacity of blood:

  • Men — Hgb <13.5 g/dL
  • Women — Hgb <12 g/dL
  • Pregnancy — Hgb <11 g/dL (physiologic hemodilution)

Anemia is a sign, not a diagnosis. The clinical task is identifying the cause.

🔄 MCV Categories

Mean corpuscular volume categorizes anemia by RBC size:

↓
Microcytic (MCV <80): Iron deficiency, thalassemia, chronic disease, sideroblastic
→
Normocytic (MCV 80-100): Acute blood loss, hemolysis, chronic disease, marrow failure
↑
Macrocytic (MCV >100): B12/folate deficiency, liver disease, hypothyroidism, MDS

💡 Clinical Pearl

The reticulocyte count indicates marrow response. Reticulocyte index >2% = appropriate response (blood loss, hemolysis). Low reticulocyte index = impaired production.

⚠️ Always Find the Cause

In men and postmenopausal women, iron deficiency = GI blood loss until proven otherwise. Colonoscopy and EGD rule out malignancy.

💡 Clinical Pearl

Ferritin is an acute phase reactant — it can be "falsely normal" with concurrent inflammation. Ferritin <30 is diagnostic; 30-100 is indeterminate.

🔥 Anemia of Chronic Disease

The Role of Hepcidin

Inflammation drives iron sequestration via hepcidin:

  • Inflammation — IL-6 stimulates liver hepcidin production
  • Hepcidin action — Blocks ferroportin, trapping iron in macrophages
  • Result — Iron present but unavailable ("functional iron deficiency")

ACD vs Iron Deficiency

TestIron DeficiencyACD
Ferritin↓ Low (<30)Normal or ↑
Serum iron↓ Low↓ Low

💡 Clinical Pearl

TIBC is the key distinguisher. In iron deficiency, liver makes more transferrin (high TIBC). In ACD, inflammation suppresses it (low TIBC).

📐 Macrocytic Anemia

B12 Deficiency

Vitamin B12 is essential for DNA synthesis and neurologic function:

  • Absorption — Requires intrinsic factor for ileal absorption
  • Causes — Pernicious anemia, gastric surgery, ileal disease, vegan diet
  • Neurologic — Subacute combined degeneration (posterior columns + corticospinal tracts)

⚠️ Don't Give Folate Alone

Folate can improve the anemia but NOT prevent neurologic damage from B12 deficiency. Always check B12 before treating macrocytic anemia.

💥 Hemolytic Anemia

Recognizing Hemolysis

The lab pattern reflects RBC breakdown and marrow compensation:

  • ↑ Reticulocyte count — Marrow response
  • ↑ LDH — Released from lysed RBCs
  • ↑ Indirect bilirubin — From heme catabolism
  • ↓ Haptoglobin — Binds free Hgb and is cleared (most specific)

Intravascular vs Extravascular

FeatureIntravascularExtravascular
LocationIn blood vesselsSpleen/liver
HaptoglobinVery low/absentLow
HemoglobinuriaPresentAbsent
ExamplesPNH, transfusion rxnAIHA, spherocytosis

💡 Clinical Pearl

The peripheral smear is essential. Spherocytes → AIHA or hereditary spherocytosis. Schistocytes → MAHA (TTP, HUS, DIC). Bite cells → G6PD deficiency.

🧬 Thalassemia

💡 Clinical Pearl

Beta-thalassemia trait shows elevated HbA2 (>3.5%). Alpha-thalassemia trait has normal electrophoresis — diagnosis requires genetic testing or inference from microcytosis with normal iron studies.
