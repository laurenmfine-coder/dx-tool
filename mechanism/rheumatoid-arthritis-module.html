MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

RA is a chronic systemic autoimmune disease characterized by inflammatory synovitis → pannus formation → cartilage and bone destruction. Driven by CD4+ T cells, macrophages, and cytokines (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-1) in the synovium.

🔑 Key Concept: The hallmark of RA is symmetrical inflammatory polyarthritis of small joints (MCPs, PIPs, wrists, MTPs). UNLIKE osteoarthritis, RA involves the MCPs and wrists while SPARING the DIPs. Morning stiffness >1 hour is characteristic (improves with activity).

Hand Deformities

Swan neck: PIP hyperextension + DIP flexion. Boutonnière: PIP flexion + DIP hyperextension. Ulnar deviation: MCPs deviate ulnarly. Z-thumb: thumb MCP flexion + IP hyperextension.

Extra-Articular Manifestations

⚠️ Occur in seropositive (RF+) patients: Rheumatoid nodules (extensor surfaces, lungs), interstitial lung disease (most common cause of death after CV disease), Felty syndrome (RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia), rheumatoid vasculitis, secondary amyloidosis (AA), atlanto-axial subluxation (C1-C2 instability → cord compression), episcleritis/scleritis, pericarditis.
TREATMENT
Management

Treatment Strategy: Treat-to-Target

StepAgentsKey Points
First-line DMARDMethotrexate (MTX)Start within 3 months of diagnosis. Dose 15-25 mg/week. ALWAYS give with folic acid (reduces side effects). Monitor CBC, LFTs, renal function. Avoid in pregnancy (teratogenic), liver disease, heavy alcohol use
Bridge therapyLow-dose prednisoneShort-term bridge while DMARDs take effect (6-12 weeks). Taper as soon as possible
Alternative DMARDsLeflunomide, sulfasalazine, hydroxychloroquineIf MTX contraindicated or insufficient. Triple therapy (MTX + SSZ + HCQ) is effective
Biologics (inadequate MTX response)TNF inhibitors: etanercept, adalimumab, infliximab, certolizumab, golimumabScreen for TB (latent → treat before starting), hepatitis B. Risk of serious infections. Do not combine biologics
Other biologicsAbatacept (CTLA4-Ig), rituximab (anti-CD20), tocilizumab (anti-IL-6), baricitinib/tofacitinib (JAK inhibitors)Used after TNF inhibitor failure. JAK inhibitors are oral
🔑 Methotrexate Pearls: Mechanism: inhibits dihydrofolate reductase → impairs purine synthesis. Major toxicities: hepatotoxicity (avoid alcohol), bone marrow suppression, interstitial pneumonitis, mucositis. ALWAYS supplement with folic acid 1 mg daily. Leucovorin is rescue for MTX toxicity.
