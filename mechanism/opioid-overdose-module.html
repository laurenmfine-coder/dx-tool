⚙️ Opioid Overdose

Pathophysiology | Toxicology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Triad: CNS depression + respiratory depression + miosis
  • Naloxone: start low (0.04-0.4mg), titrate to respiratory effort
  • Watch for renarcotization - naloxone has shorter half-life than most opioids
  • Pulmonary edema can occur even after naloxone reversal

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Respiratory Depression

Opioids suppress brainstem respiratory centers, the primary cause of death.

Pathway

Mu-receptor activation in brainstem→Decreased respiratory drive→Hypoventilation→Hypoxia + hypercarbia→Respiratory arrest

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Miosis (pinpoint pupils)

Mechanism: Mu-receptor activation in Edinger-Westphal nucleus

Significance: Classic sign of opioid toxicity; present even in coma

Respiratory rate <12

Mechanism: Brainstem respiratory center depression

Significance: Key vital sign - predictor of severity

Decreased bowel sounds

Mechanism: Mu-receptor activation in GI tract

Significance: Opioid-induced ileus
