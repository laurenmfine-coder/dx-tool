MECHANISM
Pathophysiology & Diagnosis

Schizophrenia is a chronic psychotic disorder characterized by positive symptoms, negative symptoms, and cognitive dysfunction. Neurobiology: dopamine hypothesis (mesolimbic hyperactivity → positive symptoms; mesocortical hypoactivity → negative symptoms) + glutamate NMDA receptor hypofunction.

DSM-5 Criteria (≥2 for ≥1 month, with ≥6 months total disturbance)

At least TWO of: 1) Delusions, 2) Hallucinations, 3) Disorganized speech, 4) Grossly disorganized or catatonic behavior, 5) Negative symptoms. At least ONE must be #1, #2, or #3.

Symptom Categories

CategorySymptomsDopamine Pathway
PositiveHallucinations (auditory #1), delusions (persecutory #1), disorganized speech/behaviorMesolimbic excess → D2 blockade helps
NegativeFlat affect, alogia (poverty of speech), avolition, anhedonia, asociality (the 5 A's)Mesocortical deficit → atypicals may help
CognitiveWorking memory, attention, executive function deficitsPrefrontal dopamine/glutamate dysfunction
🔑 Key Epidemiology: Onset late teens-early 20s in males, late 20s-early 30s in females. Lifetime prevalence ~1%. Equal prevalence across cultures. Strong genetic component (10% risk with one affected parent, 50% concordance in monozygotic twins). Cannabis use in adolescence increases risk 2-6x.
CLINICAL
Clinical Course & Differential

Psychotic Disorder Spectrum

DisorderDurationKey Distinction
Brief psychotic disorder1 day - 1 monthFull return to baseline. Often stress-triggered
Schizophreniform1 - 6 monthsSame criteria as schizophrenia but shorter duration
Schizophrenia≥6 monthsChronic, often deteriorating course
SchizoaffectivePsychosis + mood episodesPsychotic symptoms persist ≥2 weeks WITHOUT mood symptoms
Delusional disorder≥1 month of delusionsNon-bizarre delusions, functioning relatively preserved, NO other psychotic symptoms
⚠️ Must Rule Out: Substance-induced psychosis (methamphetamine, PCP, cannabis, cocaine, LSD), medical conditions (delirium, seizures, autoimmune encephalitis [anti-NMDA receptor — especially in young women with ovarian teratoma], thyroid, Cushing, porphyria, Wilson disease, neurosyphilis, HIV). UDS and medical workup are essential before diagnosing primary psychotic disorder.

Prognostic Factors

Good prognosis: Late onset, female sex, mood symptoms, acute onset, good premorbid functioning, married, family hx of mood disorders. Poor prognosis: Early onset, male sex, negative symptoms predominant, gradual onset, poor premorbid functioning, family hx of schizophrenia, substance use.

TREATMENT
Antipsychotics

First-Generation (Typical) Antipsychotics — D2 Antagonists

AgentPotencyKey Side Effects
HaloperidolHighEPS (dystonia, akathisia, parkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia), NMS, elevated prolactin. Less sedation/anticholinergic
ChlorpromazineLowSedation, orthostatic hypotension, anticholinergic effects. Less EPS

Second-Generation (Atypical) Antipsychotics — 5-HT2A + D2 Antagonists

AgentKey Feature
ClozapineMOST EFFECTIVE antipsychotic. Reserved for treatment-resistant schizophrenia (failed ≥2 adequate trials). Risk of agranulocytosis (monitor ANC). Also: metabolic syndrome, seizures, myocarditis, sialorrhea
OlanzapineEffective but WORST metabolic effects (weight gain, diabetes, dyslipidemia). Monitor metabolic panel
RisperidoneDose-dependent EPS. Highest prolactin elevation among atypicals (galactorrhea, amenorrhea, osteoporosis)
QuetiapineVery sedating. Fewer EPS. Used off-label for insomnia, bipolar depression
AripiprazoleD2 PARTIAL AGONIST (unique mechanism). Least metabolic effects. Less sedation. Can cause akathisia
LurasidoneApproved for bipolar depression. Take with food (absorption). Less metabolic effects
🔑 Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS): Life-threatening reaction to antipsychotics. Classic tetrad: Fever, muscular Rigidity (lead-pipe), Autonomic instability, altered Mental status (FRAM). Labs: massively elevated CK, leukocytosis, metabolic acidosis. Treatment: STOP antipsychotic, dantrolene (muscle relaxant), bromocriptine (dopamine agonist), supportive ICU care. Mortality ~10%.
