⚡ Status Epilepticus

A neurologic emergency defined by prolonged or repetitive seizures without return to baseline. Rapid, escalating treatment prevents excitotoxic brain injury.

⏱️ 20 min📊 Neurologic Emergency🎯 Neurology/ED

📋 Definition (Updated)

🎯 Operational Definition

Status Epilepticus: Seizure lasting ≥5 minutes OR ≥2 seizures without return to baseline between them.

Refractory SE: Seizures persisting despite benzodiazepine + one second-line agent
Super-refractory SE: Seizures persisting ≥24 hours despite anesthetic agents

🔬 Pathophysiology: Excitotoxicity

Early (<30 min)
  • Compensatory mechanisms active
  • ↑BP, ↑HR, ↑glucose, ↑lactate
  • Adequate cerebral perfusion
  • GABA receptors still responsive
Late (>30 min)
  • GABA receptor internalization → benzos less effective
  • NMDA receptor upregulation → glutamate excitotoxicity
  • Cerebral autoregulation fails
  • Neuronal death begins

⚠️ Time-Dependent Drug Resistance

GABA receptors internalize during prolonged seizures, making benzodiazepines progressively less effective. This is why early, adequate dosing is critical - delay means higher benzo doses with diminishing returns.

⏱️ Treatment Algorithm

0-5 min

Stabilization Phase

ABCs, IV access, glucose check, labs (BMP, AED levels, tox screen). Give thiamine if alcohol suspected.
5-20 min

First-Line: Benzodiazepines

IV Lorazepam 4mg (can repeat x1) OR
IM Midazolam 10mg (if no IV access) OR
IV Diazepam 10mg
Don't underdose! Full doses upfront.
20-40 min

Second-Line: Anti-Seizure Medications

Fosphenytoin 20 PE/kg OR
Levetiracetam 60 mg/kg (max 4500mg) OR
Valproate 40 mg/kg (max 3000mg)
ESETT trial showed equivalent efficacy
>40 min

Third-Line: Anesthetics (RSE)

Propofol, Midazolam infusion, or Pentobarbital
Requires intubation and continuous EEG monitoring
ICU admission mandatory

🔎 Workup: Find the Cause

Common Causes
  • AED non-compliance (most common in epileptics)
  • Alcohol withdrawal
  • Metabolic (glucose, Na, Ca, Mg)
  • CNS infection
  • Stroke/hemorrhage
  • Drug toxicity
Workup
  • Glucose (bedside!)
  • BMP, Mg, Ca, Phos
  • AED levels
  • Tox screen, alcohol level
  • CT head (after stabilization)
  • LP if infection suspected

📝 Key Takeaways

🎯 Must Know
  • SE = seizure ≥5 min or repeated without recovery
  • GABA receptors internalize over time
  • Don't underdose benzos!
  • Second-line agents equivalent (ESETT)
💊 Treatment Pearls
  • Lorazepam 4mg IV or Midazolam 10mg IM
  • Move to second-line if no response in 5-10 min
  • Fosphenytoin, levetiracetam, valproate all work
  • Refractory SE needs anesthetics + EEG
