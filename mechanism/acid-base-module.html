🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Metabolic Acidosis

Primary decrease in HCO3 with respiratory compensation.

Pathway

↓ HCO3 (acid gain or base loss)→pH falls→Respiratory compensation (hyperventilation)→↓ pCO2 (Winter's formula)→Expected pCO2 = 1.5(HCO3) + 8 ± 2

Respiratory Acidosis

Primary increase in pCO2 from hypoventilation.

Pathway

Hypoventilation→↑ pCO2→pH falls→Renal compensation (↑ HCO3)→Acute: ↑1 HCO3 per 10 ↑pCO2→Chronic: ↑3.5 HCO3 per 10 ↑pCO2

Anion Gap Calculation

AG = Na - (Cl + HCO3). Normal 8-12.

Pathway

Calculate AG→If elevated → MUDPILES causes→Calculate delta-delta ratio→Delta AG / Delta HCO3→Identifies additional disorders

⚠️ MUDPILES - Causes of Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis

Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Kussmaul respirations

Mechanism: Deep, rapid breathing to blow off CO2 and compensate for metabolic acidosis

Significance: Classic for DKA; indicates severe metabolic acidosis

Elevated anion gap

Mechanism: Unmeasured anions (lactate, ketones, toxins) accumulate

Significance: Points to specific causes (MUDPILES) vs normal AG acidosis

Delta-delta ratio >2

Mechanism: HCO3 hasn't dropped as much as expected for the AG elevation

Significance: Indicates concurrent metabolic alkalosis
