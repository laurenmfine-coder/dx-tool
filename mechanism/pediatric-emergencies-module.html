Critical Pediatric Presentations

Pediatric emergencies differ from adult emergencies in physiology, differential diagnosis, and management. Children compensate well until they decompensate rapidly\u2014tachycardia is the earliest sign of shock, while hypotension is a late and ominous finding.

\u26a0\ufe0f Epiglottitis: DO NOT examine the throat. Manipulating the airway can cause complete obstruction. If suspected, keep child calm, allow parent to hold child, and mobilize anesthesia/ENT for controlled intubation in the OR.

Intussusception

Most common cause of bowel obstruction in children 6-36 months. Lead point usually ileal lymphoid tissue (Peyer patches) after viral illness. Classic triad: intermittent colicky abdominal pain, "currant jelly" stool (blood + mucus), and palpable sausage-shaped mass in RUQ. Diagnosis: ultrasound shows "target sign." Treatment: air or barium enema reduction. Surgery if peritonitis or failed reduction.

\U0001f511 Intussusception red flags for surgical lead point: Age >3 years or <3 months, recurrent intussusception, or identifiable lead point on imaging (Meckel diverticulum, polyp, lymphoma) \u2192 higher likelihood of pathologic lead point requiring surgical exploration.

TypeCausesKey Features
HypovolemicDehydration, hemorrhageTachycardia, dry mucous membranes, delayed cap refill, decreased urine output
SepticMeningococcemia, GBSWarm shock early (bounding pulses), cold shock late (mottled, cool extremities)
CardiogenicMyocarditis, CHDHepatomegaly, gallop rhythm, pulmonary edema. Fluids can worsen!
ObstructiveTension pneumo, tamponade, ductal-dependent CHDJVD (if old enough to assess), unequal breath sounds
\U0001f511 Fluid resuscitation: 20 mL/kg NS bolus, reassess, repeat up to 60 mL/kg in first hour. If no improvement after 40-60 mL/kg \u2192 start vasopressors (epinephrine for cold shock, norepinephrine for warm shock). In cardiogenic shock, use smaller boluses (5-10 mL/kg) with extreme caution.

Febrile Neonate (<28 days)

\u26a0\ufe0f Any fever (\u226538\u00b0C) in a neonate <28 days = full sepsis workup. CBC, blood culture, UA/UCx, LP (CSF studies + culture), CXR if respiratory symptoms. Start empiric ampicillin + gentamicin (or cefotaxime) IMMEDIATELY. Do not wait for results. HSV PCR if risk factors or vesicular lesions \u2192 add acyclovir.

Kawasaki Disease

Vasculitis of medium-sized arteries, primarily coronary arteries. Diagnosis requires fever \u22655 days PLUS 4 of 5 criteria: bilateral conjunctival injection (non-exudative), oral changes (strawberry tongue, cracked lips), rash, extremity changes (edema, desquamation), cervical lymphadenopathy (\u22651.5cm, usually unilateral). Treatment: IVIG + high-dose aspirin. Untreated \u2192 25% develop coronary artery aneurysms.
