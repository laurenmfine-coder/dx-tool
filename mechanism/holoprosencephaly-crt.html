📖 Teaching Point

Holoprosencephaly (HPE) = failure of the prosencephalon (forebrain) to cleave during weeks 3-4. Spectrum: Alobar (most severe — no cleavage, single ventricle) → Semilobar (partial posterior cleavage) → Lobar (near-complete cleavage, mildest). Incidence: 1/250 conceptions, but most are lost; 1/10,000-20,000 live births.

📖 Teaching Point

SHH pathway in HPE: SHH gene (chromosome 7q36) mutations account for ~25% of familial HPE. SHH requires cholesterol modification for proper function. Teratogens that disrupt cholesterol: alcohol (FAS can include HPE features), cyclopamine (found in Veratrum californicum — sheep that ate this plant had cyclopic lambs, which led to the discovery of the SHH pathway). Smith-Lemli-Opitz syndrome (cholesterol synthesis defect) is associated with HPE.

📖 Teaching Point

'The face predicts the brain' — the severity of facial dysmorphism correlates with the severity of brain malformation. Facial spectrum: Cyclopia (most severe) → Ethmocephaly (proboscis between close-set eyes) → Cebocephaly (flat nose, hypotelorism) → Premaxillary agenesis (median cleft) → Solitary median maxillary central incisor (mildest). Chromosomal associations: T13 (most common), T18, triploidy, del(7q).

📖 Teaching Point

HPE prognosis by type: Alobar — most die in infancy; Semilobar — variable, often severe disability, some survive years; Lobar — best prognosis, mild-moderate disability possible; Middle interhemispheric variant — seizures common, motor > cognitive impairment. Endocrine problems are common across all types (diabetes insipidus, growth hormone deficiency, adrenal insufficiency) because the hypothalamic-pituitary axis is affected.

📖 Teaching Point

HPE clinical manifestations: (1) Neurologic — seizures (50-80%), spasticity, cognitive impairment; (2) Endocrine — diabetes insipidus, GH deficiency, cortisol deficiency, hypothyroidism (all from hypothalamic-pituitary dysfunction); (3) Facial — spectrum from cyclopia to single central incisor; (4) Feeding — oromotor dysfunction, GER. Management is supportive: antiepileptics, hormone replacement, feeding support.

Stage 6 of 6
🎓 Integration & Key Takeaways
The lobar HPE child is started on hormone replacement and antiepileptic therapy. At 2 years, he has moderate developmental delay but is interactive and making progress with therapy.
