⚙️ PID & TOA

Pathophysiology | OB/GYN

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Chlamydia and GC most common
  • Minimal criteria: pain + CMT or adnexal tenderness
  • Cover GC + Chlamydia
  • TOA may need IV abx + drainage

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Ascending Infection

STI ascends to upper genital tract.

Pathway

Cervical infection→Ascent through endometrium→Salpingitis→Peritonitis→TOA if abscess forms

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Bilateral pain + CMT

Mechanism: Peritoneal inflammation

Significance: CMT (chandelier sign) classic

Mucopurulent discharge

Mechanism: Active cervicitis

Significance: Test and treat empirically
