⚙️ Serotonin Syndrome

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Toxicology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Hunter criteria: serotonergic drug + clonus OR tremor + hyperreflexia
  • Clonus = serotonin syndrome; rigidity = NMS
  • Treatment: stop offending agents, cyproheptadine, supportive care
  • Usually resolves in 24-72 hours with drug cessation

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Serotonergic Excess

Overstimulation of central and peripheral serotonin receptors, primarily 5-HT2A.

Pathway

Serotonergic drug(s) exposure→Excess 5-HT at synapses→5-HT2A receptor overstimulation→Neuromuscular excitation→Autonomic instability + hyperthermia

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Clonus (especially lower extremity)

Mechanism: 5-HT2A activation in spinal cord

Clinical Significance: Key distinguishing feature from NMS

Mydriasis

Mechanism: Serotonergic stimulation of iris dilator

Clinical Significance: Consistent finding in serotonin syndrome

Hyperthermia

Mechanism: Hypothalamic dysregulation + muscle hyperactivity

Clinical Significance: Can be severe (>41°C); requires aggressive cooling
