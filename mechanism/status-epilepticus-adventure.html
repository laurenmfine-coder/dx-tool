✅
Correct!
Correct! She already got midazolam IM from EMS (1st line). Give IV lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg (4mg) as the repeat dose. Simultaneously: position airway (recovery position or jaw thrust), NPA, high-flow O2 via NRB mask, suction PRN. BVM if needed. Do NOT attempt oral airway during active seizure. Draw labs: glucose, BMP, AED levels, CBC, tox screen.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Intubation during active generalized seizure is extremely difficult and dangerous (trismus, patient movement, aspiration risk). Treat the seizure first — most patients' oxygenation improves once seizure activity stops. Use BVM + jaw thrust to support ventilation while benzodiazepines take effect. Intubate only if seizures are refractory and airway cannot be maintained.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
She only had one dose of benzodiazepine. Guidelines recommend two adequate doses of benzodiazepines before moving to second-line agents. ~60% of SE responds to appropriate benzodiazepine dosing. Phenytoin/fosphenytoin takes 20+ minutes to infuse — it's not an emergency rescue medication.

Suboptimal Choice
After two adequate doses of benzodiazepines, additional doses have diminishing returns and increasing risk of respiratory depression. The SE protocol mandates escalation to a second-line agent (fosphenytoin, valproic acid, or levetiracetam) at this stage. Continued benzodiazepine stacking leads to respiratory failure without stopping seizures.
❌
Suboptimal Choice
Propofol/midazolam/pentobarbital drips are for REFRACTORY SE — defined as seizure continuing despite both first-line (benzos) AND second-line agents (fosphenytoin/VPA/LEV). Jumping to anesthetic doses requiring intubation before trying second-line agents exposes the patient to unnecessary intubation risks and ICU stay.
RESOLUTION
Seizure Terminates
Fosphenytoin loading + D50 push + LEV reload given. Seizure terminates after 3 minutes of fosphenytoin infusion. Total seizure duration: ~22 minutes. Patient is postictal but breathing spontaneously. SpO2 improves to 95% on 4L NC.
OUTCOME
🏆 Excellent Clinical Reasoning
Excellent SE management! Post-SE care: (1) Continuous EEG monitoring for 24-48h (20-30% of SE patients have subclinical seizures). (2) Treat UTI with appropriate antibiotics. (3) Correct hyponatremia carefully (Na+ 128 — avoid overcorrection >8 mEq/24h to prevent osmotic demyelination). (4) Optimize AED regimen. (5) Neurology consult. (6) Monitor for complications: rhabdomyolysis (check CK), aspiration, hyperthermia.
