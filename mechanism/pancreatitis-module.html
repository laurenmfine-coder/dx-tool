🔥 Acute Pancreatitis

Master the pathophysiology of pancreatic autodigestion, severity scoring, and evidence-based management of this common and potentially lethal condition.

⏱️ 25 min 📊 High Yield 🎯 GI/Emergency

📋 Diagnosis: Revised Atlanta Criteria

Requires 2 of 3 criteria:

  • Characteristic epigastric pain (radiating to back)
  • Lipase or amylase ≥3× upper limit of normal
  • Imaging findings consistent with pancreatitis

🎯 Lipase vs Amylase

Lipase is preferred: More specific, stays elevated longer (8-14 days vs 3-5 days), not elevated in other conditions like parotitis or macroamylasemia.

🔬 Pathophysiology

1
Triggering Event
Gallstone impaction, alcohol, hypertriglyceridemia → acinar cell injury
2
Premature Enzyme Activation
Trypsinogen converts to trypsin INSIDE the pancreas (normally in duodenum)
3
Autodigestion Cascade
Active trypsin activates other zymogens → lipase, elastase, phospholipase → pancreatic autodigestion
4
SIRS Response
Cytokine release → systemic inflammation → can progress to MODS, ARDS, shock

📝 "I GET SMASHED" - Causes of Pancreatitis

Idiopathic, Gallstones (#1), Ethanol (#2), Trauma, Steroids, Mumps/Malignancy, Autoimmune, Scorpion stings, Hyperlipidemia/Hypercalcemia/Hypothermia, ERCP, Drugs

📊 Severity Classification (Revised Atlanta)

Severity Definition Mortality
Mild No organ failure, no local complications <1%
Moderately Severe Transient organ failure (<48h) OR local complications ~3%
Severe Persistent organ failure (>48h) 30-50%

BISAP Score (Bedside Severity)

Criteria (1 point each)
  • BUN >25 mg/dL
  • Impaired mental status
  • SIRS criteria ≥2
  • Age >60 years
  • Pleural effusion
Interpretation
  • Score 0-2: Low risk (~2% mortality)
  • Score 3-5: High risk (~15-20% mortality)
  • Calculate within 24 hours
  • Simpler than Ranson (available at admission)

💊 Management

🔑 Cornerstone: Aggressive Fluids
  • LR preferred (less hyperchloremic acidosis)
  • Goal: 250-500 mL/hr initially
  • Target UOP >0.5 mL/kg/hr
  • Reassess frequently (avoid over-resuscitation)
🍽️ Nutrition
  • Early oral feeding when tolerated (within 24-48h)
  • Enteral > parenteral (maintains gut barrier)
  • Low-fat, soft diet to start
  • NPO only if intractable vomiting/ileus

Gallstone Pancreatitis - Special Considerations

  • ERCP within 24-48h if cholangitis or persistent biliary obstruction
  • Cholecystectomy during same admission (if mild) to prevent recurrence
  • Do NOT delay cholecystectomy for "cooling off" - increases recurrence risk

📝 Key Takeaways

🎯 Must Know
  • 2 of 3 Atlanta criteria for diagnosis
  • Lipase > amylase (more specific)
  • Gallstones #1, Alcohol #2 cause
  • Severity = organ failure persistence
💊 Treatment Pearls
  • Aggressive IV fluids (LR preferred)
  • Early enteral nutrition
  • Cholecystectomy same admission if gallstone
  • ERCP only if cholangitis/obstruction
