MECHANISM
Pathophysiology

Chronic liver injury → hepatic fibrosis → cirrhosis (irreversible architectural distortion with regenerative nodules) → portal hypertension (HVPG >5 mmHg, clinically significant >10 mmHg).

CauseKey Features
Alcohol#1 in Western countries. Spectrum: steatosis → hepatitis → cirrhosis. AST:ALT >2:1 (B6 deficiency impairs ALT)
MASLD (NAFLD)Most common liver disease worldwide. Metabolic syndrome association. Diagnosis of exclusion
Hepatitis C#1 indication for liver transplant historically. Now curable with DAAs (95%+ SVR)
Hepatitis BVertical transmission. Can cause HCC without cirrhosis. Surface antigen/antibody pattern
Autoimmune hepatitisYoung women, ANA/anti-smooth muscle Ab. Treat: steroids + azathioprine
HemochromatosisIron overload (HFE gene, C282Y). Bronze diabetes. Transferrin sat >45%, elevated ferritin. Treat: phlebotomy
Wilson diseaseCopper overload. Young patients. Kayser-Fleischer rings, neuropsych symptoms. Low ceruloplasmin. Treat: penicillamine, zinc
CLINICAL
Complications of Cirrhosis
ComplicationManagement
AscitesSalt restriction + spironolactone ± furosemide (100:40 ratio). Paracentesis if tense. SBP prophylaxis if protein <1.5 g/dL. TIPS for refractory
SBPPMN >250/mm³ in ascitic fluid = diagnostic (don't wait for culture). Treat: ceftriaxone (or cefotaxime). IV albumin 1.5 g/kg day 1 + 1 g/kg day 3 (reduces renal failure). Lifelong prophylaxis with daily norfloxacin/TMP-SMX after first episode
Hepatic encephalopathyLactulose (goal: 2-3 BM/day) + rifaximin. Identify/treat precipitant (infection, GI bleed, constipation, electrolytes, meds)
Hepatorenal syndromeType 1 (rapid, Cr doubles in 2 weeks) vs Type 2 (gradual). Treatment: midodrine + octreotide + albumin, or norepinephrine + albumin. Definitive: liver transplant
Variceal bleedingOctreotide + antibiotics + EBL (see GI bleeding module)
HCC screeningUltrasound + AFP every 6 months in all cirrhotics
🔑 SAAG (Serum-Ascites Albumin Gradient): SAAG ≥1.1 = portal hypertension (cirrhosis, HF, Budd-Chiari). SAAG <1.1 = non-portal (malignancy, TB peritonitis, nephrotic syndrome). Calculate: serum albumin – ascitic albumin.
TREATMENT
Transplant & Scoring

MELD-Na Score

Determines transplant priority: uses bilirubin, INR, creatinine, sodium. Higher = sicker = higher priority. Also prognostic for 3-month mortality. MELD >15 generally benefits from transplant listing.

Child-Pugh Score

Albumin, Bilirubin, Coagulation (INR), ascites (Degree), Encephalopathy → A (5-6, well-compensated), B (7-9, significant dysfunction), C (10-15, decompensated).

⚠️ Transplant Contraindications: Active alcohol/substance use (typically 6-month sobriety), uncontrolled infection, extrahepatic malignancy, advanced cardiopulmonary disease, active psychiatric issues preventing compliance, anatomic contraindications.
