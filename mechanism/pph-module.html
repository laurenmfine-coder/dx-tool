Etiology (4 T's)
🔑 The 4 T's of PPH:
CauseFrequencyKey Features
Tone (uterine atony)#1 cause (70-80%)Boggy, soft uterus fails to contract. Risk: overdistension (multiples, polyhydramnios, macrosomia), prolonged labor, grand multiparity, chorioamnionitis, tocolytics (MgSO4)
Trauma~20%Lacerations (cervical, vaginal, perineal), hematomas, uterine rupture (prior CS scar). Operative delivery increases risk
Tissue~10%Retained placental fragments, succenturiate lobe, placenta accreta spectrum. US to evaluate
Thrombin~1%Coagulopathy: DIC (abruption, amniotic fluid embolism, HELLP), pre-existing disorders (vWD), anticoagulant use

Definition: Cumulative blood loss ≥1000 mL OR blood loss with signs of hypovolemia within 24h of delivery (regardless of route).

CLINICAL
Placenta Accreta Spectrum
TypeInvasion Depth
AccretaAttaches to myometrium (doesn't invade)
IncretaInvades INTO myometrium
PercretaInvades THROUGH myometrium to serosa (± bladder)
⚠️ Risk factors: Prior cesarean delivery (#1 — risk increases with number of prior CS) + placenta previa (together = very high risk). Diagnosed prenatally with MRI/US. Management: planned cesarean hysterectomy with multidisciplinary team. Do NOT attempt manual placental removal — massive hemorrhage.
TREATMENT
Stepwise Management
StepIntervention
1. Bimanual massageFirst intervention for uterine atony. One hand in uterus, one on abdomen — compress and massage
2. UterotonicsOxytocin (first-line), methylergonovine (contraindicated in HTN), carboprost/hemabate (15-methyl PGF2α — contraindicated in ASTHMA), misoprostol (rectal), TXA (tranexamic acid — within 3h)
3. TamponadeBakri balloon (intrauterine — applies pressure to uterine wall). Effective in 80%+
4. SurgicalB-Lynch compression suture, uterine artery ligation, internal iliac artery ligation. Hysterectomy (definitive — last resort)
🔑 TXA (Tranexamic Acid): Give within 3 hours of PPH onset (WOMAN trial — reduced death from bleeding). 1g IV over 10 min. Repeat at 30 min if still bleeding. Antifibrinolytic — stabilizes clots. Now part of standard PPH protocols worldwide.
