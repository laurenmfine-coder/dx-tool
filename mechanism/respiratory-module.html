🫁 Respiratory Failure

Types of Respiratory Failure

Type 1: Hypoxemic

Definition: PaO₂ < 60 mmHg on room air
Problem: Oxygenation failure
CO₂: Normal or LOW (hyperventilating)
A-a gradient: Usually ELEVATED
Causes: V/Q mismatch, shunt, diffusion impairment
Examples: Pneumonia, ARDS, PE, pulmonary edema, ILD

Type 2: Hypercapnic

Definition: PaCO₂ > 45 mmHg with acidosis
Problem: Ventilation failure
A-a gradient: Often NORMAL
Causes: Can't move enough air
Examples: COPD exacerbation, neuromuscular (MG, GBS), overdose, obesity hypoventilation

A-a Gradient: Localizing the Problem

A-a = PAO₂ - PaO₂ = [FiO₂(713) - PaCO₂/0.8] - PaO₂
Normal = (Age/4) + 4 (roughly 10-15 in young adult)

Normal A-a Gradient

Lungs are fine, problem is elsewhere
  • • Hypoventilation (CNS, neuromuscular)
  • • Low FiO₂ (high altitude)

Elevated A-a Gradient

Problem IS in the lungs
  • • V/Q mismatch (most common)
  • • Shunt (blood bypasses alveoli)
  • • Diffusion impairment

5 Causes of Hypoxemia

1. V/Q Mismatch (Most Common)
Responds to O₂
Ventilation and perfusion not matched. Examples: COPD, pneumonia, PE, asthma
2. Shunt
Does NOT respond to O₂
Blood bypasses ventilated alveoli. Examples: ARDS, pneumonia (consolidation), AVM, intracardiac shunt
3. Hypoventilation
Responds to O₂
Not moving enough air. Normal A-a gradient. Examples: Overdose, obesity, neuromuscular
4. Diffusion Impairment
Responds to O₂
Thickened alveolar membrane. Examples: ILD, pulmonary fibrosis (worsens with exercise)
5. Low Inspired O₂
Responds to O₂
High altitude. Normal A-a gradient.
Key: If hypoxemia doesn't improve with 100% O₂ → Think SHUNT

🌊 ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)

Berlin Criteria

  • • Timing: Within 1 week of insult
  • • Imaging: Bilateral opacities (not effusions/nodules)
  • • Origin: Not fully explained by heart failure
  • • Oxygenation (P/F ratio on PEEP ≥5):
    • - Mild: 200-300
    • - Moderate: 100-200
    • - Severe: <100

Mechanism

Insult → Alveolar inflammation → Capillary leak → Protein-rich edema → Surfactant dysfunction → Alveolar collapse → Shunt
Causes: Sepsis, pneumonia, aspiration, trauma, pancreatitis, transfusion (TRALI)

Ventilator Basics

Oxygenation Controls

  • FiO₂ ↑FiO₂ = ↑PaO₂ (goal FiO₂ <60% to avoid O₂ toxicity)
  • PEEP Keeps alveoli open at end-expiration. ↑PEEP = ↑Oxygenation (but ↓venous return)

Ventilation (CO₂) Controls

  • Tidal Volume (Vt) ↑Vt = ↓CO₂ (but risk lung injury)
  • Respiratory Rate (RR) ↑RR = ↓CO₂
Minute Ventilation = Vt × RR

Common Vent Modes

AC (Assist-Control)
Guarantees Vt or pressure for each breath (patient or machine triggered)
Full support - common initial mode
SIMV
Set number of supported breaths; others are spontaneous
Partial support
PSV (Pressure Support)
Patient triggers all breaths; vent adds pressure boost
Weaning mode
