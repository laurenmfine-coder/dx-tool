Myasthenic Crisis

Respiratory Failure in Myasthenia Gravis

Pathophysiology Review

Myasthenia Gravis: Autoimmune attack on neuromuscular junction

  • 85%: Anti-AChR (acetylcholine receptor) antibodies
  • 5-8%: Anti-MuSK antibodies (more bulbar, respiratory)
  • 3-5%: Seronegative (may have LRP4 antibodies)

Result: Reduced functional AChRs → fatigable weakness

💡 MuSK-positive MG: Worse respiratory involvement, may not respond to cholinesterase inhibitors

The 20/30/40 Rule

Bedside pulmonary monitoring in MG crisis:

  • FVC <20 mL/kg: Consider ICU admission
  • NIF <-30 cmH2O: Weak inspiratory muscles
  • FVC <15 mL/kg or NIF <-20: Intubate

Don't rely on: SpO2 (late sign), blood gas (late sign), patient's subjective dyspnea

💡 Serial measurements more important than single value - trend toward threshold = act

Crisis Precipitants

Medication triggers:

  • Aminoglycosides, fluoroquinolones, macrolides
  • Beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers
  • Magnesium (blocks NMJ)
  • Neuromuscular blockers (prolonged paralysis)
  • Checkpoint inhibitors (can induce MG)

Other triggers:

  • Infection (most common)
  • Surgery/stress
  • Pregnancy/postpartum
  • Tapering immunosuppression too quickly

Treatment: PLEX vs IVIG

Plasmapheresis (PLEX):

  • Faster onset (24-48 hours)
  • Removes pathogenic antibodies
  • Requires large-bore access
  • May cause hypotension, hypocalcemia

IVIG:

  • Onset 3-5 days
  • Mechanism: immunomodulation, anti-idiotype antibodies
  • Easier to administer
  • Risk: headache, renal dysfunction, thrombosis

Both: Temporary benefit (weeks), bridge to immunosuppression

💡 PLEX preferred if rapid deterioration; IVIG comparable efficacy overall

RSI Considerations

Avoid: Succinylcholine (prolonged paralysis, unpredictable response)

Use: Rocuronium at reduced dose (1/3-1/2 normal) due to receptor paucity

Have available: Sugammadex for reversal

Post-intubation:

  • Lung-protective ventilation
  • Hold pyridostigmine initially (reduces secretions)
  • Start PLEX/IVIG promptly
  • Avoid neuromuscular blockers for sedation

🎯 Key Points

  • 20/30/40: FVC <20 mL/kg ICU, NIF <-30 weak, intubate <15/<-20
  • Don't trust SpO2 or ABG - serial FVC/NIF most reliable
  • Common triggers: infection, aminoglycosides, magnesium, surgery
  • PLEX faster (24-48h), IVIG easier; both bridge to immunosuppression
  • RSI: NO succinylcholine, reduce rocuronium dose, have sugammadex ready
