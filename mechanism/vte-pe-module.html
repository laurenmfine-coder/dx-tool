MECHANISM
Risk Factors & Pathophysiology

PE = obstruction of pulmonary vasculature by thrombus (most from DVT in lower extremities → embolize to lungs). Virchow triad: stasis + endothelial injury + hypercoagulability.

Risk Factor CategoryExamples
StasisImmobility, long travel, post-surgical, paralysis, hospitalization
Endothelial injurySurgery, trauma, central lines, prior DVT
HypercoagulabilityFactor V Leiden (#1 inherited), malignancy, OCP/pregnancy, antiphospholipid syndrome, protein C/S deficiency
🔑 PE kills by right heart failure. Massive PE → acute RV pressure overload → RV dilation → decreased LV filling → cardiogenic shock → death. Not primarily a gas exchange problem — it's a hemodynamic catastrophe.
CLINICAL
Diagnosis

Presentation

Dyspnea (most common), pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, tachypnea. Hemoptysis, syncope (massive PE), leg swelling (concurrent DVT). ABG: respiratory alkalosis (hyperventilation), elevated A-a gradient.

Diagnostic Algorithm

StepToolDetails
1Wells scoreClinical probability: <2 = low, 2-6 = moderate, >6 = high. Components: DVT signs (3), PE most likely dx (3), HR >100 (1.5), immobility/surgery (1.5), prior DVT/PE (1.5), hemoptysis (1), malignancy (1)
2aD-dimerLow probability → D-dimer. If negative → PE excluded. High sensitivity, low specificity. NOT useful in high-probability patients
2bCT pulmonary angiography (CTPA)Gold standard imaging. High probability → go directly to CTPA (skip D-dimer)
AltV/Q scanIf CT contraindicated (renal failure, contrast allergy). Normal scan excludes PE; high probability + high clinical suspicion = treat
⚠️ Massive PE signs: Hypotension (SBP <90), syncope, RV dilation on echo, elevated troponin/BNP. Requires emergent intervention.
TREATMENT
Management by Severity
SeverityDefinitionTreatment
Low riskHemodynamically stable, no RV dysfunctionAnticoagulation: DOAC preferred (apixaban/rivaroxaban — no bridging needed) or LMWH → warfarin. Duration: provoked 3 months, unprovoked ≥indefinite
SubmassiveStable BP but RV dysfunction (echo/CT) or elevated troponinAnticoagulation + close monitoring in ICU. Consider catheter-directed therapy. Systemic thrombolytics controversial
MassiveHemodynamic instability (SBP <90)Systemic thrombolytics (alteplase). If contraindicated: catheter-directed therapy or surgical embolectomy. IVC filter if anticoagulation contraindicated
🔑 DOACs (apixaban, rivaroxaban): Now first-line for PE/DVT. No bridging with heparin needed. Exceptions requiring heparin → warfarin: antiphospholipid syndrome, severe renal failure, pregnancy (LMWH only).
