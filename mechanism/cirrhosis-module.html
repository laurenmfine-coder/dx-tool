💧 Ascites

Pathophysiology

Portal hypertension + splanchnic vasodilation → ↓effective arterial volume → RAAS activation → Na/H2O retention + low albumin (↓oncotic pressure)

Diagnostic Paracentesis

Always do on new ascites or admission!
Send: Cell count, albumin, protein, culture

SAAG (Serum-Ascites Albumin Gradient)

SAAG ≥1.1 = Portal HTN
Cirrhosis, CHF, Budd-Chiari, portal vein thrombosis
SAAG <1.1 = Non-Portal HTN
Peritoneal carcinomatosis, TB, nephrotic, pancreatitis

Treatment

First-Line:
  • • Sodium restriction (2g/day)
  • • Spironolactone 100mg + Furosemide 40mg (100:40 ratio)
  • • Titrate q3-5 days (max spiro 400mg, lasix 160mg)
  • • Goal: 0.5kg/day (no edema) or 1kg/day (with edema)
Refractory Ascites:
  • • Serial large-volume paracentesis (LVP)
  • • Give albumin 6-8g per liter removed if >5L
  • • TIPS (transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt)
  • • Liver transplant evaluation

🦠 Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)

Diagnosis

Ascitic fluid PMN ≥250 cells/mm³
Culture may be negative in ~40%
Symptoms: Fever, abdominal pain, AMS, worsening ascites - but may be subtle!

Common Organisms

• E. coli (most common)
• Klebsiella
• Streptococcus pneumoniae
• Enterococcus

Treatment

Antibiotics (5 days):
  • • Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily (preferred)
  • • Or cefotaxime 2g q8h
  • • Fluoroquinolone if penicillin allergic
IV Albumin (prevents HRS):
  • • 1.5 g/kg on day 1
  • • 1.0 g/kg on day 3
  • • Especially if Cr >1, BUN >30, or bili >4
SBP Prophylaxis: Fluoroquinolone (norfloxacin) or TMP-SMX if: prior SBP, GI bleed, or ascites protein <1.5 with renal dysfunction or liver failure

🧠 Hepatic Encephalopathy (HE)

Pathophysiology

↑Ammonia (from gut bacteria/protein) → crosses BBB → astrocyte swelling, neurotoxicity
Note: Ammonia level correlates poorly with severity

Precipitants (look for these!)

  • • Infection (SBP, UTI, pneumonia)
  • • GI bleeding
  • • Constipation
  • • Medications (benzos, opioids)
  • • Electrolyte abnormalities (hypoK, hypoNa)
  • • Dietary protein excess
  • • Dehydration / Over-diuresis

West Haven Criteria

Grade 0
Normal
Grade 1
Mild confusion, sleep disturbance
Grade 2
Lethargy, disorientation, asterixis
Grade 3
Somnolent but arousable, marked confusion
Grade 4
Coma

Treatment

First-Line:
  • • Lactulose 25-30mL q1-2h until bowel movement
  • • Then titrate to 2-3 soft BMs/day
  • • Can give as enema if obtunded
  • • Mechanism: ↓intestinal pH → traps NH3 as NH4+
Add-on if Refractory:
  • • Rifaximin 550mg BID
  • • ↓gut ammonia production
  • • Reduces recurrence
Also: Treat precipitant, avoid sedatives, adequate nutrition (do NOT restrict protein!), zinc supplementation

🫘 Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)

Pathophysiology

Splanchnic vasodilation → ↓effective arterial volume → renal vasoconstriction → functional AKI (kidneys are structurally normal)

Diagnostic Criteria

  • • Cirrhosis with ascites
  • • AKI (Cr >1.5) without other cause
  • • No improvement after volume expansion (albumin 1g/kg × 2 days)
  • • No shock, nephrotoxins, or parenchymal disease
HRS-AKI (Type 1)
Rapidly progressive. Cr doubles in <2 weeks. Often triggered by SBP. Median survival: 2 weeks untreated.
HRS-NAKI (Type 2)
Slowly progressive. Associated with refractory ascites. Better prognosis.

Treatment

Medical Therapy:
  • • Albumin 20-40g daily (volume expansion)
  • • Midodrine (α-agonist) + Octreotide
  • • Or Norepinephrine (ICU setting)
  • • Or Terlipressin (if available)
Definitive:
  • • Liver transplant (curative)
  • • TIPS (bridge to transplant)
  • • Dialysis (bridge, not definitive)
⚠️ Stop diuretics, NSAIDs, nephrotoxins. Avoid large volume paracentesis without albumin replacement.

🩸 Variceal Bleeding

See GI Bleeding Module for full details.
Acute Management:
  • • Octreotide (splanchnic vasoconstriction)
  • • Antibiotics (ceftriaxone × 7 days - ↓mortality!)
  • • EGD within 12h → Band ligation
  • • Restrictive transfusion (Hgb >7)
Primary Prophylaxis:
  • • Non-selective β-blocker (propranolol, nadolol, carvedilol)
  • • Or band ligation if β-blocker intolerant
  • • Screen all cirrhotics with EGD
