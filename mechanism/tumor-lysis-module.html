⚙️ Tumor Lysis Syndrome

Pathophysiology & Clinical Correlations | Oncology

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • High-risk: high tumor burden, high proliferation rate
  • Lab TLS: ↑K, ↑PO4, ↑uric acid, ↓Ca
  • Prevention: hydration, allopurinol, monitor labs
  • Rasburicase for established hyperuricemia

🔬 Core Pathophysiology

Cellular Lysis Release

Rapid tumor cell death releases intracellular contents.

Pathway

Chemotherapy/spontaneous lysis→Massive cell death→K+, PO4, uric acid release→Hypocalcemia (Ca-PO4 binding)→Uric acid nephropathy → AKI

🏥 Clinical Correlations

Hyperkalemia

Mechanism: Release of intracellular potassium stores

Clinical Significance: Most immediately life-threatening; cardiac arrhythmias

Hyperuricemia

Mechanism: Purine breakdown to uric acid

Clinical Significance: Uric acid crystals precipitate in renal tubules → AKI

Hypocalcemia

Mechanism: Calcium binds to excess phosphate

Clinical Significance: Causes tetany, QT prolongation, seizures
