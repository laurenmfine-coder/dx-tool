Drug Allergy Mechanisms

Type I-IV Hypersensitivity in Drug Reactions

Type I (IgE-Mediated)

Mechanism: Drug acts as hapten → binds carrier protein → IgE sensitization on mast cells/basophils

Timeline: Minutes to 1 hour (immediate)

Examples: Penicillin anaphylaxis, cephalosporin reactions

Clinical: Urticaria, angioedema, bronchospasm, anaphylaxis

💡 Beta-lactam cross-reactivity is ~2% between penicillins and cephalosporins

Type II (Cytotoxic)

Mechanism: Drug binds cell surface → IgG/IgM antibodies → complement activation/cell lysis

Timeline: Days to weeks

Examples: Drug-induced hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia, agranulocytosis

Key Drugs: Methyldopa (hemolysis), heparin (HIT), quinidine (thrombocytopenia)

Type III (Immune Complex)

Mechanism: Circulating drug-antibody complexes → deposit in vessels → complement activation

Timeline: 1-3 weeks after exposure

Clinical: Serum sickness, drug-induced vasculitis, fever, arthralgias, lymphadenopathy

Key Drugs: Biologics, horse-derived antivenoms, rituximab

Type IV (Delayed/T-Cell)

Mechanism: Drug-specific T cells → cytokine release → tissue damage

Timeline: 24-72 hours to weeks

Subtypes:

  • IVa: Monocyte activation (contact dermatitis)
  • IVb: Eosinophilic inflammation (DRESS)
  • IVc: CD8 cytotoxicity (SJS/TEN)
  • IVd: Neutrophilic inflammation (AGEP)

💡 DRESS: Fever + rash + eosinophilia + internal organ involvement 2-8 weeks after drug

Non-Immunologic Reactions

Types that mimic allergy:

  • Pseudoallergy: Direct mast cell degranulation (opioids, vancomycin "red man")
  • Aspirin/NSAID: COX-1 inhibition → leukotriene shift (not IgE-mediated)
  • ACE Inhibitor Angioedema: Bradykinin accumulation
  • Radiocontrast: Direct mast cell activation (reduced with modern agents)

💡 NSAID reactions can occur on first exposure - no sensitization required

🎯 Key Points

  • Type I (immediate): IgE-mediated, minutes-hour, anaphylaxis risk
  • Type IV (delayed): T-cell mediated, days-weeks, includes DRESS/SJS/TEN
  • Cross-reactivity varies by drug class structure
  • Many "allergies" are actually non-immunologic reactions
  • Detailed history essential: timing, symptoms, rechallenge tolerance
