🧠 Bacterial Meningitis

A CNS infection requiring rapid diagnosis and treatment. Don't delay antibiotics for imaging - minutes matter for neurologic outcomes.

⏱️ 22 min📊 ID Emergency🎯 ID/Neurology

🩺 Classic Triad + Jolt Accentuation

Classic Triad (Full triad in <50%)
  • Fever
  • Neck stiffness (nuchal rigidity)
  • Altered mental status

95% have at least 2 of: fever, neck stiffness, AMS, or headache

Physical Exam Signs
  • Kernig's: Pain with knee extension (hip flexed)
  • Brudzinski's: Hip flexion with neck flexion
  • Jolt accentuation: Headache worse with horizontal head rotation (sensitive)

⚠️ Don't Forget Listeria!

Add ampicillin for patients >50 years, pregnant, immunocompromised, or alcoholic. Listeria is NOT covered by cephalosporins!

💊 Treatment Sequence

🎯 Antibiotics BEFORE LP if:

• Signs of increased ICP
• Need CT before LP (focal deficits, immunocompromised, papilledema)
• LP will be delayed >30 minutes

Sequence: Blood cultures → Antibiotics + Dexamethasone → CT → LP

💊 Dexamethasone
  • Give BEFORE or WITH first antibiotic dose
  • 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h x 4 days
  • Reduces mortality in pneumococcal meningitis
  • Continue only if pneumococcus confirmed
⏱️ Treatment Duration
  • N. meningitidis: 7 days
  • H. influenzae: 7 days
  • S. pneumoniae: 10-14 days
  • Listeria: 21+ days
  • Gram-negative bacilli: 21 days
