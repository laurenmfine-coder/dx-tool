Fever + Travel History = Geographic Differentials

The same symptom means different things in different places

Fever + Recent Travel: Think Geographically

🌴 Sub-Saharan Africa
  • • Malaria - #1 always rule out
  • • Typhoid fever
  • • Dengue (urban areas)
  • • Viral hemorrhagic fevers
  • • Schistosomiasis (freshwater)
🌿 South/Southeast Asia
  • • Dengue - very common
  • • Typhoid fever
  • • Malaria (certain areas)
  • • Chikungunya
  • • Japanese encephalitis
🏝️ Latin America/Caribbean
  • • Dengue - #1
  • • Zika, Chikungunya
  • • Chagas disease (rural)
  • • Malaria (Amazon)
  • • Yellow fever (unvaccinated)
🏔️ Middle East/N. Africa
  • • Typhoid fever
  • • Brucellosis (unpasteurized)
  • • MERS-CoV (Arabian Peninsula)
  • • Leishmaniasis
  • • Schistosomiasis (Nile)

Incubation Periods - Timing Matters!

< 2 weeks Malaria, Dengue, Chikungunya, Typhoid, Bacterial enteritis
2-6 weeks Malaria (can be longer), Hepatitis A/E, Acute HIV, Leptospirosis
> 6 weeks TB, Hepatitis B, Visceral leishmaniasis, Amoebic liver abscess

Species Differences (Mechanism → Clinical)

P. falciparum (Most Deadly)
Mechanism: Infected RBCs express PfEMP1 → bind endothelium → sequestration
Result: Cerebral malaria, organ failure, high parasitemia
No dormant liver stage - treat and it's gone
P. vivax / P. ovale (Relapsing)
Mechanism: Hypnozoites persist in liver (dormant)
Result: Relapses months-years later
Requires primaquine for radical cure (check G6PD first!)

Neglected Tropical Diseases

1+ billion people affected. Often forgotten in medical education.

🪱 Schistosomiasis
Exposure: Freshwater (lakes, rivers)
Mechanism: Eggs trapped in tissues → granuloma → fibrosis
S. mansoni: Liver fibrosis, portal HTN
S. haematobium: Bladder CA risk
🦟 Chagas Disease
Vector: Triatomine "kissing bug"
Mechanism: T. cruzi → chronic inflammation
Result: Cardiomyopathy, megacolon, megaesophagus
Endemic: Latin America
🪰 Leishmaniasis
Vector: Sandfly
Cutaneous: Skin ulcers
Visceral (Kala-azar): Hepatosplenomegaly, pancytopenia, fever
Endemic: South Asia, East Africa, Brazil
🪱 Soil-Transmitted Helminths
Ascaris: Intestinal obstruction, biliary
Hookworm: Iron deficiency anemia
Strongyloides: Hyperinfection in immunocompromised
Mechanism: Chronic blood/nutrient loss

Resource-Stratified Diagnosis & Treatment

Not everywhere has CT, MRI, or PCR. Clinical skills matter more.

Diagnosis Without Advanced Labs

Malaria
Ideal: Thick/thin smear, RDT
Resource-limited: Treat empirically if high suspicion + endemic area
Clinical: Fever + splenomegaly + anemia + travel
Typhoid
Ideal: Blood culture
Resource-limited: Widal test (imperfect), clinical diagnosis
Clinical: Stepladder fever, relative bradycardia, rose spots
TB
Ideal: GeneXpert, culture
Resource-limited: Sputum smear (3 samples), CXR
Clinical: Cough >2 weeks + weight loss + night sweats + contact

WHO Essential Medicines Perspective

Available Widely
Artemisinin combinations, Ampicillin, Metronidazole, Doxycycline
Often Unavailable
Liposomal amphotericin B, Many IV antibiotics, Advanced antivirals
