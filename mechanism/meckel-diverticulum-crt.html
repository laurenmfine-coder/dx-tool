📖 Teaching Point

Meckel diverticulum = the most common congenital anomaly of the GI tract (2% of population). It's a remnant of the vitelline (omphalomesenteric) duct that connects the midgut to the yolk sac during embryonic development. The duct normally obliterates by weeks 7-8. Persistence → Meckel diverticulum (incomplete obliteration of the intestinal end).

📖 Teaching Point

Vitelline duct remnant spectrum: (1) MECKEL DIVERTICULUM — most common, incomplete obliteration of the intestinal end. (2) Vitelline cyst/omphalomesenteric cyst — only the middle portion persists. (3) Vitelline fistula — entire duct patent (umbilical drainage of intestinal contents). (4) Fibrous band — obliterated cord connecting ileum to umbilicus (can cause bowel obstruction/volvulus). (5) Umbilical sinus — only the umbilical end persists.

📖 Teaching Point

Meckel scan sensitivity: ~85% in children, ~60% in adults (lower because adults have less ectopic gastric tissue). False negatives: no gastric mucosa in the diverticulum (only 50% of all Meckels contain ectopic tissue). To improve sensitivity: pretreat with pentagastrin (stimulates mucosal uptake) or cimetidine (blocks secretion but not uptake, preventing washout). The scan also detects gastric mucosa in other ectopic locations (enteric duplications).

📖 Teaching Point

Meckel complications (in ~4-6% of cases): (1) BLEEDING (most common in children) — from ectopic gastric mucosa; (2) OBSTRUCTION — from intussusception (diverticulum acts as lead point), volvulus around a fibrous band, or Littre hernia (Meckel in inguinal hernia); (3) DIVERTICULITIS — mimics appendicitis but in the wrong location; (4) PERFORATION — from ulceration or diverticulitis.

📖 Teaching Point

Complete Rule of 2s: 2% of population; 2 feet (60cm) from ileocecal valve; 2 inches (5cm) long; 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric + pancreatic); 2:1 male:female ratio for symptomatic cases; most commonly symptomatic before age 2. Additional pearl: Meckel diverticulum is a TRUE diverticulum (contains all 3 layers: mucosa, submucosa, muscularis propria) — unlike colonic diverticula which are false (mucosa and submucosa only).
