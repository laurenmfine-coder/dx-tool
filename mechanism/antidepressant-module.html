Antidepressant Pharmacology

🧪 Antidepressant Classes

Major Classes & Mechanisms

ClassMechanismKey DrugsPrimary Use

Atypical Antidepressants

  • Bupropion: NE/DA reuptake inhibitor. No sexual side effects, no weight gain. Also for smoking cessation. CONTRAINDICATED in seizure disorders, bulimia, anorexia.
  • Mirtazapine: α2 antagonist + 5-HT2/3 antagonist + H1 antagonist. Causes weight gain + sedation (useful in underweight/insomniac patients). 'NaSSA' — increases both NE and 5-HT release.
  • Trazodone: 5-HT2A antagonist + weak SRI. Primarily used for insomnia at low doses. Rare but serious: priapism.
  • Vilazodone/Vortioxetine: Multimodal — SRI + 5-HT1A partial agonist. Less sexual dysfunction than pure SSRIs.

⚠️ Side Effects & Dangers

SSRI/SNRI Common Side Effects

  • GI: Nausea (most common early side effect), diarrhea — usually transient (1-2 weeks)
  • Sexual dysfunction: Decreased libido, anorgasmia, delayed ejaculation — common, persistent. Consider bupropion or mirtazapine if problematic.
  • Weight gain: Paroxetine worst among SSRIs. Mirtazapine also causes significant weight gain.
  • QTc prolongation: Citalopram (dose cap 40mg; 20mg in elderly). Escitalopram lower risk.
  • Bleeding risk: SSRIs inhibit platelet serotonin uptake → increased bleeding, especially with NSAIDs/anticoagulants.
  • Hyponatremia/SIADH: Especially in elderly. Check sodium if confusion develops.

Serotonin Syndrome

FeatureDetails
CauseExcess serotonergic activity — usually from DRUG COMBINATIONS (SSRI + MAOI, SSRI + tramadol, SSRI + linezolid)
TriadMental status changes (agitation, confusion) + Autonomic instability (hyperthermia, tachycardia, diaphoresis) + Neuromuscular excitation (CLONUS, hyperreflexia, tremor)
Key findingCLONUS (especially lower extremity) — distinguishes from NMS
TreatmentStop offending agents, IV fluids, benzodiazepines for agitation, cyproheptadine (serotonin antagonist). Avoid dantrolene (that's for NMS).
Prevention14-day washout between MAOI and other serotonergic drugs. Fluoxetine needs 5 WEEKS (long half-life).

⚠️ TCA Overdose = Cardiac Emergency

TCAs block sodium channels → QRS widening → cardiac arrhythmias → death. Overdose triad: altered mental status + seizures + cardiac arrhythmias. QRS >100ms = sodium bicarb immediately. Most lethal antidepressant in overdose. This is why TCAs are rarely first-line.

Black Box Warning

  • ALL antidepressants carry FDA black box warning for increased suicidality in patients <25 years
  • Important context: Risk is primarily suicidal IDEATION, not completed suicide. The risk of untreated depression far exceeds this risk.
  • Clinical approach: Monitor closely in first 4-8 weeks, especially in adolescents/young adults. Don't avoid treatment — monitor more frequently.
