💥 Mast Cell Biology & Mastocytosis

Understanding the sentinel cells of allergic inflammation

🔬 Mast Cell Biology

Mast cells originate from CD34+ hematopoietic stem cells. Unlike basophils, mast cell precursors migrate to tissues where they mature under the influence of stem cell factor (SCF) binding to KIT (CD117).

⚠️ MRGPRX2: The "Pseudo-Allergy" Receptor

MRGPRX2 explains why some drugs cause reactions on FIRST exposure without prior sensitization. Key triggers: vancomycin, fluoroquinolones, opioids, neuromuscular blockers.

💎 Hereditary Alpha-Tryptasemia (HαT)

TPSAB1 gene duplications cause chronically elevated tryptase (10-25 ng/mL) without mastocytosis. Present in ~5% of population. Associated with increased anaphylaxis severity and dysautonomia.

🔴 Systemic Mastocytosis

WHO Diagnostic Criteria

1 major + 1 minor, OR 3 minor criteria

Major:

M
Multifocal dense mast cell infiltrates (≥15 cells in aggregates) in bone marrow

Minor:

1
>25% spindle-shaped or atypical mast cells
2
KIT D816V mutation detected
3
Mast cells express CD25 and/or CD2
4
Serum tryptase >20 ng/mL
⚠️ Anaphylaxis Risk

Mastocytosis patients have markedly increased risk of severe anaphylaxis, especially to Hymenoptera venom. Up to 30% of patients with venom anaphylaxis and elevated baseline tryptase have mastocytosis.
