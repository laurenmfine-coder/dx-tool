Error Types (Reason's Classification)

🎯 Slips
Right intention, wrong execution
Example: Picking wrong syringe
⏰ Lapses
Forgetting to do intended action
Example: Forgetting DVT prophylaxis
📏 Rule-Based Mistakes
Applied wrong rule
Example: Adult dose in pediatric patient
🧠 Knowledge Mistakes
Lack of knowledge
Example: Missing rare diagnosis

Errors reach patients when holes in multiple defense layers align

✓ or 💥

10x Insulin Reaches Patient - All Layers Failed:

Prescriber: Slip due to interruption
CPOE: Alert fatigue - clicked through warning
Pharmacy: High volume, missed unusual dose
Nurse: New grad, unfamiliar with typical doses
Blaming one person doesn't fix the system. All layers need strengthening.

Root Cause Analysis: The 5 Whys

Case: Patient received wrong blood type
Why 1Nurse hung wrong unit →
Why 2Wristband was missing →
Why 3Removed for IV, never replaced →
Why 4No standard process requires replacement →
Why 5Policy gap - never addressed
Root Cause: Policy gap
Fix: Mandatory wristband replacement policy + backup ID

High Reliability Organizations

1. Preoccupation with Failure
Treat near-misses as actual events
2. Reluctance to Simplify
Don't stop at "human error" - find system causes
3. Sensitivity to Operations
Leaders stay close to frontline
4. Commitment to Resilience
Train to recover from unexpected
5. Deference to Expertise
Anyone can stop for safety
