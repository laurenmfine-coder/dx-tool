🤔 The Key Mechanistic Question

Why does DKA cause ketoacidosis but HHS doesn't?

Answer: In HHS, there's enough residual insulin to suppress lipolysis (preventing ketone production) but not enough to allow glucose uptake. In DKA, there's virtually NO insulin, so lipolysis proceeds unchecked, flooding the liver with FFAs that are converted to ketones.

The DKA Cascade

1. Insulin Deficiency + Counter-regulatory Hormones ↑
Glucagon, catecholamines, cortisol, GH all rise
2. Hyperglycemia
↑ Hepatic gluconeogenesis + glycogenolysis + ↓ peripheral uptake
3. Osmotic Diuresis
Glucose exceeds renal threshold → glucosuria → polyuria → dehydration
4. Lipolysis → Ketogenesis
FFAs flood liver → β-oxidation → acetoacetate + β-hydroxybutyrate
5. Metabolic Acidosis
Ketoacids consume bicarb → anion gap acidosis
6. Electrolyte Chaos
K+ shifts out of cells (acidosis) → total body K+ depleted (diuresis)

Treatment → Mechanism Match

IV Fluids (NS → 1/2NS)
Replace massive volume deficit (avg 6L)
Insulin (after K+ >3.3)
Stop ketogenesis, allow glucose uptake
Potassium Replacement
Total body depleted; insulin shifts K+ into cells
Dextrose (when glucose <200)
Prevent hypoglycemia while continuing insulin for ketosis

💡 Critical Clinical Pearl

Don't stop insulin when glucose normalizes! The goal is to close the anion gap, not just lower glucose. Continue insulin + add dextrose to prevent hypoglycemia. DKA is resolved when: AG <12, pH >7.3, HCO3 >18, patient eating.

HHS Deep Dive

The insidious progression to hyperosmolar crisis

Why HHS Develops Differently

1.
Partial Insulin Present
Enough to prevent lipolysis/ketosis, not enough for glucose uptake
2.
Slower Onset (days to weeks)
No ketoacidosis = no nausea/vomiting = no early warning
3.
Impaired Thirst/Access
Often elderly, nursing home, dementia - can't compensate for water loss
4.
Extreme Dehydration
Average 9L deficit (vs 6L in DKA)

Calculating Serum Osmolality

Osm = 2(Na) + Glucose/18 + BUN/2.8
Normal
275-295 mOsm/kg
HHS Criteria
>320 mOsm/kg (often >350)
Example: Na 145, Glucose 1000, BUN 40 → 2(145) + 1000/18 + 40/2.8 = 290 + 56 + 14 = 360 mOsm/kg

⚠️ Why Mental Status Changes

Hyperosmolality causes water to shift OUT of brain cells → neuronal shrinkage → altered consciousness

Rule of thumb: Mental status changes typically correlate with osmolality:
  • • 320-330: Mild confusion
  • • 330-350: Lethargy, obtundation
  • • >350: Coma

Treatment: Key Differences from DKA

Fluids are Priority #1
Even more aggressive than DKA; often 1-1.5L/hr initially
Lower Insulin Rates
Often 0.05-0.1 u/kg/hr (vs 0.1-0.14 in DKA); fluids alone drop glucose significantly
Watch for Cerebral Edema
If you correct too fast, water rushes INTO brain → dangerous swelling
Thromboprophylaxis Critical
Hyperviscosity → high thrombosis risk; many advocate full-dose anticoagulation
