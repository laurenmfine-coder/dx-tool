MCAT High Yield Step 1

🧬 Overview: Horizontal Gene Transfer

Bacteria can acquire new genes horizontally from other bacteria or the environment. This enables rapid spread of antibiotic resistance and virulence factors.

Know the THREE mechanisms: Transformation (naked DNA), Transduction (bacteriophage), Conjugation (direct contact via F pilus).

🧠 Memory Aid

"Naked DNA = TransFORMation"

TransDuction = virus (phage) Transfer | Conjugation = Contact Connection

🧫

Transformation

"Uptake of naked DNA from the environment by a competent bacterium"

Mechanism: Donor cell lyses → releases DNA → competent recipient takes up DNA → integrates via homologous recombination

Classic Example: Griffith's experiment (1928) - heat-killed S. pneumoniae transformed live avirulent bacteria

Naturally competent: S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, N. gonorrhoeae

🦠

Transduction

"Transfer of bacterial DNA via bacteriophage"

Generalized: Packaging error - phage accidentally packages bacterial DNA. ANY gene can transfer.

Specialized: Temperate phage excises imprecisely, takes ADJACENT bacterial genes.

Phage-encoded toxins: Shiga toxin (E. coli O157:H7), Diphtheria toxin, Botulinum toxin, Cholera toxin, Erythrogenic toxin (scarlet fever)

🔗

Conjugation

"Direct cell-to-cell transfer via sex pilus (F plasmid system)"

F⁺ → F⁻: F plasmid transferred, recipient becomes F⁺

Hfr → F⁻: F plasmid integrated into chromosome; chromosomal genes transferred; recipient usually stays F⁻

Conjugation is the PRIMARY mechanism for spreading antibiotic resistance (R plasmids) in hospitals!

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Transformation: Naked DNA uptake. Griffith's experiment. Requires competence.
  • Transduction: Phage-mediated. Generalized (any gene) vs Specialized (adjacent genes).
  • Conjugation: F pilus contact. F⁺→F⁻ transfers plasmid. Hfr→F⁻ transfers chromosomal genes.
  • Clinical: Conjugation spreads R plasmids (antibiotic resistance); Transduction spreads toxin genes.
