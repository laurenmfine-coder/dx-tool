Salmonella is MOTILE + H₂S positive (black on TSI). Shigella is NON-motile, no H₂S, extremely LOW infectious dose (10-100 organisms!).

🔶 Salmonella

Types: S. Typhi/Paratyphi (typhoid fever) vs. non-typhoidal (gastroenteritis)

  • Gastroenteritis: Watery diarrhea, fever, cramps. Self-limited. DON'T treat (prolongs carriage).
  • Typhoid fever: "Stepwise" fever, rose spots, hepatosplenomegaly. TREAT with FQ/ceftriaxone/azithro.
  • Bacteremia: More common in immunocompromised, sickle cell, infants/elderly.
  • Sources: Poultry, eggs, reptiles/turtles
Sickle cell + osteomyelitis: Salmonella is #1 cause (vs. S. aureus in general population). Functional asplenia increases risk.

🔷 Shigella

Species: S. sonnei (US, mildest), S. flexneri (developing world), S. dysenteriae (most severe, Shiga toxin → HUS)

  • Bacillary dysentery: BLOODY diarrhea, mucus, tenesmus, high fever
  • Extremely contagious: Low infectious dose, person-to-person spread
  • ALWAYS treat: Azithromycin first-line (shortens illness, reduces spread)
  • Complications: HUS (S. dysenteriae), reactive arthritis, seizures in children
S. dysenteriae type 1 produces Shiga toxin (same as EHEC) → can cause HUS!

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Both: GNR, NON-lactose fermenters, fecal-oral transmission
  • Salmonella: MOTILE, H₂S+, animal reservoir. Don't treat uncomplicated gastroenteritis.
  • Shigella: NON-motile, H₂S-, humans only, LOW infectious dose. ALWAYS treat.
  • Sickle cell osteomyelitis: Think Salmonella
  • Reactive arthritis: Post-Shigella or Salmonella infection
