🔬 Overview

Giardia lamblia (also called G. intestinalis or G. duodenalis) is the most common intestinal parasite in the US. Causes watery diarrhea and malabsorption. Transmitted fecal-orally via contaminated water or person-to-person.

👽

"Old Man Face" Trophozoite

Trophozoite has 2 nuclei ("eyes"), curved median bodies ("mouth"), and 4 pairs of flagella. Pear-shaped. This appearance is pathognomonic on microscopy!

🔬 Life Cycle

Cyst (infectious, hardy, survives chlorination) → ingested → Trophozoite (active, flagellated, adheres to duodenum) → encysts → shed in stool.

💧 Transmission

Contaminated water (streams, wells, pools), fecal-oral, daycare outbreaks, MSM. Beavers are classic reservoir ("beaver fever").

Board Pearl: Giardia cysts are resistant to chlorination — this is why camping/hiking water exposure is classic. Requires filtration or boiling.

🏥 Clinical Features

💩 GI Symptoms

Watery, foul-smelling, fatty diarrhea (steatorrhea). NO blood or WBCs. Bloating, flatulence, nausea, abdominal cramps. Greasy stools that float.

⏱️ Timeline

Incubation 1-2 weeks. Acute: watery diarrhea. Chronic (untreated): malabsorption, weight loss, failure to thrive (children).

🎯 High-Risk Groups

Campers/hikers, daycare children, travelers, IgA deficiency, hypogammaglobulinemia.

Classic Vignette: Hiker returns from camping trip + 2 weeks of bloating, foul-smelling greasy diarrhea (NO blood), weight loss = Giardiasis! Check stool O&P or antigen.
🧪 Diagnosis
  • Stool antigen test (EIA): Preferred — sensitive, specific, single sample
  • Stool O&P (ova and parasites): Cysts or trophozoites; may need 3 samples (intermittent shedding)
  • Stool PCR: Most sensitive if available
  • String test / duodenal aspirate: Rarely needed; for difficult cases
Note: No blood, no fecal WBCs — Giardia causes noninflammatory diarrhea (no mucosal invasion).
💊 Treatment
First-Line Options
  • Metronidazole 250mg TID × 5-7 days — most commonly used
  • Tinidazole 2g single dose — preferred for compliance
  • Nitazoxanide — FDA-approved; good for children (liquid form)
Special Situations
  • Pregnancy: Paromomycin (non-absorbed aminoglycoside) — safe in pregnancy
  • Treatment failure: Longer course, combination therapy, or different agent
  • Post-infectious lactose intolerance: May persist weeks after treatment
📝 High-Yield Summary
  • Organism: Flagellated protozoan; trophozoite has "face" appearance
  • Transmission: Fecal-oral, contaminated water (chlorine-resistant!), daycare
  • Classic: Camper/hiker with foul, fatty, watery diarrhea — NO blood
  • Diagnosis: Stool antigen (EIA) or O&P (may need multiple samples)
  • Treatment: Metronidazole or tinidazole (single dose)
  • Complications: Chronic malabsorption, post-infectious lactose intolerance
