🔬 Overview of Rickettsial Diseases

Rickettsia and related organisms (Ehrlichia, Anaplasma) are obligate intracellular bacteria transmitted primarily by arthropod vectors. They cause potentially fatal diseases that require empiric treatment — do NOT wait for confirmatory testing!

🎯 Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever (RMSF)

The most lethal rickettsial disease in the US. Despite its name, most cases occur in Southeast and South-Central states (NC, TN, OK, AR), not the Rocky Mountains.

Classic Triad (Only 60-70% at presentation)

🔴 The Rash

Appears day 3-5. Starts on ankles and wrists → spreads centrally (centripetal). Blanching macules → petechiae. Involves palms and soles. May be absent in 10-15%.

🩸 Pathophysiology

Infects vascular endothelium → vasculitis → increased permeability, edema, hypovolemia, end-organ damage. Can cause DIC, ARDS, renal failure.

⚠️ Mortality

20-25% if untreated; less than 5% with early doxycycline. Death usually in second week. Risk factors: delayed treatment, G6PD deficiency.

⚠️ RMSF — Time-Sensitive Emergency

  • Treatment within first 5 days dramatically reduces mortality
  • Do NOT wait for rash — may be absent or delayed
  • Do NOT wait for serology — takes 7-10 days to become positive
  • Doxycycline is safe in children — AAP and CDC recommend it regardless of age
Classic Board Vignette: Summer, child from North Carolina, fever + headache + myalgias → develops rash on wrists/ankles spreading to trunk, involving palms/soles. Recent camping trip. = RMSF until proven otherwise. Start doxycycline immediately!

🦠 Ehrlichiosis & Anaplasmosis

🔬 HME (Ehrlichiosis)

Ehrlichia chaffeensis infects monocytes. Lone star tick (Southeast, South-Central). Look for morulae (mulberry-like inclusions) in monocytes.

🔬 HGA (Anaplasmosis)

Anaplasma phagocytophilum infects granulocytes. Ixodes tick (same as Lyme — coinfection possible!). Morulae in neutrophils.

Clinical Features — "Spotless Rocky Mountain Spotted Fever"

  • Fever, headache, myalgias, malaise (nonspecific)
  • Rash is UNCOMMON (~30% in ehrlichiosis, rare in anaplasmosis)
  • Leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, elevated transaminases — classic lab findings
  • Can progress to septic shock, ARDS, meningoencephalitis
Diagnostic Clue: Fever + tick exposure + leukopenia + thrombocytopenia + elevated LFTs + NO RASH = Think Ehrlichiosis/Anaplasmosis! Look for morulae on peripheral smear.

💊 Treatment

First-Line: DOXYCYCLINE for ALL Ages

  • Adults: Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV BID
  • Children: Doxycycline 2.2 mg/kg (max 100mg) PO/IV BID
  • Duration: Until afebrile for 3 days AND clinical improvement (minimum 5-7 days)
  • Pregnancy: Doxycycline for suspected RMSF — benefits outweigh risks
Doxycycline in Children: AAP and CDC recommend doxycycline for suspected RMSF in children of ALL ages. Short courses do not cause tooth staining.

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • RMSF: R. rickettsii, Dermacentor tick, rash starts wrists/ankles → central, palms/soles, petechiae
  • Ehrlichiosis: E. chaffeensis, Lone star tick, monocytes, morulae, leukopenia, usually NO rash
  • Anaplasmosis: A. phagocytophilum, Ixodes tick (like Lyme), neutrophils, can coinfect
  • Treatment: DOXYCYCLINE for all — including children and pregnancy
  • Don't wait: Treat empirically. Serology is negative early when treatment matters most
