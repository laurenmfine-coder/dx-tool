HIV / AIDS

Human Immunodeficiency Virus - The CD4+ T Cell Destroyer

Retrovirus Lentivirus +ssRNA → dsDNA Reverse Transcriptase

🔬 Virology Overview

HIV is a retrovirus that infects and depletes CD4+ T cells, leading to profound immunodeficiency. Key features:

  • Genome: Two copies of (+) ssRNA (diploid)
  • Envelope: gp120 (binds CD4) and gp41 (fusion)
  • Capsid: p24 antigen (used in diagnostics)
  • Enzymes: Reverse transcriptase, integrase, protease - all drug targets!
HIV is a RETROVIRUS with REVERSE TRANSCRIPTASE that converts RNA → DNA. This reverses the central dogma, hence "retro-virus."

🔄 HIV Life Cycle & Drug Targets

1

Attachment & Entry

gp120 binds CD4 → then CCR5/CXCR4 coreceptor → gp41 mediates fusion

Maraviroc (CCR5 blocker) • Enfuvirtide (fusion inhibitor)
2

Reverse Transcription

Viral RNA → DNA by reverse transcriptase (error-prone → high mutation rate!)

NRTIs (tenofovir, emtricitabine) • NNRTIs (efavirenz)
3

Integration

Viral DNA enters nucleus → integrase inserts into host chromosome (provirus)

Integrase inhibitors (dolutegravir, bictegravir)
4

Assembly & Maturation

Polyproteins cleaved by protease → mature virions bud from cell

Protease inhibitors (darunavir)
Why combination therapy? HIV RT is error-prone → rapid mutations. Using 3+ drugs prevents resistance emergence.

🔬 Diagnosis

Screening Algorithm

  1. 4th gen test: Detects HIV-1/2 antibodies AND p24 antigen (earlier detection!)
  2. If positive → HIV-1/HIV-2 differentiation assay
  3. If indeterminate → HIV-1 RNA viral load
4th gen tests detect p24 antigen + antibodies, narrowing the "window period" to ~2-4 weeks. RNA can detect even earlier.

💊 Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)

Start ART in ALL patients regardless of CD4. Typical regimen = 2 NRTIs + integrase inhibitor.

NRTIs (Nucleoside RTIs)

Tenofovir (TDF/TAF) Emtricitabine (FTC) Abacavir Lamivudine

Chain terminators - incorporate into DNA, lack 3'-OH → stop elongation

Integrase Inhibitors (INSTIs) ⭐ Preferred

Dolutegravir ⭐ Bictegravir ⭐ Raltegravir

Block integrase → prevent viral DNA insertion. High barrier to resistance, well-tolerated.

Preferred Initial Regimens

  • Biktarvy (bictegravir/TAF/FTC) - single tablet daily
  • Dolutegravir + TDF/FTC

🛡️ Prevention: PrEP and PEP

PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis)

Daily antiretrovirals for high-risk HIV-negative individuals. >99% effective!

  • TDF/FTC (Truvada) or TAF/FTC (Descovy) daily
  • Cabotegravir injectable every 2 months

PEP (Post-Exposure Prophylaxis)

Within 72 hours of exposure, continue 28 days. TDF/FTC + dolutegravir.

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Virus: Retrovirus, reverse transcriptase, (+)ssRNA → dsDNA → integrates
  • Target: CD4+ T cells via CD4 receptor + CCR5/CXCR4 coreceptor
  • Diagnosis: 4th gen Ag/Ab test (p24 + antibodies)
  • AIDS: CD4 <200 OR AIDS-defining illness
  • OI thresholds: <200 = PCP/toxo; <100 = crypto; <50 = MAC/PML
  • Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX when CD4 <200 (covers PCP + toxo)
  • ART: 2 NRTIs + integrase inhibitor (dolutegravir/bictegravir preferred)
