🔬 Organism Overview

Haemophilus influenzae is a small Gram-negative coccobacillus that requires factors X (hemin) and V (NAD) for growth. Despite its name, it does NOT cause influenza (that's a virus!). Two main types cause disease: encapsulated (especially type b) and nontypeable (NTHi).

🔬 Lab Identification

Small Gram-negative coccobacillus. Requires both Factor X (hemin) and Factor V (NAD) — grows on chocolate agar but NOT blood agar.

🧫 Satellite Phenomenon

On blood agar with S. aureus streak, H. influenzae grows only near staph (which lyses RBCs and releases factors).

📊 Serotypes

6 capsular serotypes (a-f); type b (Hib) is most virulent. Nontypeable H. influenzae (NTHi) lacks capsule.

Board Pearl: H. influenzae needs factors X and V. Grows on chocolate agar (factors released), NOT blood agar (factors trapped in intact RBCs).

Hib Disease (Type b) — Now Rare

🧠 Meningitis

Was #1 cause of bacterial meningitis in children pre-vaccine. Now rare due to Hib vaccination.

🔥 Epiglottitis

"Cherry-red" swollen epiglottis. Pediatric emergency: drooling, stridor, tripod position, "thumb sign" on X-ray. DO NOT examine throat!

🦠 Other Invasive

Septic arthritis, cellulitis (periorbital with violaceous hue), pneumonia with empyema.

NTHi Disease — Still Common

👂 Otitis Media

One of top 3 causes of acute otitis media (with S. pneumoniae and M. catarrhalis).

🤧 Sinusitis

Common cause of acute bacterial sinusitis.

🫁 COPD Exacerbations

Major cause of acute COPD exacerbations. Also causes pneumonia in elderly.

Clinical Pearl: If you see invasive Hib disease today, ask about vaccination status! It's rare in vaccinated children.

💉 Hib Vaccine

Hib Conjugate Vaccine

  • Type: Polysaccharide-protein conjugate
  • Schedule: 2, 4, 6 months + booster at 12-15 months
  • Efficacy: >95% against invasive Hib disease
  • Impact: 99% reduction in invasive Hib disease since 1987
  • Does NOT protect against: Nontypeable H. influenzae

💊 Treatment

Invasive Disease (Meningitis, Epiglottitis)

  • Empiric: Ceftriaxone or Cefotaxime
  • Meningitis: Add dexamethasone before first antibiotic
  • Duration: Meningitis 7-10 days

Respiratory Infections

  • First-line: Amoxicillin-clavulanate (covers beta-lactamase)
  • Alternatives: Cefuroxime, azithromycin, fluoroquinolones

⚠️ Beta-Lactamase Resistance

  • 20-40% produce beta-lactamase (inactivates ampicillin)
  • Use amoxicillin-clavulanate or cephalosporins

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • ID: Gram-negative coccobacillus; requires factors X + V; chocolate agar
  • Hib: Encapsulated; meningitis, epiglottitis; NOW RARE due to vaccine
  • NTHi: No capsule; otitis, sinusitis, COPD exacerbations; still common
  • Vaccine: 99% reduction in invasive disease
  • Treatment: Invasive = ceftriaxone; Respiratory = amox-clav
