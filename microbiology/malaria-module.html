🔬 Overview

Malaria: Plasmodium protozoa via female Anopheles mosquitoes. ~250 million cases/year, ~600,000 deaths. Five species; P. falciparum most severe.

🔬 Life Cycle

Sporozoites → liver → merozoites → RBCs (ring forms) → gametocytes → mosquito.

🩸 Diagnosis

Thick smear = sensitive. Thin smear = species ID. Ring forms in RBCs.

Board Pearl: P. vivax and P. ovale have hypnozoites (liver dormancy) → RELAPSE. Need primaquine (check G6PD first!).

⚠️ Severe Malaria (P. falciparum)

Criteria for IV Artesunate

  • Cerebral malaria (coma, seizures)
  • ARDS / respiratory distress
  • Severe anemia (Hgb less than 7)
  • Acute kidney injury
  • Hypoglycemia (glucose less than 40)
  • Parasitemia greater than 5%
  • Shock / circulatory collapse
Vignette: Traveler from Africa + cyclic fevers + rigors + anemia + thrombocytopenia + ring forms on smear = Malaria!

💊 Treatment

Severe Malaria

  • IV Artesunate (call CDC: 770-488-7788)
  • Then oral ACT when stable

Uncomplicated Malaria

  • Artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem) — first-line
  • Atovaquone-proguanil — alternative

P. vivax / P. ovale (Relapsing)

  • Treat acute + Primaquine × 14 days (kills hypnozoites)
  • CHECK G6PD FIRST — causes hemolysis if deficient

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Vector: Female Anopheles; dusk-to-dawn
  • P. falciparum: Severe, cerebral malaria, banana gametocytes
  • P. vivax/ovale: Hypnozoites → relapse; need primaquine (G6PD!)
  • Dx: Thick smear (sensitive), thin smear (specific)
  • Severe: IV artesunate
  • Uncomplicated: Artemether-lumefantrine
