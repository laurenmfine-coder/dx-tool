🔬 Overview

Obligate intracellular protozoan. Cats = definitive host (shed oocysts in feces). Humans acquire via cat feces, undercooked meat (tissue cysts), or transplacentally. ~30% seropositive worldwide.

🐱 Transmission

Cat feces (oocysts), undercooked meat (pork, lamb), transplacental (congenital).

🔬 Forms

Tachyzoites: Acute, rapidly dividing. Bradyzoites: Latent tissue cysts. Oocysts: Environmental (cats).

📊 Clinical Syndromes

Immunocompetent

Usually asymptomatic. 10-20% have painless cervical lymphadenopathy. Ocular toxoplasmosis = retinochoroiditis ("headlight in fog").

AIDS (CD4 under 100)

🧠 Toxoplasmic Encephalitis

Most common CNS mass lesion in AIDS. Reactivation of latent infection. Headache, confusion, focal deficits, seizures.

📷 Imaging

Multiple ring-enhancing lesions in basal ganglia with edema. (vs CNS lymphoma = usually single lesion)

Congenital

Classic Triad: Chorioretinitis + Hydrocephalus + Intracranial calcifications (diffuse). Risk increases later in pregnancy but severity worse early.

Classic Vignette: AIDS patient, CD4 under 100, headache + focal deficits + MRI with multiple ring-enhancing basal ganglia lesions + positive Toxo IgG = Treat empirically for toxoplasmic encephalitis!

🧪 Diagnosis

SettingApproach
AIDS encephalitisPositive IgG + typical MRI = presumptive dx. Treat empirically; biopsy if no response in 2 weeks.
PregnancySerology; if seroconversion, amniocentesis PCR for fetal infection.
CongenitalNeonatal IgM/IgA (fetal origin, not maternal).

⚠️ Negative IgG in AIDS Patient

  • Makes toxoplasmosis unlikely (usually reactivation)
  • Consider CNS lymphoma, PML; may need biopsy

💊 Treatment

Toxoplasmic Encephalitis

  • First-line: Pyrimethamine + Sulfadiazine + Leucovorin
  • Alternative: Pyrimethamine + Clindamycin + Leucovorin
  • Duration: 6 weeks acute → secondary prophylaxis until CD4 over 200 for 6+ months

Prophylaxis (CD4 under 100)

  • TMP-SMX — same as PCP prophylaxis! Covers both.
TMP-SMX Bonus: PCP prophylaxis with TMP-SMX also prevents toxoplasmosis!

📝 High-Yield Summary

  • Cats = definitive host; transmission via feces, undercooked meat, transplacental
  • AIDS (CD4 under 100): Encephalitis with ring-enhancing basal ganglia lesions
  • Congenital: Chorioretinitis + hydrocephalus + diffuse calcifications
  • Diagnosis: Serology + imaging; treat empirically in AIDS
  • Treatment: Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine + leucovorin
  • Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX (also covers PCP)
