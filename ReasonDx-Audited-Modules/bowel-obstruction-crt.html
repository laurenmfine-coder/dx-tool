💡 Non-operative Management Criteria

Partial SBO without strangulation can be managed conservatively: NPO, NG tube, IV fluids, serial exams. ~70-80% of adhesive SBO resolves without surgery.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • #1 cause of SBO: Adhesions from prior surgery
  • #1 cause of LBO: Colorectal cancer
  • SBO X-ray: Central, valvulae conniventes, >3cm, air-fluid levels
  • LBO X-ray: Peripheral, haustra, >6cm (cecum >9 = emergent)
  • CT for: Transition point, cause, strangulation signs
  • Strangulation signs: ↓ enhancement, wall thickening, mesenteric haziness, free fluid/air
  • Conservative trial: 48-72h for uncomplicated adhesive SBO
  • Surgery if: Strangulation, complete obstruction, failed conservative
