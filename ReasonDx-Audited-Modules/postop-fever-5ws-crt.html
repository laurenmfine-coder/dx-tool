Introduction: The 5 W's
A 58-year-old woman is post-operative day 3 after open cholecystectomy. The nurse calls you because she has developed a temperature of 38.6°C (101.5°F).
💡 The 5 W's of Post-Op Fever (by POD):

Wind (POD 1-2) - Atelectasis
Water (POD 3-5) - UTI (especially if catheter)
Wound (POD 5-7) - Surgical site infection
Walking (POD 5+) - DVT/PE
Wonder drugs (Any time) - Drug fever, C. diff
Based on timing (POD 3), which cause is MOST likely?
A. Atelectasis (Wind) +40
B. Urinary tract infection (Water) +100
C. Surgical site infection (Wound) +30
D. DVT/PE (Walking) +20
2
Gathering Information
You go to evaluate the patient. She reports mild dysuria and lower abdominal discomfort. She had a Foley catheter for the first 2 days post-op. On exam, her abdomen is soft with appropriate healing incision. Lungs are clear.
What is your next best step?
A. Urinalysis and urine culture +100
B. CT abdomen to rule out abscess +40
C. Chest X-ray +60
D. Blood cultures only +30
3
Diagnosis Confirmed
Urinalysis results:
Leukocyte esterase: Positive
Nitrites: Positive
WBC: >50/hpf
Bacteria: Many
These findings are most consistent with:
A. Urinary tract infection +100
B. Contaminated specimen +20
C. Asymptomatic bacteriuria +30
D. Interstitial cystitis +20
4
Management
You diagnose catheter-associated UTI (CAUTI). The patient is hemodynamically stable with normal renal function.
What is appropriate initial empiric antibiotic therapy?
A. IV vancomycin +40
B. Oral fluoroquinolone (ciprofloxacin or levofloxacin) +100
C. IV piperacillin-tazobactam +60
D. Oral nitrofurantoin +30
5
Prevention
The patient improves with antibiotics. Culture grows E. coli sensitive to your chosen antibiotic.
The most important measure to prevent CAUTI in post-op patients is:
A. Prophylactic antibiotics while catheterized +40
B. Early catheter removal +100
C. Silver-coated catheters +30
D. Bladder irrigation with antiseptic +60

📚 Key Learning Points

The 5 W's of Post-Op Fever:
Wind (POD 1-2) • Water (POD 3-5) • Wound (POD 5-7) • Walking (POD 5+) • Wonder drugs (anytime)
  • POD 1-2 (Wind): Atelectasis - most common early fever, encourage incentive spirometry
  • POD 3-5 (Water): UTI, especially with Foley catheter - UA/Urine culture
  • POD 5-7 (Wound): Surgical site infection - examine incision
  • POD 5+ (Walking): DVT/PE - consider if immobile, prophylaxis important
  • Any time (Wonder drugs): Drug fever, C. diff (especially with antibiotic use)
🎯 CAUTI Prevention: Early catheter removal is the single most important intervention. Each day with a catheter increases UTI risk by 3-7%.
