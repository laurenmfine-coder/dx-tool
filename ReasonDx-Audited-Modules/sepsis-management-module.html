📋 Sepsis-3 Definitions

Sepsis = Life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by dysregulated host response to infection

Operationalized as: Suspected infection + SOFA score increase ≥2

qSOFA (Quick SOFA) - Screening

≥2 of 3 = high risk for poor outcome:
• Respiratory rate ≥22
• Altered mental status (GCS <15)
• Systolic BP ≤100 mmHg

Note: qSOFA is for screening outside ICU; SOFA used for diagnosis

Septic Shock

Sepsis + BOTH:
  • Vasopressors needed to maintain MAP ≥65 mmHg
  • Lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation
Mortality: ~40%

⏱️ Hour-1 Bundle

Start within 1 HOUR of recognition:
Every hour of antibiotic delay increases mortality
Hour-1 Bundle Elements:
  1. Measure lactate (remeasure if >2)
  2. Obtain blood cultures before antibiotics
  3. Administer broad-spectrum antibiotics
  4. Begin fluid resuscitation (30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4)
  5. Apply vasopressors if hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation (MAP target ≥65)

Lactate Goals

Lactate LevelSignificance
<2 mmol/LNormal
2-4 mmol/LIntermediate risk; guide resuscitation
>4 mmol/LHigh risk; aggressive resuscitation needed
Goal: Normalize lactate or decrease by ≥10% in first 2-4 hours
Serial lactate monitoring guides adequacy of resuscitation

💧 Fluid Resuscitation & Vasopressors

Initial Fluid Resuscitation

30 mL/kg crystalloid within first 3 hours
(for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L)

Crystalloid choice: Balanced solutions (LR, Plasmalyte) preferred over NS

Fluid Responsiveness Assessment

  • Passive leg raise test
  • Pulse pressure variation (if on vent)
  • IVC ultrasound (collapsibility)
  • Fluid challenge with reassessment

Vasopressors

Start vasopressors if MAP <65 despite initial fluids
Don't delay - can run peripherally initially while getting central access
AgentMechanismNotes
Norepinephrineα1 > β1FIRST LINE; potent vasoconstriction with some inotropy
VasopressinV1 receptorADD to norepi (max 0.03-0.04 U/min); catecholamine-sparing
Epinephrineα + β1 + β2Add if refractory; increases HR, lactate
DopamineDose-dependentNOT preferred - more arrhythmias; consider only in bradycardia
PhenylephrinePure α1Only if tachyarrhythmia limits other agents

Refractory Shock

Consider:
  • Hydrocortisone 200mg/day (stress-dose steroids)
  • Inotrope (dobutamine) if cardiac dysfunction
  • Source control (drain abscess, remove line)

💊 Antibiotic Selection

Give within 1 hour!
Broad-spectrum, then de-escalate based on cultures

Empiric Regimens by Source

SourceCommon PathogensEmpiric Coverage
Pulmonary (CAP) Strep pneumo, H. flu, atypicals, Legionella Ceftriaxone + azithromycin (or respiratory FQ)
Pulmonary (HAP/VAP) Pseudomonas, MRSA, GNRs Anti-pseudomonal β-lactam + vanc ± aminoglycoside
UTI/Urosepsis E. coli, Klebsiella, Enterococcus Ceftriaxone or FQ; add vanc if concern for Enterococcus
Intra-abdominal GNRs, anaerobes, Enterococcus Pip-tazo OR ceftriaxone + metronidazole
Skin/Soft tissue Strep, Staph (MSSA/MRSA) Vancomycin + pip-tazo (if necrotizing)
Unknown source Broad coverage Vancomycin + pip-tazo (or meropenem)

Special Considerations

  • MRSA risk: Add vancomycin (prior MRSA, HD, recent hospitalization)
  • Pseudomonas risk: Anti-pseudomonal β-lactam (CF, bronchiectasis, recent antibiotics)
  • ESBL risk: Consider carbapenem
  • Fungal risk: Add echinocandin (TPN, immunosuppression, prior antibiotics)

De-escalation

Once cultures return: Narrow antibiotics to targeted therapy
Duration: Typically 7-10 days (shorter courses emerging for some infections)

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: What defines septic shock per Sepsis-3?

A. Sepsis with hypotension
B. Sepsis requiring vasopressors for MAP ≥65 AND lactate >2 despite fluids
C. Sepsis with organ dysfunction
Correct: B. Septic shock requires BOTH: vasopressors to maintain MAP ≥65 AND lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation.

Q2: What is the first-line vasopressor for septic shock?

A. Norepinephrine
B. Dopamine
C. Vasopressin
D. Epinephrine
Correct: A. Norepinephrine is first-line. Vasopressin is added second (catecholamine-sparing). Dopamine is NOT preferred due to more arrhythmias.

Q3: How much crystalloid should be given in initial resuscitation?

A. 10 mL/kg
B. 30 mL/kg
C. 50 mL/kg
Correct: B. Initial resuscitation is 30 mL/kg crystalloid for hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L, given within first 3 hours.

Q4: What are the qSOFA criteria?

A. RR ≥22, altered mental status, SBP ≤100
B. Temp >38, WBC >12k, HR >90
C. Lactate >4, creatinine >2, bilirubin >2
Correct: A. qSOFA: RR ≥22, altered mental status (GCS <15), SBP ≤100. ≥2 points suggests high risk of poor outcome.

Q5: When should antibiotics be given in sepsis?

A. Within 1 hour of recognition
B. After blood cultures return
C. Within 6 hours
Correct: A. Antibiotics within 1 hour! Obtain cultures first if possible, but don't delay antibiotics. Every hour of delay increases mortality.
