🔬 The Embryology

💡 Cloaca Development

The cloaca is the common chamber for the hindgut and allantois. The urorectal septum descends to divide it into:

  • Anterior: Urogenital sinus (→ bladder, urethra)
  • Posterior: Anorectal canal (→ rectum, anal canal)

Failure of this process causes anorectal malformations, often with fistulas to the urogenital tract.

🔪 Surgical Planning

💡 High vs Low Malformation

HIGH lesion: Rectum ends above puborectalis muscle. Requires colostomy first, then definitive repair (posterior sagittal anorectoplasty - PSARP).

LOW lesion: Rectum ends below puborectalis. May be amenable to primary repair without colostomy.

Anorectal Malformation Mastered!

🔑 Key Learning Points

  • Embryology: Failure of urorectal septum to partition the cloaca
  • Cloaca divides into: Urogenital sinus (anterior) + Anorectal canal (posterior)
  • Male fistulas: Usually to urethra (rectourethral)
  • Female fistulas: Usually to vestibule or vagina (rectovestibular)
  • ALWAYS screen for VACTERL - multiple anomalies common

📋 VACTERL Workup

  • Vertebral: Spine X-ray (hemivertebrae, scoliosis)
  • Anorectal: Clinical exam (this case!)
  • Cardiac: Echocardiogram
  • TE fistula: Watch for drooling, choking; esophagram if suspected
  • Renal: Renal ultrasound
  • Limb: Physical exam (radial ray defects)
