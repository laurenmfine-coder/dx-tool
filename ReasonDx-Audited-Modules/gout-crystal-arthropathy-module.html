🔬 Diagnosis

Gout vs Pseudogout

FeatureGout (MSU)Pseudogout (CPPD)
Crystal shapeNeedle-shapedRhomboid
BirefringenceNegatively birefringent (yellow parallel)Positively birefringent (blue parallel)
Common joints1st MTP (podagra), ankle, kneeKnee, wrist, shoulder
X-rayErosions with overhanging edgesChondrocalcinosis
Risk factorsDiet, alcohol, diuretics, CKDAge, hemochromatosis, hyperparathyroidism
Definitive diagnosis = Joint aspiration with crystal identification
Gout: Needle-shaped, negatively birefringent crystals
Pseudogout: Rhomboid, positively birefringent crystals

Clinical Presentation

  • Acute monoarticular arthritis (can be polyarticular)
  • Rapid onset (peaks in 12-24 hours)
  • Severe pain, swelling, erythema, warmth
  • May have fever (mimics septic arthritis)
  • Self-limiting (7-14 days without treatment)

🔥 Acute Flare Treatment

Start treatment ASAP - within 24 hours of symptom onset

First-Line Options

AgentDosingConsiderations
NSAIDsIndomethacin 50mg TID, Naproxen 500mg BIDAvoid in CKD, GI bleeding, CVD
Colchicine1.2mg, then 0.6mg 1 hour laterBest if started <36h; adjust for CKD
GlucocorticoidsPrednisone 30-40mg/day × 5-7 daysSafe in CKD; use if NSAIDs contraindicated
Intra-articular steroidTriamcinolone 20-40mgMonoarticular flare; rule out infection first
Do NOT start or stop ULT during acute flare!
Continue ULT if already on it; starting/stopping can worsen flare

💊 Long-Term Management

Indications for Urate-Lowering Therapy (ULT)

  • ≥2 flares per year
  • Tophi present
  • CKD stage ≥3
  • Urolithiasis
  • Serum urate >9 mg/dL

ULT Options

AgentMechanismDosing
AllopurinolXanthine oxidase inhibitorStart 100mg/day, titrate to goal
FebuxostatXanthine oxidase inhibitor40-80mg daily
ProbenecidUricosuric500mg BID (needs adequate renal function)
PegloticaseRecombinant uricaseIV infusion (refractory gout)
Target serum urate: <6 mg/dL (or <5 mg/dL if tophi present)

Flare Prophylaxis During ULT Initiation

Always provide prophylaxis when starting ULT:
  • Colchicine 0.6mg daily or BID × 3-6 months
  • Alternative: Low-dose NSAID if colchicine contraindicated

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Gout crystals under polarized microscopy?

A. Needle-shaped, negatively birefringent
B. Rhomboid, positively birefringent
Correct: A. MSU crystals are needle-shaped and negatively birefringent (yellow when parallel to compensator).

Q2: Target serum urate for gout management?

A. <6 mg/dL
B. <8 mg/dL
Correct: A. Target <6 mg/dL (or <5 if tophi present).
