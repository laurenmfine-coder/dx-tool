📚 Neural Development Overview

The nervous system develops from ectoderm through neurulation. The neural plate folds to form the neural tube (future CNS) and neural crest cells (future PNS and more). Failure of neural tube closure causes devastating birth defects.

Developmental Timeline

🎯 Critical Pearl: Neural tube closure is complete by day 28 - often before a woman knows she's pregnant! This is why periconceptional folic acid (400 mcg daily) is crucial for ALL women of reproductive age.

🔄 Neurulation

Primary neurulation (folding) forms brain + most spinal cord. Secondary neurulation (canalization) forms caudal spinal cord.

Primary Neurulation (Days 18-28)

Step 1: Neural Plate (Day 18)

Notochord induces overlying ectoderm to thicken via Sonic hedgehog (SHH) and BMPs.

Step 2: Neural Groove & Folds (Days 20-21)

Neural plate invaginates, creating neural groove with elevated neural folds.

Step 3: Fusion (Days 22-28)

Folds fuse at cervical region, proceeding bidirectionally. Neural crest cells emigrate.

Step 4: Neuropore Closure

  • Anterior neuropore: Closes day 24-26
  • Posterior neuropore: Closes day 26-28
⚠️ Teratogens Affecting Neurulation
  • Valproic acid: 1-2% NTD risk
  • Carbamazepine: 0.5-1% NTD risk
  • Maternal diabetes: 2-3x increased risk
  • Folic acid deficiency: Most preventable cause

🧩 Brain Vesicle Development

After neural tube closure, the cranial end expands into 3 primary vesicles (week 4), then 5 secondary vesicles (week 5).

Prosencephalon → Telencephalon + Diencephalon

⚠️ Holoprosencephaly
Failure of prosencephalon to divide into two hemispheres. Associated with deficient SHH, diabetes, alcohol, trisomy 13.
"The face predicts the brain" - facial defect severity correlates with brain malformation.

🦴 Spinal Cord Development

Organization: motor ventral, sensory dorsal - established by SHH (ventral) and BMPs (dorsal).

Dorsoventral Patterning

ROOF PLATE → BMPs → ALAR PLATE → SENSORY neurons (dorsal horn) ↓ SULCUS LIMITANS (dividing line) ↓ FLOOR PLATE → SHH → BASAL PLATE → MOTOR neurons (ventral horn)
💡 "SAME DAVE"
Sensory = Alar, Motor = Efferent
Dorsal = Afferent, Ventral = Efferent

Cord vs. Vertebral Growth

  • Birth: Cord ends at L3
  • Adult: Cord ends at L1-L2 (conus medullaris)
🎯 Clinical Pearl: Lumbar punctures at L3-L4 or L4-L5 - below conus to avoid cord injury.

⭐ Neural Crest Cells

Neural crest cells emigrate from dorsal neural tube, migrate extensively, and give rise to diverse cell types - the "4th germ layer."

Neural Crest Derivatives

CategoryDerivatives
PNSSensory ganglia, autonomic ganglia, Schwann cells, enteric NS
EndocrineAdrenal medulla, C-cells of thyroid
CraniofacialFacial bones, cartilage, odontoblasts
CardiovascularAorticopulmonary septum, smooth muscle of great vessels
PigmentMelanocytes
⚠️ Neurocristopathies
  • Hirschsprung: Failed vagal neural crest → aganglionic bowel
  • DiGeorge: Failed cardiac neural crest → conotruncal defects
  • Waardenburg: Deafness, pigmentation abnormalities
  • Treacher Collins: Facial hypoplasia
🎯 Hirschsprung - The Key Story: Neural crest migrates cranial to caudal. Rectum is ALWAYS involved because it's the most distal structure.

⚠️ Neural Tube Defects

NTDs result from failure of neural tube closure. Most are preventable with folic acid.
🔴 Anencephaly - Anterior neuropore failure (day 24-26)
Absence of brain and calvarium. Incompatible with life.

📍 Other Malformations

  • Dandy-Walker: Cystic 4th ventricle, vermis hypoplasia
  • Corpus callosum agenesis: Variable presentation
  • Syringomyelia: Fluid cavity in cord; "cape-like" sensory loss
🟢 Prevention
  • All women: 400 mcg folic acid daily
  • Previous NTD: 4 mg/day
  • Reduces NTD risk by 50-70%

📝 Test Your Knowledge

Question 1

Anencephaly results from failure of closure of which structure?

A. Anterior (cranial) neuropore
B. Posterior (caudal) neuropore
C. Neural crest cells
D. Notochord
Answer: A. Anencephaly = anterior neuropore failure (days 24-26). Posterior neuropore failure → spina bifida.

Question 2

Which is derived from neural crest cells?

A. Oligodendrocytes
B. Astrocytes
C. Schwann cells
D. Ependymal cells
Answer: C. Schwann cells (PNS myelin) = neural crest. Oligodendrocytes (CNS myelin), astrocytes, ependymal = neural tube.

Question 3

Failure of prosencephalon division causes which malformation?

A. Holoprosencephaly
B. Dandy-Walker
C. Chiari II
D. Anencephaly
Answer: A. Holoprosencephaly = prosencephalon fails to divide. Associated with SHH deficiency, diabetes, trisomy 13.

Question 4

A newborn has myelomeningocele. Which is found in nearly 100% of cases?

A. Chiari I
B. Chiari II
C. Dandy-Walker
D. Corpus callosum agenesis
Answer: B. Chiari II is present in ~100% of myelomeningocele cases.

Question 5

Motor neurons develop from which neural tube region?

A. Alar plate
B. Basal plate
C. Roof plate
D. Floor plate
Answer: B. Basal plate (ventral) → motor neurons (SHH influence). Alar plate (dorsal) → sensory (BMP influence).
