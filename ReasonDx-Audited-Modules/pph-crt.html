🔑 Key Learning Points

  • 4 T's: Tone (atony) is most common (70-80%)
  • Risk factors for atony: Prolonged labor, macrosomia, oxytocin use, overdistension
  • Step-wise approach: Massage → Uterotonics → TXA → Balloon → Surgery
  • Oxytocin first-line, then methylergonovine (avoid in HTN), carboprost (avoid in asthma)
  • TXA 1g IV within 3 hours of delivery reduces death from bleeding
  • Massive transfusion: 1:1:1 ratio (PRBC:FFP:platelets)
  • Don't delay surgery for refractory hemorrhage
