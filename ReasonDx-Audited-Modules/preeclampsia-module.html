📚 Overview

Preeclampsia: New-onset HTN (≥140/90) after 20 weeks with proteinuria OR end-organ dysfunction. Affects 2-8% of pregnancies.

Classification

TypeDefinition
Chronic HTNHTN before pregnancy or <20 weeks
Gestational HTNHTN ≥20 weeks, no proteinuria/organ damage
PreeclampsiaHTN + proteinuria or end-organ damage ≥20 weeks
EclampsiaPreeclampsia + seizures

Risk Factors

  • High risk: Prior preeclampsia, multifetal, chronic HTN, diabetes, CKD, autoimmune
  • Moderate: Nulliparity, age >35, BMI >30, family history
Prevention: Low-dose aspirin 81 mg starting 12-16 weeks in high-risk women (≥1 high-risk OR ≥2 moderate factors)

🔍 Diagnostic Criteria

HTN: SBP ≥140 OR DBP ≥90 (two readings ≥4h apart)
PLUS: Proteinuria (≥300 mg/24h) OR end-organ damage

End-Organ Dysfunction (can diagnose WITHOUT proteinuria)

  • Platelets <100,000
  • Creatinine >1.1 or doubling
  • Liver transaminases >2x normal
  • Pulmonary edema
  • New headache or visual disturbances
SEVERE FEATURES (any one):
  • BP ≥160/110
  • Platelets <100,000
  • AST/ALT >2x normal or RUQ pain
  • Creatinine >1.1 or doubling
  • Pulmonary edema
  • Cerebral/visual symptoms
💡 Severe Features: "HELP BP"
Headache • Epigastric pain • Liver enzymes • Platelets • BP ≥160/110 • Pulmonary edema

⚕️ Management

Definitive treatment = DELIVERY

Timing of Delivery

ScenarioDeliver At
Without severe features37 weeks
With severe features ≥34 weeks34 weeks (after steroids)
Unstable at any GAAfter maternal stabilization

Acute Severe HTN (≥160/110) - Treat within 30-60 min!

  • Labetalol IV: 20→40→80 mg q10min
  • Hydralazine IV: 5-10 mg q20min
  • Nifedipine PO: 10-20 mg q20min
Goal: SBP 140-150, DBP 90-100

Magnesium Sulfate - Seizure Prophylaxis

  • Load: 4-6 g IV over 15-20 min
  • Maintenance: 1-2 g/hour
  • Continue: 24-48h postpartum
Monitor: Reflexes, RR >12, UOP >25 mL/hr
Toxicity antidote: Calcium gluconate 1g IV

Eclampsia Management

  • Protect airway, lateral position
  • Mg sulfate 4-6g IV (or 2g if already on Mg)
  • Stabilize mother FIRST, then deliver
  • Do NOT use benzos/phenytoin first-line

🩸 HELLP Syndrome

Hemolysis • Elevated Liver enzymes • Low Platelets

Criteria

  • Hemolysis: Schistocytes, LDH ≥600, bili ≥1.2
  • Elevated LFTs: AST ≥70
  • Low platelets: <100,000

Presentation

  • RUQ/epigastric pain (65-90%)
  • Nausea/vomiting
  • May lack hypertension (15-20%)!
Complications: DIC, abruption, renal failure, hepatic rupture, eclampsia
Timeline: Labs worsen 24-48h after delivery; platelet nadir ~day 2. If no improvement by day 4, consider TTP/HUS/AFLP.

📝 Quiz

Q1: BP 162/108, platelets 85k, proteinuria. Diagnosis?

A. Gestational HTN
B. Preeclampsia with severe features
C. HELLP syndrome
Severe features: BP ≥160/110 AND platelets <100k. HELLP requires elevated LFTs + hemolysis.

Q2: First-line seizure prophylaxis in severe preeclampsia?

A. Phenytoin
B. Magnesium sulfate
C. Lorazepam
Magnesium sulfate is superior to phenytoin and benzos for eclampsia prevention/treatment.

Q3: Earliest sign of magnesium toxicity?

A. Loss of reflexes
B. Respiratory depression
C. Cardiac arrest
Reflexes lost at 8-12 mEq/L → respiratory depression 12-17 → cardiac arrest >25.

Q4: When to start aspirin prophylaxis?

A. 12-16 weeks
B. 20-24 weeks
C. 28-32 weeks
Low-dose aspirin 81mg starting 12-16 weeks in high-risk women, continue until delivery.
