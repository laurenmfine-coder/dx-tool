💡 Wells Criteria for PE

Clinical signs of DVT (+3), PE most likely diagnosis (+3), HR >100 (+1.5), Immobilization/surgery (+1.5), Previous DVT/PE (+1.5), Hemoptysis (+1), Malignancy (+1)

💡 Systematic Dyspnea Approach

Always consider the major categories: Cardiac, Pulmonary, Vascular, Other

Cardiac: ACS, CHF, tamponade, arrhythmia
Pulmonary: PE, pneumonia, COPD/asthma, PTX, effusion
Airway: Anaphylaxis, foreign body, angioedema
Other: Anemia, acidosis, anxiety, neuromuscular

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • Wells criteria: Risk stratify before testing
  • High probability: Go straight to CTPA, skip D-dimer
  • Low probability: D-dimer can rule out PE
  • RV strain + elevated biomarkers: Intermediate-high risk
  • Hemodynamically stable: Anticoagulation alone
  • Hemodynamically unstable: Thrombolytics (tPA)
  • Orthopnea/PND: Think cardiac cause
