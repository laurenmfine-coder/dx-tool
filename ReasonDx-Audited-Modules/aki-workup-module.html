📋 Definition & Classification

KDIGO AKI Definition (any of):
  • ↑ Creatinine ≥0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours
  • ↑ Creatinine ≥1.5× baseline within 7 days
  • Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6 hours

KDIGO Staging

🔬 Diagnostic Workup

Initial Assessment

Key Questions:
  • What is the baseline creatinine?
  • Time course? (acute vs acute-on-chronic)
  • Urine output? (oliguric vs non-oliguric)
  • Recent medications, contrast, nephrotoxins?
  • Symptoms of obstruction?

FENa (Fractional Excretion of Sodium)

FENa = (UNa × PCr) / (PNa × UCr) × 100
FENaInterpretation
<1%Pre-renal (kidneys avidly retaining Na)
>2%Intrinsic (ATN - tubules can't reabsorb Na)
1-2%Indeterminate
FENa NOT reliable if:
  • Diuretics given → Use FEUrea instead (<35% = pre-renal)
  • Contrast nephropathy (can be <1% despite ATN)
  • Myoglobinuria, acute GN (can be <1%)

📝 Causes by Category

Pre-renal

Decreased effective circulating volume:
Hypovolemia (bleeding, vomiting, diarrhea, burns)
Heart failure (decreased CO)
Cirrhosis (splanchnic vasodilation)
Sepsis (distributive shock)
NSAIDs, ACEi/ARBs (alter renal hemodynamics)

Intrinsic - ATN (Acute Tubular Necrosis)

Most common cause of intrinsic AKI

Ischemic ATN: Prolonged pre-renal state
Nephrotoxic ATN:
  • Contrast (radiocontrast nephropathy)
  • Aminoglycosides, amphotericin B
  • Cisplatin
  • Myoglobin (rhabdomyolysis)
  • Hemoglobin (hemolysis)

Intrinsic - AIN (Acute Interstitial Nephritis)

Classic triad: Fever, rash, eosinophilia (but often incomplete)

Drug causes (most common):
  • Antibiotics: penicillins, cephalosporins, sulfonamides, fluoroquinolones
  • NSAIDs
  • PPIs
  • Allopurinol
Other: Infections, autoimmune (sarcoid, Sjögren's)

Intrinsic - Glomerulonephritis

  • Rapidly progressive GN (RPGN) - medical emergency
  • Lupus nephritis
  • IgA nephropathy
  • Post-infectious GN
  • Anti-GBM disease
  • ANCA vasculitis

Post-renal (Obstruction)

LocationCauses
Bladder outletBPH (most common in men), prostate cancer
Bilateral uretersMalignancy, retroperitoneal fibrosis, stones (if solitary kidney)
UrethraStricture, catheter obstruction

💊 Management

General Principles

  1. Treat underlying cause
  2. Optimize volume status
  3. Avoid nephrotoxins
  4. Adjust medications for renal function
  5. Monitor for complications

By Type

TypeTreatment
Pre-renalIV fluids (crystalloid), treat underlying cause (sepsis, HF), hold ACEi/ARB/diuretics
ATNSupportive; maintain euvolemia; avoid further nephrotoxins
AINStop offending drug; consider steroids if no improvement
GNUrgent nephrology consult; immunosuppression (steroids, cyclophosphamide)
Post-renalRelieve obstruction (Foley, nephrostomy, stent)

Indications for Dialysis

AEIOU:
  • Acidosis (severe metabolic, pH <7.1 refractory)
  • Electrolytes (hyperkalemia refractory to medical therapy)
  • Ingestion (toxic alcohols, lithium, salicylates)
  • Overload (volume overload refractory to diuretics)
  • Uremia (encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)

Contrast-Induced AKI Prevention

  • Pre-hydration with IV NS or isotonic bicarbonate
  • Use low-osmolar or iso-osmolar contrast
  • Minimize contrast volume
  • Hold metformin (risk of lactic acidosis if AKI occurs)
  • Avoid concurrent nephrotoxins
N-acetylcysteine (NAC) no longer recommended - no proven benefit

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: FENa <1% suggests which type of AKI?

A. Pre-renal
B. ATN
C. Post-renal
Correct: A. FENa <1% indicates kidneys are avidly retaining sodium, consistent with pre-renal azotemia. ATN typically has FENa >2%.

Q2: Muddy brown casts on urinalysis suggest:

A. Pre-renal azotemia
B. Acute tubular necrosis
C. Glomerulonephritis
Correct: B. Muddy brown (granular) casts are characteristic of ATN from sloughed tubular epithelial cells.

Q3: A patient on diuretics has AKI. Which test is more reliable than FENa?

A. FEUrea
B. BUN/Cr ratio
C. Urine osmolality
Correct: A. FEUrea <35% suggests pre-renal and is more reliable than FENa when diuretics have been given.

Q4: What is the most common cause of post-renal AKI in elderly men?

A. BPH
B. Kidney stones
C. Bladder cancer
Correct: A. BPH causing bladder outlet obstruction is the most common cause of post-renal AKI in elderly men.

Q5: Which is an indication for emergent dialysis?

A. Creatinine >5 mg/dL
B. Uremic pericarditis
C. Mild metabolic acidosis
Correct: B. AEIOU: Uremic complications (pericarditis, encephalopathy) are indications for emergent dialysis, regardless of creatinine level.
