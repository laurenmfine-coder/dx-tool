🎯 AFib Overview

Definition: Irregularly irregular rhythm with absent P waves
Mechanism: Multiple re-entrant wavelets in atria
ECG: Irregularly irregular RR intervals, fibrillatory baseline, no discrete P waves

Classification

TypeDefinition
ParoxysmalSelf-terminates within 7 days
PersistentLasts >7 days, requires intervention to terminate
Long-standing persistent>12 months
PermanentDecision to not pursue rhythm control

Causes (H-PIRATES)

Hypertension, Heart failure
Pulmonary disease (COPD, PE)
Ischemia, Infection
Rheumatic heart disease
Anemia, Alcohol ("holiday heart")
Thyroid (hyperthyroidism)
Electrolytes, Elderly
Sleep apnea, Surgery (post-op)

⏱️ Rate Control

Goal: Resting HR <110 bpm (lenient) or <80 bpm (strict)
Studies show lenient control is acceptable for most patients
AgentDoseNotes
Metoprolol25-100mg BIDFirst-line; avoid in decompensated HF
Diltiazem120-360mg daily (ER)Good for rate control; avoid in HFrEF
Verapamil120-360mg dailyAvoid in HFrEF, hypotension
Digoxin0.125-0.25mg dailyAdd-on; good for HFrEF; monitor levels
Amiodarone200mg dailyLast resort for rate control; toxicities
Avoid non-DHP CCBs (diltiazem, verapamil) in:
  • HFrEF (negative inotrope)
  • WPW with AFib (can accelerate conduction)
  • Hypotension

Acute Rate Control (Unstable)

If hemodynamically unstable → Synchronized cardioversion
If stable: IV diltiazem or metoprolol

⚡ Rhythm Control

Goal: Restore and maintain sinus rhythm
Consider if: Symptomatic despite rate control, younger patients, new-onset AFib, HFrEF

Cardioversion

TypeDetails
ElectricalSynchronized DC cardioversion (150-200J biphasic)
PharmacologicIbutilide, flecainide, propafenone, amiodarone
Before cardioversion:
If AFib duration <48 hours → Can cardiovert (still need anticoagulation)
If >48 hours or unknown → TEE to rule out LA thrombus OR 3 weeks anticoagulation BEFORE cardioversion

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: A 72-year-old with HTN and diabetes has new AFib. CHA₂DS₂-VASc score?

A. 2
B. 3
C. 4
Correct: B. HTN (1) + DM (1) + Age 65-74 (1) = 3. Anticoagulation is indicated.

Q2: Which rate control agent should be avoided in HFrEF?

A. Metoprolol
B. Diltiazem
C. Digoxin
Correct: B. Non-DHP CCBs (diltiazem, verapamil) are negative inotropes and should be avoided in HFrEF. Use beta-blockers and digoxin instead.

Q3: Before cardioverting AFib of unknown duration, what must you do?

A. Get TSH level
B. TEE to rule out LA thrombus OR 3 weeks anticoagulation
C. Check digoxin level
Correct: B. If AFib >48 hours or unknown duration, must either perform TEE to exclude LA thrombus or anticoagulate for ≥3 weeks before cardioversion to prevent stroke.

Q4: DOACs are contraindicated in which condition?

A. Mechanical heart valve
B. Hypertension
C. Diabetes
Correct: A. DOACs are contraindicated with mechanical valves and moderate-severe mitral stenosis. Use warfarin (INR 2.5-3.5 for mechanical valves).
