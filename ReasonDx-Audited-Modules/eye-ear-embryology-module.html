📚 Sensory Development Overview

Eye and ear development involves complex interactions between surface ectoderm, neuroectoderm, neural crest, and mesoderm. Both organs require precise inductive signaling - disruption causes characteristic congenital anomalies.

👁️ Eye Development

Eye development demonstrates inductive signaling: the optic vesicle (neuroectoderm) induces the overlying surface ectoderm to form the lens placode. The lens then induces cornea formation. Disruption at any step causes specific defects.

Step-by-Step Development

Step 1: Optic Vesicle (Week 4)

Lateral outgrowths from diencephalon (forebrain) form optic vesicles. These push toward surface ectoderm.

Step 2: Lens Induction (Week 4-5)

Optic vesicle contacts surface ectoderm → induces lens placode. This is a classic example of embryonic induction. PAX6 gene is critical.

Step 3: Optic Cup Formation (Week 5)

Optic vesicle invaginates to form double-walled optic cup:

  • Outer layer → Retinal pigment epithelium (RPE)
  • Inner layer → Neural retina (photoreceptors, etc.)

Step 4: Optic Fissure (Week 5-7)

Groove on ventral surface of optic cup and stalk. Allows hyaloid vessels to enter. Must close by week 7.

Failure to close = COLOBOMA (keyhole-shaped defect in iris, retina, or optic nerve)

Step 5: Lens Development

Lens placode → lens pit → lens vesicle (detaches from surface ectoderm)

  • Posterior cells elongate → primary lens fibers
  • Anterior cells remain as lens epithelium
  • Secondary fibers added throughout life

🎯 PAX6: Master regulator of eye development. Mutations cause aniridia (absent iris), Peters anomaly, and other defects. PAX6 is so conserved that mouse Pax6 can induce ectopic eyes in Drosophila!

👂 Ear Development

The ear has THREE embryologically distinct parts: inner ear (otic placode → membranous labyrinth), middle ear (1st pharyngeal pouch + neural crest ossicles), and outer ear (1st pharyngeal cleft + auricular hillocks).

Inner Ear Development

Otic Placode → Otocyst → Membranous Labyrinth

  1. Week 4: Otic placode appears (thickening of surface ectoderm near hindbrain)
  2. Week 4-5: Placode invaginates → otic pit → otic vesicle (otocyst)
  3. Week 5+: Otocyst divides:
    • Dorsal: Utricle + semicircular canals (vestibular)
    • Ventral: Saccule + cochlea (auditory)
  4. Week 8: Cochlea completes 2.5 turns
Membranous vs Bony Labyrinth: The membranous labyrinth (from otic vesicle) is surrounded by the bony labyrinth (from mesenchyme that undergoes endochondral ossification).

External Ear Development

Auricular Hillocks

6 mesenchymal swellings (auricular hillocks) around 1st pharyngeal cleft:

  • Hillocks 1-3: From 1st arch (mandibular) → tragus, anterior helix
  • Hillocks 4-6: From 2nd arch (hyoid) → antihelix, antitragus, lobule

External auditory canal: 1st pharyngeal cleft (ectoderm-lined)

🎯 Clinical Pearl: Auricular abnormalities (preauricular pits, tags, microtia) may indicate underlying renal anomalies because kidneys and ears develop at the same time (weeks 4-8). Always check renal ultrasound!

🔴 Congenital Anomalies

Eye Anomalies

Coloboma

Defect: Failure of optic fissure to close (week 7)

Appearance: Keyhole-shaped defect, typically INFERIOR (6 o'clock position)

Can affect: Iris, ciliary body, retina, choroid, optic nerve

Associations: CHARGE syndrome, COACH syndrome, Cat-eye syndrome

Congenital Cataracts

Defect: Opacification of lens

Causes:

  • TORCH: Rubella (#1 infectious), CMV, toxoplasmosis, HSV
  • Metabolic: Galactosemia ("oil droplet"), hypocalcemia
  • Genetic: Down syndrome, Lowe syndrome, NF2

Red reflex: Absent or white (leukocoria) - always investigate!

Aniridia

Defect: Absence of iris (partial or complete)

Cause: PAX6 mutation (autosomal dominant)

Association: WAGR syndrome (Wilms tumor, Aniridia, GU anomalies, intellectual disability) - 11p13 deletion

Persistent Hyperplastic Primary Vitreous (PHPV)

Defect: Failure of hyaloid artery/primary vitreous to regress

Presentation: Unilateral leukocoria, microphthalmos

Ear Anomalies

Congenital Hearing Loss

TypeDefect LocationCauses
SensorineuralInner ear / CN VIIICMV (#1), rubella, genetic (connexin 26), aminoglycosides
ConductiveOuter/middle earOssicular malformation, middle ear effusion, atresia

Preauricular Pits & Tags

Defect: Abnormal fusion of auricular hillocks

Associations: Branchio-oto-renal syndrome (check renal US!)

Microtia / Anotia

Defect: Underdevelopment or absence of external ear

Associations: Hemifacial microsomia, Treacher Collins, thalidomide exposure

⚠️ CMV is #1 infectious cause of congenital hearing loss - often progressive and may not be detected at newborn screening. Rubella causes cataracts + deafness + cardiac defects.

🧬 Associated Syndromes

CHARGE Syndrome ⭐⭐

Cause: CHD7 gene mutation (autosomal dominant)

Coloboma
Heart defects (conotruncal)
Atresia choanae
Retardation of growth/development
Genital hypoplasia
Ear anomalies (hearing loss, abnormal shape)

Key features: Coloboma + choanal atresia = highly suggestive. Also: CN dysfunction (especially facial), semicircular canal hypoplasia, cleft lip/palate

WAGR Syndrome

Cause: 11p13 deletion (includes PAX6 and WT1 genes)

  • Wilms tumor (screen with serial US)
  • Aniridia
  • Genitourinary anomalies
  • Retardation (intellectual disability)

Branchio-Oto-Renal (BOR) Syndrome

Cause: EYA1 gene mutation

  • Branchial: Cleft anomalies, fistulae, cysts
  • Oto: Hearing loss, preauricular pits, malformed ears
  • Renal: Hypoplasia, dysplasia, agenesis

Treacher Collins Syndrome

Cause: TCOF1 mutation (autosomal dominant)

Features: Bilateral, symmetric abnormalities from 1st and 2nd arch:

  • Downslanting palpebral fissures, coloboma of lower eyelid
  • Malar and mandibular hypoplasia
  • Microtia, conductive hearing loss
  • Normal intelligence

Goldenhar Syndrome (Hemifacial Microsomia)

Features: UNILATERAL underdevelopment of 1st and 2nd arch derivatives:

  • Hemifacial microsomia, mandibular hypoplasia
  • Microtia, preauricular tags
  • Epibulbar dermoid (eye)
  • Vertebral anomalies
🎯 Board Pearl: Treacher Collins = bilateral, symmetric. Goldenhar = unilateral, asymmetric. Both involve 1st/2nd arch structures.

🎯 Test Your Knowledge

Question 1

The lens of the eye develops from which embryonic tissue?

A. Neuroectoderm
B. Surface ectoderm
C. Neural crest
D. Mesoderm
Answer: B. The lens develops from surface ectoderm. The optic vesicle (neuroectoderm) induces the overlying surface ectoderm to form the lens placode, which invaginates to become the lens vesicle.

Question 2

A newborn has a keyhole-shaped defect in the inferior iris. This is caused by failure of which structure to close?

A. Lens vesicle
B. Optic fissure
C. Optic cup
D. Neural tube
Answer: B. Coloboma results from failure of the optic fissure to close (normally by week 7). The defect is typically inferior because the optic fissure is located on the ventral/inferior aspect of the developing eye.

Question 3

Which ossicle derives from the 2nd pharyngeal arch?

A. Malleus
B. Incus
C. Stapes
D. All three
Answer: C. Remember "MIS-12": Malleus and Incus from 1st arch (Meckel's cartilage), Stapes from 2nd arch (Reichert's cartilage).

Question 4

A child has coloboma, choanal atresia, and abnormal ears. What gene is most likely mutated?

A. PAX6
B. CHD7
C. EYA1
D. TCOF1
Answer: B. This describes CHARGE syndrome (Coloboma, Heart, Atresia choanae, Retardation, Genital, Ear). It's caused by CHD7 mutation. PAX6 = aniridia; EYA1 = BOR syndrome; TCOF1 = Treacher Collins.

Question 5

What is the #1 infectious cause of congenital sensorineural hearing loss?

A. CMV
B. Rubella
C. Toxoplasmosis
D. HSV
Answer: A. CMV is the #1 infectious cause of congenital hearing loss. It can be progressive and may not be detected at birth. Rubella causes the triad of cataracts, cardiac defects, and deafness.
