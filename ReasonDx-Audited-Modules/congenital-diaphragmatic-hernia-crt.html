🔬 Diaphragm Development

Diaphragm forms from 4 structures:
  1. Septum transversum (central tendon) - from cervical somites C3-C5
  2. Pleuroperitoneal membranes (posterolateral portions)
  3. Dorsal mesentery of esophagus (crura)
  4. Body wall musculature (peripheral rim)
Timeline: Diaphragm complete by week 8
Phrenic Nerve: "C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive"
Derived from cervical somites - explains referred shoulder pain with diaphragm irritation

Pathogenesis of CDH

CDH occurs when: Pleuroperitoneal canal fails to close before midgut returns to abdomen (week 10)

Result: Abdominal viscera herniate into chest → compresses developing lung → pulmonary hypoplasia

Key concept: The pulmonary hypoplasia (not the hernia itself) causes the morbidity/mortality

Two-Hit Hypothesis

  • Hit 1: Early lung maldevelopment (before herniation)
  • Hit 2: Compression from herniated viscera compounds hypoplasia

This explains why some infants do poorly despite successful repair - the lungs were already abnormal.

Why Left-Sided Bochdalek is More Common

The pleuroperitoneal canal closes later on the left side. The liver also protects the right hemidiaphragm.

Associated Anomalies (~40-50%)

  • Cardiac defects (most common)
  • Neural tube defects
  • Chromosomal abnormalities (trisomy 18, 21)
  • Fryns syndrome, Pallister-Killian syndrome
  • Malrotation (always present due to abnormal gut position)

🩺 Clinical Presentation

Severe/Early Presentation (most common)

Immediate respiratory distress at birth:
  • Cyanosis
  • Respiratory distress - tachypnea, retractions, grunting
  • Scaphoid (flat/concave) abdomen
  • Absent breath sounds on affected side
  • Bowel sounds in chest
  • Heart sounds shifted (usually to right)
  • Barrel-shaped chest

Late/Mild Presentation

  • May present days to months later
  • Recurrent respiratory infections
  • Feeding difficulties, vomiting
  • Better prognosis (lungs had time to develop)

Prenatal Diagnosis

Ultrasound findings:
  • Stomach/bowel/liver in chest
  • Mediastinal shift
  • Polyhydramnios (esophageal compression)
  • Absence of gastric bubble in abdomen
Prognostic indicators:
  • Lung-to-head ratio (LHR) - <1.0 poor prognosis
  • Observed/expected LHR (O/E LHR)
  • Liver position (intrathoracic liver = worse)
  • Presence of associated anomalies

CXR Findings

  • Bowel loops in chest (may look like cysts early)
  • NG tube in chest
  • Mediastinal shift to opposite side
  • Absent/small lung on affected side

🏥 Management

Immediate Resuscitation

DO NOT bag-mask ventilate! This distends stomach/bowel → worse lung compression

Immediate steps:
  • Intubate immediately
  • NG tube to decompress stomach (CRITICAL)
  • Gentle ventilation with low pressures (permissive hypercapnia)
  • Avoid high peak pressures (→ pneumothorax in hypoplastic lung)
  • Pre-ductal SpO2 monitoring (right hand)
  • IV access, sedation/paralysis

Stabilization Goals

Pre-ductal SpO2 >85%
Permissive hypercapnia (PaCO2 50-70 acceptable)
Avoid acidosis (worsens pulmonary hypertension)
Mean airway pressure <25
Optimize volume status

Surgical Repair

Timing: Surgery is NOT emergent! Stabilize first (usually 24-48+ hours)

Approach:
  • Abdominal approach (most common)
  • Thoracoscopic for stable patients
  • Primary repair if possible
  • Patch repair (Gore-Tex, muscle flap) for large defects

🎯 Clinical Reasoning Trainer

Case: Newborn in Respiratory Distress

A term newborn is delivered and immediately develops cyanosis and respiratory distress. On exam, the abdomen appears scaphoid. Breath sounds are absent on the left, and bowel sounds are heard in the left chest. Heart sounds are shifted to the right.

What is the most likely diagnosis?

Pneumothorax
Left-sided Bochdalek hernia
Meconium aspiration
Transient tachypnea of newborn

What is the FIRST step in management?

Bag-mask ventilation
Intubation and NG decompression
Immediate surgical repair
Chest tube placement

What is the main cause of morbidity/mortality in CDH?

Bowel obstruction
Cardiac compression
Pulmonary hypoplasia and persistent pulmonary hypertension
Summary: This is classic left-sided Bochdalek CDH - immediate respiratory distress, scaphoid abdomen (contents in chest), absent breath sounds with bowel sounds in chest. DO NOT bag-mask (distends bowel, worsens compression). Intubate immediately, place NG for decompression, use gentle ventilation. The pulmonary hypoplasia and pulmonary hypertension are what kill these babies - surgery is NOT emergent. Stabilize first!

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: What structures form the diaphragm? Select the one that forms the central tendon.

A. Septum transversum
B. Pleuroperitoneal membranes
C. Dorsal mesentery of esophagus
D. Body wall musculature
Correct: A. The septum transversum forms the central tendon. The pleuroperitoneal membranes form the posterolateral portions (where Bochdalek hernias occur).

Q2: Why should you AVOID bag-mask ventilation in CDH?

A. Risk of pneumothorax
B. Distends stomach/bowel, worsening lung compression
C. Causes aspiration
D. Increases pulmonary blood flow too rapidly
Correct: B. Bag-mask ventilation forces air into the stomach and bowel, which are in the chest. This further compresses the already hypoplastic lung. Intubate immediately and place NG tube.

Q3: Most Bochdalek hernias occur on which side?

A. Left
B. Right
C. Bilateral equally
D. Anterior
Correct: A. 85% of Bochdalek hernias are left-sided because the pleuroperitoneal canal closes later on the left, and the liver protects the right hemidiaphragm.

Q4: When should surgical repair of CDH be performed?

A. Immediately in the delivery room
B. Within the first 6 hours
C. After cardiopulmonary stabilization (usually 24-48+ hours)
D. At 1 week of life
Correct: C. Surgery is NOT emergent in CDH. The priority is cardiopulmonary stabilization - managing pulmonary hypertension and optimizing ventilation. Surgery typically occurs after 24-48+ hours of stabilization.

Q5: What prenatal ultrasound finding suggests poor prognosis in CDH?

A. Right-sided defect
B. Liver herniation into chest with low lung-to-head ratio
C. Stomach in chest only
D. Late diagnosis at 30 weeks
Correct: B. Intrathoracic liver and low lung-to-head ratio (<1.0) are poor prognostic indicators, suggesting severe pulmonary hypoplasia. Right-sided defects and later diagnosis actually suggest better prognosis.
