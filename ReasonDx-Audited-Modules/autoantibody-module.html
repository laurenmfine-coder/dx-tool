🎯 ANA Basics

ANA is a screening test. Positive in up to 15% of healthy people. The pattern and titer guide workup.

ANA Patterns

PatternAntigensConditions
HomogeneousdsDNA, histonesSLE, drug-induced lupus
SpeckledSm, RNP, SSA, SSBSLE, Sjögren's, MCTD
NucleolarRNA polymeraseSystemic sclerosis (diffuse)
CentromereCENP proteinsLimited scleroderma (CREST)

Titer Interpretation

  • 1:40-80: Low; often non-specific
  • 1:160+: More clinically significant
  • 1:640+: Strongly suggests autoimmune disease

Antiphospholipid Antibodies

  • Lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin, anti-β2-GP1
  • Clinical: Thrombosis, pregnancy loss
  • Need 2 positive tests 12+ weeks apart
⚠️ Anti-SSA in Pregnancy: Risk of neonatal lupus and congenital heart block. Monitor fetal heart starting ~16 weeks.

Sjögren's Syndrome

Key Antibodies

  • Anti-SSA (Ro): 70-90% of primary Sjögren's
  • Anti-SSB (La): 40-50%; more specific
  • Also check: RF (often positive), ANA

Rheumatoid Arthritis

RF and Anti-CCP

  • RF: Sensitive (~80%) but not specific (also in Sjögren's, infections, age)
  • Anti-CCP: More specific (95%); predicts erosive disease
  • Seronegative RA: 20-30% are RF and anti-CCP negative

📝 Quiz

Q1: Which antibody is most specific for SLE?

A. Anti-dsDNA
B. Anti-Sm
C. Anti-SSA
D. ANA
Anti-Sm is 99% specific (pathognomonic). Anti-dsDNA is 95% specific but correlates with activity. ANA is sensitive but not specific.

Q2: Anti-centromere antibody is associated with:

A. Limited scleroderma (CREST)
B. Diffuse scleroderma
C. SLE
D. Dermatomyositis
Anti-centromere = Limited scleroderma (CREST). Anti-Scl-70 = Diffuse scleroderma with ILD.

Q3: A pregnant woman with positive anti-SSA needs monitoring for:

A. Gestational diabetes
B. Fetal congenital heart block
C. Neural tube defects
D. Preterm labor
Anti-SSA crosses placenta and can cause neonatal lupus including congenital heart block. Monitor fetal heart from ~16 weeks.

Q4: c-ANCA with PR3 specificity is most associated with:

A. GPA (Wegener's granulomatosis)
B. Microscopic polyangiitis
C. Eosinophilic GPA
D. Polyarteritis nodosa
c-ANCA/PR3 = GPA (Wegener's). p-ANCA/MPO = MPA and EGPA. PAN is ANCA-negative.
