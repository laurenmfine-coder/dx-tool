📊 Heart Failure Classification

Definition: Clinical syndrome caused by structural/functional cardiac abnormalities resulting in reduced cardiac output and/or elevated intracardiac pressures

💊 HFrEF: Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy

The "Fantastic Four" - All Patients with HFrEF Should Receive:

Drug ClassExamplesMortality Benefit
1. ACEi/ARB/ARNISacubitril-valsartan (ARNI) preferred~20% reduction
2. Beta-blockerCarvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol~35% reduction
3. MRASpironolactone, eplerenone~30% reduction
4. SGLT2iDapagliflozin, empagliflozin~20% reduction

Start all four ASAP! Quadruple therapy is the new standard of care.

Treatment Approach

Warm & Wet (most common):
  • IV loop diuretic (furosemide, bumetanide, torsemide)
  • Goal: net negative 1-2L/day
  • Continue GDMT if BP tolerates
  • May add thiazide for diuretic resistance
Cold & Wet (cardiogenic shock):
  • Inotropes: dobutamine, milrinone
  • May need vasopressors if hypotensive
  • Consider mechanical support (IABP, Impella)

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: What are the "Fantastic Four" medications for HFrEF?

A. ACEi/ARB/ARNI, Beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i
B. ACEi, Digoxin, Furosemide, Aspirin
C. Beta-blocker, CCB, Statin, Diuretic
Correct: A. The 2022 AHA/ACC guidelines recommend all four medication classes for HFrEF patients: ACEi/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker (carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol), MRA (spironolactone/eplerenone), and SGLT2i (dapagliflozin/empagliflozin).

Q2: What defines HFrEF (reduced ejection fraction)?

A. EF ≤40%
B. EF ≤50%
C. EF ≤60%
Correct: A. HFrEF is defined as EF ≤40%. HFmrEF is 41-49%, and HFpEF is ≥50%.

Q3: Which SGLT2 inhibitor trial demonstrated mortality benefit in HFrEF?

A. DAPA-HF
B. EMPA-REG OUTCOME
C. CANVAS
Correct: A. DAPA-HF (dapagliflozin) was the first SGLT2i trial designed specifically for HFrEF patients, showing reduced cardiovascular death and HF hospitalization regardless of diabetes status.
