📚 MSK Development Overview

The MSK system develops from mesoderm: paraxial mesoderm (somites → axial skeleton, muscles), lateral plate mesoderm (limb bones), and neural crest (craniofacial skeleton).

Mesodermal Contributions

SourceDerivatives
Paraxial mesoderm (somites)Vertebrae, ribs, skeletal muscles, dermis of back
Lateral plate mesodermLimb bones, sternum, body wall
Neural crestCraniofacial bones and cartilage

Sclerotome → Skeleton | Myotome → Muscles | Dermatome → Dermis

🔶 Somite Development

Somites (42-44 pairs) form from paraxial mesoderm and differentiate into sclerotome (vertebrae), myotome (muscle), and dermatome (dermis).

Somite Differentiation

SOMITE ├── SCLEROTOME (ventromedial) → Vertebrae, ribs [SHH from notochord] └── DERMOMYOTOME (dorsolateral) ├── DERMATOME → Dermis of back └── MYOTOME → Skeletal muscles ├── Epimere → Back extensors └── Hypomere → Body wall, limb muscles

Resegmentation

Each sclerotome splits; caudal portion fuses with cranial portion of next somite. Result: each vertebra derives from two adjacent somites, allowing nerves to exit between vertebrae.

🎯 Clinical Pearl: Failure of resegmentation → fusion anomalies (Klippel-Feil syndrome, block vertebrae).

🦵 Limb Development

Limbs develop from limb buds with three signaling centers: AER (proximal-distal via FGFs), ZPA (anterior-posterior via SHH), dorsal ectoderm (dorsal-ventral via Wnt7a).

Timeline

  • Day 24: Upper limb buds appear
  • Day 28: Lower limb buds appear
  • Week 7: Digital rays visible
  • Week 8: Digits separate (interdigital apoptosis)

📍 ZPA and Digit Identity

SHH gradient: high posterior (digit 5) → low anterior (digit 1). Extra ZPA → polydactyly.

Limb Rotation

  • Upper limb: 90° lateral rotation
  • Lower limb: 90° medial rotation

This explains why elbow and knee flex in opposite directions!

💀 Bone Formation

Intramembranous: Mesenchyme → bone directly (flat skull bones, clavicle).
Endochondral: Mesenchyme → cartilage → bone (most bones).

⚠️ Skeletal Dysplasias
  • Achondroplasia: FGFR3 gain-of-function → rhizomelic shortening
  • Osteogenesis imperfecta: Collagen I defect → fragile bones, blue sclerae

⚠️ Congenital MSK Anomalies

Digit Anomalies

📍 Polydactyly

  • Preaxial: Extra thumb side (SHH/ZPA issue)
  • Postaxial: Extra pinky side (more common)

📍 Syndactyly

Failure of interdigital apoptosis → fused digits.

Hip & Foot

⚠️ DDH (Developmental Dysplasia of Hip)
Risk: female, breech, firstborn, family history. Screen with Ortolani/Barlow. Treat with Pavlik harness.

📍 Clubfoot (CAVE)

Cavus, Adductus, Varus, Equinus. Treat with Ponseti casting.

📝 Test Your Knowledge

Question 1

Vertebrae and ribs derive from which somite portion?

A. Sclerotome
B. Myotome
C. Dermatome
D. Lateral plate
Answer: A. Sclerotome (ventromedial somite) → vertebrae, ribs. Induced by SHH from notochord.

Question 2

Which signaling center establishes thumb-to-pinky axis?

A. AER
B. ZPA
C. Dorsal ectoderm
D. Notochord
Answer: B. ZPA produces SHH gradient → digit identity. Extra ZPA → polydactyly.

Question 3

Syndactyly results from failure of:

A. AER signaling
B. Interdigital apoptosis
C. ZPA signaling
D. Limb rotation
Answer: B. Digits separate via programmed cell death. Failure → syndactyly.

Question 4

Flat skull bones form via which ossification?

A. Intramembranous
B. Endochondral
C. Perichondral
D. Periosteal
Answer: A. Intramembranous = direct (no cartilage). Forms flat skull bones, facial bones, clavicle.

Question 5

Absent thumb and radius suggests evaluation for:

A. VACTERL
B. Down syndrome
C. Achondroplasia
D. Marfan
Answer: A. Radial ray defects = "L" in VACTERL. Also Fanconi anemia, Holt-Oram, TAR.
