🩺 Prenatal Visit
A 24-year-old G1P0 presents for anatomy scan at 20 weeks. She took no folic acid before or during pregnancy. Ultrasound shows "lemon sign" (frontal bone scalloping) and "banana sign" (curved cerebellum), with a lumbar spine defect.

What is the diagnosis?

🧠
Myelomeningocele (spina bifida)
Lemon + banana signs = classic for NTD with Chiari II
💧
Isolated hydrocephalus
Would see ventriculomegaly without spine defect
🔵
Dandy-Walker malformation
Cystic posterior fossa, not banana sign
Stage 2 of 6
🔬 Embryology

💡 Neural Tube Closure

The neural tube closes during days 22-28. Anterior neuropore closes by day 24-26, posterior by day 26-28.

Which neuropore failure causes spina bifida?

⬆️
Anterior neuropore
Would cause anencephaly
⬇️
Posterior neuropore
Closes last; failure → spina bifida

📊 Screening
The patient asks about blood tests that could have detected this earlier.

What happens to maternal serum AFP (MSAFP) in open NTDs?

⬆️
Elevated MSAFP
AFP leaks from exposed neural tissue
⬇️
Decreased MSAFP
This pattern seen in Down syndrome

💬 Counseling
The family asks about expected complications of myelomeningocele.

Which complication occurs in ~90% of cases?

💧
Hydrocephalus requiring VP shunt
Due to Chiari II blocking CSF flow
❤️
Congenital heart defect
Not typically associated with NTDs
Stage 5 of 6
💊 Prevention

💡 Folic Acid

Folic acid supplementation can reduce NTD risk by 50-70%!

What dose for a woman with PREVIOUS NTD pregnancy?

1️⃣
400 mcg daily
Standard dose for all women
4️⃣
4 mg (4000 mcg) daily
High-dose for previous NTD or antiepileptics

🔑 Key Points

  • Posterior neuropore failure → Spina bifida
  • Anterior neuropore failure → Anencephaly
  • US signs: Lemon (frontal), Banana (cerebellum)
  • Chiari II: ~100% with myelomeningocele
  • Hydrocephalus: ~90% require shunt
  • MSAFP: Elevated in open NTDs
  • Prevention: Folic acid 400mcg (all), 4mg (high-risk)
