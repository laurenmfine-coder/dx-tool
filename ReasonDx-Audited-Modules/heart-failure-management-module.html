💊 HFrEF: Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy

The "Fantastic Four" of HFrEF:
1. ARNI/ACEi/ARB | 2. Beta-blocker | 3. MRA | 4. SGLT2i
All four classes improve mortality! Start all within first visit if tolerated.
ClassExamplesKey Points
ARNISacubitril/valsartanSuperior to ACEi; 36hr washout from ACEi before starting
ACEiLisinopril, enalaprilIf can't tolerate ARNI; watch K+, creatinine
ARBLosartan, valsartanIf ACEi intolerant (cough)
Beta-blockerCarvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprololOnly these 3 have mortality benefit; start low, go slow
MRASpironolactone, eplerenoneEF ≤35% + symptoms; watch K+ (avoid if >5.0)
SGLT2iDapagliflozin, empagliflozinBenefit regardless of diabetes; reduces hospitalizations

💔 HFpEF Management

Limited evidence-based therapies - focus on symptom control and comorbidities

Treatment Approach

  • Diuretics for congestion (mainstay)
  • SGLT2 inhibitors - NOW recommended (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER trials)
  • BP control
  • Rate control for AFib
  • Treat underlying conditions: CAD, obesity, sleep apnea

Common Causes of HFpEF

  • Hypertension (most common)
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Coronary artery disease
  • Atrial fibrillation
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
  • Restrictive cardiomyopathy (amyloid, sarcoid)

🚨 Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

Clinical Profiles (Forrester Classification)

Dry (no congestion)Wet (congestion)
Warm (adequate perfusion)Warm & Dry (compensated)Warm & Wet (most common ADHF)
Cold (hypoperfusion)Cold & Dry (hypovolemic)Cold & Wet (cardiogenic shock)

Treatment by Profile

Warm & Wet: IV diuretics (furosemide), vasodilators if hypertensive
Cold & Wet: Inotropes (dobutamine, milrinone) ± diuretics
Cold & Dry: Careful fluid challenge

IV Diuretic Dosing

Loop diuretic naive: Furosemide 20-40mg IV
On home diuretics: IV dose = home oral dose (or 2.5x if resistant)
Goal: 3-5 L negative fluid balance, urine output 100-150 mL/hr initially

Cardiogenic Shock

Signs: SBP <90, cold extremities, altered mental status, oliguria
Treatment: Inotropes, vasopressors, consider mechanical support (IABP, Impella, ECMO)

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Which 4 medication classes have mortality benefit in HFrEF?

A. ARNI/ACEi, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i
B. Diuretics, digoxin, CCB, beta-blocker
C. ACEi, diuretics, statin, aspirin
Correct: A. The "Fantastic Four" all reduce mortality in HFrEF and should be initiated early.

Q2: Which beta-blockers have mortality benefit in HFrEF?

A. Atenolol, propranolol, labetalol
B. Carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol
C. Any beta-blocker
Correct: B. Only carvedilol, metoprolol succinate (NOT tartrate), and bisoprolol have proven mortality benefit in HFrEF trials.

Q3: A patient with HFrEF is on max GDMT with EF 30% and LBBB with QRS 160ms. What device is indicated?

A. ICD only
B. CRT-D (CRT with ICD)
C. Pacemaker
Correct: B. CRT is indicated for EF ≤35% + LBBB + QRS ≥150ms. Combined with ICD for sudden death prevention.
