🔥 Hyperthyroidism

Symptoms

Everything is "hyper":
Heat intolerance, sweating
Weight loss (despite ↑ appetite)
Tachycardia, palpitations, AFib
Tremor, hyperreflexia
Anxiety, irritability, insomnia
Diarrhea, frequent BMs
Menstrual irregularity (oligomenorrhea)

Graves' Disease

Most common cause of hyperthyroidism
Autoimmune - TSH receptor stimulating antibodies (TRAb/TSI)

Unique features:
  • Ophthalmopathy: Proptosis, lid lag, periorbital edema (can occur independently of thyroid status)
  • Dermopathy: Pretibial myxedema (orange-peel skin over shins)
  • Thyroid acropachy: Clubbing (rare)

Cold intolerance

Bradycardia
Dry skin, coarse hair, hair loss
Delayed relaxation of reflexes

Hashimoto's Thyroiditis

Most common cause of hypothyroidism in iodine-sufficient areas
Autoimmune - anti-TPO and anti-thyroglobulin antibodies
Painless goiter (may progress to atrophy)
Associated with other autoimmune diseases

Treatment

Levothyroxine (T4)
  • Typical dose: 1.6 mcg/kg/day
  • Start low in elderly/cardiac disease (12.5-25 mcg)
  • Take on empty stomach, 30-60 min before breakfast
  • Recheck TSH in 6-8 weeks after dose changes
  • Goal: Normal TSH (0.5-4.0 mIU/L)

Special Situations

  • Pregnancy: Increased levothyroxine requirement (↑ by ~30%); TSH goal depends on trimester
  • Subclinical hypothyroidism: Treat if TSH >10 or symptoms/antibodies present

🚨 Thyroid Emergencies

Thyroid Storm

Life-threatening hyperthyroidism
Mortality up to 30% even with treatment

Triggers: Infection, surgery, trauma, RAI, iodine load, medication non-compliance

Features:
  • High fever (>104°F/40°C)
  • Tachycardia out of proportion to fever, AFib
  • Altered mental status, agitation, psychosis
  • GI: N/V, diarrhea, jaundice
  • Heart failure

Thyroid Storm Treatment

Order matters!
  1. Beta-blocker: Propranolol (blocks symptoms + T4→T3)
  2. Thionamide: PTU preferred (blocks T4→T3 conversion)
  3. Iodine: SSKI or Lugol's - GIVE 1 HOUR AFTER PTU (blocks release)
  4. Steroids: Hydrocortisone (blocks T4→T3, treats relative adrenal insufficiency)
  5. Supportive: Cooling, fluids, treat trigger

Myxedema Coma

Life-threatening hypothyroidism
Usually in elderly with long-standing hypothyroidism

Triggers: Infection, cold exposure, sedatives, surgery

Features:
  • Hypothermia
  • Altered mental status → coma
  • Bradycardia, hypotension
  • Hypoventilation, hypoxia, hypercapnia
  • Hyponatremia, hypoglycemia

Myxedema Coma Treatment

  • IV levothyroxine loading dose (200-400 mcg), then 50-100 mcg daily
  • Consider IV T3 (liothyronine) for faster effect
  • Stress-dose steroids (until adrenal insufficiency ruled out)
  • Supportive: Passive rewarming, ventilatory support, fluids
  • Treat precipitating cause

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Low TSH with high free T4 indicates:

A. Primary hyperthyroidism
B. Primary hypothyroidism
C. Central hypothyroidism
Correct: A. Low TSH + high T4 = primary hyperthyroidism. The pituitary appropriately suppresses TSH in response to high thyroid hormone.

Q2: Which finding is unique to Graves' disease?

A. Elevated free T4
B. Ophthalmopathy with proptosis
C. Tachycardia
Correct: B. Ophthalmopathy (proptosis, periorbital edema) is specific to Graves'. Other symptoms occur in any hyperthyroid state.

Q3: In thyroid storm, why must PTU be given BEFORE iodine?

A. Iodine can worsen thyrotoxicosis if synthesis not blocked first
B. PTU only works after iodine
C. Iodine inactivates PTU
Correct: A. Iodine provides substrate for MORE hormone synthesis. PTU must first block synthesis; then iodine blocks hormone release.

Q4: Most common cause of hypothyroidism in iodine-sufficient areas?

A. Hashimoto's thyroiditis
B. Iodine deficiency
C. Pituitary tumor
Correct: A. Hashimoto's (autoimmune thyroiditis) is the most common cause in developed countries. Iodine deficiency is most common worldwide.

Q5: Which medication is preferred for hyperthyroidism in first trimester pregnancy?

A. PTU (propylthiouracil)
B. Methimazole
C. Radioactive iodine
Correct: A. PTU is preferred in first trimester because methimazole is teratogenic (aplasia cutis, choanal atresia). Switch to methimazole after first trimester due to PTU hepatotoxicity risk.
