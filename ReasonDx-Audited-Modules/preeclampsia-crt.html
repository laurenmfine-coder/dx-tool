📚 Key Learning Points

  • Severe preeclampsia criteria: BP ≥160/110, headache, visual changes, thrombocytopenia, liver dysfunction, renal insufficiency
  • Acute severe hypertension must be treated within 30-60 minutes to prevent stroke
  • HELLP = Hemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes + Low Platelets - diagnose by: LDH ≥600, AST ≥70, platelets <100,000
  • Magnesium toxicity sequence: Loss of reflexes (8-12 mEq/L) → Respiratory depression → Cardiac arrest
  • HELLP labs typically worsen for 24-48 hours postpartum before improving; consider alternative dx if no improvement by day 4
  • Definitive treatment is delivery - timing depends on severity and gestational age
🎯 Clinical Pearl: The triad of severe headache + visual changes + hypertension in pregnancy = preeclampsia with severe features until proven otherwise. Do not delay treatment waiting for labs.
