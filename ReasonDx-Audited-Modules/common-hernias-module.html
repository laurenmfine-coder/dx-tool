Inguinal Hernias

Direct vs Indirect

FeatureIndirectDirect
LocationLateral to inferior epigastric vesselsMedial to inferior epigastric vessels
DefectThrough internal ring (patent processus vaginalis)Through Hesselbach's triangle
AgeAny age (congenital)Usually older adults
Descends into scrotumYes (can)Rarely
More commonYes (~70%)Less common
Hesselbach's Triangle (Direct hernia site):
  • Medial: Rectus abdominis
  • Lateral: Inferior epigastric vessels
  • Inferior: Inguinal ligament

Clinical Features

  • Bulge in groin, worse with Valsalva/standing
  • May reduce when supine
  • Dull aching discomfort
Incarceration/Strangulation: Surgical emergency!
  • Non-reducible hernia
  • Severe pain, tenderness
  • Nausea, vomiting, obstruction
  • Strangulation: compromised blood supply → bowel necrosis

Femoral Hernia

High-yield features:
  • Below and lateral to pubic tubercle (vs inguinal: above and medial)
  • More common in women
  • Higher risk of strangulation than inguinal
  • Boundaries: inguinal ligament, lacunar ligament, femoral vein, pectineal ligament

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Direct inguinal hernia passes through which structure?

A. Hesselbach's triangle
B. Internal inguinal ring
Correct: A. Direct hernias pass through Hesselbach's triangle (medial to inferior epigastric vessels).

Q2: Femoral hernia is located relative to the pubic tubercle?

A. Below and lateral
B. Above and medial
Correct: A. Femoral hernia is below and lateral to pubic tubercle. Inguinal is above and medial.
