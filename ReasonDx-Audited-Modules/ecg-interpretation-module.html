📋 Systematic Approach

Rate → Rhythm → Axis → Intervals → Hypertrophy → Ischemia

1. Rate

Methods:
  • 300 Rule: 300 ÷ (# large boxes between R waves)
  • 1500 Rule: 1500 ÷ (# small boxes between R waves)
  • 6-second strip: Count QRS complexes × 10
Normal: 60-100 bpm

2. Rhythm

  • Is it regular or irregular?
  • Are P waves present and upright in II?
  • Is there a P wave before every QRS?
  • Is there a QRS after every P wave?

3. Axis

Quick method using leads I and aVF:

Junctional Rhythms

Features: No P wave OR inverted P in II (retrograde), narrow QRS
  • Junctional escape: 40-60 bpm
  • Accelerated junctional: 60-100 bpm
  • Junctional tachycardia: >100 bpm

Supraventricular Tachycardia (SVT)

Narrow complex, regular, rate 150-250
Types: AVNRT (most common), AVRT (WPW), atrial tachycardia

Treatment:
  1. Vagal maneuvers (Valsalva, carotid massage)
  2. Adenosine 6mg IV push → 12mg → 12mg
  3. If unstable: synchronized cardioversion

🫀 Ischemia & Infarction

ST Elevation MI (STEMI)

ST elevation ≥1mm in 2 contiguous leads (≥2mm in V2-V3 for men)
Convex ("tombstone") or horizontal ST elevation

Action: Emergent reperfusion (PCI preferred, or thrombolytics)

Localization by Leads

Reciprocal Changes

ST depression in leads opposite to ST elevation
Example: Inferior STEMI (II, III, aVF elevation) → reciprocal depression in I, aVL
Reciprocal changes increase specificity for STEMI

NSTEMI / Unstable Angina

  • ST depression
  • T wave inversion
  • Dynamic changes
  • Differentiate by troponin (elevated in NSTEMI)

Wellens Syndrome

Critical LAD stenosis!
Biphasic or deeply inverted T waves in V2-V3
Patient is often pain-FREE when ECG taken
Do NOT stress test - high risk of anterior MI

De Winter T Waves

LAD occlusion equivalent!
Upsloping ST depression at J-point with tall, symmetric T waves in precordial leads
Treat as STEMI equivalent

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: ST elevation in leads II, III, aVF indicates infarction in which territory?

A. Inferior
B. Anterior
C. Lateral
Correct: A. Leads II, III, aVF represent the inferior wall, supplied by the RCA (80%) or LCx.

Q2: What is the ventricular rate in atrial flutter with 2:1 block?

A. ~150 bpm
B. ~300 bpm
C. ~75 bpm
Correct: A. Atrial flutter has an atrial rate of ~300; with 2:1 AV block, the ventricular rate is ~150 bpm.

Q3: "RSR' pattern in V1" is characteristic of:

A. Right bundle branch block
B. Left bundle branch block
C. First degree AV block
Correct: A. RBBB shows RSR' ("rabbit ears") in V1-V2. LBBB shows W pattern in V1 and M pattern in V6.

Q4: First-line treatment for stable SVT?

A. Amiodarone
B. Vagal maneuvers, then adenosine
C. Defibrillation
Correct: B. Start with vagal maneuvers (Valsalva), then adenosine 6mg rapid IV push. Defibrillation is for VF; cardioversion for unstable SVT.

Q5: Progressively lengthening PR interval before a dropped beat describes:

A. Second degree Type II
B. Second degree Type I (Wenckebach)
C. Third degree (complete)
Correct: B. Mobitz Type I (Wenckebach) has progressive PR lengthening until a QRS is dropped. Type II has constant PR with sudden dropped beats.
