🎯 Developmental Domains

Four Key Domains:
  • Gross Motor: Large muscle movements (sitting, walking, running)
  • Fine Motor: Small muscle movements (grasping, drawing, writing)
  • Language: Receptive (understanding) and expressive (speaking)
  • Social/Adaptive: Interaction, self-care, play

Key Principles

  • Development proceeds cephalocaudal (head to toe) and proximal to distal
  • Milestones have a range - concern is when outside the range
  • Premature infants: Use corrected age until age 2
  • Always assess in context of the child's environment and opportunities
Quick Age Markers:
2 months: Social smile
4 months: Rolls, laughs, reaches
6 months: Sits, babbles, stranger anxiety
9 months: Crawls, pincer, "mama/dada" nonspecific
12 months: Walks, 1-3 words, points
18 months: Runs, 10-25 words, feeds self
2 years: Jumps, 2-word phrases, parallel play
3 years: Tricycle, 3-word sentences, interactive play

🚨 Developmental Red Flags

Refer immediately if ANY of these:
  • Loss of previously acquired skills at ANY age
  • No social smile by 3 months
  • Not reaching for objects by 5 months
  • No babbling by 9 months
  • Not walking by 18 months
  • No single words by 16 months
  • No 2-word phrases by 24 months
  • Not pointing or waving by 12 months

Autism Red Flags

Screen for ASD if:
  • No babbling by 12 months
  • No gesturing (pointing, waving) by 12 months
  • No single words by 16 months
  • No 2-word spontaneous phrases by 24 months
  • Loss of language or social skills at any age
  • Poor eye contact
  • No response to name by 12 months
  • Repetitive behaviors, restricted interests

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: At what age should an infant have a social smile?

A. Birth
B. 2 months
C. 4 months
D. 6 months
Correct: B. Social smile (smiling in response to faces/voices) appears by 2 months. Absence of social smile by 3 months is a red flag for developmental delay.

Q2: A 9-month-old should be able to do all of the following EXCEPT:

A. Sit independently
B. Crawl
C. Walk independently
D. Say "mama" nonspecifically
Correct: C. Walking independently typically occurs around 12 months. At 9 months, infants should sit, crawl, pull to stand, and cruise (walk holding furniture).

Q3: How many words should an 18-month-old have?

A. 1-3 words
B. 10-25 words
C. 50+ words
D. 2-word phrases
Correct: B. At 18 months, children should have 10-25 words. By 24 months, they should have 50+ words and be using 2-word phrases.

Q4: Which finding warrants immediate developmental referral?

A. Not walking at 11 months
B. Loss of previously acquired words
C. Stranger anxiety at 8 months
D. Parallel play at 2 years
Correct: B. Loss of developmental skills (regression) at ANY age is a red flag requiring immediate evaluation. It may indicate autism, neurodegenerative disease, or other serious conditions.

Q5: A 3-year-old should be able to copy which shape?

A. Circle
B. Cross (+)
C. Triangle
D. Diamond
Correct: A. Drawing progression: circle at 3 years, cross at 4 years, triangle/square at 5 years, diamond at 6 years.
