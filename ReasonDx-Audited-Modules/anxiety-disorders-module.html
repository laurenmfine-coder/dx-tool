📚 Overview

😟 GAD

Excessive worry about multiple things, more days than not, ≥6 months, difficult to control.

😱 Panic Disorder

Recurrent UNEXPECTED panic attacks + worry about attacks OR behavior change ≥1 month
Panic Attack (≥4):
Palpitations • Sweating • Shaking • SOB • Choking • Chest pain • Nausea • Dizzy • Derealization • Fear of dying • Paresthesias • Chills/hot
  • Peak: ~10 minutes
  • Duration: 10-30 min
  • Key: UNEXPECTED (no clear trigger)
First panic attack = medical workup! Rule out MI, PE, arrhythmia, hyperthyroidism, pheo.

🕷️ Phobias

Specific Phobia

Marked fear of specific object/situation ≥6 months. Types: Animal, Natural (heights), Blood-injection, Situational (flying).

Tx: Exposure therapy (best!)

Social Anxiety

Fear of scrutiny/embarrassment in social situations ≥6 months.

Tx: CBT, SSRI; beta-blockers for performance-only

Agoraphobia

Fear of ≥2: public transport, open spaces, enclosed spaces, crowds, alone outside.

Blood-injection phobia: Unique vasovagal response (fainting). Treat with applied tension technique.

🔁 OCD

Now separate DSM-5 category, not anxiety disorder.
Obsessions: Intrusive thoughts causing anxiety
Compulsions: Behaviors to reduce anxiety (not pleasurable)

Common Obsessions

Contamination, Doubting, Symmetry, Aggressive thoughts, Religious

Common Compulsions

Washing, Checking, Ordering, Counting

  • Ego-dystonic: Recognizes as irrational
  • Time: >1 hour/day
  • Tx: High-dose SSRI + ERP
OCD vs OCPD: OCD = ego-dystonic, distressing. OCPD = ego-syntonic personality (sees it as normal).

🎯 Quiz

Q1: GAD requires symptoms ≥

A. 2 weeks
B. 6 months
C. 1 year
B. GAD = excessive worry ≥6 months.

Q2: First-line for panic disorder:

A. SSRI
B. Benzodiazepine
C. Beta-blocker
A. SSRIs first-line. Benzos short-term bridge only.

Q3: Blood-injection phobia causes:

A. Tachycardia
B. Vasovagal syncope
C. Hypertension
B. Unique vasovagal response (bradycardia, hypotension). Tx: applied tension.

Q4: OCD is:

A. Ego-dystonic
B. Ego-syntonic
A. OCD = ego-dystonic (recognizes irrational). OCPD = ego-syntonic.

Q5: Best treatment for specific phobia:

A. SSRI
B. Exposure therapy
C. Buspirone
B. Exposure therapy most effective for specific phobias.
