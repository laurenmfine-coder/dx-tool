💡 Embryology Connection

CDH occurs when the pleuroperitoneal membrane fails to close (usually LEFT side - Bochdalek hernia). Abdominal organs herniate into the chest during development, preventing normal lung growth → pulmonary hypoplasia.

Key Points - CDH
• Scaphoid abdomen + respiratory distress + bowel sounds in chest = CDH
• Do NOT bag-mask (inflates herniated bowel)
• Intubate immediately, NG to decompress stomach
• Stabilize before surgery (may need ECMO)
• LEFT-sided Bochdalek (90%) >> RIGHT or anterior Morgagni

Key Points - TEF
• Can't pass NG + drooling + choking = esophageal atresia until proven otherwise
• Gas in stomach = fistula below the atresia (Type C, 85%)
• NO gas in stomach = pure atresia (Type A) or proximal fistula
• Association: VACTERL (Vertebral, Anorectal, Cardiac, TE, Renal, Limb)
• Polyhydramnios because fetus can't swallow amniotic fluid

💡 Embryology Connection

Duodenal atresia results from failure of recanalization. The duodenum initially becomes solid (week 5-6), then recanalization creates the lumen. Failure → obstruction. 30% associated with Down syndrome.

Double bubble = dilated stomach + dilated proximal duodenum. No distal gas means COMPLETE obstruction.

Key Points - Duodenal Atresia
• Double bubble + no distal gas = complete duodenal obstruction
• Bilious vomiting = obstruction DISTAL to ampulla of Vater (D2-D3)
• Non-bilious = obstruction PROXIMAL to ampulla (pylorus, D1)
• 30% associated with Trisomy 21
• Also associated with annular pancreas, malrotation
• Failure of recanalization (different from malrotation which is rotation failure)

Recanalization failure. Double bubble. 30% Trisomy 21. Bilious = distal to ampulla.

🔑 Universal Principle

BILIOUS VOMITING in a newborn = surgical emergency until proven otherwise. It indicates obstruction distal to where bile enters (ampulla of Vater). Must rule out malrotation with volvulus (can cause midgut death in hours).
