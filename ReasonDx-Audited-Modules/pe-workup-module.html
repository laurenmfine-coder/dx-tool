📊 Pre-Test Probability Assessment

Wells Score for PE

CriterionPoints
Clinical signs/symptoms of DVT3

Interpretation:
≤4 points: PE Unlikely → D-dimer
>4 points: PE Likely → CTPA

PERC Rule

Use ONLY if Wells ≤4
If ALL 8 criteria negative, PE ruled out without D-dimer: Age <50, HR <100, SpO2 ≥95%, No hemoptysis, No estrogen, No prior VTE, No unilateral leg swelling, No recent surgery/trauma

Age-Adjusted D-dimer

For patients >50: Threshold = Age × 10 (ng/mL)

💊 Treatment

Anticoagulation

AgentDosing
Rivaroxaban15mg BID × 21d, then 20mg daily
Apixaban10mg BID × 7d, then 5mg BID
LMWHEnoxaparin 1mg/kg BID (preferred in cancer)

Duration

  • Provoked: 3 months
  • Unprovoked: ≥3 months, consider extended
  • Cancer: Indefinite while active

Thrombolysis

Indication: Massive PE with hemodynamic instability
Regimen: Alteplase 100mg IV over 2 hours

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Wells score of 3 - next step?

A. D-dimer (if PERC not met)
B. CTPA directly
Correct: A. Wells ≤4 = PE unlikely. Check D-dimer.

Q2: Preferred anticoagulant in cancer-associated VTE?

A. Warfarin
B. LMWH or DOAC
Correct: B. LMWH or DOACs preferred in cancer.
