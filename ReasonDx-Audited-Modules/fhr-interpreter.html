📋 Management

    📖 Deceleration Types

    Early Decelerations - Head compression
    • Gradual onset, mirrors contraction
    • Nadir at peak of contraction
    • Benign - vagal response to head compression
    • No intervention needed
    Variable Decelerations - Cord compression
    • Abrupt onset (<30 sec to nadir)
    • Variable timing, V or W shaped
    • Shoulders = initial acceleration before/after
    • Management: Position changes, amnioinfusion if recurrent
    Late Decelerations - Uteroplacental insufficiency ⚠️
    • Gradual onset, nadir AFTER contraction peak
    • Indicates fetal hypoxia
    • Concerning even if shallow
    • Management: Left lateral, O2, IV fluids, stop oxytocin, consider delivery
    Prolonged Decelerations - Multiple causes ⚠️
    • ≥2 minutes but <10 minutes
    • Causes: Cord prolapse, uterine rupture, abruption, hypotension
    • >10 minutes = baseline change
    • Management: Intrauterine resuscitation, prepare for emergent delivery

    📊 NICHD 3-Tier Classification

    Category I (Normal)

    • Baseline 110-160 bpm
    • Moderate variability
    • No late or variable decelerations
    • Early decelerations present or absent
    • Accelerations present or absent

    → Strongly predictive of normal fetal acid-base status

    Category II (Indeterminate)

    • Everything NOT Category I or III
    • Examples: Tachycardia, minimal variability, variable decels, absence of accels
    • Requires evaluation, surveillance, and possible intervention

    → Not predictive of abnormal acid-base status but requires monitoring

    Category III (Abnormal)

    • Absent variability WITH any of:
      • Recurrent late decelerations
      • Recurrent variable decelerations
      • Bradycardia
    • OR sinusoidal pattern

    → Abnormal fetal acid-base status - requires IMMEDIATE intervention
