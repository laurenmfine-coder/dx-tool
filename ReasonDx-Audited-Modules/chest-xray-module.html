📋 Systematic Approach

Always use the same systematic approach to avoid missing findings.
💡 "ABCDEFGHI"
Airway • Bones • Cardiac • Diaphragm • Effusion/Edge • Fields • Gastric • Hilum • Instruments

A - Airway

  • Trachea: Midline? Deviated?
  • Carina: T4-T5 level
  • Mainstem bronchi: Right more vertical

B - Bones & Soft Tissues

  • Ribs: Fractures, lesions
  • Clavicles, spine, shoulders
  • Soft tissues: Subcutaneous emphysema, mastectomy

C - Cardiac Silhouette

  • CTR <50% on PA film
  • Right border = RA, Left = LV
  • Shape: Boot (RVH), water-bottle (effusion)

D - Diaphragm

  • Right 1-2 cm higher than left
  • Costophrenic angles sharp?
  • Free air = perforation

E - Effusion/Edges (Pleura)

  • Effusion: Meniscus sign
  • Pneumothorax: Lung edge, absent markings

F - Fields (Lung)

  • Compare side-to-side
  • Opacities: Consolidation, nodules
  • Lucencies: Emphysema, cavities

G - Gastric Bubble

  • Should be on left under diaphragm

H - Hilum

  • Left slightly higher than right
  • Lymphadenopathy?

I - Instruments

  • ETT: 2-4 cm above carina
  • NG: Below diaphragm
  • Central lines: Cavoatrial junction

🔧 Technical Quality: "RIPE"

Rotation • Inspiration • Penetration • Exposure

Rotation

Medial clavicle heads equidistant from spinous processes

Inspiration

Good: 8-10 posterior ribs visible above diaphragm

Penetration

Adequate: Spine visible through cardiac silhouette

🔍 Key Radiographic Findings

Silhouette Sign

Border lost when adjacent structures have similar density:
  • Right heart border → RML
  • Left heart border → Lingula
  • Right diaphragm → RLL
  • Left diaphragm → LLL

Air Bronchograms

Air-filled bronchi visible against opacified alveoli = alveolar pathology (pneumonia, edema, ARDS)

Meniscus Sign

Curved upper border of pleural effusion on upright film

Deep Sulcus Sign

Lucent CP angle on supine film = pneumothorax (air rises anteriorly)

Kerley B Lines

Short horizontal lines at lung periphery = thickened septa (pulmonary edema)

🏥 Common Pathology

Pneumonia

TypePattern
LobarDense consolidation, air bronchograms
BronchopneumoniaPatchy, multifocal
InterstitialReticular (viral/atypical)

Pulmonary Edema

  • Cardiomegaly
  • Cephalization
  • Kerley B lines
  • Perihilar "bat wing" opacities
  • Bilateral effusions

Pneumothorax

  • Visceral pleural line visible
  • Absent lung markings beyond line
  • Tension: Mediastinal shift AWAY, flattened diaphragm

Pleural Effusion

  • Upright: Meniscus, blunted CP angle (>200mL)
  • Supine: Hazy hemithorax
  • Lateral decubitus: Most sensitive

📝 Quiz

Q1: Loss of right heart border indicates pathology in:

A. RLL
B. RML
C. RUL
RML - silhouette sign localizes pathology adjacent to obscured border.

Q2: Good inspiration shows how many posterior ribs?

A. 5-6
B. 8-10
C. 12-14
8-10 posterior ribs above diaphragm = adequate inspiration.

Q3: Kerley B lines indicate:

A. Pulmonary edema
B. Pneumothorax
C. Pneumonia
Kerley B lines = thickened interlobular septa, classic for pulmonary edema.

Q4: On supine CXR, pneumothorax shows:

A. Deep sulcus sign
B. Meniscus sign
C. Bat wing opacity
Deep sulcus sign - air collects anteriorly when supine.
