💡 ABO Hemolytic Disease

Mom's anti-A IgG antibodies cross placenta and attack baby's A-antigen RBCs. Unlike Rh disease, ABO HDN can occur in first pregnancy because anti-A/B antibodies exist naturally.

🎓 Case Resolution
✅
With intensive phototherapy + IVIG, bili peaked at 19.2 and began declining. Baby avoided exchange transfusion and was discharged on day 5 with outpatient follow-up.

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • ABO incompatibility: Mom O + Baby A or B = risk for hemolysis
  • Can occur first pregnancy (unlike Rh disease)
  • Positive DAT confirms isoimmune hemolysis
  • Phototherapy: Converts bili to water-soluble lumirubin
  • IVIG: Blocks hemolysis, reduces need for exchange
  • Kernicterus: Basal ganglia → choreoathetoid CP, hearing loss

📊 When to Worry

  • Jaundice <24 hours = ALWAYS pathologic
  • Bili rising >0.2 mg/dL/hour
  • Bili >95th percentile on nomogram
  • Direct bili >1 mg/dL = hepatobiliary disease
  • Jaundice >2 weeks = needs workup
