📚 Overview

Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) is glucose intolerance first recognized during pregnancy. It affects 6-9% of pregnancies and increases risks for both mother and fetus.

Pathophysiology

  • Pregnancy = state of insulin resistance (hPL, cortisol, progesterone)
  • Normal: β-cells compensate with ↑ insulin secretion
  • GDM: Inadequate compensation → hyperglycemia
  • Peak insulin resistance: 24-28 weeks (hence screening timing)

🔬 Screening & Diagnosis

Universal Screening: 24-28 Weeks

Two approaches are accepted:

One-Step Approach (IADPSG/WHO)

75g OGTT - Fasting, then glucose load

Diagnosis: ONE abnormal value:

  • Fasting ≥92 mg/dL
  • 1-hour ≥180 mg/dL
  • 2-hour ≥153 mg/dL

Two-Step Approach (ACOG Preferred)

Step 1: 50g glucose challenge test (GCT) - non-fasting

  • 1-hour glucose ≥130-140 mg/dL → proceed to Step 2

Step 2: 100g OGTT (fasting)

Diagnosis: TWO abnormal values (Carpenter-Coustan):

  • Fasting ≥95 mg/dL
  • 1-hour ≥180 mg/dL
  • 2-hour ≥155 mg/dL
  • 3-hour ≥140 mg/dL
🔵 Early Screening (First Trimester): Test high-risk patients at first prenatal visit using standard diabetes criteria (FPG ≥126 or A1c ≥6.5% = overt diabetes, not GDM).

⚕️ Management

Glycemic Targets

TimingTarget
Fasting<95 mg/dL
1-hour postprandial<140 mg/dL
2-hour postprandial<120 mg/dL

Step 1: Medical Nutrition Therapy

  • Carbohydrate-controlled diet (3 meals + 2-3 snacks)
  • ~40% carbs, 20% protein, 40% fat
  • Avoid simple sugars, emphasize complex carbs
  • 85-90% achieve control with diet alone

Step 2: Pharmacotherapy (if diet fails)

Insulin (Preferred)
  • Doesn't cross placenta
  • Start if >20% glucose values above target after 1-2 weeks diet
  • Common regimens: NPH + rapid-acting (lispro/aspart)
Oral Agents
  • Metformin: Crosses placenta, long-term effects unknown
  • Glyburide: Higher macrosomia/hypoglycemia rates vs insulin
  • Use if patient refuses insulin after counseling

Fetal Surveillance

  • Growth ultrasound q4 weeks in 3rd trimester
  • Diet-controlled, well-controlled: Routine care
  • Medication-requiring or poorly controlled: NST/BPP starting 32 weeks

⚠️ Complications

Fetal/Neonatal Complications

  • Macrosomia: Fetal hyperinsulinism → ↑ growth, shoulder dystocia risk
  • Neonatal hypoglycemia: Check glucose after birth
  • Respiratory distress syndrome: Delayed lung maturity
  • Hyperbilirubinemia
  • Hypocalcemia, polycythemia
  • Stillbirth: Risk with poor control

Maternal Complications

  • Preeclampsia (2-4x increased risk)
  • Cesarean delivery (due to macrosomia)
  • Birth trauma
  • Polyhydramnios

Long-Term Risks

50% of women with GDM develop Type 2 DM within 5-10 years
  • Postpartum: 75g OGTT at 4-12 weeks
  • Then screen every 1-3 years lifelong
  • Lifestyle modification reduces progression
  • Offspring: Increased risk of obesity, DM

📝 Quiz

Q1: Universal GDM screening is performed at what gestational age?

A. 24-28 weeks
B. 12-16 weeks
C. 32-36 weeks
24-28 weeks - peak insulin resistance occurs at this time.

Q2: In the two-step approach, how many abnormal values on 100g OGTT diagnose GDM?

A. One
B. Two
C. Three
Two abnormal values on the 100g OGTT (two-step approach). One-step 75g OGTT requires only one abnormal value.

Q3: The fasting glucose target in GDM is:

A. <95 mg/dL
B. <110 mg/dL
C. <126 mg/dL
Fasting <95, 1-hr postprandial <140, 2-hr postprandial <120 mg/dL.

Q4: What percentage of women with GDM develop Type 2 DM within 5-10 years?

A. 10%
B. 25%
C. 50%
~50% develop Type 2 DM. Postpartum OGTT at 4-12 weeks then screen every 1-3 years lifelong.
