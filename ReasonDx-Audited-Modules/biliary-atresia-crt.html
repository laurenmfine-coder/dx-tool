🔑 Key Learning Points

  • Red flags: Jaundice >2 weeks + pale stools + dark urine = conjugated hyperbilirubinemia
  • Conjugated bili: >1 mg/dL or >20% of total is ALWAYS pathologic
  • US findings: Absent gallbladder, triangular cord sign, no duct dilation
  • HIDA: Uptake but NO excretion confirms obstruction
  • Timing is critical: Kasai before 60 days (ideally <45 days)
  • Kasai goal: Establish bile flow, delay transplant (not a cure)
  • Prognosis: ~50% achieve bile drainage; BA is #1 indication for pediatric liver transplant

📚 Embryology Connection

Biliary atresia is a progressive fibro-obliterative disease, not a true developmental malformation. The biliary tree forms normally but undergoes inflammatory destruction perinatally. The exact cause is unknown but may involve viral infection, immune dysregulation, or genetic factors.
