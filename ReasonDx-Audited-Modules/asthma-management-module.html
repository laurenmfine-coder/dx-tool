📋 Diagnosis

Definition: Chronic inflammatory airway disease with variable expiratory airflow limitation and respiratory symptoms (wheeze, cough, dyspnea, chest tightness)

Spirometry Criteria

Obstruction: FEV1/FVC <0.70
Reversibility: FEV1 increase ≥12% AND ≥200mL post-bronchodilator

🚨 Acute Exacerbation

Severity Assessment

FeatureMild-ModerateSevereLife-threatening
SpeechPhrasesWords onlyUnable

PEF>50%25-50%<25%
Red Flags (Impending Respiratory Failure):
  • Silent chest
  • Cyanosis
  • Bradycardia
  • Altered consciousness
  • Normal or rising PaCO2

Treatment

  1. Oxygen: Target SpO2 93-95%
  2. SABA: Albuterol nebulized q20min × 3
  3. Ipratropium: Add for severe exacerbation
  4. Systemic steroids: Prednisone 40-60mg × 5-7 days
  5. Magnesium sulfate: 2g IV for severe/refractory

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: Spirometry finding confirming asthma?

A. FEV1/FVC <0.70 with ≥12% reversibility
B. FEV1/FVC >0.80
Correct: A. Obstruction with significant reversibility confirms asthma.

Q2: First-line controller for persistent asthma?

A. Inhaled corticosteroid
B. LABA alone
Correct: A. ICS is first-line. LABA should NEVER be used alone.
