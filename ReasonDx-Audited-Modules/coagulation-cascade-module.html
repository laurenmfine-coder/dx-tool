⚡ The Cascade

INTRINSIC (PTT) EXTRINSIC (PT) XII→XIIa TF + VII→VIIa ↓ ↓ XI→XIa ↓ ↓ ↓ IX→IXa ←───────────────────────┘ ↓ IXa + VIIIa + Ca + PL ↓ ═══════ COMMON PATHWAY ═══════ X → Xa ↓ Xa + Va + Ca + PL ↓ Prothrombin(II)→Thrombin(IIa) ↓ Fibrinogen(I) → Fibrin ↓ XIII → Cross-linked

Key Concepts

  • Intrinsic: XII, XI, IX, VIII (all "in" the blood)
  • Extrinsic: VII + Tissue Factor (from "outside")
  • Common: X, V, II, I (fibrinogen), XIII
  • Vitamin K-dependent: II, VII, IX, X (+ Protein C, S)
"1972" = Vit K factors: 10, 9, 7, 2

DIC - Key Points

  • Widespread activation → consume factors AND platelets
  • Causes: Sepsis, trauma, malignancy, obstetric complications
  • Labs: ↑PT, ↑PTT, ↓platelets, ↓fibrinogen, ↑D-dimer, schistocytes
  • Treat underlying cause; support with products if bleeding

🎯 Quiz

Q1: Which factors are vitamin K-dependent?

A. V, VIII, XI, XII
B. II, VII, IX, X
C. I, V, VIII, XIII
B. "1972" = 10, 9, 7, 2. Also Protein C and S.

Q2: Patient has ↑PTT, normal PT. Mixing study corrects. Most likely?

A. Factor VIII deficiency
B. Lupus anticoagulant
C. Factor VII deficiency
A. Isolated ↑PTT = intrinsic pathway. Mixing corrects = deficiency (not inhibitor). VIII def = Hemophilia A.

Q3: Which anticoagulant is reversed with idarucizumab?

A. Warfarin
B. Rivaroxaban
C. Dabigatran
C. Idarucizumab reverses dabigatran (direct thrombin inhibitor). Andexanet reverses Xa inhibitors.

Q4: HIT causes:

A. Severe bleeding
B. Thrombosis
C. Only low platelets
B. HIT = antibodies activate platelets → THROMBOSIS despite low count. Stop heparin, anticoagulate with non-heparin agent.
