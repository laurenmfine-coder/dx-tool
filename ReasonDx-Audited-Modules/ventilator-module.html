📚 Ventilator Basics

Two Key Variables:
Oxygenation (PaO2/SpO2) → Controlled by FiO2 and PEEP
Ventilation (PaCO2) → Controlled by Minute Ventilation (RR × Vt)

Key Parameters

ParameterDefinitionTypical Range
Tidal Volume (Vt)Volume per breath6-8 mL/kg IBW
Respiratory Rate (RR)Breaths per minute12-20/min
FiO2Fraction of inspired oxygen21-100%
PEEPPositive end-expiratory pressure5-20 cmH2O
Peak PressureMaximum airway pressure<35 cmH2O
Plateau PressurePressure at end-inspiration (no flow)<30 cmH2O

Ideal Body Weight Calculation

Male: 50 + 2.3 × (height in inches - 60)
Female: 45.5 + 2.3 × (height in inches - 60)
Use IBW for Vt calculations, NOT actual weight!

⚙️ Ventilator Modes

AC/VC (Assist-Control / Volume Control)

  • Set: Vt, RR, FiO2, PEEP
  • Delivers set Vt with each breath
  • Patient can trigger additional breaths (all get full Vt)
  • Pro: Guaranteed minute ventilation
  • Con: Variable pressures; risk of breath stacking

AC/PC (Assist-Control / Pressure Control)

  • Set: Inspiratory pressure, RR, FiO2, PEEP, I-time
  • Delivers set pressure; Vt varies with compliance
  • Pro: Limits airway pressure
  • Con: Variable Vt; need to monitor closely

SIMV (Synchronized Intermittent Mandatory Ventilation)

  • Set number of mandatory breaths; patient can take spontaneous breaths between
  • Spontaneous breaths only get pressure support (if set)
  • Use: Sometimes for weaning (less common now)

PSV (Pressure Support Ventilation)

  • Set: Pressure support level, PEEP, FiO2
  • Patient initiates ALL breaths (no backup rate)
  • Each breath gets pressure boost
  • Use: Weaning, spontaneous breathing trials
  • Caution: Requires intact respiratory drive

PRVC (Pressure-Regulated Volume Control)

  • Hybrid mode: Targets Vt by adjusting pressure breath-to-breath
  • Combines benefits of volume and pressure control

📊 Initial Settings

Standard Initial Settings

ParameterInitial Setting
ModeAC/VC (most common starting point)
Tidal Volume6-8 mL/kg IBW
Rate14-16 breaths/min
FiO2100% initially, wean to SpO2 92-96%
PEEP5 cmH2O (higher in ARDS)
I:E ratio1:2 or 1:3

🫁 ARDS - Lung Protective Ventilation

  • Low Vt: 4-6 mL/kg IBW
  • Plateau pressure: ≤30 cmH2O
  • Higher PEEP: Use ARDSNet tables
  • Permissive hypercapnia: Accept pH ≥7.20
  • Consider proning if P/F ratio <150

Adjusting Based on ABG

ProblemSolution
PaO2 too low↑ FiO2 or ↑ PEEP
PaO2 too high↓ FiO2 first, then ↓ PEEP
PaCO2 too high (acidosis)↑ RR or ↑ Vt
PaCO2 too low (alkalosis)↓ RR or ↓ Vt
💡 "Oxygenation = FiO2 & PEEP | Ventilation = RR & Vt"

⚠️ Troubleshooting

🚨 High Peak Pressure Alarm

Peak - Plateau spread tells you where the problem is:

  • High Peak, Normal Plateau: Airway resistance problem
    • Kinked/obstructed ETT
    • Bronchospasm
    • Secretions
    • Biting on tube
  • High Peak AND High Plateau: Compliance problem
    • Pneumothorax
    • Mainstem intubation
    • Pulmonary edema
    • ARDS progression
    • Abdominal distension
💡 "DOPE" - Sudden Deterioration
Displacement (of ETT) • Obstruction (secretions, kink)
Pneumothorax • Equipment failure

Low Minute Ventilation Alarm

  • Patient not triggering (over-sedated, weak)
  • Large cuff leak
  • Circuit disconnect

Auto-PEEP (Breath Stacking)

Cause: Incomplete exhalation (COPD, asthma, high RR)
Signs: High peak pressure, hemodynamic instability
Treatment: ↓ RR, ↓ Vt, ↑ expiratory time, treat bronchospasm

🔓 Liberation from Ventilator

Readiness Criteria

  • Underlying cause improving/resolved
  • FiO2 ≤40%, PEEP ≤8
  • Hemodynamically stable (minimal vasopressors)
  • Adequate mental status (follows commands)
  • Able to protect airway
  • Manageable secretions

Spontaneous Breathing Trial (SBT)

Methods:
  • T-piece trial (no vent support)
  • Low PSV (5-8 cmH2O) + PEEP 5
  • CPAP only
Duration: 30-120 minutes
Pass criteria: Tolerates without distress, RR <35, SpO2 >90%, stable HR/BP

Rapid Shallow Breathing Index (RSBI)

RSBI = RR / Vt (in liters)
  • <105: Likely to wean successfully
  • >105: High risk of weaning failure

SBT Failure Signs

  • RR >35 or <8
  • SpO2 <90%
  • HR increase >20% or >140
  • Systolic BP <90 or >180
  • Anxiety, diaphoresis, accessory muscle use

📝 Quiz

Q1: To improve oxygenation, you should adjust:

A. FiO2 and PEEP
B. RR and Vt
C. I:E ratio only
Oxygenation = FiO2 + PEEP. Ventilation (CO2) = RR × Vt (minute ventilation).

Q2: In ARDS, target tidal volume is:

A. 4-6 mL/kg IBW (lung protective)
B. 8-10 mL/kg IBW
C. 10-12 mL/kg actual body weight
ARDS requires lung-protective ventilation: Vt 4-6 mL/kg IBW, plateau pressure ≤30. Higher volumes cause VILI.

Q3: High peak pressure with NORMAL plateau pressure suggests:

A. Airway resistance (secretions, bronchospasm, kinked tube)
B. Decreased lung compliance (pneumothorax, ARDS)
C. Ventilator malfunction
Peak-plateau gradient = airway resistance. High peak + high plateau = compliance problem. Suction, check ETT, treat bronchospasm.

Q4: An RSBI <105 suggests:

A. Likely successful extubation
B. High risk of weaning failure
C. Need for tracheostomy
RSBI (RR/Vt) <105 predicts successful weaning. >105 suggests high risk of failure.
