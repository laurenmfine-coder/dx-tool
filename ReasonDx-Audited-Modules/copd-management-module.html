📋 Diagnosis & Classification

COPD Definition: Persistent respiratory symptoms + airflow limitation due to airway/alveolar abnormalities caused by significant noxious exposure (usually smoking)

Spirometry Criteria

Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC <0.70 confirms airflow limitation

Note: Fixed ratio may over-diagnose in elderly; LLN (lower limit of normal) may be more accurate

💊 Stable COPD Management

Non-Pharmacologic Interventions

Most important:
  • Smoking cessation - ONLY intervention proven to slow FEV1 decline
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation - improves exercise capacity, QOL
  • Vaccinations: Influenza (yearly), Pneumococcal, COVID-19, Tdap, Zoster

When to Add ICS?

Consider ICS if:
  • Eosinophils ≥300 cells/μL
  • History of asthma or asthma-COPD overlap
  • ≥2 moderate exacerbations or 1 hospitalization despite LABA/LAMA
Avoid ICS if:
  • Repeated pneumonias
  • Eosinophils <100
  • History of mycobacterial infection

Other Medications

  • Roflumilast: PDE4 inhibitor for severe COPD with chronic bronchitis and frequent exacerbations
  • Azithromycin: Prophylactic for frequent exacerbators (not current smokers)
  • Mucolytics: Limited benefit; may reduce exacerbations in some

🚨 COPD Exacerbation

Definition: Acute worsening of respiratory symptoms requiring additional therapy
Triggers: Viral/bacterial infection (most common), air pollution, PE, cardiac events

Classification

SeverityDefinitionManagement
MildTreated with SABDs onlyOutpatient
ModerateSABDs + antibiotics and/or steroidsOutpatient or short admission
SevereRequires hospitalization or ED visitInpatient

Treatment - "ABC"

Antibiotics
Bronchodilators
Corticosteroids

Bronchodilators

  • SABA ± SAMA via nebulizer or MDI with spacer
  • Increase frequency (q1-4h as needed)

Systemic Corticosteroids

Prednisone 40mg daily × 5 days
(No taper needed; longer courses not more effective)

Antibiotics

Indications:
  • Increased sputum purulence PLUS increased dyspnea or sputum volume
  • Requires mechanical ventilation
Choices:
  • Uncomplicated: Azithromycin, doxycycline, or amoxicillin
  • Risk for resistant organisms: Amox-clav, respiratory FQ (levofloxacin)
Duration: 5-7 days

Oxygen Therapy in Exacerbation

Target SpO2: 88-92%
Over-oxygenation can cause CO2 retention (lose hypoxic drive, V/Q mismatch)

Non-Invasive Ventilation (NIV/BiPAP)

Indications for NIV:
  • Respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35, PaCO2 >45)
  • Severe dyspnea with accessory muscle use
  • Persistent hypoxemia despite O2
Reduces: Intubation rate, mortality, length of stay

💨 Oxygen & Advanced Therapies

Long-Term Oxygen Therapy (LTOT)

Indications (improves mortality!):
  • PaO2 ≤55 mmHg or SpO2 ≤88% at rest
  • PaO2 56-59 mmHg with cor pulmonale, polycythemia, or RHF
Goal: SpO2 ≥90%, use ≥15 hours/day
Reassess: 60-90 days after initiation

🧠 Test Your Knowledge

Q1: What spirometry finding confirms COPD?

A. Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC <0.70
B. FEV1 <80% predicted
C. Positive bronchodilator response
Correct: A. Post-bronchodilator FEV1/FVC <0.70 confirms airflow limitation consistent with COPD. FEV1 alone determines severity grade.

Q2: What is the ONLY intervention proven to slow FEV1 decline in COPD?

A. Smoking cessation
B. Inhaled corticosteroids
C. Long-acting bronchodilators
Correct: A. Smoking cessation is the only intervention that slows the accelerated decline in FEV1. Medications improve symptoms and reduce exacerbations but don't change disease progression.

Q3: Target SpO2 in COPD exacerbation?

A. 94-98%
B. 88-92%
C. >98%
Correct: B. Target 88-92% in COPD. Over-oxygenation can cause CO2 retention due to loss of hypoxic drive and worsening V/Q mismatch.

Q4: What is the steroid dose for COPD exacerbation?

A. Prednisone 40mg × 5 days
B. Prednisone 60mg × 14 days with taper
C. Methylprednisolone 125mg × 3 days
Correct: A. Prednisone 40mg daily for 5 days is standard. Longer courses and higher doses are not more effective and increase side effects.

Q5: When is NIV (BiPAP) indicated in COPD exacerbation?

A. All patients with exacerbation
B. Respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35, elevated CO2)
C. Only after intubation fails
Correct: B. NIV is indicated for respiratory acidosis (pH <7.35, PaCO2 >45), severe dyspnea, or persistent hypoxemia. It reduces intubation and mortality.
