🏥 SCIG/IVIG Nursing Administration Guide

Complete clinical reference for immunoglobulin administration

👩‍⚕️ For RN, LPN, Infusion Nurses
📅 Last updated: January 7, 2026 ⏱️ ~35 minutes ✓ Skills Checklist Included

1. IVIG Administration Protocol

📋 Pre-Infusion Checklist

☐
Verify physician order: product, dose (mg/kg), rate, premedications
☐
Confirm patient identity using two identifiers
☐
Review allergy history (especially IgA deficiency with anti-IgA antibodies)
☐
Check baseline vital signs: BP, HR, RR, Temp, SpO₂
☐
Verify IV access patency and appropriate gauge (20-22G preferred)
☐
Ensure emergency equipment available: epinephrine, diphenhydramine, oxygen
☐
Administer premedications as ordered (typically 30-60 min prior)
☐
Encourage oral hydration (unless contraindicated)
☐
Inspect IVIG product: check expiration, clarity, no particulates
☐
Allow product to reach room temperature if refrigerated

Standard Premedication Protocol

Medication Dose Timing Purpose
Acetaminophen 650-1000mg PO 30-60 min before Fever, headache prevention
Diphenhydramine 25-50mg PO/IV 30-60 min before Allergic reaction prevention
Hydrocortisone (if ordered) 100mg IV 30 min before For patients with prior reactions
IV Fluids (if ordered) NS 250-500mL Before and during Renal protection (elderly, renal impairment)

💉 IVIG Rate Escalation Protocol (Typical)

Note: Always follow product-specific guidelines and physician orders

Time Rate (10% product) Action
0-15 min 0.5-1 mg/kg/min Start low; VS q15min
15-30 min Increase to 2 mg/kg/min If tolerated; VS q15min
30-60 min Increase to 4 mg/kg/min If tolerated; VS q30min
After 60 min Maximum rate per product (often 8 mg/kg/min) If tolerated; VS q60min

📋 Monitoring During Infusion

☐
Vital signs: q15min × 1 hour, then q30min × 1 hour, then hourly
☐
Assess for: headache, chills, flushing, nausea, chest tightness, back pain
☐
Monitor IV site for infiltration, phlebitis
☐
Document any symptoms and interventions
⚠️ IgA Deficiency Alert: Patients with complete IgA deficiency (<7 mg/dL) who have developed anti-IgA antibodies are at risk for anaphylaxis. These patients should receive IgA-depleted products (e.g., Gammagard S/D) or SCIG. Check patient's IgA status before first infusion.

2. Infusion Reaction Management

🚨 Mild Reaction Protocol (Headache, chills, mild flushing)

1
SLOW or PAUSE infusion temporarily
2
Assess vital signs and symptoms
3
Give acetaminophen 650mg and/or diphenhydramine 25-50mg if not already given
4
Wait 30 minutes for symptoms to resolve
5
Resume at 50% of previous rate; advance slowly

🚨 Severe Reaction Protocol (Hypotension, bronchospasm, angioedema)

1
STOP infusion IMMEDIATELY
2
Call for help; stay with patient
3
Epinephrine 0.3-0.5mg IM (anterolateral thigh)
4
Position supine with legs elevated (unless respiratory distress)
5
Apply oxygen, establish IV access if needed
6
Give albuterol nebulizer for bronchospasm
7
Monitor VS continuously; prepare for repeat epinephrine q5-15min
8
Notify physician; consider transfer to higher level of care

3. SCIG Administration & Patient Teaching

💉 Approved SCIG Injection Sites

🟡
Abdomen

Avoid 2 inches around navel
Most commonly used

🦵
Thighs

Anterior/lateral aspect
Good fat pad for absorption

💪
Upper Arms

Posterior aspect
May need assistance

🔙
Upper Back/Hips

Lateral to spine
May need caregiver help

📋 SCIG Self-Administration Teaching Checklist

☐
Explain disease process and purpose of Ig replacement
☐
Review medication storage (refrigerate; allow to warm before use)
☐
Demonstrate hand hygiene and clean technique
☐
Teach site selection and rotation (2 inches apart; rotate systematically)
☐
Demonstrate site preparation (clean with alcohol; allow to dry)
☐
Show needle insertion technique (90° or 45° based on SC tissue)
☐
Explain infusion rate and volume per site (max 20-25mL conventional SCIG)
☐
Teach pump operation if using infusion pump
☐
Review local reaction management (ice, warm compress, site rotation)
☐
Explain when to call provider (fever, severe swelling, signs of infection)
☐
Verify return demonstration by patient/caregiver
☐
Provide written instructions and emergency contact information
💡 Tip for New SCIG Patients: Local site reactions (swelling, redness, itching) are common during the first few weeks but typically improve with continued use. Encourage patients to persist—reactions usually diminish significantly by week 4-8. Applying warm compress AFTER infusion can help.

4. Post-Infusion Care & Documentation

📋 Post-Infusion Checklist (IVIG)

☐
Monitor for 30-60 minutes after infusion completion
☐
Final vital signs before discharge
☐
Educate on delayed reactions (headache 24-72 hrs; call if severe)
☐
Schedule next appointment
☐
Remind about trough IgG lab (if due before next infusion)

Documentation Requirements

  • Product name, lot number, expiration date
  • Dose administered (total grams and mg/kg)
  • Infusion start and end times
  • Rate changes and times
  • All vital sign checks with times
  • Premedications given
  • Any adverse reactions and interventions
  • Patient's response and condition at discharge
  • Patient/family education provided
